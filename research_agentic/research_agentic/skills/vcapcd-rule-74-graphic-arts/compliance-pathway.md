# VCAPCD Rule 74.19 — Compliance Pathways and Exemptions

## Two Compliance Pathways

### Pathway 1 — Compliant Materials (Section B.1)

Use only inks, coatings, adhesives, fountain solutions, and cleaning solvents that meet the ROC content limits specified in Rule 74.19 Section B (see `voc-limits-by-ink-type.md`).

This is the standard compliance pathway. No additional equipment approval is required beyond the underlying Permit to Operate.

### Pathway 2 — Emission Control Equipment (Section B.6)

In lieu of complying with B.1 ink/coating/adhesive limits only (B.6 does not substitute for fountain solution or solvent cleaning limits), a facility may install and operate an emission capture and control system:

**Required performance standards:**
- Publication gravure: combined capture + control efficiency **≥ 85%** by weight during any period of continuous operation not exceeding 24 hours
- All other printing types: combined capture + control efficiency **≥ 80%** by weight

**Required system elements:**
- Collection system must vent **all drying oven exhaust** to the control device
- System must have one or more inlets for collection of **fugitive emissions**
- ROC emissions under this pathway may not exceed what would result from compliant materials per B.1

**Monitoring (if using thermal or catalytic incinerator):**
- Combustion temperature shall be **continuously monitored**

**Written approval required:**
- An Authority to Construct AND a Permit to Operate from the APCO are required for any emission control equipment used under this pathway

## Exemptions from the Rule (Section C)

### C.1 — Below-Threshold Sources (Applies to Sections B.1, B.2, B.3, and B.4)

The ROC content limits and methylene chloride prohibition do NOT apply to:

**C.1.a — Low-Volume Sources:**
> "Any stationary source that emits less than 200 pounds of ROC in every rolling period of 12 consecutive calendar months from graphic arts operations."

Exclusions from this calculation:
- Aerosol products
- Cold cleaners
- Vapor degreasers
- ROC from operations used exclusively for research, classroom instruction in schools, laboratory analysis, or determination of product quality and commercial acceptance

**C.1.b — Research/Education/QC Operations:**
> "Graphic arts operations used exclusively for research, classroom instruction in schools, laboratory analysis, or determination of product quality and commercial acceptance provided total emissions of ROC from such equipment are less than 200 pounds in any rolling period of 12 consecutive calendar months from printing, coating, adhesive, and solvent cleaning operations."

**Key note:** The C.1 exemptions correspond exactly to the Rule 23 Section F.13 exemption threshold. A facility that qualifies for the Rule 23 F.13 exemption (< 200 lbs/yr ROC, not requiring a permit) will also qualify for the Rule 74.19 C.1.a exemption from the ROC content limits.

### C.2 — Operations Completely Outside Rule 74.19 Scope

Rule 74.19 does not apply to:
- **Screen Printing** — governed by Rule 74.19.1
- **Operations applying ROC-containing ink, coating, or adhesive on ceramic materials**
- **Circuitry Printing**
- **Operations using darkroom equipment** associated with lithographic printing plate making
- **Operations applying inks used to indicate that sterilization has occurred**
- **Digital Printing Operations** — defined as using digital data to control deposition of ink, toner, or dye (includes inkjet, thermography, electrophotography, magnetography)
- **Proof presses** — used only for printing sample copies to check quality, color, and content
- **Prepress operations** — cleaning or processing of film processors, color scanners, plate processors, film cleaning, and plate developers
- **Blanket repair materials** in containers of **four ounces or less**
- **Aerosol coating products** in compliance with CARB regulations (CCR Title 17, Subchapter 8.5, Section 94522)
- **Cleaning activities using Clean Air Solvents** (as defined in Section G.9 — solvents certified by SCAQMD under SCAQMD Rule 102)

### C.3 — UV Lamp and Electron Beam Processor Cleaning

The solvent cleaning requirements of Section B.3 do NOT apply to cleaning solvents used to clean ultraviolet lamps and reflectors, or electron beam processors.

## Recordkeeping Requirements (Section D)

### D.1 — Ink, Coating, and Adhesive File

Maintain a current file for each ink, coating, and adhesive in use and in storage containing:
- Material name
- Manufacturer identification
- Applicable product category from Section B.1
- Specific mixing instructions
- Grams of ROC per liter (or pound per gallon) less water and less exempt compounds
- Grams of ROC per liter (or pounds per gallon) of material

### D.2 — Fountain Solution and Cleaning Solvent File

Maintain a current file for each fountain solution and cleaning solvent in use and in storage containing:
- Material name
- Manufacturer identification
- Applicable solvent cleaning activity from Section B.3 for each cleaning solvent
- Specific mixing instructions if any
- Grams of ROC per liter (or pounds per gallon) of material
- For fountain solutions: ROC content and minimum recommended dilution (to allow calculation of ROC content percent by weight or g/L as applied)

### D.3 — Usage Records

> "Maintain records on a daily basis showing the amount of inks, coatings, adhesives, fountain solutions, and solvents used."

Exception: "If only compliant inks, coatings, adhesives, fountain solutions, and solvents are used, these records may be maintained on a **monthly basis** instead of a daily basis."

### D.4 — Exemption Substantiation Records (for C.1.a or C.1.b claims)

Must provide **monthly records** sufficient to substantiate the exemption claim.

**Ink emission record options (D.4.a):**

Option 1: Group all inks; use highest ROC content and minimum density (default density = **1.01 kg/L = 8.44 lbs/gal** when minimum density is unknown).

Option 2: Separate process inks and pantone colors; use specific ROC content and density for process inks; highest ROC and minimum density for pantone; OR highest ROC and minimum density for both.

Option 3: Itemize each ink and pantone color using specific ROC content and density values.

**Coating, adhesive, fountain solution, and solvent records (D.4.b):** Must itemize each material and use the specific ROC content and density value for each.

### D.5 — Control Equipment Records

If compliance is achieved through emission control equipment: maintain **daily records** of key system operating parameters as specified in the Permit to Operate.

### D.6 — Retention

> "Inventory, usage, and emission control equipment operation records shall be retained for a **minimum of two years** and shall be made available to District Personnel upon request."

## Violations (Section F)

> "Failure to comply with any provision of this rule, including recordkeeping requirements, shall constitute a violation of this rule."

Recordkeeping failures are independently enforceable violations — not just a secondary compliance issue.

---

## Specification Prohibition (Section B.10)

> "No person shall specify or require the use of any ink, coating, adhesive, solvent cleaner, or fountain solution, in a graphic arts operation subject to the provisions of this rule that does not meet the limits and requirements of this rule."

This provision applies to buyers/specifiers of materials — not just the printer applying them. A brand owner who contractually requires a printer to use a non-compliant ink can itself violate Rule 74.19.

## Test Methods (Section E)

| Material | Test Method |
|---|---|
| Inks, coatings, adhesives, solvents, fountain solutions (except publication gravure inks) | EPA Reference Method 24 + SCAQMD Method 303-91 (Revised Feb 1993, EPA Approved Aug 1996) |
| Publication gravure inks | EPA Reference Method 24A + SCAQMD Method 303-91 |
| UV-cured inks | ASTM Method D5403-93(2007) for ROC weight percent; ASTM D1475-98 for density |
| Cyanoacrylate adhesives | SCAQMD Test Method 316B (Revised Aug 1997) |
| Fountain solution alcohol content | SCAQMD Test Method 313-91 (Revised Feb 1997) |
| Capture and control efficiency | EPA "Guidelines for Determining Capture Efficiency" (Jan 9, 1995); 40 CFR 51, Appendix M, Methods 204-204F |
| Destruction efficiency | 40 CFR 60, Appendix A-6, Method 18; Appendix A-7, Methods 25 or 25A; or ARB Method 422 |

When multiple test methods are specified, noncompliance demonstrated by **any one** of the specified methods constitutes a violation.
