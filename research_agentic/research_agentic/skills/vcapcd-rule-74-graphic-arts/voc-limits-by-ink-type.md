# VCAPCD Rule 74.19 — ROC Limits for Graphic Arts Operations

Adopted 8/11/92, Last revised 06/14/2011.

## Applicability (Section A)

Rule 74.19 applies to:
1. "Any person who applies any ink, coating, adhesive, fountain solution, or solvent containing Reactive Organic Compounds (ROC) as part of a graphic arts operation."
2. "Any person who manufactures or supplies any ink, coating, adhesive, fountain solution, or solvent containing ROC sold for use in a graphic arts operation in the District."

**Note:** ROC and VOC are identical terms per Rule 74.19 Section G.47: "Reactive Organic Compound (ROC): As defined in Rule 2. ROC and VOC are identical and may be used interchangeably."

---

## Section B.1 — Inks, Coatings, and Adhesives ROC Limits

Limits are expressed as **grams of ROC per liter (pounds of ROC per gallon) of Coating, Ink, or Adhesive, Less Water and Exempt Organic Compounds:**

| Category | Limit (g/L) | Limit (lbs/gal) |
|---|---|---|
| Inks (general) | 300 | 2.5 |
| Flexographic Inks on Porous Substrates | 225 | 1.88 |
| Coatings | 300 | 2.5 |
| Adhesives | 150 | 1.25 |

**Special rule for low-solids products:** "For low-solids inks, coatings or adhesives, which have 120 grams per liter (1 pound per gallon) or less of solids, the ROC content is on a grams per liter of material basis."

This means the calculation basis changes for low-solids products — the denominator is the full volume of material rather than volume less water and exempt compounds.

---

## Section B.2 — Fountain Solution Limits (Effective January 1, 2012)

Post-January 1, 2012 limits by printing method (percent by weight and grams per liter):

| Printing Method | Condition | ROC Content (% by weight) | ROC Content (g/L) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Heatset Web-Fed Offset Lithographic** | No refrigeration, contains alcohol | 1.6% | 16 |
| **Heatset Web-Fed Offset Lithographic** | Refrigerated below 55°F, contains alcohol | 3.0% | 30 |
| **Heatset Web-Fed Offset Lithographic** | No alcohol in fountain solution | 5.0% | 50 |
| **Non-Heatset Web-Fed Offset Lithographic** | Use of alcohol prohibited | 5.0% | 50 |
| **Sheet-Fed Lithographic** (sheet > 11×17 in. OR reservoir > 1 gal) | No refrigeration, contains alcohol | 5.0% | 50 |
| **Sheet-Fed Lithographic** (sheet > 11×17 in. OR reservoir > 1 gal) | Refrigerated below 55°F, contains alcohol | 8.5% | 85 |
| **Sheet-Fed Lithographic** (sheet > 11×17 in. OR reservoir > 1 gal) | No alcohol | 5.0% | 50 |
| **All Other Presses** | No refrigeration | 8.0% | 80 |
| **All Other Presses** | Refrigerated below 55°F | 10.0% | 100 |

**Additional requirements for refrigerated systems:** "Any refrigerated chiller used shall be equipped with a visible or easily accessible temperature gauge, and the sensor shall measure the fountain solution temperature at the supply tank connected to the operating press."

**Alcohol prohibition:** "Effective January 1, 2012, no person shall use any fountain solution that contains alcohol in any Non-Heatset web-fed offset lithographic printing operation."

---

## Section B.4 — Methylene Chloride Prohibition

> "No person shall use a solvent for cleaning purposes if that cleaner contains any methylene chloride."

---

## Section B.5 — Required Cleaning Devices/Methods

Cleaning operations must use one of the following:
- (a) Wipe cleaning
- (b) Remote reservoir cold cleaner
- (c) Spray bottles or containers with maximum capacity of **16 fluid ounces** from which solvents are applied without propellant-induced force
- (d) Cleaning equipment utilizing a **closable solvent container** (must remain closed during cleaning operations except when depositing/removing objects and during non-operation except for maintenance)

If a solvent flow method is used: the solvent shall not be atomized.
If a solvent flushing method is used: the solvent shall be flushed through the system by pumping.

---

## Section B.6 — Emission Control Equipment Alternative

In lieu of complying with B.1 ink/coating/adhesive limits, a facility may use an emission capture and control system if:
- Combined efficiency of at least **85%** by weight for publication gravure during any period of continuous operation not to exceed 24 hours
- Combined efficiency of at least **80%** by weight for all other printing operations
- Collection system vents all drying oven exhaust to the control device and has inlet(s) for fugitive emissions
- ROC emissions are no greater than if compliant inks, coatings, and adhesives per B.1 were used
- If using thermal or catalytic incinerator: combustion temperature shall be continuously monitored
- **Written approval (ATC and PTO) from the APCO is required for this alternative**

---

## Section B.7 and B.8 — Storage and Disposal

- All ROC-containing materials including **used cleaning towels** shall be stored in **closed containers** that are nonabsorbent and do not leak.
- Waste ROC materials shall be disposed of in a manner consistent with Federal, State, and local hazardous waste regulations.

---

## Section B.9 — Manufacturer Labeling Requirements

Manufacturers of inks, coatings, adhesives, fountain solutions, or solvents subject to Rule 74.19 must include on the product container or data sheet:
- Material name, manufacturer ID, specific mixing instructions (if applicable)
- VOC content:
  - For inks, coatings, adhesives: g/L (or lbs/gal) less water and less exempt organic compounds; for low-solids: g/L of material
  - For fountain solutions and solvents: g/L (or lbs/gal) or percent by weight of material, plus recommended application dilution ratio
- Density (g/L or lbs/gal)
- Solids content of all low-solids products (g/L or lbs/gal)

## Key Definitions (Section G)

- **Low-Solids Inks, Coatings or Adhesives (G.29):** Any product containing 120 grams or less of solids per liter (1 pound or less per gallon) of material.
- **Porous Substrate (G.39):** Any surface or substrate permeable to water including but not limited to paper, cardboard, paperboard, and paper products coated with a porous material.
- **Nonporous Substrate (G.33):** Substrate whose surface prevents penetration by water including foil, polyethylene, polypropylene, cellophane, metalized polyester, nylon, or mylar. Paper-like substrates coated with a nonporous material are considered nonporous.
- **Graphic Arts Operation (G.20):** "Any packaging gravure, publication gravure, flexographic printing, screen printing, letterpress, ink jet, digital printing, or lithographic printing operation, or any associated cleaning, coating or laminating or adhesive operation to produce a printed product."
- **Specialty Flexographic Printing (G.59):** "Flexographic printing on polyethylene or polypropylene food packaging, fertilizer bags, or liquid-tight food containers."
