## Applicability (Rule 74.19 §A)

Rule 74.19 applies to:
1. Any person who **applies** any ink, coating, adhesive, fountain solution, or solvent containing ROC as part of a graphic arts operation.
2. Any person who **manufactures or supplies** any such material sold for use in a graphic arts operation in the District.

"Graphic Arts" means all screen, gravure, letterpress, flexographic, ink-jet, and lithographic printing processes or related coating or laminating processes (§G.19). "Graphic Arts Operation" includes the equipment in an uninterrupted series: printing presses, ovens, conveyors, flash-off areas, laminating equipment (§G.20).

## Inks, Coatings, and Adhesives — ROC Limits (Rule 74.19 §B.1)

Limits are in **grams of ROC per liter of coating/ink/adhesive, less water and less exempt organic compounds** (g/L LW&EOC), with lb/gal equivalent in parentheses.

| Category | ROC Limit (g/L LW&EOC) | lb/gal equivalent |
|---|---|---|
| Inks (general) | 300 | 2.5 |
| Flexographic Inks on Porous Substrates | 225 | 1.88 |
| Coatings | 300 | 2.5 |
| Adhesives | 150 | 1.25 |

**Low-solids special basis:** For inks, coatings, or adhesives with 120 g/L (1 lb/gal) or less of solids, the ROC content limit is applied on a **grams per liter of material** (as-applied) basis rather than the LW&EOC basis (§B.1, last paragraph).

**Key definitions:**
- "Low-Solids Inks, Coatings or Adhesives": any product with 120 g/L (1 lb/gal) or less of solids per liter of material (§G.29).
- "Porous Substrate": paper, cardboard, paperboard, or any paper product coated with a porous material (§G.39).
- "Nonporous Substrate": foil, polyethylene, polypropylene, cellophane, metalized polyester, nylon, mylar; paper-like substrates coated with a nonporous material are also nonporous (§G.33).

## Fountain Solution ROC Limits (Rule 74.19 §B.2)

**Effective January 1, 2012**, limits are by printing method and refrigeration status:

| Printing Method / Condition | ROC Content (% by weight, applied) | ROC Content (g/L, applied) |
|---|---|---|
| **a. Heatset Web-Fed Offset Lithographic** | | |
| No refrigeration, contains alcohol | 1.6% | 16 |
| Refrigerated below 55°F, contains alcohol | 3.0% | 30 |
| No alcohol in fountain solution | 5.0% | 50 |
| **b. Non-Heatset Web-Fed Offset Lithographic** | 5.0% | 50 |
| (alcohol prohibited in this fountain solution as of 1/1/2012) | | |
| **c. Sheet-Fed Lithographic (sheet > 11×17 in or reservoir > 1 gal)** | | |
| No refrigeration, contains alcohol | 5.0% | 50 |
| Refrigerated below 55°F, contains alcohol | 8.5% | 85 |
| No alcohol in fountain solution | 5.0% | 50 |
| **d. All Other Presses** | | |
| No refrigeration | 8.0% | 80 |
| Refrigerated below 55°F | 10.0% | 100 |

**Note:** ROC content of fountain solutions is based on the ROC weight divided by the **full weight of the applied solution including any exempt compounds** (footnote 1 to §B.2 table).

**Alcohol prohibition:** Effective January 1, 2012, no person shall use any fountain solution that contains alcohol in any **Non-Heatset web-fed offset lithographic** printing operation (§B.2, last paragraph).

**Chiller equipment requirement:** Any refrigerated chiller used shall be equipped with a visible or easily accessible temperature gauge; a sensor shall measure fountain solution temperature at the supply tank connected to the operating press (§B.2).

**Pre-2012 limits (historical reference only):**
- Fountain Solution (non-refrigerated): 80 g/L (0.67 lb/gal)
- Fountain Solution-Refrigerated (≤55°F at supply tank): 100 g/L (0.83 lb/gal)

## Prohibition of Specification (Rule 74.19 §B.10)

No person shall specify or require the use of any ink, coating, adhesive, solvent cleaner, or fountain solution in a graphic arts operation subject to this rule that does not meet the limits and requirements of this rule.

## Exemption Threshold (Rule 74.19 §C.1.a)

Subsections B.1, B.2, B.3, and B.4 (ink/coating/adhesive limits, fountain solution limits, solvent cleaning limits, and methylene chloride prohibition) **do not apply** to:

> Any stationary source that emits **less than 200 pounds of ROC** in every rolling period of 12 consecutive calendar months from graphic arts operations.

**Exclusions from the 200-lb exemption calculation:**
- Emissions from aerosol products, cold cleaners, and vapor degreasers are NOT counted.
- Emissions from graphic arts operations used exclusively for research, classroom instruction, laboratory analysis, or determination of product quality/commercial acceptance are NOT counted.

**Decision tree — does the 200-lb exemption apply?**
1. Sum ROC emissions from all graphic arts operations (printing, coating, laminating, adhesive application, solvent cleaning of application equipment) over any rolling 12-month period.
2. Exclude aerosol products, cold cleaners, vapor degreasers.
3. Exclude research/classroom/lab/QC operations (but only if total from those operations is also < 200 lbs — see §C.1.b).
4. If the sum is < 200 lbs → exemption applies to §B.1, B.2, B.3, B.4.
5. If the sum is ≥ 200 lbs → all §B limits and requirements apply.

**Recordkeeping if claiming exemption:** Monthly records sufficient to substantiate the claim are required (§D.4); see recordkeeping.md.

## Operations Entirely Outside Rule 74.19 Scope (§C.2)

These operations are not subject to Rule 74.19 at all (they have separate rules or are excluded):
- Screen Printing → subject to Rule 74.19.1
- Inks on ceramic materials
- Circuitry Printing
- Darkroom equipment (lithographic plate making)
- Sterilization indicating inks
- **Digital Printing Operations** (inkjet, thermography, electrophotography, magnetography)
- Proof presses
- Prepress operations (film processors, color scanners, plate processors, film cleaning, plate developers)
- Blanket repair materials in containers of 4 oz or less
- Aerosol coating products compliant with Cal. Code of Regulations Title 17, Subchapter 8.5, §94522
- Cleaning activities using Clean Air Solvents (as defined in §G.9 — solvents certified by SCAQMD under SCAQMD Rule 102)

## Test Methods for VOC Content (Rule 74.19 §E)

| Material | Test Method |
|---|---|
| Inks, coatings, adhesives, fountain solutions, solvents (except publication gravure inks) | EPA Reference Method 24; SCAQMD Method 303-91 for exempt compounds |
| Publication gravure inks | EPA Reference Method 24A; SCAQMD Method 303-91 |
| Ultraviolet-cured inks | ASTM Method D5403-93(2007) for ROC weight percent; ASTM D1475-98 for density |
| Alcohol content of fountain solutions | SCAQMD Test Method 313-91 |
| Cyanoacrylate adhesives | SCAQMD Test Method 316B (Revised August 1997) |

## ROC Content Calculation Formulas (Rule 74.19 §G.17–18)

**G/L LW&EOC (for normal-solids inks/coatings/adhesives):**
```
(Ws - Ww - Wes) / (Vm - Vw - Ves)
```
Where: Ws = weight of volatile compounds (g), Ww = weight of water (g), Wes = weight of exempt compounds (g), Vm = volume of material (L), Vw = volume of water (L), Ves = volume of exempt compounds (L).

**G/L of material (for low-solids inks/coatings, as applied):**
```
(Ws - Ww - Wes) / Vm
```

VOC and ROC are defined as equivalent and interchangeable in Rule 74.19 (§G.63, §G.47).
