## What Is a UST

**HSC § 25281 definition:** "Any one or combination of tanks, including pipes connected thereto, that is used for the storage of hazardous substances and that is substantially or totally beneath the surface of the ground."

**Hazardous substances** covered include: substances listed by the California Labor Code Director, substances defined in applicable regulations, materials classified by NFPA as flammable or combustible liquids, and federally regulated substances under 42 U.S.C. § 6991(7). Petroleum (gasoline, diesel, fuel oil) is the most common UST contents.

## Exclusions from UST Definition (HSC § 25281)

The following are **not** USTs under California law:
- A tank with capacity ≤ 1,100 gallons located on a farm, storing motor vehicle fuel used primarily for agricultural purposes and not for resale
- A tank of ≤ 1,100 gallons at a farm or residence storing home heating oil for consumptive use
- Sumps, separators, storm drains, catch basins, oil field gathering lines, refinery pipelines, lagoons, evaporation ponds, well cellars, and similar structures
- A tank holding hydraulic fluid or dielectric fluid for a closed-loop mechanical system

## Permit Requirement (HSC § 25284)

**HSC § 25284 states:** "No person may own or operate an underground storage tank unless a permit for its operation has been issued by the local agency."

Permits are issued by the **local Certified Unified Program Agency (CUPA)** — typically the county or city environmental health department or fire department. The State Water Resources Control Board (SWRCB) and CUPAs jointly administer the program: CUPAs issue permits and enforce requirements within their jurisdictions.

## Permit Application Requirements (HSC § 25286)

Applications use "a standardized form provided by the local agency" and must include:

| Required Information | Notes |
|---|---|
| Tank characteristics | Age, size, type, location, uses, and construction material |
| Hazardous substances stored | List all substances stored in each tank |
| Monitoring procedures | Describe the leak detection and monitoring system for each tank |
| Owner identification | Name and contact information |
| Operator identification | Name and contact information |
| Facility address | Physical address |
| 24-hour emergency phone number | Required as permit condition |
| State Board of Equalization registration number | For petroleum fuel tanks |
| Owner/operator of unified program facility location | For facilities co-located with other CUPA programs |

## Permit Conditions (HSC §§ 25284, 25286)

A permit requires compliance with "all applicable regulations adopted by the board pursuant to Section 25299.3" (California Code of Regulations, Title 23, Division 3, Chapter 16 — UST Regulations, effective January 1, 2026).

**Ongoing notification obligations** under the permit: the operator must inform the local agency of:
- Changes in tank usage (new hazardous substances added)
- Revised monitoring procedures
- Unauthorized releases within agency-specified timeframes
- Changes in operator identity

## Amendment Requirements (HSC § 25286)

"When storing hazardous substances not listed in the original application, the permittee shall apply for a new or amended permit within 30 days after commencing the storage."

## Ownership Transfer (HSC § 25284)

"Any person assuming ownership shall have 30 days from the date of assumption of ownership to apply for an operating permit" or submit the completed transfer form to the local agency. The incoming owner must "submit a completed form accepting permit obligations within 30 days of ownership transfer."

## Permit Fees (HSC § 25287)

Fees are set by the local CUPA or the state board (for state-administered facilities). Fee schedules vary by jurisdiction and tank count. Contact the local CUPA for current fee schedule.

## Single-Walled Tank Closure Deadline

**Critical deadline:** The SWRCB UST program requires **permanent closure of all single-walled underground storage tanks by December 31, 2025**. This is a post-2025 compliance milestone — single-walled tanks remaining in operation after this date are non-compliant. California Code of Regulations, Title 23, Chapter 16 is undergoing rewrite to address post-single-walled system requirements effective January 1, 2026.
