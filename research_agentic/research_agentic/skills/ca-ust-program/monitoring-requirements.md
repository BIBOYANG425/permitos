## Statutory Basis

Monitoring requirements flow from **HSC Chapter 6.7** (Underground Storage of Hazardous Substances) and are implemented through **California Code of Regulations, Title 23, Division 3, Chapter 16** (effective January 1, 2026). CUPAs administer and enforce these requirements as the implementing agencies.

HSC § 25292 establishes the core mandate: tank owners must install "a monitoring system capable of detecting unauthorized releases."

## The Four-Part Leak Prevention Program

Per the SWRCB UST program, the statewide Leak Prevention Program covers:

1. Tank installation and construction standards
2. Tank integrity testing
3. Leak detection requirements
4. Spill containment and overfill protection

## Recognized Monitoring Methods (HSC § 25292)

### Tank Integrity Testing
- Required at intervals set by regulatory authorities
- Must be conducted by **state-licensed tank testers** — per the SWRCB: "Tank integrity testing is required by law, must meet the requirements of the State Water Resources Control Board," and must be performed by testers licensed through the SWRCB Office of Tank Tester Licensing (CCR Title 23, Chapter 17)
- Cannot be performed by unlicensed personnel

### Groundwater Monitoring
- "Groundwater monitoring well or wells that are downgradient and adjacent to the underground tank system"
- Paired with soil analysis
- Used when groundwater depth and soil conditions permit direct monitoring of a release pathway

### Continuous Detection Systems (Electronic Monitoring)
- Leak detection and alarm systems in adjacent monitoring wells
- Subject to local agency approval
- Guidance on acceptable equipment specifications is published through SWRCB Local Guidance (LG) Letters — particularly **LG113** on leak detection equipment specifications

### Vapor Monitoring
- Vacuum, pressure, and hydrostatic monitoring methods are recognized
- Detailed specifications in LG Letters

### Statistical Inventory Reconciliation / Daily Inventory (Motor Fuels)
- For motor fuel tanks: daily gauging and inventory reconciliation is an acceptable method if records are maintained annually and reviewed quarterly
- Tank integrity testing is triggered when inventory shortages exceed specified thresholds

### Visual Inspection
Per HSC § 25292: "Provide a means for visual inspection of the tank system, wherever practical" — this is a supplementary method, not a standalone substitute for electronic or groundwater monitoring.

## Pressurized Piping Requirements

For pressurized delivery piping connected to USTs:

| Requirement | Deadline |
|---|---|
| Automatic line leak detectors | Required by December 22, 1990 |
| Secondary containment retrofit | Required by December 22, 1998 |
| Annual tightness testing | Ongoing, mandatory |

Exemptions from some piping requirements may apply to fiberglass-reinforced plastic or cathodically protected steel piping equipped with approved detectors and tested annually.

## Farm Tanks (Special Rules)

Tanks under 5,000 gallons used for agricultural purposes:
- Integrity testing required every **three years**
- Monthly gauging required
- Larger farm tanks follow standard protocols above

## Single-Walled Tank Deadline

**December 31, 2025** — all single-walled underground storage tanks must be permanently closed. After this date, double-walled (secondary containment) tank systems are required for continued operation. California CCR Title 23, Chapter 16 is being rewritten to address post-single-walled system requirements effective January 1, 2026.

## Operator Monitoring Obligations Under Permit (HSC § 25284)

Per HSC § 25284, permit holders must "monitor systems per Section 25299" and notify the agency of any unauthorized releases within timeframes specified by the local CUPA. The permit specifically requires:
- Ongoing leak detection monitoring per the approved monitoring plan submitted in the permit application
- Immediate reporting of a suspected or confirmed release

## Reporting Platform

Compliance monitoring data is submitted electronically through **GeoTracker**: https://geotracker.waterboards.ca.gov. GeoTracker is the SWRCB's system for UST compliance data and release tracking.

## Semi-Annual Reporting

The SWRCB publishes semi-annual Report 6 tracking program-wide compliance statistics. Quarterly data is available covering registration statistics and abandoned facility identification.

## Inspection and Enforcement

CUPAs inspect UST facilities and have authority to "issue permits for tank operation and to enforce UST requirements within their jurisdiction." The SWRCB provides state-level enforcement assistance to local agencies. Failure to maintain required monitoring is a permit violation subject to administrative, civil, and criminal penalties.

## Key Reference: LG Letters

The SWRCB publishes Local Guidance (LG) Letters that specify acceptable leak detection equipment and monitoring methods. **LG113** covers leak detection equipment specifications. CUPAs and owners should reference current LG Letters when selecting or evaluating monitoring equipment.
