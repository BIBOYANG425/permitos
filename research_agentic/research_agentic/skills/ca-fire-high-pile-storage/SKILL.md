## Triggers

- High-piled storage of combustible materials (solid-piled, palletized, rack, shelf, or back-to-back shelf storage).
- Storage area above the regulated size, or storage height above the regulated height.
- A project that adds racking/warehousing or increases storage height/area.

## Thresholds

| Factor | Expected threshold (verify in adopted code) | Primary source to fetch and quote |
| --- | --- | --- |
| High-piled storage area | generally > 500 sq ft of high-piled storage | CFC Chapter 32 (locally adopted) |
| Storage height (Class I–IV commodities) | generally > 12 ft | CFC Chapter 32 |
| Storage height (high-hazard commodities) | generally > 6 ft | CFC Chapter 32 |

## Exemptions

- Storage below the area and height thresholds is generally not "high-piled" — confirm the exact figures and commodity classification in the adopted code.
- Commodity classification (Class I–IV vs high-hazard / plastics) changes the height trigger — verify the commodity class against the fetched code.
- Local amendments may set stricter thresholds or additional permit conditions — the local fire authority's adopted code governs.
