# CalARP Five-Year Accident History Requirements

**Sources:**
- CalEPA CalARP overview — https://calepa.ca.gov/cupa/calarp/ (fetched 2026-06-03)
- HSC § 25535 — https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displaySection.xhtml?lawCode=HSC&sectionNum=25535. (fetched 2026-06-03)

- EPA RMP Rule — https://www.epa.gov/rmp (fetched 2026-06-03)

---

## What the Five-Year Accident History Is

Every RMP (Risk Management Plan) submitted under CalARP must include a **five-year accident history** covering all accidental releases of regulated substances from covered processes at the stationary source that occurred within the **five years preceding the submission date** of the RMP or its most recent revision.

This section of the RMP documents the facility's release track record and is a primary input to program level determination (a qualifying release in the prior 5 years disqualifies a process from Program 1).

---

## What Must Be Reported (40 CFR § 68.168 / Federal RMP Rule)

The accident history must include information for each accidental release that:
- Resulted in **deaths, injuries, or significant property damage on site**, **or**
- Resulted in **known offsite deaths, injuries, evacuations, sheltering in place, property damage, or environmental damage**.

### Required Data Elements Per Incident

| Data Element | Details |
|---|---|
| **Date, time, and duration** | When the release began and how long it lasted |
| **Chemical(s) released** | Name of regulated substance(s) involved |
| **Estimated quantity released** | In pounds |
| **Release type** | Gas, liquid, or both |
| **Weather conditions** | Wind speed, direction, temperature, stability class at time of release |
| **On-site impacts** | Deaths, injuries, property damage (dollar value) |
| **Known offsite impacts** | Deaths, hospitalizations, other medical treatment, evacuations, sheltering in place, property damage, environmental damage |
| **Initiating event** | Equipment failure, human error, weather, external event, etc. |
| **Contributing factors** | Process or equipment design issues, inadequate procedures, maintenance deficiencies, operator error, etc. |
| **Offsite responder notification** | Whether local emergency responders were notified |
| **Changes implemented** | What operational, equipment, or procedural changes were made as a result |

## Reporting Threshold — What Qualifies as a Reportable Release

A release must be reported in the accident history if it resulted in any of the following **on-site or offsite consequences**:

**On-site:**
- Death of any employee or contractor
- Injury requiring in-patient hospitalization
- Property damage of $500,000 or more (federal threshold; California may apply state thresholds)
- A release that activated emergency response procedures

**Offsite:**
- Any known death
- Any known injury (including chemical exposure requiring medical treatment)
- Evacuation or sheltering-in-place of the public or nearby workers
- Property damage
- Environmental damage (spills to surface water, soil contamination)
- A release reported to the National Response Center (NRC)

**Note:** Releases that had no offsite consequences and only minor on-site impacts below the thresholds above are not required to be reported in the five-year history. However, facilities should document close calls and near misses in their internal incident investigation records (required under the prevention program).

---

## Lookback Period

- The five-year period is calculated **backward from the date the RMP or revised RMP is submitted**.
- When an RMP is updated for any reason, the five-year history window resets to the new submission date.
- **Mandatory revision triggers** that restart the clock: any accidental release requiring emergency response, any change to a covered process, or every five years (the routine resubmission deadline).

---

## Relationship to Program Level Determination

The accident history directly controls Program 1 eligibility:
- If the stationary source has **any accidental release** of a regulated substance from a covered process in the preceding **five years** that caused **offsite deaths, injuries, or required response**, the process is **disqualified from Program 1**.
- A clean five-year record is a necessary (but not sufficient) condition for Program 1 classification.

---

## CalARP-Specific Context

California's CalARP program incorporates the federal five-year accident history requirements and may apply stricter state thresholds. The CUPA reviews the accident history as part of:
- Initial RMP review
- Periodic inspections
- Program level reclassification decisions under HSC § 25534

CUPAs may request additional incident details or internal investigation records during inspections, particularly for incidents that approached but did not cross federal reporting thresholds.

---

## Practical Notes

- Facilities should maintain internal incident logs with dates, substance, quantity, and response actions for **all** accidental releases — not just those meeting reporting thresholds — to support audit readiness.
- For each reportable incident, the facility must also document what changes were implemented in response; this is scrutinized during inspections as evidence of continuous improvement.
- The accident history is a **public document** once submitted in the RMP; it is accessible via the EPA's RMP database.
