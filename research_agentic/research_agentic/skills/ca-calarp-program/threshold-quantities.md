## Two Trigger Lists

### 1. California-Specific Regulated Substances (19 CCR 2770.5)
Substances listed in the California Accidental Release Prevention (CalARP) regulations that are NOT on the federal RMP list. Examples:
- Hydrogen sulfide: 1,500 lbs TQ
- Phosphine: 100 lbs TQ
- Several additional California-specific toxic and flammable gases

### 2. Federal RMP Substances (40 CFR 68.130, incorporated by reference)
**Toxic substances** (Program 1/2/3): ~77 substances with individual TQs ranging from 500 to 20,000 lbs
**Flammable substances** (Program 1/2/3): ~63 flammable gases and liquids, 10,000 lb TQ for most

## Program Level Determination
| Program | Applicability |
|---|---|
| Program 1 | No public receptors within worst-case scenario distance; no accidents in last 5 years |
| Program 2 | Not Program 1 or 3; default for most regulated processes |
| Program 3 | OSHA PSM-covered process OR NAICS codes 22111, 32211, 32411, 32511, 325181, 325188, 325192, 325199, 325211, 325311, 32532 |
