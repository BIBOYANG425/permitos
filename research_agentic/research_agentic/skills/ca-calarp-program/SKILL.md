> ORIENTATION ONLY. The triggers, thresholds, and exemptions below are *what to look for* — they tell the agent where to aim. They are NOT evidence. The agent must still fetch the cited primary source and quote it verbatim: the skill ORIENTS, the fetched regulation is the PROOF. Never cite a SKILL.md as evidence.

## Triggers

- A process at the stationary source holds a regulated substance (toxic, flammable, or CA-only).

## Thresholds

| What to look for | Expected orientation | Primary source to fetch and quote |
| --- | --- | --- |
| RMP required | A regulated substance is present above its per-substance threshold quantity | 19 CCR 5130.6 Tables 1-3 |

## Exemptions

- Thresholds are per-substance (no single number). Meeting a Table 1/2 federal quantity also triggers a federal RMP.
