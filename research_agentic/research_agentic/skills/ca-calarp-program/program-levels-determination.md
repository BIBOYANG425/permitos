## CalARP Program Background

The California Accidental Release Prevention (CalARP) program implements Health and Safety Code Chapter 6.95, Article 2 (§§ 25531–25543). It mirrors the federal RMP rule (40 CFR Part 68 under Clean Air Act § 112(r)) but adds California-specific requirements administered by Certified Unified Program Agencies (CUPAs).

**Key 2024 changes:**
- Regulations moved from Title 19, Division 2 to a new Division 5 (effective March 6, 2024) following AB 148, which transferred program oversight from Cal OES to **CalEPA**.
- SB 740 (effective January 1, 2024): Stationary sources conducting specified chemical manufacturing or CO₂ work must use skilled, trained workers for construction trades.

**Regulated substance applicability (HSC § 25532):** CalARP covers "covered processes" with regulated substances exceeding threshold quantities at stationary sources. "Regulated substance" means substances listed in 40 CFR § 68.130 (federal EPA list) **or** California-designated extremely hazardous substances. California's threshold quantity is the lesser of: (a) the federal threshold from 40 CFR § 68.130, or (b) the state threshold quantity adopted by CalEPA.

## Program Level Framework

Program levels are defined in 40 CFR Part 68 (incorporated into CalARP) and applied by the CUPA. HSC § 25534 references "Program 1, 2, and 3" classification governed by federal regulations.

### Program 1 — Lowest Tier

**Eligibility criteria (40 CFR § 68.10(b)):**
- The process has **not had an accidental release** of a regulated substance causing offsite death, injury, or response within the previous **5 years**.
- The distance to a toxic endpoint (for worst-case release scenario) is **less than or equal to the distance to the nearest public receptor**.
- **OR** — the stationary source is classified as Program 1 if the worst-case release scenario would not reach an offsite public receptor under any circumstances.
- Emergency response is coordinated with local emergency responders.

**RMP requirements under Program 1:** Hazard assessment (worst-case scenario only), 5-year accident history, coordination documentation, and basic RMP registration.

### Program 2 — Middle Tier

**Eligibility:** Processes not eligible for Program 1 and not subject to OSHA PSM (29 CFR § 1910.119) or Cal/OSHA PSM (Title 8 § 5189). In practice, Program 2 covers smaller or lower-hazard covered processes that do not qualify for Program 1 simplicity.

**RMP requirements under Program 2:** Hazard assessment (worst-case and alternative release scenarios), 5-year accident history, prevention program (more limited than Program 3), and emergency response program.

### Program 3 — Highest Tier

**Eligibility criteria:**
- The process is **subject to OSHA PSM** (29 CFR § 1910.119) **or Cal/OSHA PSM** (Title 8 § 5189), **OR**
- The CUPA determines there is a significant likelihood of accident risk and reclassifies the source to Program 3.

**RMP requirements under Program 3:** Full hazard assessment (worst-case + alternative scenarios), 5-year accident history, comprehensive prevention program (equivalent to PSM elements), and emergency response program.

## CUPA Determination Authority (HSC § 25534)

The CUPA (Unified Program Agency) has authority to adjust program levels:

**Reclassification upward (to Program 3):** If the CUPA determines there is "a significant likelihood that the use of regulated substances by a stationary source may pose a regulated substances accident risk," it may reclassify the source from Program 2 to Program 3. Implementation must occur within **12 months** of notification.

**Reclassification downward:** If the CUPA finds no significant accident risk, it may:
- Exempt the source from certain requirements, **or**
- Reclassify to a lower program level (Program 3→2 or Program 2→1).

**Special consultation:** For pesticide use at farms or nurseries, the CUPA must first consult with the **Department of Food and Agriculture** or the **county agricultural commissioner** before making a risk determination.

## Determination Sequence for a New Covered Process

1. **Is the substance a regulated substance?** Check 40 CFR § 68.130 + California's state threshold quantity list.
2. **Does the quantity at the stationary source exceed the threshold quantity?** If yes, the process is a covered process.
3. **Is the process subject to Cal/OSHA PSM (Title 8 § 5189)?** If yes → Program 3.
4. **Has there been a qualifying accident in the last 5 years?** If yes → Program 2 or 3 (not Program 1).
5. **Does the worst-case release endpoint reach a public receptor?** If yes → Program 2 or 3 (not Program 1).
6. **Default:** If not qualifying for Program 1 and not subject to PSM → Program 2.
