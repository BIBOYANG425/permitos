# VCAPCD Rule 10 — Modification and Revision Triggers

## What Counts as a Modification (Section A.1)

Any of the following requires a new or revised permit:
- Building, erecting, installing, modifying, relocating, or replacing any emissions unit.
- Any **physical change** to an emissions unit "which may alter the emissions of air contaminants."

The standard is broad: even changes that *may* alter emissions require an ATC.

## Triggers Requiring a Revised Permit to Operate (Section B.1)

Per Rule 10 Section B.1(a)-(d), a new or revised PTO must be obtained prior to operating:

| Trigger | PTO Required |
|---|---|
| New emissions unit | New PTO |
| Modified emissions unit | Revised PTO |
| Relocated emissions unit | Revised PTO |
| Replacement emissions unit | Revised PTO |
| Physical change that may alter emissions | Revised PTO |
| Operating under modified permit conditions | Revised PTO |
| Rule 23 revision removes existing exemption | New/Revised PTO before effective date |
| Transfer of ownership of any stationary source | New PTO prior to transfer |

## Ownership Transfer

> "A Permit to Operate shall be obtained prior to the transfer of ownership of any stationary source." (Section B.1(d))

This means a buyer cannot assume ownership and continue operations under the seller's permit. A new PTO application must be submitted and approved **before** the transfer closes for operations to continue legally.

## Rule 23 Exemption Removal

> "A new or revised Permit to Operate shall be obtained for a stationary source prior to the effective date of any revision to Rule 23 that removes the exemption from permit for the source." (Section B.1(c))

When VCAPCD amends Rule 23 to tighten an exemption, affected sources have until the rule's effective date to obtain a PTO — operations cannot continue without one once the exemption is gone.

## ATC Exemptions That Bypass the Modification Step (Section A.2)

Two scenarios allow a facility to skip the ATC and go directly to a PTO for modified or new units:

1. **Small stationary source** — new, modified, or replacement units at a small source (as defined by Rule 11) apply directly for a PTO.
2. **Short-distance relocation** — relocation within Ventura County where:
   - New location is ≤ 5 miles from previous location,
   - Source is small or medium (Rule 11), AND
   - No emission increase results.

## ATC Extension for Changed BACT Requirements (Section A.4)

When an ATC is pending and BACT requirements become more stringent before expiration, the applicant may seek a 2-year extension if they demonstrate:
- Current BACT requirements are not more stringent than original requirements, OR
- Current BACT requirements are not cost-effective given expenditures already made (per APCD cost-effectiveness guidelines).

Extension requests must be submitted **before** the ATC expiration date.
