# VCAPCD Rule 10 — Permits Required (except as listed in Rule 23)

Adopted 10/22/68, Revised most recently 4/13/04.

## Two-Step Permit Sequence

VCAPCD uses a two-permit structure for stationary sources:

1. **Authority to Construct (ATC)** — required before building, erecting, installing, modifying, relocating, or replacing any emissions unit.
2. **Permit to Operate (PTO)** — required before operating any emissions unit.

---

## A. Authority to Construct (Section A)

### A.1 — Requirement

> "A person shall not build, erect, install, modify, relocate or replace any emissions unit at a stationary source without first obtaining an Authority to Construct. An Authority to Construct shall be required for any new emissions unit, modified emissions unit, relocated emissions unit, or replacement emissions unit. An Authority to Construct shall also be required for any physical change to an emissions unit which may alter the emissions of air contaminants."

### A.2 — Exemptions from ATC Requirement

Two categories skip the ATC and go directly to a PTO application:

- **New, modified, or replacement emissions units at a small stationary source.**
- **Relocation within Ventura County** where:
  - New location is no more than five miles from the previous location, AND
  - The source is classified as small or medium (as defined in Rule 11), AND
  - There is no emission increase.

When an ATC is not required under A.2, the applicant shall apply **directly for a Permit to Operate**.

### A.3 — Contents of an ATC

The APCO shall list in the ATC:
- Description of the emissions units covered.
- Any reasonable conditions under Rule 29 to assure compliance with applicable federal, state, and local rules. Conditions may include source testing requirements, a requirement to apply for a PTO, or a requirement to obtain emission reduction credits.
- A statement that the ATC is not an endorsement or guarantee of compliance.

### A.4 — Expiration of an ATC

An ATC expires **two years from the date of issuance**, or when a PTO or Temporary PTO is granted or denied, whichever comes first.

**Extension:** The APCO may grant a **two-year extension** if:
- (a) BACT requirements in effect at expiration are not more stringent than those originally imposed, OR
- (b) The applicant demonstrates that more stringent current BACT requirements are not cost-effective, considering expenditures already made.

---

## B. Permit to Operate (Section B)

### B.1 — Requirement

> "A person shall not operate, use or offer for use any emissions unit at a stationary source without first obtaining a Permit to Operate or a revised Permit to Operate which lists such emissions unit in its current operating configuration."

A new or revised PTO is required before operating:
1. Any new emissions unit.
2. Any modified emissions unit.
3. Any relocated emissions unit.
4. Any replacement emissions unit.
5. Any physical change to an emissions unit which may alter emissions.

Additional triggers for a revised PTO:
- Operating under modified permit conditions.
- Prior to the effective date of any Rule 23 revision that removes an existing exemption.
- Prior to the transfer of ownership of any stationary source.

### B.2 — Contents of a PTO

The APCO shall list in the PTO:
- Description of all emissions units at the stationary source covered by the permit.
- Reasonable conditions under Rule 29 to assure compliance with all applicable emission standards.
- The amount of **Permitted Emissions** (calculated pursuant to Rule 29).
- A statement that the PTO does not allow operation in violation of any applicable State or Federal emission standard.

### B.3 — Expiration of a PTO

A PTO (except a Part 70 permit) **expires annually** and must be renewed per Rule 30.

A Part 70 permit expires if not renewed per Rule 30, notwithstanding its stated term.

### B.4 — Temporary Permit to Operate

During processing of a PTO application, the APCO may issue a **Temporary Permit to Operate**:
- Specifies conditions under which the unit may operate.
- Expires when a PTO is issued or when the PTO application is denied.

## Practical Application Process

*Source: https://www.vcapcd.org/permit-applications-and-instructions/ and https://www.vcapcd.org/air-pollution-control-permits-overview/ (fetched 2026-06-03)*

### Who Needs a Permit

Facilities of any size with equipment that could emit air pollutants, including:
- Dry cleaners, gas stations, facilities using solvents or paints
- Gas- or oil-fired equipment with capacity of 1 million BTU/hour or more
- Bulk petroleum operations, oil facilities, power plants, and aggregate operations

Permit triggers (per overview page):
- Before installing new equipment
- Before implementing new processes
- Before modifying existing equipment
- When ownership of a facility changes
- When equipment is moved to a new address
- When an operation method is changed

### Forms

| Permit Type | Form |
|---|---|
| ATC, or PTO without prior ATC | App001 — "Authority to Construct or Permit to Operate without Authority to Construct" |
| PTO when ATC was previously issued | App002 — "Permit to Operate - Authority to Construct Previously Issued" |

Forms and instructions are available at: https://www.vcapcd.org/permit-applications-and-instructions/

### What to Submit

- Cover letter with business and equipment details
- Completed, signed application form
- Equipment specification sheets and flow diagrams
- Street map and adjacent land use sketch
- Facility layout showing equipment locations
- Filing fee ($450 application filing fee per ATC/PTO; paid by check or online)

### Submission Methods

> "Permit applications can be mailed with a check for the filing fee to the district office or emailed with the receipt of the electronic payment of the filing fee to engineering@vcapcd.org."

- **Mail:** 4567 Telephone Road, Second Floor, Ventura, CA 93003
- **Email:** engineering@vcapcd.org (with electronic payment receipt)

### Timeline

Processing time "ranges from 30 to 180 days depending on the complexity."

### Fee Structure

- **Application filing fee:** $450 per ATC or PTO application
- **Processing fee:** Based on review time
- **Permit to Operate fee:** Due upon approval
- **Annual renewal fee:** Based on permitted emissions

See Rule 42 and the current VCAPCD Permit Fee Schedule for specific amounts.

### ATC → PTO Sequence

1. Submit ATC application (App001) with filing fee before construction/installation.
2. District engineer reviews compliance with local, state, and federal rules; evaluates BACT and offsets per Rule 26.2.
3. ATC issued with conditions. Construction proceeds.
4. After construction is complete, submit PTO application (App002) before commencing operations.
5. APCO issues PTO listing all emitting units, permitted emissions, and operating conditions.
6. PTO expires **annually**; renew per Rule 30.

**Small sources** (as defined by Rule 11): ATC not required; apply directly for PTO using App001.
