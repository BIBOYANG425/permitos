## When PHA is Required
Required for **every** process covered by PSM (any HHC above TQ). Must be completed **before** the process starts operations.

## Acceptable PHA Methods (8 CCR 5189(e)(2))
1. **What-If** — brainstormed list of "what if" failure scenarios
2. **Checklist** — structured list of known hazards checked against process
3. **What-If/Checklist** — combination
4. **HAZOP** (Hazard and Operability Study) — systematic deviation analysis using guide words (No/Less/More/Reverse/Other Than)
5. **FMEA** (Failure Mode and Effect Analysis)
6. **Fault Tree Analysis**
7. **Equivalent methodology** — must demonstrate rigor equal to above

## PHA Content Requirements
- Hazards of the process
- Identification of previous incidents with potential for catastrophic consequence
- Engineering and administrative controls
- Consequences of failure of controls
- Location (proximity to population)
- Human factors
- A qualitative evaluation of the range of possible safety and health effects if controls fail

## PHA Team Requirements
- Must include at least one employee with expertise in engineering and process operations
- Must include one worker who currently operates the process (or has recently)
- Team members need expertise in the PHA methodology used

## Revalidation
PHA must be **revalidated every 5 years** (or after major incident or process change). The revalidated PHA must be retained for the life of the process.
