# Cal/OSHA PSM Management of Change (MOC)

**Sources:**
- Cal/OSHA Title 8, § 5189(l) — https://www.dir.ca.gov/title8/5189.html (fetched 2026-06-03)
- Direct statutory text from § 5189(l)(1)–(5)

---

## The Core Requirement (§ 5189(l)(1))

> "Employers must establish and implement **written procedures to manage changes** (except for 'replacement in kind') to process chemicals, technology, and equipment, and changes to facilities."

MOC is subsection (l) of § 5189. It applies to all covered processes (see `covered-process-determination.md`).

---

## What Triggers MOC

**MOC applies to any change** to:
- Process chemicals (new chemicals, change in concentration, substitution of one substance for another)
- Process technology (changes to process parameters — temperature, pressure, flow rates; new reaction pathways)
- Equipment (replacement with equipment of different specification, new vessels, piping modifications, instrumentation changes)
- Facilities (physical layout changes that affect covered processes)

### The "Replacement in Kind" Exclusion

The regulation carves out **"replacement in kind"** — defined in § 5189(c) as "replacement satisfying design specifications."

If a pump, valve, or vessel is replaced with an identical unit meeting the same design specifications, MOC procedures are not required. If the replacement changes the specification in any way (different material of construction, different pressure rating, different capacity), it is a **change**, not a replacement in kind, and MOC applies.

---

## What MOC Procedures Must Address (§ 5189(l)(2))

Before a change is implemented, the written MOC procedure must require that the following be evaluated and documented:

| Required Element | Description |
|---|---|
| **Technical basis** | The engineering or technical rationale for the proposed change — why the change is being made and how it affects the process |
| **Safety and health impacts** | Assessment of how the change may affect process safety, employee health, or risk of release — including whether new hazards are introduced or existing hazards are altered |
| **Operating procedure modifications** | Identification of which operating procedures (§ 5189(f)) are affected and what updates are required |
| **Required timeframe** | The implementation schedule for the change, including how long interim conditions will be in place |
| **Authorization requirements** | Who must approve the change before implementation — the chain of sign-off authority |

All five elements must be addressed in writing before the change proceeds. There is no provision for retroactive MOC — the review must precede implementation.

---

## Employee Notification and Training (§ 5189(l)(3))

> "Employees involved in the process receive notification and training as **early as practicable prior to its start up**."

Requirements:
- Employees who operate, maintain, or otherwise work in or around the modified process must be trained on the change **before the modified process starts up**.
- "As early as practicable" means the training obligation is triggered as soon as the change is approved — employees should not encounter the modified process for the first time during startup.
- Training records must be maintained per the standard's training provisions (§ 5189(g)).

---

## Update of Process Safety Information (§ 5189(l)(4))

> "Changes affecting process safety information must update documentation per subsection (d)."

When a change modifies:
- Chemical properties or hazard information
- Process parameters (temperature, pressure, flow, inventory)
- P&IDs (piping and instrumentation diagrams)
- Equipment specifications
- Electrical classification drawings
- Relief system design

...the process safety information (PSI) documents must be updated. The PSI must reflect the **as-built** condition of the process at all times.

---

## Update of Operating Procedures (§ 5189(l)(5))

> "Changes affecting operating procedures must update documentation per subsection (f)."

Operating procedures must be revised to reflect the change before or concurrent with startup. Section 5189(f) requires that operating procedures be "accurate and current." A change that renders existing procedures inaccurate without updating them is a violation of both § 5189(f) and § 5189(l)(5).

---

## MOC Documentation Requirements

While § 5189(l) does not prescribe a specific form, best practice (and inspection expectation) is that each MOC package contain:

- MOC request form with description of the change
- Technical basis documentation (engineering review, calculations, drawings)
- Safety and health impact assessment (often a simplified PHA or checklist review)
- List of affected operating procedures with revision status
- Training records (signed by affected employees)
- Authorization signatures with dates
- Pre-startup safety review (PSSR) completion sign-off (§ 5189(i) — pre-startup safety review is a separate but related requirement that applies when a new process or modified process starts up)
- Updated P&IDs and PSI documents

---

## Interaction with Pre-Startup Safety Review (PSSR)

Section 5189(i) requires a **Pre-Startup Safety Review** before startup of new or modified covered processes. The PSSR confirms that:
- PSI is complete and accurate.
- Hazard analysis recommendations have been resolved.
- Operating, maintenance, and safety procedures are in place.
- Training of affected employees is complete.

MOC and PSSR are complementary: MOC documents the planning and authorization; PSSR is the gate check before the change goes live. Both must be completed for any change that triggers MOC.

---

## Common MOC Deficiencies Found in Inspections

| Deficiency | Explanation |
|---|---|
| Treating a spec-change as "replacement in kind" | If any specification changed, MOC is required even if the change seems minor |
| Retroactive MOC | MOC must be completed before the change, not after-the-fact |
| No training records prior to startup | Verbal briefings without documented training records do not satisfy § 5189(l)(3) |
| PSI not updated | P&IDs that don't match as-built conditions are a recurring citation |
| Operating procedures not revised | Even if the procedure "still works," failure to update after a change is a violation |
| Temporary changes without expiration | Temporary changes must have a defined timeframe (§ 5189(l)(2)) and must revert or be formalized as permanent changes |

---

## Regulatory Context

§ 5189(l) mirrors OSHA PSM MOC requirements at 29 CFR § 1910.119(l). Cal/OSHA's version is substantively equivalent but enforced by **Cal/OSHA** (not federal OSHA) at California workplaces. Cal/OSHA has jurisdiction over employee safety at facilities in California; federal OSHA does not enforce in California.

For CalARP purposes, Program 3 prevention programs must include MOC procedures equivalent to § 5189(l) (40 CFR § 68.75 for Program 3 MOC).
