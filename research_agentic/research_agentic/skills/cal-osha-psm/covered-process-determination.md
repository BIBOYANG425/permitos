# Cal/OSHA PSM Covered Process Determination

**Sources:**
- Cal/OSHA Title 8, § 5189 — https://www.dir.ca.gov/title8/5189.html (fetched 2026-06-03)
- Direct statutory text from § 5189(a), (b), (c)

---

## Purpose of the Standard

Section 5189(a) states: "These regulations contain requirements for preventing or minimizing the consequences of catastrophic releases of toxic, reactive, flammable or explosive chemicals." The standard targets "petroleum refineries, chemical plants and other facilities" to substantially reduce employee exposure risks.

---

## Primary Applicability Rule (§ 5189(b)(1))

A process is **covered** by Cal/OSHA PSM if it involves **any one** of the following:

### Category 1 — Acutely Hazardous Materials (Appendix A Chemicals)
Processes involving chemicals at or above the **threshold quantities listed in Appendix A** of § 5189. Appendix A lists specific highly hazardous chemicals (e.g., chlorine, ammonia, hydrogen fluoride, phosgene) with their individual threshold quantities in pounds.

### Category 2 — Category 1 Flammable Gases
Processes involving **Category 1 flammable gases** (as classified under GHS/SDS criteria) at or above threshold quantities.

### Category 3 — Flammable Liquids Below 100°F Flashpoint
Processes involving **flammable liquids with a flashpoint below 100°F (37.8°C)** present at one location in a quantity of **10,000 pounds (4,535.9 kg) or more**.

---

## Definition of "Process" (§ 5189(c))

> "Any activity conducted by an employer that involves an acutely hazardous material, flammable substance or explosive including any use, storage, manufacturing, handling, or on-site movement" or any combination thereof.

A process includes any group of vessels that are interconnected, and separate vessels that are located such that an acutely hazardous material, flammable substance, or explosive could be involved in a potential release.

**Practical implication:** A facility need not be manufacturing the chemical — storage, handling, and on-site movement of a covered substance at or above threshold quantity is itself a covered process.

---

## Definitions Relevant to Covered Process Determination (§ 5189(c))

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Acutely hazardous material** | "A substance possessing toxic, reactive, flammable or explosive properties" as specified in § 5189(b)(1) |
| **Flammable** | Liquids or gases per Section 5194(c) on site in quantities of 10,000 pounds or more |
| **Process** | Any activity involving an acutely hazardous material, flammable substance, or explosive (use, storage, manufacturing, handling, or on-site movement) |
| **Facility** | Buildings, containers, or equipment containing a process |
| **Replacement in Kind** | Replacement satisfying design specifications (used in MOC definition — not a change triggering MOC review) |
| **Normally unoccupied remote facility** | Facility operated periodically by visiting employees, with no permanent staffing, geographically isolated from other buildings or processes |
| **Major Accident** | Events involving fire, explosion, or substance release causing workplace fatality or serious injury |

---

## Exclusions from Coverage (§ 5189(b)(1) Exceptions)

The following are **explicitly excluded** even if the chemical and quantity thresholds are met:

| Exclusion | Condition |
|---|---|
| **Atmospheric-storage flammable liquids** | Flammable liquids with flashpoint below 100°F stored in atmospheric tanks or transferred while kept below their normal boiling point without chilling or refrigeration |
| **Workplace fuel consumption** | Hydrocarbon fuels used **solely for workplace consumption** (e.g., comfort heating propane, gasoline for motor vehicle refueling) — **only** if not combined with other acutely hazardous chemicals covered by the standard |
| **Retail facilities** | Retail facilities (as defined) |
| **Oil and gas well operations** | Oil or gas well drilling and servicing operations |
| **Normally unoccupied remote facilities** | Facilities operated periodically by visiting employees, geographically isolated, with no permanent on-site staff |
| **Explosive device final installation** | Installation of explosive devices into finished products |
| **Explosives repackaging** | Explosives repackaging (excluded per § 5189(b)(5)) |
| **Pre/post-manufacturing research** | Product testing, chemical analysis, scale-up research, failure analysis, quality assurance, and engineering model assembly conducted separately from manufacturing (§ 5189(b)(6)) |

## Covered Process Determination Checklist

**Step 1 — Identify substances and quantities:**
- List all chemicals present at the facility that are listed in § 5189 Appendix A (acutely hazardous materials).
- List any Category 1 flammable gases present.
- List any flammable liquids with flashpoint below 100°F and calculate quantity at any single location.

**Step 2 — Check thresholds:**
- For Appendix A chemicals: compare quantity present in the process against the Appendix A threshold quantity for that specific chemical.
- For flammable liquids (<100°F flashpoint): check if ≥ 10,000 lbs present at one location.
- Quantities should be determined based on the **maximum intended inventory** in the process.

**Step 3 — Apply exclusions:**
- Is the substance used solely as workplace fuel (propane for heating, gasoline for forklifts) without combination with covered AHMs? → Excluded.
- Is the facility a retail facility, oil/gas drilling operation, or normally unoccupied remote facility? → Excluded.
- Is the material stored in atmospheric tanks at ambient temperature (below boiling point without chilling)? → May be excluded for the flammable liquid category.

**Step 4 — Confirm process definition:**
- Does any activity at the facility involve covered substances at or above threshold? Uses include storage, handling, on-site movement — not just active manufacturing.
- Are interconnected vessels aggregated? § 5189 treats interconnected vessels as part of the same process.

**Step 5 — Result:**
- If any process meets Step 2 criteria and is not excluded under Step 3, the process is **covered** and all of § 5189(d)–(p) requirements apply.

## Phase-In of Requirements (§ 5189(b)(3)-(4))

- Subsections (d) (process safety information) and (e) (hazard analysis) had phase-in benchmarks at 25%, 50%, and 75% of covered processes by August 1997 milestones — these are now fully in effect.
- Subsections (f)–(p) (operating procedures through employee participation) became effective January 4, 1994.

---

## Relationship to CalARP

Processes covered by Cal/OSHA PSM (§ 5189) are automatically classified as **Program 3** under CalARP (40 CFR § 68.10(d), incorporated into CalARP). A covered PSM process is not eligible for Program 1 or Program 2 CalARP status.
