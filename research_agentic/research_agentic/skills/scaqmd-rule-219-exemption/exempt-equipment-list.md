# SCAQMD Rule 219 — Exempt Equipment List

## Purpose (Rule 219(a))

Rule 219 identifies equipment, processes, or operations that "emit small amounts of air contaminants that shall not require written permits." Exemptions are conditional — if equipment falls under the Exceptions (subdivision (e)), a written permit is required regardless.

**Key caveat:** Certain exempt equipment must still file under **Rule 222** (see operational-limits.md for which categories trigger Rule 222).

## Exemption Categories (Rule 219(d))

### (d)(1) Mobile Equipment

- Motor vehicles (California Vehicle Code definition, as of April 7, 2023).
- Marine vessels (Health & Safety Code §39037.1 definition, as of April 7, 2023).
- Motor vehicles or marine vessels using one engine for both propulsion and onboard equipment operation.
- Equipment mounted on a vehicle/vessel that **does not emit air contaminants**.
- Asphalt pavement heaters (mobile road maintenance/construction equipment). **Rule 222 may apply.**
- Mobile day tankers carrying fuel oil with organic vapor pressure ≤5 mm Hg (0.1 psi) at 70°F.

### (d)(2) Combustion and Heat Transfer Equipment

- **(A) Internal combustion engines** with manufacturer's rating **≤50 bhp**; OR engines ≤100 bhp used exclusively for electrical generation at remote two-way radio towers (no utility/gas within 0.5 mile, fired on diesel #2/CNG/LPG only). **Rule 222 may apply** to the remote-tower category.
- **(B) Stationary gas turbines / micro-turbines** with rated maximum heat input **≤3,500,000 Btu/hr**, provided: cumulative facility power output <2 MW AND CARB-certified at manufacture or in operation before May 3, 2013. **Rule 222 may apply.**
- **(C) Boilers, process heaters, or combustion equipment** with rated maximum heat input **≤2,000,000 Btu/hr (gross)**, fired exclusively on natural gas/methanol/LPG (or combination). Exceptions: ICEs, turbines, or equipment with non-combustion emissions (except food ovens ≤2 MMBtu/hr on natural gas where process VOC <1 lb/day). **Rule 222 may apply** for 1,000,000–2,000,000 Btu/hr units.
- **(D) Diesel-fueled boilers** ≤2,000,000 Btu/hr, fueled exclusively on diesel #2, located >4,000 ft elevation or >15 miles offshore, NOx output <1 lb/day, uses <50 gal/day, in operation before May 3, 2013. **Rule 222 may apply.**
- **(E) Portable diesel-fueled heaters** (space heating) ≤250,000 Btu/hr, designed exclusively for diesel fuel. **Rule 222 may apply.**
- **(F) Power pressure washers / hot water or steam washers** with diesel-fired heater/burner, ≤550,000 Btu/hr, equipped with non-resettable chronometer, NOx <1 lb/day, uses ≤50 gal/day. Not applicable to ICEs or turbines. **Rule 222 may apply.**
- **(G) Fuel cells** using phosphoric acid, molten carbonate, PEM, or solid oxide technologies; associated heating equipment either non-combustion or fueled on natural gas/methanol/LPG (including heaters >2 MMBtu/hr if supplemental heat ≤90,000 therms/year). **Rule 222 may apply** for sub-clause (ii).
- **(H) Test cells/test stands** for burners or ICEs using <800 gal diesel/year AND <3,500 gal gasoline/year (or equivalent emissions from other fuels).
- **(I) ICEs used exclusively for training** at educational institutions.
- **(J) Portable combustion equipment** registered under PERP (see (d)(18)).

### (d)(3) Structures and Equipment — General

- **(A)** Structural changes that cannot change the quality, nature, or quantity of air contaminant emissions.
- **(B)** Repairs or maintenance not involving structural changes to permitted equipment.
- **(C)** Replacement of **identical equipment** at a **non-major source** where PO was previously granted (exception: floating-roof tank seals).
- **(D)** Routine maintenance/repair/replacement at a **federal major source** consistent with U.S. EPA guidance.
- **(E)** Replacement of floating-roof tank seals with types approved by Executive Officer as compliant with Rule 463.
- **(F)** Equipment in residential structures ≤4 families used by owner/occupant.
- **(G)** Laboratory testing and quality control equipment (chemical/physical analysis only); associated venting control equipment.
- **(H)** Non-production bench-scale research equipment and associated venting.
- **(I)** Vacuum-producing devices in laboratory operations or with other exempt equipment.
- **(J)** Vacuum-cleaning systems for industrial, commercial, or residential housekeeping.
- **(K)** Hoods, stacks, or ventilators.
- **(L)** Passive and intermittently operated active methane venting systems around residential structures.
- **(M)** Sub-slab ventilation systems <200 scfm aggregate, vacuum pits ≤18 inches below slab bottom, inlet TOC concentration ≤15 ppmv as hexane, connected to carbon adsorber sized for ≥200 scfm.

### (d)(4) Utility Equipment — General

- **(A)** Comfort air conditioning/ventilation not removing equipment-generated contaminants (also exempt under (d)(2)(C) or (D)).
- **(B)** Refrigeration units (except those used in/as air pollution control equipment).
- **(C)** Water cooling towers and ponds not used for evaporative cooling of process water or barometric jet/condenser water, containing no chromium: (i) comfort cooling towers; (ii) industrial cooling towers. **Rule 222 may apply** to industrial cooling towers.
- **(D)** Ozone generation equipment for cooling tower water or water treatment processes.
- **(E)** Steam cleaning equipment (also exempt under (d)(2)(C) or (D)).
- **(F)** Space heating equipment (also exempt under (d)(2)(C) or (D)).
- **(G)** Compressed/held Purchased Quality Natural Gas equipment (ICE also exempt under (d)(2)(A)).
- **(H)** Emergency ventilation systems to scrub ammonia from refrigeration during upsets.
- **(I)** Emergency ventilation systems for compressed gas storage failure containment.
- **(J)** Passive carbon adsorbers ≤120 gal vessel capacity, no mechanical ventilation, for odor control at wastewater treatment, food waste slurry, or sewer systems.
- **(K)** Refrigerant recovery/recycling units (not reclaiming facilities).
- **(L)** Carbon arc lighting (also exempt under (d)(2)(A)).
- **(M)** Gas-insulated electrical equipment ≤245 kV with VOC-containing insulating gas and manufacturer-rated maximum leak rate <1%/year.

### (d)(5) Glass, Ceramic, Metallurgical Processing and Fabrication

- **(A)** Crucible/pot furnaces <7,400 cm³ capacity of any molten metal.
- **(B)** Crucible, pot, or induction furnaces ≤450 kg (992 lbs) each, no sweating/distilling, also exempt under (d)(2)(C), processing only: aluminum (>50%), magnesium (>50%), tin (>50%), zinc (>50%), copper (>50%), precious metals, or ceramic/glass/porcelain — and no alloying elements of arsenic, beryllium, cadmium, chromium, or lead.
- **(C)** Molds for metal casting.
- **(D)** Inspection equipment for metal, plastic, glass, or ceramic products.
- **(E)** Ovens for curing epoxy resin potting materials/castings (also exempt under (d)(2)(C)).
- **(F)** Hand-held or automatic brazing/soldering equipment using ≤1 qt/day or ≤22 qt/calendar month of VOC material; not including hot oil/hot air/vapor phase solder levelers.
- **(G)** Brazing ovens with no VOC (except flux) in processed materials (also exempt under (d)(2)(C)).
- **(H)** Welding, oxygen/gas-fuel cutting, hand-held plasma-arc cutting, hand-held laser cutting, laser etching/engraving and associated control equipment. Not applicable to cutting stainless steel or alloys with ≥0.1% Cr/Ni/Cd/Pb (except maintenance/repair), or laser cutting/etching rated >400 watts.
- **(I)** Sintering of metal (excluding lead) or glass (no coke or limestone) (also exempt under (d)(2)(C)).
- **(J)** Mold forming for foundry sand with no heat and no volatile organic materials.
- **(K)** Metal forming/heating for forging, rolling, pressing, drawing — lubricants ≤50 g VOC/L or VOC composite partial pressure ≤20 mm Hg at 20°C (also exempt under (d)(2)(C) or (D)).
- **(L)** Heat treatment / water quench tanks for glass or metals (no VOC); case hardening, carburizing, cyaniding, nitriding, carbonitriding, siliconizing, diffusion treating (combustion equipment also exempt under (d)(2)(C) or (D)).
- **(M)** Ladles for pouring molten metals.
- **(N)** Tumblers for cleaning or deburring solid materials.
- **(O)** Die casting machines (except copper-base alloys with integral furnace >450 kg, or machines using furnace not exempt under (d)(2)(C)).
- **(P)** Porcelain/vitreous enameling furnaces/ovens (also exempt under (d)(2)(C)).
- **(Q)** Wax burnout kilns <0.2 m³ (7.0 ft³) internal volume; kilns exclusively for firing ceramic ware (also exempt under (d)(2)(C)).
- **(R)** Shell-core and shell-mold manufacturing machines.
- **(S)** Furnaces for melting titanium in closed evacuated chambers (also exempt under (d)(2)(C)).
- **(T)** Vacuum metallizing chambers (electrically heated or using exempt combustion) with mist eliminator or no visible vacuum exhaust emissions.

### (d)(6) Abrasive Blasting Equipment

- **(A)** Blast cleaning cabinets using water-abrasive suspension.
- **(B)** Manually operated blast cabinets with ≥90% overall control efficiency, ≤1.5 m³ (53 ft³) blast section internal volume, vented to dust filter.
- **(C)** Enclosed equipment for shot blast removal of rubber/plastic flashing at sub-zero temperatures.
- **(D)** Shot peening operations using a flywheel.
- **(E)** Portable sand/water blasters with ≥66% water by volume during operation; associated ICE also exempt under (d)(2)(A).

### (d)(7) Mechanical Equipment

- **(A)** Buffing, polishing, carving, mechanical cutting, drilling, machining, pressing, routing, sanding, stamping, surface grinding, or turning equipment using lubricants/coolants/cutting oils ≤50 g VOC/L or VOC partial pressure ≤20 mm Hg at 20°C. Not applicable to asphalt pavement grinders or portable asphalt recycling equipment.
- **(B)** Wood shredding, extruding, handling, or storing wood chips/sawdust/shavings (source wood not painted, not treated for exterior exposure, not commingled with C&D waste). ICEs >50 bhp powering this equipment are excluded; organic waste from gardening/agricultural/landscaping is excluded.
- **(C)** Milling or grinding coatings or molding compounds in paste form.
- **(D)** Separation/segregation of plastic materials for recycling (no mechanical cutting/shredding/grinding; no odors).

### (d)(8) Printing and Reproduction Equipment

- **(A) Graphic arts operations** (printing, coating, laminating, associated dryers/curers, APCDs) provided dryers are exempt under (d)(2)(C) and control equipment is not required for source-specific rule compliance, AND meeting ANY ONE of:
  - (i) Uncontrolled VOC ≤**3 lb/day** or ≤66 lb/calendar month;
  - (ii) Plastisol inks/coatings/adhesives + VOC solvents (incl. cleanup) ≤**6 gal/day** or ≤132 gal/month;
  - (iii) UV/EB/LED (non-solvent, non-waterborne) inks/coatings/adhesives + VOC solvents ≤**6 gal/day** or ≤132 gal/month;
  - (iv) Other inks/coatings/adhesives, fountain solutions + VOC solvents ≤**2 gal/day** or ≤44 gal/month; or
  - (v) All inks/coatings/adhesives/fountain solutions ≤50 g VOC/L AND all cleanup solvents ≤25 g VOC/L AND total VOC ≤**1 ton/calendar year**. **Rule 222 may apply** for this sub-clause.
- **(B)** Photographic process equipment (except wet gate printing using perchloroethylene).
- **(C)** Laser printing lithographic equipment.
- **(D)** Printing for training/non-production at educational institutions.
- **(E)** Flexographic plate making and processing equipment.
- **(F)** Corona treating equipment for surface treatment in printing/laminating/coating operations.
- **(G)** Hand application of printing materials (squeegees, screens, stamps, stencils, hand tools) unless control equipment required for source-specific rule compliance.
- **(H)** Addition of UV/EB/LED or other curing/drying technology to existing permitted graphic arts equipment, provided: consistent with existing PO description, compliant with existing PO conditions, no physical change to APCDs or permanent total enclosure, no added Rule 1401 TACs, coatings ≤50 g VOC/L, cleanup solvents ≤25 g VOC/L.

### (d)(9) Pharmaceuticals, Cosmetics, and Food Processing

- **(A)** Smokehouses ≤2 m² (21.5 ft²) horizontal inside cross-sectional area.
- **(B)** Smokehouses using liquid smoke exclusively, completely enclosed, no vents.
- **(C)** Confection cookers (edible, human consumption) (also exempt under (d)(2)(C)).
- **(D)** Grinding, blending, or packaging for tea, cocoa, roasted coffee, flavor/fragrance extraction, dried flowers, or spices using <1 gal/day or <22 gal/month VOC solvents.
- **(E)** Food preparation equipment at **eating establishments**. **Rule 222 may apply** to commercial charbroilers.
- **(F)** Conveyors/processors for bakeries, noodles, macaroni, pasta, food/drink mixes (edible, human consumption; <1 gal/day or <22 gal/month VOC solvents; also exempt under (d)(2)(C) or (D)). Excludes outside storage bins.
- **(G)** Cooking kettles (entire product edible, human consumption). Excludes deep frying at non-eating establishments.
- **(H)** Coffee roasting equipment ≤15 kg maximum batch capacity.
- **(I)** Tableting/packaging vitamins, or coating vitamins/herbs/dietary supplements using waterborne solutions ≤25 g VOC/L, or facility uses <1 gal/day or <22 gal/month VOC solvents.
- **(J)** Tableting/packaging pharmaceuticals and cosmetics, or coating pharmaceutical tablets: waterborne solutions ≤25 g VOC/L, or facility uses <1 gal/day or <22 gal/month VOC solvents.
- **(K)** Modified atmosphere food packaging using gas mixtures ≤0.4% CO by volume.
- **(L)** Charbroilers/barbecue grills at residential units for non-commercial use by owner/occupant.
- **(M)** Beer brewing equipment at breweries producing <1,000,000 gal/year (all equipment also exempt under (d)(2)(C); cleaning equipment exempt under (d)(15)). Excludes boilers.
- **(N)** Dehydrated meat manufacturing: oven electric or ≤2 MMBtu/hr natural gas, operating temperature <190°F, non-combustion VOC and PM emissions each ≤1 lb/day.
- **(O)** Food ovens ≤325,000 Btu/hr natural gas. Not applicable to ovens baking uncooked yeast-containing products.

### (d)(10) Plastics, Composite, and Rubber Processing

- **(A)** Presses/molds for curing/post-curing/forming composite or plastic products with **no VOC or chlorinated blowing agent**.
- **(B)** Presses/molds ≤26 inch ram diameter for curing/forming rubber or composite rubber, not operating above 400°F.
- **(C)** Ovens exclusively for forming plastics/composites (no foam expanding/forming) (also exempt under (d)(2)(C)).
- **(D)** Equipment for softening/annealing plastics (also exempt under (d)(2)(C) or (D)). Excludes expanded polystyrene recycling.
- **(E)** Extrusion of rubber or plastics with **no organic plasticizer**; pelletizing polystyrene foam scrap. Excludes extruding/pelletizing acrylics, PVC, polystyrene, and copolymers.
- **(F)** Injection/blow molding for rubber or plastics with no blowing agent, or using only compressed air, water, or CO₂ as blowing agent.
- **(G)** Mixers, roll mills, calendars for rubber/plastics: **no powder-form materials** and **no VOC-containing solvents/diluents/thinners**.
- **(H)** Ovens for curing vinyl plastisols by closed-mold curing process (also exempt under (d)(2)(C)).
- **(I)** Equipment for conveying/storing plastic materials (not in powder form).
- **(J)** Hot wire cutting of expanded polystyrene foam and woven polyester film.
- **(K)** Photocurable stereolithography and associated post-curing equipment.
- **(L)** Laser sintering of nylon or plastic powders (also exempt under (d)(2)(C)).
- **(M) Roller-to-roller coating systems creating 3D images**, if meeting ANY ONE of:
  - (i) VOC ≤**3 lb/day** or ≤66 lb/month;
  - (ii) Coatings ≤25 g VOC/L used at ≤12 gal/day or ≤264 gal/month; or
  - (iii) Coatings ≤50 g VOC/L AND cleanup solvents ≤25 g VOC/L AND total VOC ≤**1 ton/year**. **Rule 222 may apply.**

### (d)(11) Mixing, Blending, and Packaging Equipment

- **(A)** Batch mixers ≤55 gallons (7.35 ft³) capacity and associated filling equipment.
- **(B)** Mixing/blending with **no VOC-containing solvents** and **no powder-form materials**, and associated filling equipment.
- **(C)** Mixing/blending water emulsions of asphalt, grease, oils, or waxes with **no powder or fiber form materials**.
- **(D)** Blending/grinding/mixing/thinning liquids ≤950 L (251 gal) capacity, **no supplemental heat**, no ingredient (excl. water) >135°F, powders may be added.
- **(E)** Cosmetics filling stations hard-piped to mixer and holding tank (mixer and tank also exempt under this rule).
- **(F)** Concrete mixers ≤1 cubic yard rated working capacity.
- **(G)** Packaging lubricants or greases.
- **(H)** Packaging sodium hypochlorite-based household cleaning or pool products.
- **(I)** Foam packaging equipment using ≤20 gal/day or ≤440 gal/month liquid foam material, OR containing ≤50 g VOC/L.

### (d)(12) Coating and Adhesive Process/Equipment

- **(A)** Coating objects with oils, melted waxes, or greases containing **no VOC materials**.
- **(B)** Dip-coating in waxes or natural/synthetic resins containing **no VOC materials**.
- **(C)** Batch ovens ≤1.5 m³ (53 ft³) internal volume, **no melting** (also exempt under (d)(2)(C)). Excludes vinyl plastisol curing or brake shoe debonding ovens.
- **(D)** Ovens curing powder coatings ≤30 lb/day or ≤660 lb/month (also exempt under (d)(2)(C)).
- **(E)** Spray coating inside control enclosures.
- **(F) Coating/adhesive/laminating application equipment** (air, airless, HVLP, electrostatic, roller, dip, vacuum, flow coaters, spray machines) meeting ANY ONE of:
  - (i) VOC ≤**3 lb/day** or ≤66 lb/month;
  - (ii) UV/EB/LED (non-solvent, non-waterborne) coatings/adhesives + solvents ≤**6 gal/day** or ≤132 gal/month;
  - (iii) Organic solvent-based coatings/adhesives + solvents ≤**1 gal/day** or ≤22 gal/month;
  - (iv) Water reducible/waterborne coatings/adhesives + solvents ≤**3 gal/day** or ≤66 gal/month;
  - (v) Polyester resin/gel coat materials + solvents ≤**1 gal/day** or ≤22 gal/month; or
  - (vi) All coatings/adhesives/polyester resin ≤50 g VOC/L (excl. cleanup) AND all cleanup solvents ≤25 g VOC/L AND total VOC ≤**1 ton/year**. **Rule 222 may apply.**
- **(G)** Spray coating and drying for educational purposes at educational institutions.
- **(H)** Control enclosures ≤27 ft³ internal volume using aerosol cans, air brushes, or hand application exclusively.
- **(I)** Portable coating equipment and pavement stripers for architectural coatings (also exempt under (d)(1) or (d)(2)(A); no supplemental heat during pavement striping).
- **(J)** Hand application of resins, adhesives, dyes, and coatings (brushes, daubers, rollers, trowels).
- **(K) Drying equipment** (flash-off ovens, drying ovens, curing ovens) associated with coating/adhesive/laminating equipment, provided drying equipment is also exempt under (d)(2)(C) and the associated coating equipment meets the same thresholds as (d)(12)(F)(i)–(vi).
- **(L)** Addition of UV/EB/LED or other curing/drying technology to existing permitted coating equipment (same conditions as (d)(8)(H) above).

### (d)(13) Storage and Transfer Equipment

- **(A)** Storage/transfer of fresh, commercial, or purer grades of: sulfuric or phosphoric acid ≤99% strength; nitric acid ≤70% strength; or water-based salt/sodium hydroxide solutions.
- **(B)** Storage/transfer of liquefied gases, **except**: LPG >10,000 lbs; hydrogen fluoride >100 lbs; anhydrous ammonia >500 lbs.
- **(C)** Transfer of <75,700 L (20,000 gal)/day of **unheated** VOC materials with initial boiling point ≥150°C (302°F) or organic vapor pressure ≤5 mm Hg at 70°F.
- **(D)** Storage/dispensing of unheated VOC materials with initial boiling point ≥150°C or vapor pressure ≤5 mm Hg at 70°F. **Excludes** liquid fuel storage >160,400 L (40,000 gal).
- **(E)** Transfer of VOC liquids/materials/compressed gases into containers <225 L (60 gal). **Excludes** equipment transferring >4,000 L (1,057 gal)/day of materials with vapor pressure >25.8 mm Hg (0.5 psia) at operating conditions.
- **(F)** Storage/transfer of liquid soaps, detergents, vegetable oils, fatty acids, fatty esters, fatty alcohols, waxes, and wax emulsions.
- **(G)** Storage/transfer of refined lubricating or hydraulic oils.
- **(H)** Storage/transfer of crankcase drainage oil.
- **(I)** VOC-containing liquid storage/transfer, holding capacity <950 L (251 gal); or odorant storage for natural gas/propane/oil <950 L. **Rule 222 may apply** to odorant storage. **Excludes** asphalt. Group of tanks (same product) on shared mobile platform excluded if combined capacity >950 L.
- **(J)** Retail mobile fuelers with cumulative gasoline storage ≤10 gal (excluding one portable container up to 6.6 gal).
- **(K)** Non-retail mobile fuelers with cumulative gasoline storage ≤120 gal (excluding one portable container up to 6.6 gal).
- **(L)** "Top white" / cosmetic grade tallow or edible animal fats intended for human consumption.
- **(M)** Tar pots/kettles: holding capacity <600 L (159 gal) for any burner type; OR ≤3,785 L (1,000 gal) if equipped with burner(s) designed exclusively for LPG. **Rule 222 may apply** to the ≤1,000 gal LPG category.
- **(N)** Pipeline transfer pumps.
- **(O)** Unheated underground storage ≤23,000 L (6,077 gal) for organic liquids with vapor pressure ≤77.5 mm Hg (1.5 psia) at actual storage conditions, and associated transfer equipment.
- **(P)** Asphalt-water emulsion storage/transfer heated to ≤150°F.
- **(Q)** Liquid fuel storage piped exclusively to emergency ICE-generators, turbines, or pump drivers.
- **(R)** Bins ≤2,080 L (550 gal) for temporary material storage and transport.
- **(S)** Equipment where no venting occurs during filling or normal use.
- **(T)** Storage/blending/transfer of water emulsion intermediates/products (incl. latex) with ≤5% VOC by volume or VOC partial pressure ≤5 mm Hg at 20°C.
- **(U)** Storage/transfer of sodium hypochlorite solution.
- **(V)** Storage of VOC materials at temperature ≥130°C (234°F) below initial boiling point, or organic vapor pressure ≤5 mm Hg at actual storage temperature (requires temperature measurement device if heated). **Excludes** liquid fuel >160,400 L, asphalt, coal tar pitch.
- **(W)** Stationary equipment for storage/transfer of **organic compounds that do not contain VOCs**.
- **(X)** Unheated equipment for storage/transfer of fluorosilicic acid ≤30% by weight with vapor pressure ≤24 mm Hg at 77°F, and HF concentration ≤1% by weight.
- **(Y) Asphalt day tankers** on motor vehicles: <600 L (159 gal) capacity; OR ≤18,925 L (5,000 gal) capacity if equipped with burner(s) designed exclusively for LPG. **Rule 222 may apply** in both cases.
- **(Z)** Aqueous urea solution tanks ≤6,500 gal (excludes blending powdered urea with water). **Rule 222 may apply.**
- **(AA)** Replacement of pole floats on floating-roof storage tanks with pole sleeves or pole sleeve + flexible enclosure. (Exceptions in (e)(1) do not apply; equipment must still comply with listed requirements.)

### (d)(14) Natural Gas and Crude Oil Production Equipment

- **(A)** Well heads and well pumps. **Rule 222 may apply.**
- **(B)** Crude oil and natural gas pipeline transfer pumps. **Rule 222 may apply** to natural gas pumps.
- **(C)** Gas, hydraulic, or pneumatic repressurizing equipment. **Rule 222 may apply** to natural gas repressurizing.
- **(D)** Water boilers, water/hydrocarbon heaters, closed heat transfer systems (excluding steam generators for oilfield injection): ≤2,000,000 Btu/hr, fired on Purchased Quality Natural Gas, LPG, Produced Gas (<10 ppm H₂S), or combination.
- **(E)** Equipment used exclusively for **Primary Recovery** (not Community Lease Units): (i) gas separators and boots; (ii) initial receiving, gas dehydrating, storage, washing, and Shipping Tanks ≤34,069 L (9,000 gal) individual capacity; (iii) crude oil tank truck loading facilities (not loading racks) and gas recovery systems for tanks exempt under (E)(ii); (iv) Produced Gas dehydrating equipment.
- **(F)** Gravity-type oil/water separators with total air/liquid interfacial area <45 ft², separating oil with specific gravity ≥0.8251 (≤40.0 API).

### (d)(15) Cleaning Equipment

Note: Exemptions in (d)(15) do not apply to equipment using solvents >5% by weight of perchloroethylene, methylene chloride, carbon tetrachloride, chloroform, 1,1,1-trichloroethane, trichloroethylene (or combination) for non-Rule 1122 equipment with >0.01% of those solvents, with either capacity >7.6 L (2 gal) or designed as a self-contained solvent cleaning/drying machine.

- **(A) Solvent cleaning equipment** and associated waste storage tanks: (i) Unheated batch with ≤1 gal reservoir OR VOC ≤3 lb/day or ≤66 lb/month; (ii) Devices for cleaning ink/adhesive/coating equipment with ≤5 gal reservoir OR VOC ≤3 lb/day or ≤66 lb/month; (iii) Remote reservoir cleaners with ≤7 ft² sink opening, solvent drains immediately into enclosed container.
- **(B)** Vapor degreasers ≤1 ft² air/vapor interface, solvent loss ≤3 gal/day or ≤66 gal/month (excl. water).
- **(C)** Cleaning equipment using materials ≤25 g VOC/L (not for DPF cleaning).
- **(D)** Hand application of solvents for cleaning (rags, daubers, swabs, squeeze bottles) unless control equipment required for source-specific rule compliance.

### (d)(16) Miscellaneous Process Equipment

- **(A)** Dyeing, stripping, or bleaching of textiles with **no VOC materials** (also exempt under (d)(2)(C) or (D)).
- **(B)** Bonding lining to brake shoes with **no VOC materials**.
- **(C)** Liquefying/separating oxygen, nitrogen, or rare gases from air (also exempt under (d)(2)(A), (B), (C), or (D)).
- **(D) Surface preparation** (paint stripping, pickling, desmutting, de-scaling, passivation, deoxidation) and rinse/waste storage tanks using exclusively: (i) materials ≤50 g VOC/L; (ii) formic, acetic, boric, citric, phosphoric, or sulfuric acids; (iii) HCl ≤12% by weight; (iv) alkaline oxidizing agents; (v) hydrogen peroxide; (vi) salt solutions (except heated/sparged/rectified processes with hexavalent chromium, chromates, dichromates, Ni, Cd, Pb); (vii) NaOH (not sparged/rectified); (viii) nitric acid, HCl, or HF in equipment with ≤1 ft² open surface area, unheated, no visible emissions. **Excludes** rectified/air sparged/heated tanks with Cr/Ni/Pb/Cd; chemical milling; circuit board etching with ammonia-based etchants.
- **(E) Metal plating, stripping, or anodizing** (not rectified/sparged/heated tanks with Cr/Ni/Pb/Cd): (i) electrolytic plating of brass, bronze, copper, iron, tin, zinc, precious metals; (ii) electroless nickel plating (not sparged/heated, no electrolytic reverse plating); (iii) electrolytic stripping of brass, bronze, Cu, Fe, Sn, Zn, precious metals (no chromic/HCl/nitric/sulfuric acid); (iv) non-electrolytic stripping (not sparged, no nitric acid); (v) anodizing with sulfuric and/or boric acid ≤20% total by weight, ≤10,000 amp-hours/day; (vi) anodizing with phosphoric acid ≤15% by weight, ≤20,000 amp-hours/day; (vii) water/rinse tanks and waste storage tanks for the above operations.
- **(F)** Closed-loop solvent recovery using refrigerated/liquid-cooled condenser, or air-cooled condenser <10 gal reservoir.
- **(G)** Soap or detergent bar manufacturing (mixing tanks, roll mills, plodders, cutters, wrappers — no heating, drying, or chemical reactions).
- **(H)** Inert gas generators (also exempt under (d)(2)(C) or (D)).
- **(I)** Hammermills for aluminum and/or tin cans.
- **(J)** Paper shredding; carpet/paper shearing; fabric brushing and sueding; associated conveying/baling. Excludes carpet/fabric recycling.
- **(K)** Chemical vapor sterilization equipment (no ethylene oxide), ≤2 ft³ chamber volume, used by healthcare facilities.
- **(L)** Hot melt adhesive equipment.
- **(M)** Pyrotechnic/special effects/fireworks equipment for entertainment (also exempt under (d)(2)).
- **(N)** Ammunition or explosive testing equipment.
- **(O)** Fire extinguishing equipment using halons.
- **(P)** Industrial wastewater treatment performing only pH adjustment, precipitation, gravity separation, and/or filtration (incl. Cr⁶⁺ reduction/cyanide destruction); no VOC/toxic emissions; inlet cyanide salts ≤200 mg/L prior to pH adjustment.
- **(Q)** Rental equipment operated by lessee at a South Coast AQMD facility for ≤12 consecutive months where equipment owner holds a PO and lessee complies with PO terms.
- **(R)** Industrial wastewater evaporators treating on-site process water with no VOC/toxic emissions (also exempt under (d)(2)(C) or (D)).
- **(S)** Foam application equipment using two-component polyurethane foam where blowing agent contains no VOCs, CFCs, or methylene chloride.
- **(T)** Toner refilling and associated control equipment.
- **(U)** Evaporators at dry cleaning facilities for separator wastewater disposal.
- **(V)** Aerosol can recycling equipment (puncturing in enclosed system vented through activated carbon filter; cans must have been used at the facility or a facility under common ownership).
- **(X) Negative air machines** and HEPA filtration for asbestos abatement (residential/commercial). **Rule 222 may apply.**

### (d)(17) Agricultural Sources

- **(A)** Agricultural sources (not Title V) with **actual emissions below Table 1 thresholds** (or one-half applicable major source threshold, whichever is lower). Fugitive dust and emissions from soil amendments/fertilizers are excluded from the threshold calculation.

  **Table 1 — Agricultural Exemption Thresholds (tons/year):**

  | Pollutant | South Coast Air Basin | Riverside Co. Salton Sea AB | Riverside Co. Mojave Desert AB |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | VOC | 5.0 | 5.0 | 50.0 |
  | NOx | 5.0 | 5.0 | 50.0 |
  | SOx | 35.0 | 35.0 | 50.0 |
  | CO | 25.0 | 50.0 | 50.0 |
  | PM10 | 35.0 | 35.0 | 50.0 |
  | PM2.5 | 35.0 | 50.0 | 50.0 |
  | Single HAP | 5.0 | 5.0 | 5.0 |
  | Combined HAPs | 12.5 | 12.5 | 12.5 |

  **Note:** ICEs or gasoline transfer/dispensing equipment purchased or modified after July 7, 2006, that are not exempt under (d)(2)(A), (d)(2)(H), or (d)(13)(I) are subject to permit requirements notwithstanding this exemption.

- **(B)** Emergency ICEs at agricultural sources. **Rule 222 may apply.**
- **(C)** Agricultural permit units at agricultural sources not subject to Title V with actual emissions below Table 1.
- **(D)** **Rule 222 may apply** to ICEs.
- **(E)** Orchard wind machines powered by ICE >50 bhp operated ≤**30 hours/calendar year**.
- **(E)** Orchard heaters approved by CARB to produce ≤1 gram/minute of unconsumed solid carbonaceous material.

### (d)(18) Registered Equipment

- **(A)** Any portable equipment (including CARB-qualified military tactical support turbines) **registered under PERP** (Statewide Portable Equipment Registration Program, H&S Code §41750 et seq.).
- **(B)** PERP-registered engines used in the Outer Continental Shelf (OCS) — provided: owner notifies Executive Officer, equipment does not reside at one OCS location for >12 consecutive months, and PERP registration requirements are met. **Rule 222 may apply.**
- **(C)** PERP-registered equipment at RECLAIM facilities is classified as Major/Large Source/Process Units for NOx and SOx MRR purposes under Rules 2011 and 2012.

## Exceptions — When Permits Are Required Despite Rule 219 Exemption (Rule 219(e))

Written permits are required regardless of exemption status when:

1. **(e)(1)** Equipment/process materials are subject to:
   - Regulation IX (NSPS) — except ICEs ≤50 bhp;
   - Regulation X (NESHAP — 40 CFR 61) — except ICEs ≤50 bhp; or
   - State ATCM or NESHAP 40 CFR 63 emission limitation requirements.

2. **(e)(2)** Executive Officer determines and gives written notice that:
   - Risk from uncontrolled emissions exceeds Rule 1401 thresholds; or
   - Equipment may not comply with all applicable rules including Rule 402 (Nuisance); or
   - Equipment has been modified/operated/maintained inconsistently with the exemption or causing excess emissions.

3. **(e)(3)** Executive Officer determines health risk information is inadequate; applicant must submit permit application within 60 days of written notification.

4. **(e)(4)** Equipment subject to Regulation XIV (Toxics and Other Non-criteria Pollutants) permitting requirements.

## Recordkeeping (Rule 219(f))

Owners/operators claiming any exemption must maintain documentation demonstrating the exemption applies, including:
- VOC-containing material throughput and emissions;
- VOC content of each material (g VOC/L of regulated product and g VOC/L of material);
- Hours of operation; materials used/processed; fuel type/usage; throughput; operating parameters; manufacturer specs; rating plate; Safety Data Sheets.

Records must be maintained **on-site for 3 years** and made available to the Executive Officer upon request.
