# SCAQMD Rule 219 — Operational Limits by Category

This file consolidates the specific numerical limits that determine whether equipment qualifies for an exemption under Rule 219. All limits are from the rule text (Amended April 7, 2023).

## Combustion Equipment — Rule 219(d)(2)

| Equipment Type | Key Limit(s) |
|---|---|
| Internal combustion engines (ICEs) — general | ≤50 bhp manufacturer's rating |
| ICEs at remote radio towers (no utility within 0.5 mi) | ≤100 bhp; diesel #2, CNG, or LPG only |
| Stationary gas turbines / micro-turbines | Rated max heat input ≤3,500,000 Btu/hr; cumulative facility power output <2 MW; CARB-certified at manufacture or in operation before May 3, 2013 |
| Boilers, process heaters, combustion equipment (natural gas/methanol/LPG) | Rated max heat input ≤2,000,000 Btu/hr (gross); Rule 222 required for 1,000,000–2,000,000 Btu/hr |
| Food ovens (natural gas) — sub-exception to boiler rule | Rated max heat input ≤2,000,000 Btu/hr; process VOC <1 lb/day |
| Diesel-fueled boilers | ≤2,000,000 Btu/hr; diesel #2 only; ≤50 gal/day; NOx <1 lb/day; >4,000 ft elevation or >15 mi offshore; in operation before May 3, 2013 |
| Portable diesel-fueled heaters (space heating) | ≤250,000 Btu/hr; diesel fuel only |
| Power pressure washers / hot water-steam washers | Diesel-fired; ≤550,000 Btu/hr; non-resettable chronometer; NOx <1 lb/day; ≤50 gal/day |
| Fuel cell supplemental heating (>2 MMBtu/hr category) | Supplemental heat ≤90,000 therms/year |
| Test cells/test stands (burners or ICEs) | <800 gal diesel/year AND <3,500 gal gasoline/year (or equivalent emissions with other fuels) |

## Printing and Graphic Arts — Rule 219(d)(8)(A)

Exemption requires meeting **at least one** of the following thresholds:

| Threshold Basis | Daily Limit | Monthly Limit |
|---|---|---|
| Uncontrolled VOC emissions (all operations) | ≤3 lb/day | ≤66 lb/calendar month |
| Plastisol inks/coatings/adhesives + VOC solvents (incl. cleanup) | ≤6 gal/day | ≤132 gal/month |
| UV/EB/LED (non-solvent, non-waterborne) inks/coatings/adhesives + VOC solvents | ≤6 gal/day | ≤132 gal/month |
| Other inks/coatings/adhesives + fountain solutions + VOC solvents | ≤2 gal/day | ≤44 gal/month |
| Low-VOC content path: all inks/coatings/adhesives/fountain solutions | ≤50 g VOC/L AND cleanup solvents ≤25 g VOC/L | Total VOC ≤1 ton/calendar year (Rule 222 may apply) |

**Mix-and-match rule:** If multiple ink/coating types are used, the most stringent applicable usage limit applies, OR the operation must meet the 3 lb/day or 1 ton/year VOC thresholds.

## Coating and Adhesive Application — Rule 219(d)(12)(F)

Exemption requires meeting **at least one** of the following thresholds:

| Threshold Basis | Daily Limit | Monthly Limit |
|---|---|---|
| VOC emissions from equipment (incl. cleanup) | ≤3 lb/day | ≤66 lb/month |
| UV/EB/LED (non-solvent, non-waterborne) coatings/adhesives + solvents | ≤6 gal/day | ≤132 gal/month |
| Organic solvent-based coatings/adhesives + solvents | ≤1 gal/day | ≤22 gal/month |
| Water reducible/waterborne coatings/adhesives + solvents | ≤3 gal/day | ≤66 gal/month |
| Polyester resin/gel coat materials + solvents | ≤1 gal/day | ≤22 gal/month |
| Low-VOC content path: all coatings/adhesives/polyester resin (excl. cleanup) | ≤50 g VOC/L AND cleanup solvents ≤25 g VOC/L | Total VOC ≤1 ton/year (Rule 222 may apply) |

**Associated drying equipment** (flash-off ovens, drying ovens, curing ovens) — Rule 219(d)(12)(K) — must also be exempt under (d)(2)(C) AND the associated coating equipment must meet the same thresholds listed above.

**Powder coating ovens** — Rule 219(d)(12)(D): ≤30 lb/day or ≤660 lb/calendar month of powder coatings cured.

## Roller-to-Roller Coating Systems (3D Images) — Rule 219(d)(10)(M)

| Threshold Basis | Limit |
|---|---|
| VOC emissions (incl. cleanup) | ≤3 lb/day or ≤66 lb/month |
| Coatings ≤25 g VOC/L path | ≤12 gal/day or ≤264 gal/month |
| Low-VOC content path: coatings ≤50 g VOC/L, cleanup ≤25 g VOC/L | Total VOC ≤1 ton/year (Rule 222 may apply) |

## Cleaning Equipment — Rule 219(d)(15)

| Equipment Type | Limit |
|---|---|
| Unheated batch solvent cleaner | Reservoir ≤1 gal OR VOC ≤3 lb/day or ≤66 lb/month |
| Device for cleaning ink/adhesive/coating equipment | Reservoir ≤5 gal OR VOC ≤3 lb/day or ≤66 lb/month |
| Remote reservoir cleaner | ≤7 ft² sink opening area; solvent drains immediately into enclosed container |
| Vapor degreaser | ≤1 ft² air/vapor interface; solvent loss ≤3 gal/day or ≤66 gal/month (excl. water) |
| Cleaning equipment using low-VOC materials | ≤25 g VOC/L (not for diesel particulate filter cleaning) |

**Perchloroethylene/chlorinated solvent restriction:** Exemptions in (d)(15) do not apply to equipment using >5% by weight of perc, methylene chloride, carbon tetrachloride, chloroform, 1,1,1-TCA, or TCE (for Rule 1122 equipment; >0.01% for non-Rule 1122 equipment) if the equipment has capacity >7.6 L (2 gal) or is designed as a self-contained solvent cleaning/drying machine.

## Storage and Transfer Equipment — Rule 219(d)(13)

| Equipment Type | Limit |
|---|---|
| General VOC-containing liquid storage | <950 L (251 gal) capacity |
| Odorant storage (natural gas/propane/oil) | <950 L (251 gal) (Rule 222 may apply) |
| Liquid fuel storage | ≤160,400 L (40,000 gal) [applicable to multiple exemptions] |
| Unheated underground organic liquid storage | ≤23,000 L (6,077 gal); vapor pressure ≤77.5 mm Hg (1.5 psia) at actual conditions |
| Transfer into containers | Containers <225 L (60 gal); limited to ≤4,000 L (1,057 gal)/day if vapor pressure >25.8 mm Hg (0.5 psia) |
| Tar pots/kettles (any burner type) | <600 L (159 gal) holding capacity |
| Tar pots/kettles (LPG burner only) | ≤3,785 L (1,000 gal) holding capacity (Rule 222 may apply) |
| Asphalt day tankers — small | <600 L (159 gal) (Rule 222 may apply) |
| Asphalt day tankers on motor vehicles — large | ≤18,925 L (5,000 gal); LPG burner only (Rule 222 may apply) |
| Aqueous urea solution tanks | ≤6,500 gal (not for blending powdered urea) (Rule 222 may apply) |
| LPG liquefied gas storage | ≤10,000 lbs |
| Hydrogen fluoride storage | ≤100 lbs |
| Anhydrous ammonia storage | ≤500 lbs |
| Retail mobile fueler gasoline | ≤10 gal cumulative (+ 1 portable container ≤6.6 gal) |
| Non-retail mobile fueler gasoline | ≤120 gal cumulative (+ 1 portable container ≤6.6 gal) |
| Temporary storage bins | ≤2,080 L (550 gal) |

**Vapor pressure thresholds used across (d)(13):**
- Unheated transfer or storage: initial boiling point ≥150°C (302°F) **or** organic vapor pressure ≤5 mm Hg (0.1 psi) at 21.1°C (70°F).
- Mobile day tanker fuel oil: organic vapor pressure ≤5 mm Hg at 70°F.

| Portable sand/water blaster | ≥66% water by volume during operation |

| Equipment Type | Limit |
|---|---|

| Batch mixers — Rule 219(d)(11)(A) | ≤55 gal (7.35 ft³) maximum capacity |

## Pharmaceuticals/Food Processing — Rule 219(d)(9)

|---|---|
| Smokehouses | ≤2 m² (21.5 ft²) horizontal inside cross-sectional area |
| Coffee roasting | ≤15 kg maximum batch capacity |

|---|---|

## Miscellaneous Plating — Rule 219(d)(16)(E)

| Anodizing Type | Concentration Limit | Electricity Limit |
|---|---|---|
| Sulfuric and/or boric acid | ≤20% total acids by weight | ≤10,000 amp-hours/day |

## VOC Content Definitions Used Throughout Rule 219

- **Grams of VOC per liter of material (g VOC/L):** = (Ws − Ww − Wes) / Vm
- **Grams of VOC per liter of regulated product, less water and exempt compounds:** = (Ws − Ww − Wes) / (Vm − Vw − Ves)

Where: Ws = weight of volatile compounds (g); Ww = weight of water (g); Wes = weight of exempt compounds (g); Vm = volume of material (L); Vw = volume of water (L); Ves = volume of exempt compounds (L).
