## Triggers

- The facility generates hazardous waste in a calendar month — the monthly quantity sets the generator category.
- A project change (new spent-solvent stream, increased process waste) raises monthly generation across a category boundary.
- Acute hazardous waste is generated — separate, much lower thresholds apply.
- Note: California (DTSC) administers an authorized RCRA program and may apply stricter state thresholds; the federal categories below are the orientation.

## Thresholds

| Generator category | Expected monthly orientation (non-acute hazardous waste) | Primary source to fetch and quote |
| --- | --- | --- |
| VSQG (Very Small Quantity Generator) | <= 100 kg/month (about 220 lb) | EPA generator categories |
| SQG (Small Quantity Generator) | > 100 and < 1,000 kg/month | EPA generator categories |
| LQG (Large Quantity Generator) | >= 1,000 kg/month, or > 1 kg/month acute hazardous waste | EPA generator categories |

## Exemptions

- Category is determined per calendar month; a one-time spike can change category for that month — confirm counting rules in the fetched source.
- Certain wastes are excluded or counted differently (e.g. waste managed in specific units); verify against the fetched regulation rather than assuming.
- State programs (such as California DTSC) can be more stringent than the federal floor — the EPA categories orient the analysis but the controlling authority may impose lower thresholds.
