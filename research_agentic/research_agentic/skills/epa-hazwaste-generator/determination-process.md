## Step-by-Step Determination (40 CFR 262.11)

### Step 1: Is it a Solid Waste?
Material must first be a "solid waste" under 40 CFR 261.2. Excluded: in-process materials, products, certain recycled materials. If not a solid waste → not a hazardous waste.

### Step 2: Is it Excluded from Hazardous Waste Regulations?
Check 40 CFR 261.4 exclusions:
- Domestic sewage
- Industrial wastewater discharged under CWA NPDES permit
- Household hazardous waste
- Agricultural waste returned to soil
- Mining overburden

### Step 3: Is it a Listed Hazardous Waste?
| List | Description | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| F-list | From nonspecific industrial processes | F001-F039 (spent solvents, electroplating) |
| K-list | From specific industries | K001-K161 (petroleum refining, pesticide mfg) |
| P-list | Acute hazardous (discarded commercial chemical products) | P001-P205 |
| U-list | Toxic (discarded commercial chemical products) | U001-U411 |

### Step 4: Is it a Characteristic Hazardous Waste?
| Characteristic | Code | Test |
|---|---|---|
| Ignitability | D001 | Flashpoint < 60°C (140°F) for liquids; capable of fire for solids/gases |
| Corrosivity | D002 | pH ≤ 2 or ≥ 12.5, or corrodes steel at > 6.35 mm/yr |
| Reactivity | D003 | Unstable, explosive, cyanide/sulfide generating |
| Toxicity | D004-D043 | TCLP leaching test (40 metals/organics) |

## California Additional Characteristic Wastes
California adds characteristics not in federal rules:
- **Toxic** (broader than D-list): 22 CCR 66261.24
- **Persistent bioaccumulative toxic** materials
- Check 22 CCR 66261.20-66261.24 for CA-only characteristics
