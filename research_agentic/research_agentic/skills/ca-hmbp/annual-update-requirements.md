## Two Update Regimes

California HMBP update requirements operate on two parallel tracks: (1) scheduled periodic submissions and (2) event-triggered updates within 30 days of a change.

## 1. Scheduled Periodic Submissions (HSC § 25508)

HSC § 25508 sets the recurring submission schedule:

| Facility Type | Required Frequency | Deadline |
|---|---|---|
| Facilities required to submit under federal 42 U.S.C. § 11022 (Tier II) | **Annually** | March 1 |
| Chapter 6.67 facilities | **Annually** | March 1 |
| All other handlers | **Once every three years** | March 1 of the filing year |

Statutory language: "Unless a unified program agency establishes an earlier date, handlers must submit on or before March 1 of every year that the plan is required to be submitted."

Local CUPAs may set **earlier deadlines** than March 1. Verify with the local CUPA.

## What "Annual Update" Covers

At each scheduled submission, the business must review and update all five components of the HMBP per HSC § 25505:

1. **Hazardous Materials Inventory** — confirm all chemicals above threshold are listed; remove chemicals no longer handled; update quantities and storage locations to reflect current conditions as of the reporting year.

2. **Site Map** — verify the map reflects current facility layout. HSC § 25505 requires the site map to be updated within one year of any local ordinance additions affecting required map elements.

3. **Emergency Response Plans and Procedures** — confirm contact information for the emergency coordinator and notification list is current; verify procedures reflect current chemicals and facility layout.

4. **Employee Training Records** — confirm annual refresher training was completed for all employees. Per HSC § 25505: "annual training, including refresher courses, for all employees in safety procedures." Training documentation must be retained on-site for **minimum three years**.

5. **Business and Owner/Operator Information** — confirm business name, address, ownership, and 24-hour emergency contact are current.

## 2. Event-Triggered Updates: Within 30 Days (HSC § 25508.1)

HSC § 25508.1 states: "Within 30 days of any one of the following events, a business subject to this article shall electronically update the information."

| Triggering Event | 30-Day Clock Starts |
|---|---|
| 100% or more increase in quantity of a previously disclosed material | Date the increased quantity is first present |
| Any handling of a previously undisclosed hazardous material subject to inventory requirements | Date first handled/stored |
| Change of business or facility address | Date of change |
| Change of business ownership | Date of change |
| Change of business name | Date of change |
| A "substantial change" in the handler's operations | Date the change occurs |

**Statutory definition of "substantial change"** (HSC § 25508.1): "any change in a facility that would inhibit immediate response during an emergency by either site personnel or emergency response personnel, or that could inhibit the handler's ability to comply with Section 25507, change the operational knowledge of the facility, or impede implementation of the business plan."

Examples of changes that likely constitute "substantial change": adding a new chemical process, moving storage locations, installing or removing emergency response equipment, changes to access routes or evacuation paths.

## Deficiency Response Deadline (HSC § 25508)

If the CUPA notifies the business of a plan deficiency, the business must "electronically submit a corrected business plan within 30 days from the date of the notice."

## New Business Grace Period (HSC § 25508)

Businesses have a 30-day grace period from the date they become subject to HMBP requirements before a violation occurs. A new business handling threshold quantities must file within 30 days of commencing operations.

## Employee Training — Ongoing Annual Requirement

Regardless of the triennial filing schedule, **employee training is an annual requirement** per HSC § 25505: "annual training, including refresher courses, for all employees." This continues every year even when a full HMBP submission is not due. Training records must be available at the facility.
