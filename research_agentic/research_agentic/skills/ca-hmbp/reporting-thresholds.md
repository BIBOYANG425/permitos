## Statutory Authority

California Health and Safety Code (HSC) Division 20, Chapter 6.95, Article 1.
Primary threshold section: **HSC § 25507** (Amended by Stats. 2024, Ch. 989, Sec. 4, effective January 1, 2025).

## Who Is a "Handler"

HSC § 25501 defines a **handler** as "a business that handles a hazardous material." A **hazardous material** is "a material listed in paragraph (2) that, because of its quantity, concentration, or physical or chemical characteristics, poses a significant present or potential hazard to human health and safety or to the environment if released into the workplace or the environment, or a material specified in an ordinance adopted pursuant to paragraph (3)."

## Core Reporting Thresholds (HSC § 25507)

| State of Matter | Threshold (at or above) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Liquid | **55 gallons** | Aggregate per hazardous material name at the facility |
| Solid | **500 pounds** | Aggregate per hazardous material name at the facility |
| Compressed gas | **200 cubic feet** (at standard temperature and pressure) | Aggregate per hazardous material name at the facility |
| Extremely Hazardous Substances (EHS) | Threshold Planning Quantity (TPQ) or 500 lbs, whichever is less | EPCRA § 302 list; federal Tier II filers also file HMBP annually |
| Irritants/sensitizers — solid | 5,000 pounds | Separate statutory tier per HSC § 25507 |
| Irritants/sensitizers — liquid | 550 gallons | Separate statutory tier per HSC § 25507 |
| Cryogenic, refrigerated, or compressed gases (irritant/sensitizer class) | 1,000 cubic feet | Separate statutory tier per HSC § 25507 |

## Aggregation Rule

Count **all** quantities of the same hazardous material present at the facility at any one time — storage, use, and manufacturing combined. Example: 10 drums × 6 gallons each = 60 gallons of the same chemical → exceeds 55-gallon threshold → HMBP required.

## Statutory Exemptions (HSC § 25507)

The following are explicitly excluded from threshold calculations per HSC § 25507:

- Refrigerant gases in closed cooling systems (non-ammonia, non-flammable)
- Lubricating oil not exceeding **55 gallons per type**, **275 gallons total**
- Consumer products at retail establishments used in the same manner as an ordinary consumer would use them
- Propane for on-premises use not exceeding **500 gallons**
- Fuel in a vehicle's integral fuel tank used for propulsion of the vehicle
- Hazardous waste subject to HSC Chapter 6.5 (managed under RCRA manifest system)
- Radioactive materials regulated by NRC where separate reporting applies
- Materials in pipelines (not in storage)

## Key Quantities for Common EHS Chemicals

| Chemical | EHS TPQ | Applicable HMBP Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Chlorine (gas) | 10 lbs | 200 cf compressed gas also applies |
| Ammonia (anhydrous) | 500 lbs | 200 cf compressed gas also applies |
| Sulfuric acid | 1,000 lbs | 500 lb solid or 55 gal liquid threshold |
| Hydrofluoric acid | 100 lbs | 55 gal liquid threshold |
| Formaldehyde | 500 lbs | 55 gal liquid threshold |

## CUPA Variations

Local CUPAs **may adopt more stringent thresholds** than state minimums. From the CalEPA HMBP guidance page: "Local governments may have requirements that are more stringent than state and should be contacted for further information." Use the CalEPA Unified Program Regulator Directory to find local CUPA contact information. See also: `local-cupa-variation-note.md`.
