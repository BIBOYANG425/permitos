## Statutory Basis for Local Variation

The HMBP program establishes **state minimums**. Local CUPAs have explicit authority to adopt stricter requirements.

**HSC § 25507** defines the standard thresholds (55 gal / 500 lb / 200 cf) as the statewide baseline. Local CUPAs may lower these thresholds by local ordinance.

**HSC § 25501** provides that the definition of "hazardous material" includes "a material specified in an ordinance adopted pursuant to paragraph (3)" — meaning local governments may expand the covered materials list beyond the state list.

**CalEPA official guidance language** (from the HMBP program page): "Local governments may have requirements that are more stringent than state and should be contacted for further information." The page further directs businesses to: "refer to the Unified Program Regulator Directory to search for and view location/contact information for your local CUPA to determine if that jurisdiction has more stringent reporting requirements."

## How to Find Local CUPA Requirements

1. Use the **CalEPA Unified Program Regulator Directory**: https://calepa.ca.gov/cupa/
2. Contact the local CUPA directly — contact information is in the directory
3. Review the local CUPA's website or permit application for local ordinance requirements
4. Email HMBP@calepa.ca.gov for guidance on specific jurisdictions

## Known Areas of Local Variation

Local CUPAs commonly impose stricter requirements in these areas:

### Lower Quantity Thresholds
Some CUPAs adopt lower reporting thresholds for specific chemical classes, particularly acutely hazardous materials. A CUPA may, for example, require reporting at 1 gallon of certain acutely toxic liquids rather than the state's 55-gallon threshold. Always confirm local thresholds for highly toxic materials.

### Additional Chemicals Covered
Local ordinances may add chemicals to the covered materials list that are not on the state EHS or standard hazardous materials lists. This is most common in jurisdictions with industrial concentration of specific industries (e.g., semiconductor manufacturing areas, agricultural counties).

### Earlier Filing Deadlines
HSC § 25508 explicitly permits local CUPAs to set deadlines earlier than March 1: "Unless a unified program agency establishes an earlier date." Some CUPAs require submission by January 1 or February 1.

### Additional Plan Contents
Local CUPAs may require additional site map elements, more detailed emergency response procedures, or additional training documentation beyond the state minimum requirements under HSC § 25505.

### More Frequent Inspections
State law does not mandate a specific inspection cycle. Local CUPAs set their own inspection frequency. Some high-risk-industry CUPAs inspect annually; others follow a 3-year cycle.

## Practical Rule

**Always treat state thresholds as the floor, not the ceiling.** When a facility is in any California jurisdiction, the state thresholds (55 gal / 500 lb / 200 cf) identify the minimum reporting obligation. Local CUPA requirements may apply at lower quantities or to additional materials.

When in doubt: contact the local CUPA before assuming the state threshold is determinative.
