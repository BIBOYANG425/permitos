## Statutory Basis

The CalEPA HMBP guidance states: "The business must electronically file the HMBP to the California Environmental Reporting System (CERS)." This requirement flows from HSC § 25508, which mandates electronic submission. Paper filing is not accepted.

## Portal

**CERS Business Portal:** https://cers.calepa.ca.gov

CERS is administered by CalEPA and provides separate sign-in portals for businesses (submitting reports) and regulators (reviewing submissions). Technical support: CERS@calepa.ca.gov.

**Account maintenance:** CERS accounts with no login activity for more than one year are deleted per the system's security policy (effective May 2, 2025). Businesses should log in at least annually to maintain account access.

## Step-by-Step Filing Process

### Step 1 — Create or Access a CERS Business Account

Navigate to https://cers.calepa.ca.gov and select the **Business** sign-in portal. First-time users must register for an account. Returning users log in with existing credentials.

### Step 2 — Add or Locate Your Facility

Within CERS, locate your existing facility record or add a new facility. Each physical address is a separate facility record. Confirm the facility address and tie it to the correct local CUPA jurisdiction.

### Step 3 — Complete Business Activities and Owner/Operator Information

Enter required business identification data:
- Business name, DBA (if applicable)
- Owner name and operator name
- Facility address and mailing address
- 24-hour emergency contact phone number
- NAICS code, SIC code, employee count, operating hours
- Business activities subject to CUPA requirements

### Step 4 — Enter Hazardous Materials Inventory (HMI)

Per HSC § 25505: submit "a list of all reportable hazardous materials handled at the facility at any one time of the reporting year." For each chemical at or above threshold quantities (55 gal / 500 lb / 200 cf):

- Chemical name and CAS number
- Trade/product name
- Maximum daily quantity and average daily quantity, with unit of measure
- State of matter (liquid, solid, gas)
- Physical state and hazard categories (health, fire, reactivity)
- Storage location(s) on site with description or coordinates
- Whether the material is an Extremely Hazardous Substance (EHS)

### Step 5 — Upload or Draw Site Map

Upload a facility site map that includes, per HSC § 25505:
- North orientation
- Adjacent streets
- Access and exit points
- Evacuation staging areas
- Hazardous material handling and storage areas
- Emergency response equipment locations
- Internal roads, floor drains, emergency shutoffs
- Loading areas (where applicable)

### Step 6 — Complete Emergency Response Plan

Enter or upload the emergency response plan per HSC § 25505, which must include:
- Name and 24-hour phone number of emergency coordinator
- Notification contact list for emergency personnel and the Governor's Office of Emergency Services
- "Procedures for the mitigation of a release or threatened release to minimize any potential harm or damage to persons, property, or the environment"
- Evacuation routes and assembly areas

### Step 7 — Document Employee Training Program

Per HSC § 25505: "Training for all new employees and annual training, including refresher courses, for all employees in safety procedures." The CERS record must reflect:
- Training topics covered (safe handling, local emergency coordination, equipment use, emergency procedures)
- Training frequency (at hire + annual refresher)
- Documentation retained on-site for **minimum three years**

### Step 8 — Review and Submit

Review all entries for completeness and accuracy. Submit the HMBP electronically through CERS. The local CUPA will receive the submission and may review it for deficiencies.

### Step 9 — Respond to Deficiency Notices

Per HSC § 25508: if the CUPA finds the plan deficient, it will notify the business. The business must "electronically submit a corrected business plan within 30 days from the date of the notice."

## Submission Deadlines

| Situation | Deadline |
|---|---|
| Annual submission (Tier II / Chapter 6.67 facilities) | March 1 annually |
| Triennial submission (other handlers) | March 1 of the required year |
| New business commencing operations with threshold quantities | Within 30 days |
| Event-triggered update (see annual-update-requirements.md) | Within 30 days of triggering event |

HSC § 25508: "Unless a unified program agency establishes an earlier date, handlers must submit on or before March 1 of every year that the plan is required to be submitted."
