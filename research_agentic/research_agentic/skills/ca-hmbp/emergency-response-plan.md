## Required Plan Elements (19 CCR 2660)
1. **Emergency coordinator** — name, title, 24-hour phone number; must be reachable at all times during facility operations
2. **List of emergency equipment** on site (fire extinguishers, spill kits, PPE, emergency shutoffs)
3. **Evacuation plan** — routes, assembly points, headcount procedure
4. **Notification procedures** — who to call and when:
   - Local fire department (911 for immediate danger)
   - CUPA (within 15 minutes of a release requiring emergency response)
   - State OES Warning Center: (800) 852-7550
   - National Response Center: (800) 424-8802 (if federal threshold release)
5. **Response procedures by hazard class** — actions for spill/fire/release of each chemical category
6. **Training documentation** — annual training records for all employees

## Minimum Training Requirements
- All employees who work with/near hazardous materials: annual training on hazards, PPE, emergency procedures
- Emergency response team: OSHA HAZWOPER (29 CFR 1910.120) training if they will respond to releases

## Integration with Other Plans
- If facility also has a CalARP program: the RMP emergency response plan may satisfy HMBP requirements if it covers all HMBP chemicals
- RCRA contingency plan (LQGs): can be consolidated with HMBP ERP if CUPA approves
