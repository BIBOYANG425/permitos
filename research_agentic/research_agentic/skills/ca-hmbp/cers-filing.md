## Filing Portal

**CERS** (California Environmental Reporting System): https://cers.calepa.ca.gov

The CalEPA HMBP guidance states: "The business must electronically file the HMBP to the California Environmental Reporting System (CERS)." Paper submission is no longer accepted. CERS provides separate sign-in portals for businesses and regulators. For technical assistance contact CERS@calepa.ca.gov or your local UPA regulator.

**Account security note:** CERS accounts inactive for more than one year are subject to deletion per the system's security policy (effective May 2, 2025).

## What to Submit in CERS (HSC § 25505)

HSC § 25505 specifies that every business plan must contain:

1. **Business Activities and Owner/Operator Information**
   - NAICS/SIC code, employee count, operating hours
   - Owner and operator name, address, 24-hour emergency phone number

2. **Hazardous Materials Inventory (HMI)** — "a list of all reportable hazardous materials handled at the facility at any one time of the reporting year." For each material above threshold:
   - Chemical name, CAS number, trade name
   - Maximum daily quantity, average daily quantity
   - State of matter, physical state
   - Health/fire/reactivity hazard categories
   - Storage location(s) with coordinates or description

3. **Site Map** — HSC § 25505 requires a map with: north orientation, adjacent streets, access and exit points, evacuation staging areas, hazardous material handling and storage areas, emergency response equipment locations, internal roads, drains, emergency shutoffs, and loading areas. Must be updated within one year of any local ordinance additions.

4. **Emergency Response Plans and Procedures** — per HSC § 25505: "Emergency response plans and procedures in the event of a release or threatened release of a hazardous material," including:
   - Notification contacts for emergency personnel and the Governor's Office of Emergency Services
   - "Procedures for the mitigation of a release or threatened release to minimize any potential harm or damage to persons, property, or the environment"
   - Evacuation procedures

5. **Employee Training Program** — per HSC § 25505: "Training for all new employees and annual training, including refresher courses, for all employees in safety procedures." Training records must be "maintained for a minimum of three years."

## Submission Frequency (HSC § 25508)

| Facility Type | Filing Frequency | Annual Deadline |
|---|---|---|
| Tier II facilities (federal 42 U.S.C. § 11022) | Annually | March 1 |
| Chapter 6.67 facilities | Annually | March 1 |
| All other handlers | Once every three years | March 1 of the filing year |

HSC § 25508 states: "Unless a unified program agency establishes an earlier date, handlers must submit on or before March 1 of every year that the plan is required to be submitted."

## Event-Triggered Update Deadlines (HSC § 25508.1)

HSC § 25508.1 requires electronic update "within 30 days of any one of the following events":

| Triggering Event | Deadline |
|---|---|
| New business handling threshold quantities | 30 days of commencing business |
| 100% or more increase in a previously disclosed material | 30 days of change |
| Any handling of a previously undisclosed hazardous material | 30 days of first use/storage |
| Change of business or facility address | 30 days |
| Change of business ownership | 30 days |
| Change of business name | 30 days |
| "Substantial change in the handler's operations" | 30 days |

**Definition of "substantial change"** (HSC § 25508.1): "any change in a facility that would inhibit immediate response during an emergency by either site personnel or emergency response personnel, or that could inhibit the handler's ability to comply with Section 25507, change the operational knowledge of the facility, or impede implementation of the business plan."

## Deficiency and Enforcement (HSC § 25508)

If a plan is found deficient, the agency notifies the handler, who must "electronically submit a corrected business plan within 30 days from the date of the notice." Failure to comply allows the unified program agency to "take appropriate action to enforce this article, including the imposition of administrative, civil, and criminal penalties."

## Fees

Fees are set by the local CUPA and vary by jurisdiction and inventory size. Verify current fee schedule with the local CUPA. Use the CalEPA Unified Program Regulator Directory at https://calepa.ca.gov/cupa/ to find your local CUPA.

## Inspection

CUPAs conduct inspections on periodic cycles. Inspectors verify: CERS data matches on-site inventory, site map is accurate, employees are trained, and the emergency response plan is current.
