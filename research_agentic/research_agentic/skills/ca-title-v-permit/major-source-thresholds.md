## Defining "Major Source" Under Title V

A stationary source is a Title V major source if its **potential to emit (PTE)** equals or exceeds the applicable threshold for **any** regulated air pollutant. PTE means the maximum capacity to emit, assuming 8,760 hours/year of operation at full capacity, **before** voluntary operational limits that are not federally enforceable (see tpy-calculations.md for PTE methodology).

## Threshold Table — Hazardous Air Pollutants (HAPs)

Under CAA §112(a)(1) and 40 CFR 70.2, a source is a major source for HAPs if PTE equals or exceeds:
- **10 tons per year (tpy)** of any single HAP, OR
- **25 tpy** of any combination of HAPs (aggregate)

The HAP list is set by CAA §112(b) and EPA's current 187-substance list (40 CFR Part 63 Appendix A). [VERIFY IN PRIMARY SOURCE — confirm current HAP list; EPA has added/deleted substances over time]

## "Synthetic Minor" — Avoiding the Major Source Threshold

A source with physical PTE above a major-source threshold can avoid Title V by accepting **federally enforceable** operational limits (often called a "synthetic minor" permit or a district Permit to Operate with enforceable conditions). Requirements:

1. The limit must be contained in a **federally enforceable** permit condition (not just an internal policy).
2. The district must have EPA-approved authority to make such limits federally enforceable.
3. Monitoring, recordkeeping, and reporting must be sufficient to demonstrate continuous compliance with the limit.
4. A source operating as a synthetic minor must still apply for Title V if PTE before limits would exceed the threshold.

In California, the district Permit to Operate (Rule 2020/equivalent) can impose federally enforceable limits, effectively keeping a source "synthetic minor" and outside Part 70.

## Source Categories Presumptively Subject (Designated Facilities)

Certain source categories are Title V major sources by EPA rule regardless of individual PTE calculations:
- Sources subject to a NSPS under 40 CFR Part 60
- Sources subject to a NESHAP under 40 CFR Part 61
- Sources subject to a MACT standard under 40 CFR Part 63

## Aggregation Rule (Contiguous/Adjacent Facilities)

Multiple emission units count together if they are:
1. Under **common control** (same owner/operator), AND
2. **Contiguous or adjacent** land, AND
3. Belong to the **same major industrial grouping** (same 2-digit SIC code)

This is the "single stationary source" aggregation rule under 40 CFR 70.2. Off-site satellite facilities under different SIC codes generally do not aggregate.
