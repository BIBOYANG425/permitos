## CUPA (Unified Program)

- Tehama County Environmental Health Division — the **single CUPA for the entire county including all incorporated cities** (Red Bluff, Corning, Tehama). Administers HMBP/hazardous materials business plans, hazardous waste generators, USTs (underground storage tanks), ASTs/APSA aboveground petroleum storage (gas stations, vehicle repair shops), CalARP/accidental release prevention, and CERS reporting. Office at 633 Washington Street, Room 36, Red Bluff, CA 96080; phone (530) 527-8020. No city in Tehama County runs its own CUPA.
- https://www.tehama.gov/government/departments/environmental-health/
- CalEPA CERS Unified Program Regulator Directory (search "Tehama County Environmental Health"): https://cersapps.calepa.ca.gov/Public/Directory

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- CAL FIRE Tehama-Glenn Unit (TGU) / Tehama County Fire Department — combination department operating under a cooperative agreement (in place since 1927); CAL FIRE and Tehama County Fire serve the unincorporated county (State Responsibility Area + local). Red Bluff Emergency Command Center; HQ in Red Bluff — https://www.tehamacountyfire.org/
- City of Red Bluff Fire Department — independent municipal department (combination career/reserve) serving the City of Red Bluff, 555 Washington Street, Red Bluff — https://www.rbfd.org/
- Corning Volunteer Fire Department — serves the City of Corning (single-station, all-volunteer); dispatched by the City of Corning — https://www.corning.org/departments/fire-department/

## Building authority

- Tehama County Building and Safety Department (unincorporated county only) — 444 Oak Street, Red Bluff, CA 96080; phone (530) 527-7002; online permitting at tehamacountyca.viewpointcloud.com — https://www.tehama.gov/government/departments/building-and-safety/

## Local code amendments

- Tehama County Code Title 15 (Buildings and Construction) adopts the California Building Standards Code, Title 24. The County adopted the **2025 code cycle via Ordinance #2150** (replacing the 2022 cycle adopted via Ordinance #2127). Confirm the currently effective edition on the live page. Codes and ordinances page: https://www.tehama.gov/government/departments/building-and-safety/codes-and-ordinances/ — Municode: https://library.municode.com/ca/tehama_county/codes/code_of_ordinances

## Notes

- GOTCHA: **No city in Tehama County runs its own CUPA.** Tehama County Environmental Health is the sole CUPA countywide, including Red Bluff, Corning, and the City of Tehama.
- GOTCHA: County fire AHJ is delivered through the long-standing CAL FIRE / Tehama County Fire cooperative agreement (CAL FIRE Tehama-Glenn Unit), but the two largest cities run their OWN fire services: Red Bluff has a municipal fire department and Corning has its own volunteer fire department — neither is served by Tehama County Fire.
