> Local EHS authority reference for the **City of Stockton** (San Joaquin County's largest city, ~327k). Orientation for the research subagent: who administers local programs and where adopted codes live. Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- **Not its own CUPA** — served by the San Joaquin County Environmental Health Department (countywide CUPA). HMBP/CalARP/UST/APSA/hazwaste-generator submissions go through county EHD via CERS. — https://www.sjgov.org/department/envhealth/programs/certified-unified-program-agency-(cupa)

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- Stockton Fire Department (full municipal department). Fire Prevention Division operates from the Permit Center at 501 W. Weber Ave., Bldg 2; fire permits via the Accela online permitting system (since April 2025). The department's hazmat team participates in the San Joaquin County Joint Hazardous Materials Response Team (mutual aid). — https://www.stocktonca.gov/services/fire_department/fire_prevention.php

## Building authority

- City of Stockton Community Development Department — Building & Life Safety Division, in the Permit Center at 501 W. Weber Ave., Bldg 2. — https://www.stocktonca.gov/business/building___life_safety/online_permitting.php

## Notes

- The Stockton hazmat team being part of a countywide joint response team is emergency-response mutual aid only; the regulatory CUPA is county EHD, not the city.
- Stockton fire/building code amendments live in the Stockton Municipal Code (eCode360, e.g., Ch. 15.12 Fire Code). Confirm the adopted CBC edition (2022 vs 2025) on the live code page. — https://ecode360.com/43711522
