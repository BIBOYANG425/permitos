## CUPA (Unified Program)

- San Joaquin County Environmental Health Department (EHD) — sole **countywide CUPA** (state-approved January 1997); administers all Unified Program elements (HMBP, CalARP, Aboveground Petroleum Storage Act/APSA, Underground Storage Tank/UST, Hazardous Waste Generator, Hazardous Waste Onsite Treatment/Tiered Permitting). No incorporated city in the county runs its own CUPA or is a separate Participating Agency; all jurisdictions are served by the county EHD. Submit via CERS. Office at 1868 E. Hazelton Ave., Stockton.
- https://www.sjgov.org/department/envhealth/programs/certified-unified-program-agency-(cupa)

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- Unincorporated San Joaquin County has **no single county fire department**. Fire protection is provided by numerous independent fire protection districts (e.g., Lathrop-Manteca, Mokelumne, Woodbridge, Clements, Escalon, Farmington, Linden-Peters, and others). The county Community Development Department's **Fire Prevention Bureau/Division** provides fire-code review, permitting, and inspection services on behalf of those districts throughout the unincorporated areas. — https://www.sjgov.org/department/cdd/fire-prevention
- Incorporated cities run their own fire AHJ: Stockton, Tracy (via South San Joaquin County Fire Authority), Manteca, and Lodi each have municipal fire departments. Lathrop is served by the Lathrop-Manteca Fire Protection District.

## Notes

- GOTCHA: The county CUPA (EHD/Environmental Health) is organizationally separate from fire. Despite multiple cities having municipal fire departments and the unincorporated county being covered by independent fire protection districts, the CUPA for hazmat/HMBP/UST/APSA/CalARP/hazwaste is the **county Environmental Health Department countywide** — no city or fire district runs its own CUPA.
- GOTCHA: The unincorporated county has no consolidated county fire department; it is a patchwork of ~17 independent fire protection districts, with the county Fire Prevention Bureau handling fire-code/prevention functions on their behalf rather than direct suppression.
- Stockton Fire Department's hazmat team participates in a San Joaquin County Joint Hazardous Materials Response Team (mutual-aid pool) — this is emergency-response coordination, NOT CUPA delegation; the CUPA remains county EHD.
