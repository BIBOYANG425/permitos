> Local EHS authority reference for the **City of Lodi** (San Joaquin County, ~68k; included because it runs its own municipal fire department). Orientation for the research subagent: who administers local programs and where adopted codes live. Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- **Not its own CUPA** — served by the San Joaquin County Environmental Health Department (countywide CUPA). HMBP/CalARP/UST/APSA/hazwaste-generator submissions go through county EHD via CERS. — https://www.sjgov.org/department/envhealth/programs/certified-unified-program-agency-(cupa)

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- City of Lodi Fire Department (full municipal department; all-risk: structural/wildland suppression, EMS, technical rescue, hazmat mitigation, water rescue). — https://www.lodi.gov/281/Fire

## Building authority

- City of Lodi Community Development Department — Building Division (Plan Check and Inspection). — https://www.lodi.gov/173/Building-Division

## Notes

- The unincorporated rural area east of Lodi (Lockeford, Victor) is served by the separate **Mokelumne Fire District**, and the community of Woodbridge by the **Woodbridge Fire District** — different AHJs from the City of Lodi FD. — https://www.sjgov.org/department/cdd/fire-prevention
- Lodi building/fire code amendments are in the Lodi Municipal Code (Municode, Title 15 Building Code, Ch. 15.20 Fire Code). Confirm the adopted CBC edition (2022 vs 2025) on the live code page. — https://library.municode.com/ca/lodi/codes/code_of_ordinances?nodeId=TIT15BUCO_CH15.20FICO
