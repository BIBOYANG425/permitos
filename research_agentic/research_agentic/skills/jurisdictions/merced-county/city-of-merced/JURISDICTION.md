> Local EHS authority reference for the **City of Merced** (county seat, largest city in Merced County). Orientation for the research subagent: who administers local programs inside city limits and where the adopted codes live. Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- **Not its own CUPA** — served by the Merced County Division of Environmental Health (MCDEH), the countywide CUPA.
- https://www.countyofmerced.com/1830/CUPA-Hazardous-Materials-Waste-Informati

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- City of Merced Fire Department — full municipal department (career staff, multiple stations) serving the city; handles fire prevention/inspections within city limits. — https://www.cityofmerced.gov/departments/fire

## Building authority

- City of Merced Development Services — Building Division (permits and inspections within city limits). — https://www.cityofmerced.org/departments/development-services/building-division

## Local code amendments

- City of Merced adopts the California Building Standards Code via the municipal code (Title 17 — Buildings and Construction). Confirm the adopted Title 24 edition (2022 vs. 2025). — https://library.municode.com/ca/merced/codes/code_of_ordinances?nodeId=TIT17BUCO

## Notes

- The City of Merced runs its own fire AHJ and building authority but relies on the county (MCDEH) for the Unified Program/CUPA.
