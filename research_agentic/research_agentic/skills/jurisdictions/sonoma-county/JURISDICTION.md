> Local EHS authority reference for **Sonoma County**. Orientation for the research subagent: who administers the local programs and where their adopted codes live. Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- **Permit Sonoma — Fire Prevention and Hazardous Materials Division, Hazardous Materials Unit** is the county CUPA for **unincorporated** Sonoma County (~1,800 regulated businesses). Administers the Unified Program elements: HMBP/Hazardous Materials Inventory, Hazardous Waste Generator/Tiered Permitting, UST, Aboveground Petroleum Storage (APSA), CalARP, and the hazardous-materials portions of the California Fire Code.
- **GOTCHA — multi-CUPA county (split):** Sonoma County is served by FOUR separate CUPAs, not one countywide agency. Three cities run their own CUPAs and Permit Sonoma covers the remainder: (1) **City of Santa Rosa Fire Department** (Santa Rosa), (2) **City of Petaluma Fire Department — Fire Prevention Bureau** (Petaluma), (3) **Healdsburg/Sebastopol CUPA** — City of Healdsburg Fire Department runs Healdsburg AND Sebastopol via a 1994 Joint Powers Agreement. Permit Sonoma's CUPA covers unincorporated areas plus the remaining incorporated cities (e.g., Cloverdale, Cotati, Rohnert Park, Sonoma, Town of Windsor) — confirm the specific city against the live page.
- https://permitsonoma.org/divisions/firepreventionandhazmat/servicesandfees/hazardousmaterialsunitandcupaprogram

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- Much of unincorporated Sonoma County is served by the **Sonoma County Fire District (SOCO Fire / SCFD)**, an independent special district formed April 2019 via consolidation of Windsor, Rincon Valley, Bennett Valley, and Mountain volunteer agencies; Russian River FPD joined July 2020 and Forestville FPD joined July 2021. Serves Town of Windsor, Larkfield-Wikiup, Mark West, Bennett Valley, Forestville, Guerneville, Bodega Bay, and surrounding unincorporated areas. — https://www.sonomacountyfd.org/fire-district-consolidations
- Other unincorporated areas fall under various independent fire protection districts and County Service Area (CSA) 40 (formed 1993 to fund fire service in unincorporated areas not within an existing district). The county-led **Fire Services Project** has been driving further consolidations. — https://sonomacounty.gov/administrative-support-and-fiscal-services/county-executives-office/strategic-initiatives/fire-services-project
- Incorporated cities run their own municipal fire departments (Santa Rosa FD, Petaluma FD, Healdsburg FD, etc.); see city files.

## Building authority

- **Permit Sonoma — Building Division** reviews, permits, and inspects building/construction in the **unincorporated** county (the nine incorporated cities run their own building departments). — https://permitsonoma.org/building

## Local code amendments

- Sonoma County adopted by reference, with local amendments, Title 24 / 2025 editions of the California Building Standards Code (incl. California Building Code Vols. 1 & 2) in **Chapter 7 (Building Regulations)** of the County Code, effective 2026-01-01 for applications submitted on/after that date. — https://library.municode.com/ca/sonoma_county/codes/code_of_ordinances?nodeId=CH7BURE

## Notes

- GOTCHA (CUPA split): FOUR CUPAs operate in Sonoma County — Permit Sonoma (county), Santa Rosa FD, Petaluma FD, and the Healdsburg/Sebastopol JPA CUPA. A facility's HMBP/CUPA submittal must go to the correct city CUPA, not automatically to the county. — https://permitsonoma.org/divisions/firepreventionandhazmat/servicesandfees/hazardousmaterialsunitandcupaprogram
- GOTCHA (fire consolidation): The independent **Sonoma County Fire District** (2019, with 2020/2021 annexations) is NOT the same as the county government and NOT a CAL FIRE contract; do not conflate "Sonoma County Fire District" with "Permit Sonoma" or the County Fire/CUPA function. — https://www.sonomacountyfd.org/fire-district-consolidations
