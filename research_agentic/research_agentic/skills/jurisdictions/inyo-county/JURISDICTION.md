## CUPA (Unified Program)

- Inyo County Department of Environmental Health Services (ICDEHS) — the single countywide CUPA / Administering Agency, CalEPA-certified for the Unified Program. Administers HMBP, Hazardous Waste Generator, Underground Storage Tanks (UST), Aboveground Petroleum Storage Act (APSA), and California Accidental Release Program (CalARP). No participating-agency split and no city-run CUPA; Bishop (the county's only incorporated city) is served by the county. Farms/ranches are excluded and regulated separately by the Inyo County Agricultural Commissioner.
- https://www.inyocounty.us/services/environmental-health/environmental-health-programs/hazardous-materials-cupa

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- No single countywide structural fire department. Local structural protection is provided by independent fire protection districts and community volunteer departments (e.g., Southern Inyo FPD, Lone Pine FPD, Paradise FPD, Independence FD, Olancha-Cartago FD, and the Bishop Fire Department). Wildland State Responsibility Area (SRA) protection falls to CAL FIRE's San Bernardino Unit (BDU), which covers San Bernardino, Inyo, and Mono counties; Inyo/Mono SRA was consolidated under the San Bernardino unit in the late 1980s.
- https://www.fire.ca.gov/who-we-are/contact-us/find-your-local-unit (San Bernardino/BDU unit) — also https://www.sifpd.org/

## Building authority

- Inyo County Building and Safety Department, established within the Department of Public Works; the Director of Public Works serves as the County Building Official. Office at 377 W. Line St., Bishop, CA 93514; (760) 873-7857.
- https://www.inyocounty.us/services/public-works/building-and-safety

## Notes

- GOTCHA (single incorporated city): Bishop is the **only** incorporated city in Inyo County; everything else is unincorporated. There are no municipal CUPAs and no fully independent municipal fire department — the county EHS is the sole CUPA.
- GOTCHA (federal-land carve-outs): very large shares of the county are federal/tribal land (Death Valley National Park, Inyo National Forest, BLM, and LADWP-owned Owens Valley lands plus the Bishop Paiute Reservation). Expect carve-outs where the federal land manager or tribe — not the county — is the authority. Southern Inyo FPD covers ~1,200 sq mi including Death Valley NP areas (Furnace Creek, Shoshone, Tecopa).
- GOTCHA (Bishop fire is a joint operation): the Bishop Fire Department is a cooperation between the City of Bishop and the Bishop (Rural) Fire Protection District, governed by both the City Council and the District Board, and also serves the Bishop Paiute Reservation under contract — it is not a standalone municipal fire department.
