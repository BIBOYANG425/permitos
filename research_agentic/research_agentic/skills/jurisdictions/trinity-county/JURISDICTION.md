> Local EHS authority reference for **Trinity County**. Orientation for the research subagent: who administers the local programs and where their adopted codes live. Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- Trinity County Environmental Health Division (within the Community Development Department) is the local CUPA, administering HMBP, Hazardous Waste Generator / Tiered Permitting, UST, APSA, and CalARP. NOTE: DTSC (the state Department of Toxic Substances Control) ran this CUPA historically; administration transferred to the county Environmental Health Division on 2025-07-01.
- https://www.trinitycounty.org/623/CUPA-Certified-Unified-Program-Agency
- https://dtsc.ca.gov/trinity-county-cupa/

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- No single countywide fire department. Wildland/State Responsibility Area protection is provided by CAL FIRE (Trinity County is largely SRA / federal forest land); structural fire protection is provided by local fire protection districts and community volunteer fire departments (e.g., Weaverville Fire Protection District / Weaverville Volunteer Fire Department, Hayfork, Trinity Center, Hyampom). Confirm the specific district/VFD for the project address.
- https://www.fire.ca.gov/
- https://www.wfdca.org/

## Building authority

## Notes

- Trinity County has NO incorporated cities — it is one of only three California counties (with Alpine and Mariposa) where every community, including the county seat Weaverville, is unincorporated. All local EHS authority is therefore county-level; no separate city files apply.
- CUPA gotcha: This was a DTSC-administered (state-run) CUPA until 2025-07-01, when the Trinity County Environmental Health Division took over administration. Older records, CERS listings, and program guidance may still reference DTSC as the regulator.
