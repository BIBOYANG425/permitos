## CUPA (Unified Program)

- **Mendocino County Division of Environmental Health — Certified Unified Program Agency (CUPA)**, housed in the Public Health Department; office at 501 Low Gap Road, Ukiah. Single countywide CUPA covering both unincorporated areas and the incorporated cities; administers the Unified Program elements including HMBP / Hazardous Materials Management Plan, Hazardous Waste Generator program, UST, APSA (aboveground petroleum), and CalARP. No incorporated city in Mendocino County runs its own CUPA (UNVERIFIED whether any city is a separate Participating Agency).
- https://www.mendocinocounty.gov/departments/public-health/environmental-health/hazardous-materials

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- **CAL FIRE Mendocino Unit (MEU)** provides State Responsibility Area / wildland fire protection across the county; structural fire service in unincorporated areas is provided by numerous independent **fire protection districts** (mostly volunteer). — https://www.firesafemendocino.org/fire-agencies
- Incorporated cities are served by municipal/JPA or district departments (see city files): Ukiah Valley Fire Authority, Fort Bragg Fire Department, Little Lake Fire Protection District (Willits).

## Building authority

- **Mendocino County Department of Planning & Building Services — Building Division**, offices in Ukiah and Fort Bragg. Administers and enforces state and local building construction regulations in the **unincorporated** county **and** in the cities of **Fort Bragg and Point Arena** (those two cities do not run their own building authority). — https://www.mendocinocounty.gov/departments/planning-building-services/building-division

## Notes
