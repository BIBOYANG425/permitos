> Local EHS authority reference for the **City of Willits** (inland city in Mendocino County). Orientation for the research subagent: who administers the local programs within the city limits and where their adopted codes live. Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- **Not its own CUPA** — served by the **Mendocino County Division of Environmental Health (CUPA)**. — https://www.mendocinocounty.gov/departments/public-health/environmental-health/hazardous-materials

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- **Little Lake Fire Protection District** — independent special district (formed 1974 from the merger of the City of Willits and the adjacent fire department) serving the City of Willits and surrounding rural areas; primarily volunteer with some career staff. — https://www.cityofwillits.org/136/Fire-Protection-District

## Building authority

- **City of Willits — Community Development Department, Building Division**, 111 E. Commercial Street, Willits. Issues building permits and conducts inspections within the city limits (separate from the county Building Division). — https://cityofwillits.org/141/Building-Safety

## Local code amendments

- Title 24 adopted via the **Willits Municipal Code, Title 15** (Buildings and Construction) on Municode. Specific 2025-edition adopting ordinance UNVERIFIED. — https://library.municode.com/ca/willits/codes/code_of_ordinances?nodeId=TIT15BUCO

## Notes

- GOTCHA (fire is a district, not municipal): Willits is served by the **Little Lake Fire Protection District**, a standalone special district, rather than a city fire department.
- Willits runs its **own building department** (unlike Fort Bragg and Point Arena, which use the county).
