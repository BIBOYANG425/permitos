> Local EHS authority reference for the **City of St. Helena** (Napa Valley, ~5,900 residents). Orientation for the research subagent: who administers local programs inside city limits and where adopted codes live. Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- **Not its own CUPA** — served by the **Napa County Division of Environmental Health (DEH)**, the single countywide CUPA. — https://www.napacounty.gov/1919/CUPA

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- **St. Helena Fire Department** — municipal department serving the City of St. Helena (one engine staffed 24/7; six full-time plus part-time/paid-call firefighters). Independent of the county CAL FIRE contract; dispatched by the CAL FIRE Emergency Command Center in St. Helena. The County separately contracts WITH the City for service to specified adjoining unincorporated areas. — https://www.cityofsthelena.gov/162/Fire

## Building authority

- **City of St. Helena — Building Division** issues building permits within city limits. — https://www.cityofsthelena.gov/245/Building-Division

## Notes

- GOTCHA (reverse contract): St. Helena runs its OWN municipal fire department; the County of Napa contracts with St. Helena (not the other way around) to cover adjoining unincorporated areas.
