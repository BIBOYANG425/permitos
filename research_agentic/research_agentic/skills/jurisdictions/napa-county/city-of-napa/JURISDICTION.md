> Local EHS authority reference for the **City of Napa** (largest city in Napa County, ~75,000–80,000 residents). Orientation for the research subagent: who administers local programs inside city limits and where adopted codes live. Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- **Not its own CUPA** — served by the **Napa County Division of Environmental Health (DEH)**, the single countywide CUPA. — https://www.napacounty.gov/1919/CUPA

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- **City of Napa Fire Department** — municipal department (established 1906) serving the City of Napa. Independent of the county CAL FIRE contract; the City of Napa FD is the one Napa-county fire agency NOT dispatched by the CAL FIRE ECC in St. Helena. — https://www.cityofnapa.org/352/Fire-Department

## Building authority

- **City of Napa — Community Development Department / Building Division** issues building permits within city limits (county PBES handles only unincorporated areas). — https://www.cityofnapa.org/228/Building-Division

## Notes

- GOTCHA: The City of Napa runs its own fire department AND its own dispatch arrangement separate from the CAL FIRE St. Helena ECC that covers the rest of the county.
