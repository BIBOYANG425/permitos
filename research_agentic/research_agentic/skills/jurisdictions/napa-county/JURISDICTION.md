> Local EHS authority reference for **Napa County**. Orientation for the research subagent: who administers the local programs and where their adopted codes live. Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- **Napa County Division of Environmental Health (DEH)** — single countywide CUPA covering the unincorporated county **and all incorporated cities** (Napa, American Canyon, St. Helena, Calistoga, Town of Yountville). No city runs its own CUPA and there are no separate Participating Agencies. Administers the Unified Program elements: HMBP (~1,250 facilities), Hazardous Waste Generator/Tiered Permitting (~400 facilities), UST (44 facilities), Aboveground Petroleum Storage Act (APSA), CalARP (~9 RMP facilities; most common EHS is anhydrous ammonia), and the Methamphetamine-Contaminated Property Cleanup program. DEH sits under Planning, Building & Environmental Services (PBES).
- https://www.napacounty.gov/1919/CUPA

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- **Napa County Fire Department = CAL FIRE contract** (CAL FIRE Sonoma-Lake-Napa Unit / "CAL FIRE Napa County Fire Department"). The County contracts with CAL FIRE for fire protection in unincorporated Napa County (career stations plus volunteer companies operating under the County Fire Plan approved by the Board of Supervisors). Dispatch via the CAL FIRE Emergency Command Center in St. Helena, which dispatches all Napa fire agencies except the City of Napa FD. — https://www.napacounty.gov/390/About-Napa-County-Fire-Department
- Incorporated areas run their own departments: **City of Napa Fire Dept**, **St. Helena Fire Dept**, **Calistoga Fire Dept** (each municipal), and **American Canyon Fire Protection District** (subsidiary district of the City). **Town of Yountville** does NOT staff its own department — it contracts to Napa County/CAL FIRE for service. See city files.

## Building authority

- **Napa County Planning, Building & Environmental Services (PBES) — Building Division**, 1195 Third Street, 2nd Floor, Napa, CA 94559. Enforces the California Building/Electrical/Plumbing/Mechanical codes in the **unincorporated** county; permits in cities/towns are handled by those jurisdictions directly. — https://www.napacounty.gov/3492/Building-Division

## Local code amendments

- County building/fire code adoptions are in the Napa County Code of Ordinances, Title 15 (Buildings & Construction): Ch. 15.08 Building Code, Ch. 15.32 Fire Code. — https://library.municode.com/ca/napa_county/codes/code_of_ordinances?nodeId=TIT15BUCO

## Notes

- GOTCHA (single countywide CUPA in Environmental Health): unlike some Bay Area counties where the CUPA sits in Public Works or a Fire Hazmat division, Napa's CUPA is **DEH under PBES** and is the sole CUPA for the entire county including all five incorporated jurisdictions.
- GOTCHA (fire is fragmented despite CUPA being unified): four municipal/local fire organizations operate independently of the county CAL FIRE contract — City of Napa FD, St. Helena FD, Calistoga FD, and the American Canyon FPD — while Yountville and parts of the unincorporated county are CAL FIRE-served. Do not assume "Napa County Fire" is the AHJ inside city limits.
- GOTCHA (reverse contract direction): the County contracts WITH the cities of St. Helena and Calistoga (and the Schell-Vista / South Lake County FPDs) to cover adjoining unincorporated areas — these cities are not served BY the county for their own city limits.
