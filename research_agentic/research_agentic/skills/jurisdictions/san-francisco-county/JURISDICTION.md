> Local EHS authority reference for **San Francisco County**. Orientation for the research subagent: who administers the local programs and where their adopted codes live. Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- City and County of San Francisco, Department of Public Health (SFDPH), Environmental Health Branch — Hazardous Materials and Waste Program. Single consolidated CUPA for the entire City and County of San Francisco, administering the Unified Program elements: Hazardous Materials Business Plan (HMBP), Hazardous Waste Generator (and tiered permitting), Underground Storage Tank (UST), Aboveground Petroleum Storage Act (APSA/AST), and California Accidental Release Prevention / Risk Management Plan (CalARP/RMP). Local implementing legislation is in the San Francisco Health Code: Article 21 (HMBP + UST, per H&S Code Ch. 6.95 and Ch. 6.7/6.75), Article 21A (Risk Management Plans), and Article 22 (Hazardous Waste Management, per H&S Code Ch. 6.5). There are no separate Participating Agencies (PAs) — because SF is a single consolidated city-county, the SFDPH CUPA covers the whole jurisdiction.
- https://www.sf.gov/topics/hazardous-materials-and-waste

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- San Francisco Fire Department (SFFD) — single municipal fire department for the entire City and County. The Division of Fire Prevention & Investigation / Bureau of Fire Prevention enforces the San Francisco Fire Code via plan review and inspection (new construction, remodels, fire sprinkler/alarm systems, mandated annual high-rise/residential/school inspections). — https://sf-fire.org/our-organization/division-fire-prevention-investigation

## Building authority

- City and County of San Francisco Department of Building Inspection (DBI) — issues permits and enforces the San Francisco Building, Housing, Plumbing, Electrical, and Mechanical Codes and disability-access regulations citywide. — https://www.sf.gov/departments--department-building-inspection

## Local code amendments

- 2025 San Francisco Building Code (amends 2025 California Building Code / Title 24 Parts 2 & 2.5, plus the other adopted Title 24 parts), hosted on American Legal (amlegal): https://codelibrary.amlegal.com/codes/san_francisco/latest/sf_building/0-0-0-91586
- Adopted Title 24 edition: 2025 edition adopted by local ordinance with an operative date of 2026-01-01 (San Francisco enacts the 2025 California Building Standards Code with local amendments as the 2025 San Francisco Building Code; companion 2025 SF Existing Building Code also enacted). Confirm the live amlegal/ordinance text before quoting specific amendments. — https://media.api.sf.gov/documents/2025_SFBC_Amendments.pdf

## Notes

- CONSOLIDATED CITY-COUNTY: San Francisco is a single consolidated city and county. There are no separately incorporated cities within it, so only this county file is written — all local EHS authority (CUPA, fire AHJ, building) is held by City and County of San Francisco departments (SFDPH Environmental Health, SFFD, DBI). No city files apply.
- Single CUPA, no Participating Agencies: unlike split counties (e.g., a county Environmental Health CUPA with a city fire department acting as PA), San Francisco's SFDPH Hazardous Materials and Waste Program is the sole certified CUPA covering the whole jurisdiction.
- Federal-land / special-jurisdiction carve-outs to be aware of (not separately verified for CUPA delegation here): the Presidio of San Francisco is managed by the Presidio Trust / National Park Service (federal), and the Port of San Francisco and San Francisco International Airport (SFO, which is physically located in San Mateo County) are City-owned but operate under their own authorities — confirm jurisdiction case-by-case for facilities on these properties.
