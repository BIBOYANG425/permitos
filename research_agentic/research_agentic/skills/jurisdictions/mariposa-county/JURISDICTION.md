## CUPA (Unified Program)

- Mariposa County Health Department, Environmental Health Services — the CalEPA-certified CUPA (Certified Unified Program Agency) for the **entire county**. Identifies businesses that store/use hazardous materials or generate hazardous waste; conducts routine inspections (UST facilities inspected annually, other facilities at least once every three years). Administers the Unified Program elements (HMBP/hazardous materials business plans, hazardous waste generator, UST, APSA aboveground petroleum storage, CalARP). Because Mariposa County has no incorporated cities, this single county CUPA covers all regulated businesses countywide. CERS reporting required.
- http://www.mariposacounty.gov/435/Haz-MatCUPA

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- CAL FIRE Madera-Mariposa-Merced Unit (MMU) — state-responsibility/wildland and structural fire protection for Mariposa County; unit HQ and Emergency Command Center at 5366 Highway 49 North, Mariposa, CA 95338 (the ECC dispatches 9-1-1 for the three-county unit). Mariposa County has no incorporated cities and therefore no independent municipal fire department.
- https://www.facebook.com/CALFIREMMU/ (official CAL FIRE MMU unit page; the fire.ca.gov unit roster page at https://www.fire.ca.gov/contact-us/cal-fire-units returned HTTP 403 on automated fetch — verify the MMU listing in a live browser)

## Building authority

- Mariposa County Building Department — issues residential/commercial building, electrical, mechanical, plumbing, manufactured-home, and grading permits and enforces the California Building Standards Code plus local amendments for the unincorporated county (all of Mariposa County is unincorporated). Permits processed through the county Development Services Permitting Center; phone 209-966-3934.
- http://www.mariposacounty.gov/67/Building

## Notes

- GOTCHA: Mariposa County has **no incorporated cities** — it is one of only three California counties (with Alpine and Trinity) that contains zero incorporated municipalities. The town of Mariposa (county seat) is unincorporated. Consequently there are no city-level CUPAs, no municipal fire departments, and no city building departments; the county agencies above are the sole local AHJs countywide. Land use is organized around 11 "Town Planning Areas" rather than cities.
- FEDERAL-LAND CARVE-OUT: A large share of Mariposa County is Yosemite National Park (NPS) and Sierra/Stanislaus National Forest (USFS) federal land, where federal authorities — not county EHS/fire/building — hold jurisdiction. Confirm whether a regulated facility sits on federal land before applying county programs.
