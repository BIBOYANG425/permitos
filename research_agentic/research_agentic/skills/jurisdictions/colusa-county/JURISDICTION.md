Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- Colusa County Environmental Health (within the Community Development / Development Services Department) — single CUPA for the **entire county including the cities of Colusa and Williams**; administers HMBP, hazardous waste generator, UST, APSA, and CalARP. No participating agencies or city-run CUPAs.
- https://www.countyofcolusaca.gov/426/Hazardous-Materials-Waste-CUPA

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- Unincorporated county is served by independent fire protection districts, not a single county fire department: Sacramento River FPD, Glenn-Colusa FPD, Arbuckle-College City FPD, Maxwell FPD, Princeton FPD, Williams FPD (see county Fire Districts page) — https://www.countyofcolusaca.gov/239/Fire-Districts
- City of Colusa Fire Department (municipal, combination paid/volunteer) serves the City of Colusa — https://cityofcolusa.com/fire-department/
- Williams Fire Protection Authority (JPA) serves the City of Williams and surrounding area — https://www.cityofwilliams.org/services/fire/index.php

## Building authority

- Colusa County Community Development Department, Building Division — building permits and inspection for the unincorporated county — https://www.countyofcolusaca.gov/133/Building

## Local code amendments

- Colusa County adopts the California Building Standards Code (Title 24) by reference in its County Code (Building Code chapter on Code Publishing). Confirm the adopted edition on the live page; UNVERIFIED whether the county has formally adopted the 2025 edition (statewide effective 2026-01-01) versus the prior 2022 edition. — https://www.codepublishing.com/CA/ColusaCounty/

## Notes

- Colusa County has only two incorporated cities: **Colusa** (county seat) and **Williams**. Arbuckle, Maxwell, Princeton, and Stonyford are unincorporated communities.
- GOTCHA: neither the City of Colusa nor the City of Williams runs its own CUPA — both are covered by the single Colusa County Environmental Health CUPA.
- There is no consolidated county fire department; the unincorporated county is a patchwork of independent fire protection districts (some, like Glenn-Colusa FPD, cross the county line into Glenn County).
