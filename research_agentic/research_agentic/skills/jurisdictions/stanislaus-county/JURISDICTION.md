> Local EHS authority reference for **Stanislaus County**. Orientation for the research subagent: who administers the local programs and where their adopted codes live. Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- Stanislaus County Department of Environmental Resources (DER), Hazardous Materials Division — sole **countywide CUPA** (the only CUPA in Stanislaus County per the CalEPA Unified Program directory). Administers all Unified Program elements: Hazardous Materials Business Plan (HMBP)/disclosure, CalARP, Aboveground Petroleum Storage Act (APSA), Underground Storage Tank (UST), and Hazardous Waste Generator/onsite-treatment (tiered permitting). No incorporated city (Modesto, Turlock, Ceres, etc.) runs its own CUPA or is listed as a separate Participating Agency; all jurisdictions are served by the county DER. Submit via CERS. Office at 3800 Cornucopia Way, Suite C, Modesto, CA 95358; (209) 525-6700.
- https://www.stancounty.com/er/hazmat/cupa.shtm

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- Unincorporated Stanislaus County has **no single county fire department**; it is served by ~14 independent fire protection districts. The Stanislaus County Office of Emergency Services / **Fire Warden** coordinates these districts and the **Fire Prevention Bureau** provides fire-prevention, plan-review, and inspection services to the unincorporated areas on their behalf. — https://oes.stancounty.gov/divisions/fire-warden/fire-prevention-bureau
- Incorporated cities run their own fire AHJ: City of Modesto (Modesto Fire Department), City of Turlock (Turlock Fire Department), and City of Ceres (Ceres Fire Department). See city files for the Modesto administrative-services contracts covering Turlock and Ceres.

## Building authority

- Stanislaus County Planning & Community Development Department — Building Permits Division (unincorporated areas). — https://www.stancounty.com/planning/bp/

## Local code amendments

- Stanislaus County adopted **Ordinance No. C.S. 1402**, amending Title 16 of the County Code to adopt by reference the **2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24)** — including the 2025 California Building Code, 2025 California Residential Code, 2025 California Plumbing Code, and 2025 California Electrical Code (NFPA 2023 ed.). Title 16 building code text on eCode360. — https://ecode360.com/43724379 (adoption staff report: https://www.stancounty.com/bos/agenda/2025/20251118/PH02.pdf)

## Notes

- GOTCHA: The county CUPA is the Department of Environmental Resources (DER) Hazardous Materials Division — organizationally separate from fire. Despite several cities running their own municipal fire departments and the unincorporated county being a patchwork of ~14 fire protection districts, the CUPA for hazmat/HMBP/UST/APSA/CalARP/hazwaste is the **county DER countywide**; no city or fire district runs its own CUPA.
- GOTCHA: The unincorporated county has no consolidated county fire department; the OES/Fire Warden and Fire Prevention Bureau handle fire-code/prevention functions on behalf of the independent fire protection districts rather than providing direct suppression countywide.
- GOTCHA: Modesto Fire Department provides administrative/management/command services to the Turlock Fire Department (contract approved Jan 2022, running through June 2026) and provides fire protection services within the City of Ceres (contract approved June 2021). Each city's department remains a legally separate AHJ; this is operational/administrative consolidation, NOT CUPA delegation.
