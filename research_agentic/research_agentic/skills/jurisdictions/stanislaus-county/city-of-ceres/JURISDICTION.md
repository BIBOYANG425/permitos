> Local EHS authority reference for the **City of Ceres** (third-largest city in Stanislaus County). Orientation for the research subagent: who administers local EHS programs within city limits and where the city's adopted codes live. Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- **Not its own CUPA** — served by Stanislaus County Department of Environmental Resources (DER), Hazardous Materials Division (the sole countywide CUPA). — https://www.stancounty.com/er/hazmat/cupa.shtm

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- Ceres Fire Department — fire protection within the City of Ceres is provided by the Modesto Fire Department under a joint services agreement (Ceres City Council approval June 14, 2021). The Modesto-Ceres Fire Protection Agency (a JPA between Modesto and Ceres) also serves the Industrial Fire District. — https://www.ceres.gov/173/Fire

## Building authority

- City of Ceres Building Division (Community Development). — https://www.ceres.gov/ (verify exact building-division page)

## Notes

- GOTCHA: Fire protection within Ceres is delivered by Modesto Fire under a June 2021 joint services agreement, with a Modesto-Ceres Fire Protection Agency JPA covering the Industrial Fire District. This is an operational arrangement, NOT CUPA delegation — hazmat CUPA authority remains with the county DER.
