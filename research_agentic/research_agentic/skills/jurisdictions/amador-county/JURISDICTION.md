## CUPA (Unified Program)

- Amador County Environmental Health Department — single CUPA for the **entire county**, including all five incorporated cities (Amador City, Ione, Jackson, Plymouth, Sutter Creek). Runs HMBP, hazardous waste generator, UST, APSA (aboveground petroleum storage), and CalARP programs. Certified by CalEPA as the county CUPA in December 1997. CERS reporting required for regulated businesses.
- https://www.amadorcounty.gov/departments/environmental-health/hazardous-materials-cupa

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- CAL FIRE Amador-El Dorado Unit (AEU) — state-responsibility/wildland and contract structural fire protection for **unincorporated Amador County** (HQ at Camino, El Dorado County). https://www.fire.ca.gov/contact-us/cal-fire-units (Amador-El Dorado Unit)
- Amador Fire Protection District — local fire protection district (formed July 1990) serving unincorporated communities. https://www.amadorfire.org/
- Several cities run their own departments/districts (see city files): City of Jackson Fire Department, City of Ione Fire Department, Sutter Creek Fire Protection District (covers Sutter Creek and Amador City).

## Building authority

- Amador County Building Department — permits, plan review, and inspection for the **unincorporated area**; located at the County Administrative Center, 810 Court Street, Jackson. Incorporated cities issue their own building permits.
- https://www.amadorcounty.gov/departments/building
