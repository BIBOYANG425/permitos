## CUPA (Unified Program)

- Calaveras County Environmental Management Agency (EMA), Environmental Health Department — the single CUPA for the **entire county** (certified by CalEPA effective 2002-06-01). Administers HMBP/Hazardous Materials Inventory, CalARP, UST, APSA, Hazardous Waste Generator + tiered on-site treatment, and California Fire Code hazmat requirements. No participating agencies; no city runs its own CUPA.
- https://ema.calaverasgov.us/Environmental-Health/CUPA-Hazardous-Materials

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- Unincorporated county: CAL FIRE **Tuolumne-Calaveras Unit (TCU)**, headquartered in San Andreas, provides state-responsibility-area protection; local structure protection is delivered through independent fire protection districts (e.g., Central Calaveras Fire and Rescue, Altaville-Melones FPD) — https://www.fire.ca.gov/ (TCU); district AHJ boundaries UNVERIFIED per-district
- City of Angels Camp runs its OWN municipal fire department — see city file.

## Building authority

- Calaveras County Building Department, San Andreas (unincorporated county) — https://building.calaverasgov.us/

## Local code amendments

- Title 24 building/construction provisions are in Calaveras County Code of Ordinances Title 15 (Buildings and Construction) on Municode — https://library.municode.com/ca/calaveras_county/codes/code_of_ordinances — specific adopted CBC edition (2022 vs 2025) UNVERIFIED from live pages reached.

## Notes

- Calaveras County has only ONE incorporated city: **Angels Camp** (incorporated 1912). All other communities (San Andreas the county seat, Arnold, Murphys, Valley Springs, etc.) are unincorporated.
- The CUPA sits inside the Environmental Management Agency (EMA), which also houses Air Pollution Control, On-Site Wastewater, and Ag/Weights & Measures — but the regulatory CUPA function is the Environmental Health Department.
