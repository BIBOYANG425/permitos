## CUPA (Unified Program)

- Sierra County Environmental Health — single countywide CUPA, designated by CalEPA in 2002. Administers HMBP, hazardous waste generator, UST, APSA (aboveground petroleum storage), and CalARP. No participating-agency split; the lone incorporated city (Loyalton) is served by this county CUPA, not its own. Located at 202 Front Street, Loyalton.
- https://sierracounty.ca.gov/234/Hazardous-MaterialsCUPA

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- No single countywide fire department. Unincorporated eastern Sierra County is served by Sierra County Fire Protection District #1 (volunteer; Sierraville/Sattley/Calpine stations, ~515 sq mi from the Sierra crest to the Nevada line, excluding Loyalton). Western/other unincorporated communities are served by other local volunteer fire entities (specific district assignments per community UNVERIFIED). State Responsibility Area wildland protection is CAL FIRE's Nevada-Yuba-Placer Unit (NEU), which covers unincorporated Nevada, Placer, Sierra, Sutter, and Yuba counties.
- https://www.sierracountyfireprotectiondistrict1.org/about-us

## Building authority

- Sierra County Building Inspection Division, under the Sierra County Department of Planning (Downieville). Serves unincorporated Sierra County only; the County directs City of Loyalton projects to the city.
- https://www.sierracounty.ca.gov/194/Building

## Notes

- GOTCHA (one incorporated city only): Loyalton (incorporated 1901, general-law city) is the sole incorporated city in Sierra County. It is NOT its own CUPA (served by Sierra County Environmental Health) and its building permits route through the county, but it does run its own municipal volunteer fire department — see the city file.
- GOTCHA (fire is fragmented): there is no countywide fire department. Sierra County FPD #1 covers eastern unincorporated areas but explicitly excludes Loyalton; the District separately contracts to provide fire/EMS to Loyalton-adjacent unincorporated pockets (Sierra Brooks, Loyalton Pines, the Loyalton Mill Site). Always confirm the responding district by community.
- Heavily federal land (Tahoe and Plumas National Forests; Humboldt-Toiyabe NF on the east) — expect federal-land carve-outs where the USFS, not the county, is the land manager. California's second-least-populous county (~3,200 residents).
