> Local EHS authority reference for the **City of Loyalton** (Sierra County's only incorporated city). Orientation for the research subagent: which programs the city runs itself versus which fall to Sierra County. Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- **Not its own CUPA** — served by Sierra County Environmental Health (the countywide CUPA for HMBP, hazardous waste generator, UST, APSA, CalARP).
- https://sierracounty.ca.gov/234/Hazardous-MaterialsCUPA

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- City of Loyalton Fire Department — the city's own all-volunteer municipal fire department (located on Front Street, Loyalton; ISO/PPC class 6/6x). Distinct from Sierra County Fire Protection District #1, which serves the surrounding eastern unincorporated areas and contracts to cover Loyalton-adjacent pockets (Sierra Brooks, Loyalton Pines, the Loyalton Mill Site). SRA wildland protection is CAL FIRE Nevada-Yuba-Placer Unit.
- https://www.sierracountyfireprotectiondistrict1.org/loyalton-fire-department-direct-service-contract

## Building authority

- The City of Loyalton does not maintain a full building department; permit/indemnification forms are available at City Hall but plan check and inspection authority effectively rest with the county-level process. The City handles building services within the city limits (the County Building Division explicitly excludes the city and directs projects to Loyalton). Exact division of plan-check responsibility between City Hall and Sierra County Planning is UNVERIFIED.
- https://www.cityofloyalton.org/government.html

## Notes

- GOTCHA (city fire dept vs. district): Loyalton runs its OWN municipal volunteer fire department; do not assume Sierra County FPD #1 is the AHJ inside city limits — the District explicitly excludes Loyalton from its core coverage area.
- Building-permit administration is split: forms originate at Loyalton City Hall, but the County provides the building-inspection function for the broader area; confirm who issues the actual permit for a given city project.
