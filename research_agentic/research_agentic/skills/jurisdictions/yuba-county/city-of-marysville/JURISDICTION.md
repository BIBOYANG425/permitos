> Local EHS authority reference for the **City of Marysville** (Yuba County seat). Orientation for the research subagent: who administers local programs inside the city and where their adopted codes live. Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- **Not its own CUPA** — served by the Yuba County Environmental Health Division (Community Development & Services Agency), the countywide CUPA — https://www.yuba.gov/departments/community_development/environmental_health/hazardous_materials_(cupa)/index.php

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- Marysville Fire Department — an **independent municipal fire department** operating out of one station; serves the city (~12,000 residents) and mutual-aid areas, and operates a hazardous-materials response vehicle serving Yuba and Sutter counties — https://www.marysville.ca.us/fire-department

## Building authority

- City of Marysville Building Department (building permits) — https://www.marysville.ca.us/building-permit

## Notes

- GOTCHA: Marysville runs its **own** municipal fire department (one of the few independent departments in the county) but does **not** run its own CUPA — hazmat/UST/hazwaste programs are administered by the Yuba County CUPA.
- Do not confuse Marysville (Yuba County) with the City of Yuba City, which is across the river in Sutter County.
