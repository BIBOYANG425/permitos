> Local EHS authority reference for the **City of Alturas** (Modoc County seat and the county's only incorporated city). Orientation for the research subagent: who administers the local programs and where their adopted codes live. Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- **Not its own CUPA** — served by Modoc County Environmental Health (the countywide CUPA), 202 W. 4th St., Alturas, CA 96101; phone 530-233-6310.
- https://environmentalhealth.co.modoc.ca.us/nav/certified_unified_program_agency_(cupa).php

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- City of Alturas Fire Department — the city operates its own municipal fire department (primarily volunteer-staffed with some career personnel; handles burn permits, fire marshal duties, and EMS). Station at 103 S. Howard St., Alturas, CA 96101. SRA wildland coverage around the city falls to CAL FIRE Lassen-Modoc Unit.
- https://www.cityofalturas.us/
- https://www.fire.ca.gov/ (CAL FIRE) — Lassen-Modoc Unit

## Building authority

- City of Alturas Building Department — the city issues its own building permits within city limits (the county Building and Safety Department covers the unincorporated area only). City Hall at 200 W. North St., Alturas, CA 96101; phone 530-233-2512.
- https://www.cityofalturas.us/
- City Code of Ordinances (Municode), Chapter 5 Building, Construction and Housing: https://library.municode.com/ca/alturas

## Local code amendments

- City of Alturas Code of Ordinances, Chapter 5 (Building, Construction and Housing) adopts the California Building Standards Code (Title 24) via Municode. The specific adopted Title 24 *edition* (2022 vs. 2025) is UNVERIFIED — confirm whether the city has adopted the 2025 edition that took effect statewide 2026-01-01.
- https://library.municode.com/ca/alturas/codes/code_of_ordinances?nodeId=CH5BUCOHO

## Notes

- Alturas is the only incorporated city in Modoc County. It runs its own fire department, police department, and building department, but relies on the **county** Environmental Health Division for CUPA / Unified Program (HMBP, hazwaste generator, UST, APSA) administration.
