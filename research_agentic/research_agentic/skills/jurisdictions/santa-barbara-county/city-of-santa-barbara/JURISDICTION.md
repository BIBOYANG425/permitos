> Local EHS authority reference for the **City of Santa Barbara** (Santa Barbara County). Orientation for the research subagent: who administers the local programs within the city limits and where the city's adopted codes live. Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- **Not its own CUPA** — served by the County of Santa Barbara, Public Health Department, Environmental Health Services — Hazardous Materials Unit (the county-wide CUPA). No City of Santa Barbara Participating Agency is documented.
- https://www.countyofsb.org/2171/Hazardous-Materials

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- **Santa Barbara City Fire Department (SBFD)** — a career municipal fire department serving the City of Santa Barbara; 8 fire stations — https://santabarbaraca.gov/government/departments/fire-department

## Building authority

- City of Santa Barbara, **Community Development Department — Building & Safety Division**; enforces Title 24 (California Building, Electrical, Plumbing, Mechanical, Energy Codes and disabled-access regulations) — https://santabarbaraca.gov/government/departments/community-development/building-safety

## Local code amendments

- Title 24 adoption: City of Santa Barbara Building & Safety enforces the **2025 Edition of the California Building Standards Codes beginning January 2, 2026** (projects submitted on or before December 17, 2025 may still comply with the 2022 edition) — https://santabarbaraca.gov/government/departments/community-development/building-safety
- City of Santa Barbara Municipal Code — https://library.municode.com/ca/santa_barbara

## Notes

- Runs its own municipal fire department (SBFD), but is NOT its own CUPA — hazmat HMBP/UST/APSA/hazwaste/CalARP permitting for facilities in the city is handled by the Santa Barbara County Environmental Health CUPA, not the City Fire Department.
