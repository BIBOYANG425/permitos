> Local EHS authority reference for the **City of Santa Maria** (Santa Barbara County's largest city). Orientation for the research subagent: who administers the local programs within the city limits and where the city's adopted codes live. Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- **Not its own CUPA** — served by the County of Santa Barbara, Public Health Department, Environmental Health Services — Hazardous Materials Unit (the county-wide CUPA). In the 2017 J&A-Santa Maria cogeneration fire, Santa Maria Fire deferred hazmat response to the Santa Barbara County Fire Hazmat team and the County Environmental Health CUPA conducted the regulatory enforcement, indicating the County CUPA — not the City — administers HMBP/UST/APSA/hazwaste within Santa Maria.
- https://www.countyofsb.org/2171/Hazardous-Materials

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- **Santa Maria Fire Department** — a career municipal fire department providing fire, EMS, and hazardous-materials emergency response (member of the North County multi-agency Hazardous Materials Team) — https://www.cityofsantamaria.org/services/departments/fire-services

## Building authority

- City of Santa Maria, **Community Development Department — Building Division**; reviews and inspects residential, commercial, and industrial projects under the uniform construction codes — https://www.cityofsantamaria.org/services/departments/community-development/building-division-homepage

## Local code amendments

- Title 24 adoption: UNVERIFIED — the 2025 California Building Standards Code became the statewide standard effective 2026-01-01; confirm the City of Santa Maria adopted edition on the live Building Division page and in the Santa Maria Municipal Code (https://www.cityofsantamaria.org/about-us/codes-and-documents).

## Notes

- Largest city in Santa Barbara County. Runs its own municipal fire department, but is NOT its own CUPA — hazmat permitting is handled by the Santa Barbara County Environmental Health CUPA.
