## CUPA (Unified Program)

- County of Santa Barbara, Public Health Department, **Environmental Health Services — Hazardous Materials Unit**, which is the certified **Certified Unified Program Agency (CUPA)** for the County of Santa Barbara. Single county-wide CUPA administering the Unified Program elements: Hazardous Materials Business Plan (HMBP), California Accidental Release Prevention (CalARP), Hazardous Waste Generator (and tiered permitting), Underground Storage Tank (UST), and Aboveground Petroleum Storage Act (APSA/AST). Reporting via CERS. No city-run CUPA or Participating Agency is documented in the county — the county CUPA covers all eight incorporated cities (Buellton, Carpinteria, Goleta, Guadalupe, Lompoc, Santa Barbara, Santa Maria, Solvang) and the unincorporated areas.
- https://www.countyofsb.org/2171/Hazardous-Materials

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- Unincorporated areas + Cities of Buellton, Goleta, and Solvang (which do not run their own fire departments): **Santa Barbara County Fire Department** — a County department (it also operates a CAL FIRE cooperative/contract function); 16 fire stations across South/Central/North battalions — https://sbcfire.com/about/
- Wildland / State Responsibility Area (SRA): CAL FIRE (Santa Barbara County Fire serves as the local government cooperator) — https://www.fire.ca.gov/contact-us/locations/units
- Cities running their OWN municipal fire departments: **Santa Barbara**, **Santa Maria**, and **Lompoc** (see city files). Carpinteria and Guadalupe fire service: UNVERIFIED here — confirm whether served by County Fire or a separate district.

## Building authority

- County of Santa Barbara, **Planning & Development Department — Building & Safety Division** (unincorporated areas); administers and enforces State and County building, grading, electrical, plumbing, and mechanical codes; permitting via the County Citizen Portal (Accela) — https://www.countyofsb.org/522/Building-Safety-Division

## Notes

- CUPA structure: single county-wide CUPA run by Public Health / Environmental Health Services Hazardous Materials Unit — NOT by the Fire Department, and NOT split among cities. Confirmed by the Santa Barbara Independent reporting on the 2017 J&A-Santa Maria cogeneration fire, which describes "the Santa Barbara County Certified Unified Program Agency ('CUPA') — a division of Santa Barbara County Environmental Health Services" (https://www.independent.com/2021/12/14/settlement-reached-with-santa-maria-fruit-processing-company-titan/).
- GOTCHA (county fire ≠ CAL FIRE contract county): unlike neighboring San Luis Obispo County, Santa Barbara County Fire is a stand-alone County department (it operates a CAL FIRE cooperative function but is not a CAL FIRE contract county). It directly serves only the three cities without their own departments (Buellton, Goleta, Solvang) plus unincorporated land.
- GOTCHA (hazmat emergency response vs CUPA permitting): a multi-agency North County Hazardous Materials Team (Santa Barbara County Fire, Santa Maria Fire, Lompoc Fire) handles hazmat emergency response. In the 2017 Santa Maria cogeneration fire, Santa Maria Fire called the Santa Barbara County Fire Hazmat team for response, and the County Environmental Health CUPA conducted the regulatory follow-up — illustrating that emergency response and CUPA permitting/enforcement are separate functions.
