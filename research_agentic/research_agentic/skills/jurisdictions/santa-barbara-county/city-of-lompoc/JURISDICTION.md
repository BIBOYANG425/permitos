> Local EHS authority reference for the **City of Lompoc** (Santa Barbara County). Orientation for the research subagent: who administers the local programs within the city limits and where the city's adopted codes live. Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- **Not its own CUPA** — served by the County of Santa Barbara, Public Health Department, Environmental Health Services — Hazardous Materials Unit (the county-wide CUPA). No City of Lompoc Participating Agency is documented.
- https://www.countyofsb.org/2171/Hazardous-Materials

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- **Lompoc Fire Department** — a municipal fire department serving the City of Lompoc (primary response area ~12 square miles, including USP Lompoc); member of the North County multi-agency Hazardous Materials Team — https://www.cityoflompoc.com/services/fire

## Building authority

- City of Lompoc, **Building & Safety Services Division**; handles building permits, plan review (building, fire, mechanical, electrical, plumbing), and code compliance — https://www.cityoflompoc.com/government/departments/building-safety-services

## Local code amendments

- Title 24 adoption: UNVERIFIED — the 2025 California Building Standards Code became the statewide standard effective 2026-01-01; confirm the City of Lompoc adopted edition on the live "Building and Fire Codes" page (https://www.cityoflompoc.com/government/departments/building-safety-services/building-and-fire-codes) and in the Lompoc Municipal Code.

## Notes

- Runs its own municipal fire department, but is NOT its own CUPA — hazmat permitting is handled by the Santa Barbara County Environmental Health CUPA. Note Lompoc's Fire Prevention function sits within the city's Building & Safety Services Division.
- Federal-land note: the City's response area includes the United States Penitentiary, Lompoc (federal facility) — federal facilities may fall outside local CUPA/AHJ jurisdiction for some programs.
