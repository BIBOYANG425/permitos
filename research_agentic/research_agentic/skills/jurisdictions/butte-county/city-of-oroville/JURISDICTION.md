> Local EHS authority reference for the **City of Oroville** (Butte County seat, second-largest city). Orientation for the research subagent: who administers local programs inside city limits and where the adopted codes live. Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- **Not its own CUPA** — served by Butte County Public Health Department, Environmental Health Division (the countywide CUPA). The county CUPA office is itself located in Oroville (202 Mira Loma Dr).
- https://www.buttecounty.net/764/CUPA

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- "Oroville Fire Department" — staffed and operated by CAL FIRE / Butte County Fire Department under a cooperative agreement effective July 2022 (NOT an independent municipal department). Same stations/equipment/jurisdiction, state-employed firefighters — https://www.orovilleca.gov/208/Fire-Department
- Cooperative-agreement transition source: https://krcrtv.com/news/local/oroville-fire-to-cal-fire-cooperative-agreement-transitions-17-firefighters-to-the-state

## Building authority

- City of Oroville Planning & Development Services Department, Building Division — plan check / permits / occupancy for plumbing, electrical, mechanical, energy, green building — https://www.orovilleca.gov/242/Building-Division

## Local code amendments

- Oroville Municipal Code Chapter 15.08 (Building Code) adopts the California Building Standards Code, Title 24 — confirm the currently adopted edition on the live page — https://ecode360.com/44174026

## Notes

- GOTCHA (recent consolidation): the city's fire service was transitioned to CAL FIRE / Butte County Fire under a cooperative agreement in July 2022; the "Oroville Fire Department" name persists but it is no longer city-operated.
- Despite being the county seat and hosting the county CUPA office, Oroville does **not** run its own CUPA — it is covered by Butte County Environmental Health.
