> Local EHS authority reference for the **City of Chico** (largest city in Butte County). Orientation for the research subagent: who administers local programs inside city limits and where the adopted codes live. Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- **Not its own CUPA** — served by Butte County Public Health Department, Environmental Health Division (the countywide CUPA).
- https://www.buttecounty.net/764/CUPA

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- City of Chico Fire Department (Chico Fire-Rescue) — independent municipal fire department (serving Chico since 1873); provides fire suppression, hazmat mitigation, and fire prevention/code enforcement within city limits — https://chicoca.gov/City-Services/Public-Safety/Fire-Department/index.html

## Building authority

- City of Chico Community Development Department, Building Division — issues building permits, plan check, inspections (eTRAKiT portal) — https://chicoca.gov/Departments/Community-Development/Building-Division/

## Local code amendments

- Chico Municipal Code Title 16 (Buildings and Construction), Chapter 16.02 adopts the California Building Standards Code, Title 24 — verify the currently adopted edition (the code text last referenced the 2022 editions; confirm whether the 2025 edition has been adopted) — https://codelibrary.amlegal.com/codes/chico/latest/chico_ca/0-0-0-11444

## Notes

- Chico has its own fire department and building division but is still under the **county-run CUPA** (Butte County Environmental Health) for all hazmat/hazwaste/UST/APSA/CalARP programs.
