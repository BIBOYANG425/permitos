> Local EHS authority reference for **Butte County**. Orientation for the research subagent: who administers the local programs and where their adopted codes live. Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- Butte County Public Health Department, Environmental Health Division — the **single CUPA for the entire county including all incorporated cities** (HMBP/HMRRP, hazardous waste generator, UST, APSA aboveground tanks, CalARP). No city in Butte County runs its own CUPA. Located at 202 Mira Loma Dr, Oroville; CERS contact ccers@buttecounty.net / (530) 552-3880.
- https://www.buttecounty.net/764/CUPA
- CalEPA CERS regulator entry ("Butte County Environmental Health", type CUPA): https://cersapps.calepa.ca.gov/Public/Directory/RegulatorDetails/1008

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- CAL FIRE / Butte County Fire Department — county fire dept operated by CAL FIRE under a long-standing Schedule A cooperative agreement; serves the unincorporated county plus the City of Oroville, Town of Paradise, and Cities of Gridley and Biggs — https://www.buttecounty.net/472/Fire
- City of Chico Fire Department (Chico Fire-Rescue) — independent municipal department, the one city with its own fire dept — https://chicoca.gov/City-Services/Public-Safety/Fire-Department/index.html

## Building authority

- Butte County Department of Development Services, Building Division (unincorporated county) — Permit Center, 7 County Center Dr, Oroville — https://www.buttecounty.net/252/Building

## Local code amendments

- Butte County Code of Ordinances, Chapter 26 (Buildings) adopts the California Building Standards Code, Title 24 — confirm the currently adopted edition (2025 vs 2022) on the live page — https://library.municode.com/ca/butte_county/codes/code_of_ordinances?nodeId=CH26BU

## Notes

- GOTCHA (recent consolidation): As of July 2022 the **City of Oroville Fire Department is staffed and operated by CAL FIRE / Butte County Fire** under a cooperative agreement (17 city firefighters transitioned to state employees). It is still branded "Oroville Fire Department" but is no longer an independent municipal department — source: https://krcrtv.com/news/local/oroville-fire-to-cal-fire-cooperative-agreement-transitions-17-firefighters-to-the-state
- GOTCHA: **No city in Butte County runs its own CUPA** — Butte County Environmental Health is the sole CUPA countywide (including Chico, despite Chico having its own fire dept and building division).
- The only fully independent municipal fire department in the county is the City of Chico's.
