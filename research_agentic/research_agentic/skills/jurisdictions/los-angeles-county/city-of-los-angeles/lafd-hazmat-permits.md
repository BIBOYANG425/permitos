# LAFD Hazmat Operational Permits — City of Los Angeles

## Two Parallel Permit Tracks

Businesses storing or handling hazardous materials in Los Angeles must navigate two overlapping permit systems:

| Track | Issuing Agency | What It Covers |
|---|---|---|
| Annual Consolidated CUPA Permit | LAFD CUPA | HMBP, hazardous waste, UST, APSA, CalARP — state unified program |
| Operational Fire Permit | LAFD / Office of Finance | Authority to operate equipment or store materials posing a fire hazard under the LA Fire Code |

Both are required for most industrial hazmat storage operations. They run on different administrative tracks and have separate fees.

---

## Track 1: Annual Consolidated CUPA Permit

### Trigger (Who Must Obtain)

Any business that stores, handles, or uses hazardous materials in reportable quantities under CA HSC Chapter 6.95:
- **Solids:** ≥ 500 pounds
- **Liquids:** ≥ 55 gallons
- **Gases:** ≥ 200 cubic feet

Also required for facilities with: hazardous waste generation (RCRA), underground storage tanks (USTs), aboveground petroleum storage (APSA), or CalARP-regulated processes.

### Application Process

1. **Create account in CERS** (California Environmental Reporting System) at https://cers.calepa.ca.gov
2. **Submit Hazardous Materials Business Plan (HMBP)** — six required sections:
   - Business Activities Questionnaire (determines which regulatory programs apply)
   - Business Owner/Operator Information (enter LLC/corporation name; include billing contact)
   - Hazardous Materials Inventory (all chemicals above reportable thresholds)
   - Site Map (show hazmat locations for emergency responders)
   - Emergency Response/Contingency Plan (per Title 19 CCR § 2658)
   - Employee Training Plan (initial + annual; records retained 3 years)
3. **Annual submission window:** January 1 – March 1 each year
4. **Invoice mailed** to business once HMBP submission is complete in CERS
5. **Permit mailed** after payment is received; post at facility

### HMBP Compliance Status

LAFD maintains a monthly-updated permitted locations list. Statuses:
- **PERMITTED** — current
- **NOT PERMITTED** — outstanding fees or incomplete submission
- **SUSPENDED** — formal enforcement action initiated; may lead to revocation

Permit may be revoked for non-compliance. CERS late submittal fee: **$500**

### Renewal Cycle

Annual. Certify or re-submit HMBP in CERS between January 1 and March 1 every year.

## Track 2: Operational Fire Permit (LAFD Fire Code)

### Trigger

Required for any installation or operation of appliances, systems, or equipment that pose a fire hazard, including equipment used to handle hazardous material (LA Fire Code / LAMC Chapter 5, Article 7).

### Application Process (New Businesses)

1. Submit **Business Tax Registration Certificate (BTRC)** to the LA Office of Finance
2. Obtain a **Business Referral Slip** from the Office of Finance (contains legal name, account number, permit type, business classification, and address)
3. Present to **LADBS Zoning Section** for signing and stamping
4. Return to **Office of Finance** and pay the fire permit fee
5. **LAFD inspection** by a Fire Inspector
6. Permit granted or denied; if approved, **permit mailed** once entered in LAFD database

### Renewal

- **Due date:** December 31 annually
- **Payment:** in person, by mail, or online at finance.lacity.gov
- **Contact for renewal:** Finance.CustomerService@lacity.org | (844) 663-4411

### Note on Fee Collection

The **Office of Finance** collects operational fire permit fees; the **LAFD** handles inspection and enforcement. Permits are non-transferable — business sales require new certificates.

## CUPA Project Permits (Separate from Annual)

Required for specific activities beyond routine storage:

| Category | Scope |
|---|---|
| New Project Permit | UST installation, abandonment, modification, repair, relining, temporary closure, site assessments |
| Fuel Transfer Permit | Removing fuel from UST or aboveground tank |
| Amendment | Changes to existing non-expired permit |
| Expedited Review | 1-week turnaround (additional prepayment required) |
| Extension | Adds 180 days to expiring permit (requires justifiable cause) |
| Contractor Change | Notification of contractor substitution |

**Application:** via Google Forms (link on LAFD CUPA site)
**Payment:** Call LAFD CUPA at (213) 978-3680, Tue–Thu 9am–2pm (Plan Check option)
**Standard review:** up to 3 months | **Expedited:** ~1 week
**Inactive applications** voided after 30 days of no activity
