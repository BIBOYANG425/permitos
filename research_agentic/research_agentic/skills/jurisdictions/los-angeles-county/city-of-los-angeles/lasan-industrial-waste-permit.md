# LASAN Industrial Wastewater Permit (IWP) — City of Los Angeles

## Program Overview

The Industrial Waste Management Division (IWMD) administers the Industrial Wastewater Permit program for the City of Los Angeles. All industries and certain commercial facilities that discharge wastewater other than domestic sewage to the City's publicly owned treatment works (POTW) must obtain an IWP before discharging. The program implements the federal Clean Water Act pretreatment requirements and the local Industrial Waste Control Ordinance (LAMC § 64.30).

## Who Must Obtain a Permit

Any facility discharging process wastewater to the City sewer system unless explicitly exempt. Exempt categories include:

- Food service establishments that generate no oil/fat/grease waste
- Cooling tower blow-down from systems under 25-ton capacity
- Self-service laundries with machines under 20 lb capacity
- Facilities discharging less than 200 gallons per day total

## Discharger Classifications

**Significant Industrial User (SIU)** — triggers strictest monitoring and reporting obligations:
- Subject to federal Categorical Pretreatment Standards (40 CFR Parts 405–471); **or**
- Discharges ≥ 25,000 gallons per day of process wastewater; **or**
- Designated by the LASAN Director as having a reasonable potential to adversely affect the POTW or violate applicable standards

SIUs are subdivided into:
- **Categorical Industrial Users (CIU)** — subject to both federal categorical standards and LASAN local limits
- **Non-Categorical Significant Industrial Users (NCSIU)** — subject only to LASAN local limits

**Local Industrial User (LIU)** — lighter requirements; includes laboratories, medical/dental offices, restaurants, auto shops, and similar smaller generators.

## Application Process

1. Complete the Industrial Wastewater Permit Application form
2. Submit application and pay the application fee to IWMD
3. IWMD staff reviews submission for completeness and accuracy
4. Inspector conducts on-site facility inspection verifying:
   - Accuracy of the application information
   - Wastewater conveyance methods (drains, sumps, pre-treatment connections)
   - Existing or proposed treatment systems
5. Permit issued; permit document specifies facility-specific discharge monitoring requirements, effluent limitations, and reporting schedule

The online application portal (when accessible): https://www.lacitysan.org/san/faces/home/portal/s-lsh-wwd/s-lsh-wwd-cw/s-lsh-wwd-cw-iwm/iwp

## Regulated Parameters (Local Limits)

LASAN enforces local limits to protect the POTW and receiving waters. Parameters typically included in permits:

| Parameter | Basis |
|---|---|
| BOD (Biochemical Oxygen Demand) | Quality surcharge trigger; domestic baseline = 265 mg/L |
| TSS (Total Suspended Solids) | Quality surcharge trigger; domestic baseline set by ordinance |
| pH | Corrosion protection of collection system |
| Oil & Grease (O&G) | Blockage prevention |
| Cadmium (Cd) | Local limit |
| Chromium (Cr, total and hexavalent) | Local limit |
| Copper (Cu) | Local limit |
| Lead (Pb) | Local limit |
| Nickel (Ni) | Local limit |
| Zinc (Zn) | Local limit |
| Cyanide (CN) | Local limit |

Categorical Industrial Users are also subject to applicable federal standards (e.g., 40 CFR Part 433 for metal finishing, Part 413 for electroplating, etc.) and must sample per 40 CFR 403.12(g) unless LASAN specifies otherwise.

## Fees

### Application Fee
- **$616** per Industrial Wastewater Permit application (as of FY2024/2025 update; prior rate was $370 effective 2012)

### Annual Permit / Inspection-Control Fee
- Assessed annually to all permit holders; covers inspection and program administration
- Amount varies by facility classification and discharge volume — confirm with IWMD

### Quality Surcharge Fee (QSF)
Assessed when facility wastewater exceeds domestic sewage strength (BOD baseline 265 mg/L):
- Charges are calculated based on the concentration of BOD and TSS above the domestic baseline, multiplied by the volume discharged
- Rates are adjusted periodically by City ordinance; FY2024/2025 rates are in the fee notice at the source URL above

### Volume Charge
- Applied based on total volume of process wastewater discharged to the sewer; rate per 1,000 gallons — confirm current rate with IWMD

### Non-Transferability
Permits are non-transferable. Change of ownership requires a new application and fee.

## Monitoring and Reporting Requirements

- SIUs must self-monitor and submit Discharge Monitoring Reports (DMRs) to IWMD on a schedule set in the permit (typically semi-annual or annual for NCSIUs; more frequent for CIUs)
- CIUs must comply with 40 CFR 403.12(g) sampling and analysis requirements
- Facilities must notify IWMD of significant operational changes that could affect discharge character or volume; permit amendment may be required
- Violations trigger compliance schedules, fines, and potential permit revocation under LAMC § 64.30

## Renewal

- Annual renewal cycle
- Permit holders must keep permit current; LASAN invoices permit holders for annual fees
