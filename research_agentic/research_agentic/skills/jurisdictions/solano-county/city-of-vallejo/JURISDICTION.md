> Local EHS authority reference for the **City of Vallejo** (largest city in Solano County, ~123,000 residents). Orientation for the research subagent: who administers local EHS programs inside city limits and where the adopted codes live. Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- **Not its own CUPA** — served by the **Solano County Department of Resource Management, Environmental Health Division (HazMat Program)**, the countywide CUPA. HMBP / hazwaste / UST / APSA / CalARP for Vallejo facilities are handled by the County, not the City.
- https://www.solanocounty.gov/government/resource-management/environmental-health/hazardous-materials-hazmat-program

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- **City of Vallejo Fire Department** — municipal department (~108 staff, six stations, ~16,000 calls/yr). Its **Fire Prevention Division** handles inspection services, plan checks, permitting, fire investigations, and weed abatement within city limits. — https://www.vallejo.gov/our_city/departments_divisions/fire_department

## Building authority

- **City of Vallejo Planning & Development Services — Building Division** permits and inspects construction within city limits. — https://www.vallejo.gov/our_city/departments_divisions/planning_development_services/building_division

## Notes

- Vallejo is the most populous city in Solano County but, like every other city in the county, is **not its own CUPA** — the County Environmental Health Division is the CUPA for all Solano jurisdictions.
