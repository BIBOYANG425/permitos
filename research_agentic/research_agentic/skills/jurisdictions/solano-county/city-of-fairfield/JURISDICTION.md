> Local EHS authority reference for the **City of Fairfield** (Solano County seat; ~120,000 residents). Orientation for the research subagent: who administers local EHS programs inside city limits and where the adopted codes live. Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- **Not its own CUPA** — served by the **Solano County Department of Resource Management, Environmental Health Division (HazMat Program)**, the countywide CUPA (office is located in Fairfield at 675 Texas St). HMBP / hazwaste / UST / APSA / CalARP for Fairfield facilities are handled by the County, not the City.
- https://www.solanocounty.gov/government/resource-management/environmental-health/hazardous-materials-hazmat-program

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- **City of Fairfield Fire Department** — municipal department. Services include fire suppression, fire prevention/education, disaster preparedness, hazardous materials/confined-space rescue, and operational fire permits. Fire Prevention Division: (707) 399-5627, fireprevention@fairfield.ca.gov. — https://www.fairfield.ca.gov/government/city-departments/fire/fire-prevention-and-inspection-program/fire-permit-program

## Building authority

- **City of Fairfield Community Development Department — Building & Fire Safety / Building Safety Division**, 1000 Webster St, Fairfield, CA 94533; permits applied for via the "Fairfield BUILD" online portal. — https://www.fairfield.ca.gov/government/city-departments/community-development/building-safety

## Local code amendments

- City of Fairfield Building & Housing Code is codified in the City Code; the City has adopted the **California Building Code, 2025 Edition** (based on the 2024 IBC) as adopted/amended by the California Building Standards Commission. — https://www.codepublishing.com/CA/Fairfield/html/Fairfield05.html

## Notes

- Fairfield is the county seat and hosts the County CUPA office, but the **City is not its own CUPA** — County Environmental Health is the CUPA.
- GOTCHA (federal-land carve-out): **Travis Air Force Base** sits just east of Fairfield on federal land with its own Travis AFB Fire Department; it is outside City of Fairfield fire AHJ and local building jurisdiction. — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Travis_Air_Force_Base
