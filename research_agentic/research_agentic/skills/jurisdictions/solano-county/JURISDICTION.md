> Local EHS authority reference for **Solano County**. Orientation for the research subagent: who administers the local programs and where their adopted codes live. Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- **Solano County Department of Resource Management, Environmental Health Division (Hazardous Materials / HazMat Program)** — single countywide CUPA. It explicitly covers the unincorporated county **and all seven incorporated cities** (Benicia, Dixon, Fairfield, Rio Vista, Suisun City, Vacaville, Vallejo). No city runs its own CUPA and none is listed as a separate Participating Agency. Administers all six Unified Program elements: HMBP (Hazardous Materials Business Plan / inventory + emergency response), Hazardous Waste Generator + onsite treatment (Tiered Permitting), Underground Storage Tanks (UST), Aboveground Petroleum Storage Act (APSA / SPCC), and California Accidental Release Prevention (CalARP). Office: 675 Texas St, Suite 5500, Fairfield, CA 94533; (707) 784-6765.
- https://www.solanocounty.gov/government/resource-management/environmental-health/hazardous-materials-hazmat-program

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- **No countywide county fire department.** Solano County has no single county fire agency; the unincorporated area is served by **independent fire protection districts** (e.g., Montezuma FPD, Dixon FPD, Cordelia FPD, Vacaville FPD) plus city departments under mutual/automatic aid, coordinated through the Solano County Fire Chiefs Association. — https://www.solanocounty.gov/government/office-emergency-services/respond-during-emergency/public-safety-contacts
- Each incorporated city runs its **own municipal fire department**: **Vallejo FD**, **Fairfield FD**, **Vacaville FD**, **Benicia FD**, **Suisun City FD**, **Dixon FD** (combination dept that also serves the Dixon FPD by contract), and Rio Vista FD. See city files for the three largest (Vallejo, Fairfield, Vacaville).

## Building authority

- **Solano County Department of Resource Management, Building & Safety Services Division**, 675 Texas St, Fairfield, CA 94533; building@solanocounty.gov, (707) 784-6765. Reviews plans, issues permits, and inspects construction in the **unincorporated** county only; each incorporated city permits within its own limits. — https://www.solanocounty.gov/government/resource-management/building-safety-services

## Local code amendments

- Solano County Code **Chapter 6.3 (Building Standards and Codes)** adopts the **California Building Standards Code, 2025 Edition (Title 24)** — operative 2026-01-01 per the amending ordinance — incorporating Parts 1–12 (Building, Residential, Electrical, Mechanical, Plumbing, Energy, Fire, Green Building, etc.) with local amendments. — https://www.codepublishing.com/CA/SolanoCounty/html/SolanoCounty0603.html
- County also adopts a **2025 Fire Hazard Severity Zone Maps Ordinance**, a **Consensus Fire Ordinance**, and a **Water Efficient Landscape Ordinance**. — https://www.solanocounty.gov/government/resource-management/building-safety-services/building-safety-services-ordinances

## Notes

- GOTCHA (single countywide CUPA, fragmented fire): the CUPA is consolidated in **Environmental Health under Resource Management** and is the **sole CUPA for the entire county including all seven cities** — no city is its own CUPA. Fire AHJ, by contrast, is highly fragmented: every city has its own municipal FD and the unincorporated county is split among multiple independent fire protection districts (there is no county fire department).
- GOTCHA (city FD vs same-name fire protection district): **Vacaville FD** (municipal, City limits) is a SEPARATE entity from the **Vacaville Fire Protection District** (independent special district serving ~135 sq mi of surrounding unincorporated area, split off from the City in 1981). Likewise **Dixon FD** is a combination department that serves both the City and, by contract, the **Dixon Fire Protection District**. Do not conflate the city department with the like-named district. — https://www.vfpd.net/
- GOTCHA (federal-land carve-out): **Travis Air Force Base** (east of Fairfield) is federal land with its own **Travis AFB Fire Department** and is outside city/county fire AHJ and local building jurisdiction. — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Travis_Air_Force_Base
- GOTCHA (mixed Title 24 editions across jurisdictions during the 2026 transition): the County and City of Fairfield adopted the **2025** Title 24; City of Vacaville's posted ordinance still referenced the **2022** edition (2021 IBC) and was required to move to the 2025 edition by 2026-01-01 — confirm each jurisdiction's currently operative edition on its live Municode/code page.
