## CUPA (Unified Program)

- Placer County Environmental Health is the designated Certified Unified Program Agency (CUPA) for all areas of the county **except the City of Roseville**. It administers the five Unified Program elements: Hazardous Materials Business Plan (HMBP), Hazardous Waste Generator (HWG), Underground Storage Tank (UST), Aboveground Petroleum Storage Act (APSA), and California Accidental Release Program (CalARP). The City of Roseville Fire Department runs its own CUPA covering facilities inside Roseville city limits (see Roseville city file).
- https://www.placer.ca.gov/3164/Hazardous-Materials

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- Unincorporated county: Placer County Fire Department (PCFD) provides fire and EMS to unincorporated Placer County under a cooperative agreement with CAL FIRE Nevada-Yuba-Placer Unit (NEU), which provides state-responsibility-area protection. Numerous independent fire districts also serve local-responsibility areas. Incorporated cities Roseville, Rocklin, and Lincoln each run their own municipal fire departments (see city files). Source (PCFD): https://www.placer.ca.gov/10415/Fire-Department — Source (CAL FIRE NEU): https://www.fire.ca.gov/who-we-are/organization/units (verify the live NEU unit page; specific district roster UNVERIFIED)

## Building authority

- Placer County Community Development Resource Agency (CDRA), Building Services Division (offices in Auburn and at the Tahoe office). https://www.placer.ca.gov/2128/Building-Services

## Notes

- CUPA split: Placer County Environmental Health is the CUPA countywide EXCEPT within the City of Roseville, where the Roseville Fire Department is the CUPA (verified). No other city in the county runs its own CUPA.
- Six incorporated municipalities: Roseville, Rocklin, Lincoln, Auburn (county seat), Loomis, and Colfax. The three largest (Roseville, Rocklin, Lincoln) operate their own municipal fire departments (verified for Roseville and Rocklin; Lincoln Fire Department verified via mutual-aid references — confirm on city site).
