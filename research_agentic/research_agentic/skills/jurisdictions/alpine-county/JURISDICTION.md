## CUPA (Unified Program)

- Alpine County Health Department (Health and Human Services) — single CUPA for the entire county; CalEPA-certified to implement all six Unified Program elements (HMBP, hazardous waste generator, UST, APSA/SPCC, CalARP, Cal Fire Code HMRRP). No participating-agency split (county has no incorporated cities).
- https://www.alpinecountyca.gov/297/Hazardous-Materials-Mgmt-and-CERS-Report

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- No countywide fire department. Served by independent local volunteer / district fire entities: Eastern Alpine Fire and Rescue, Bear Valley Fire Department, and Kirkwood Fire Department (Kirkwood Meadows Public Utility District). State Responsibility Area wildland protection falls under CAL FIRE / its Amador-El Dorado-Alpine Unit (CAL FIRE unit assignment UNVERIFIED for live confirmation).
- https://alpinecountyca.gov/691/Fire-Departments

## Building authority

- Alpine County Building Safety Division, under the Community Development Department (building permits, plan checks, inspections).
- https://alpinecountyca.gov/157/Building-Safety-Division

## Notes

- GOTCHA (no incorporated cities): Alpine County has **zero incorporated cities** — every community (Markleeville the county seat, Bear Valley, Kirkwood, Woodfords, etc.) is unincorporated. All local EHS authority is the county's; there are no municipal CUPAs or municipal fire departments. (Only Mariposa and Trinity counties share this trait in CA.)
- California's least populous county (~1,200 residents), heavily federal land (Humboldt-Toiyabe / Eldorado / Stanislaus National Forests) — expect federal-land carve-outs where USFS, not county, is the land manager.
