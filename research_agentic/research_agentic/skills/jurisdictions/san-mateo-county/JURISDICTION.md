> Local EHS authority reference for **San Mateo County**. Orientation for the research subagent: who administers the local programs and where their adopted codes live. Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- **San Mateo County Environmental Health Services — Certified Unified Program Agency (CUPA)** — single countywide CUPA, designated by the State Secretary for Environmental Protection in 1996. Covers all incorporated cities and the unincorporated area; no separate city-run CUPA verified. Administers all six Unified Program elements: Hazardous Materials Business Plan (HMBP / Community Right-to-Know), Hazardous Waste Generator, Tiered Permitting, Underground Storage Tank (UST), California Accidental Release Prevention (CalARP), and Aboveground Petroleum Storage (APSA). Electronic reporting via CERS.
- https://www.smchealth.org/hazardous-materials-cupa

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- No single countywide structural fire department. Service is split among municipal departments, JPAs, fire protection districts, and a CAL FIRE contract:
  - **San Mateo Consolidated Fire Department** — serves Belmont, Foster City, and the City of San Mateo (consolidated 2019). https://www.smcfire.org/
  - **North County Fire Authority (NCFA)** — JPA serving Brisbane, Daly City, and Pacifica (formed 2003). https://northcountyfire.gov/
  - **Redwood City Fire Department** — municipal (county seat; ISO Class 1). https://www.redwoodcity.org/departments/fire-department
  - **South San Francisco Fire Department** — municipal. https://www.ssfca.gov/Departments/Fire-Department
  - **CAL FIRE San Mateo–Santa Cruz Unit (CZU)** — provides service to unincorporated areas / County Fire (coastside and rural). See also county Fire Chiefs Association: https://www.smcfireservice.org/
- HazMat response/cleanup for the county is provided by the **Belmont Fire Protection District Hazardous Materials Team** under contract with the County / Emergency Services Council (response only — not the CUPA regulatory authority). https://www.smcgov.org/ceo/emergency-management-hazardous-materials-team

## Building authority

- **San Mateo County Planning and Building Department — Building Section** — issues permits and performs inspections for **unincorporated** San Mateo County via the Development Review Center / Accela online permit center. — https://www.smcgov.org/planning/building

## Local code amendments

- County adopted the **2025 Edition of the California Building Standards Code (Title 24, Parts 1–12)** plus the 2024 International Property Maintenance Code and local amendments; see County building regulations pages and Municode. The County also adopted (Feb 25, 2020) reach-code amendments to the Energy Code (Part 6) and CALGreen (Part 11) requiring all-electric buildings, solar, and EV charging in unincorporated areas. — https://www.smcgov.org/planning/county-division-vii-building-regulations and https://library.municode.com/ca/san_mateo_county/codes/code_of_ordinances

## Notes

- GOTCHA (CUPA placement): San Mateo's CUPA sits in **Environmental Health Services** (County Health), not in a fire department or Public Works. It is a **single countywide CUPA** — incorporated cities (incl. those with their own fire departments like Redwood City and South San Francisco) are still regulated by the county CUPA for HMBP/hazwaste/UST/APSA/CalARP.
- GOTCHA (fire fragmentation): There is **no countywide fire department**. Watch for JPAs/consolidations that mask the AHJ — **San Mateo Consolidated Fire** (Belmont, Foster City, San Mateo) and **North County Fire Authority** (Brisbane, Daly City, Pacifica) each cover multiple cities; do not assume a city with no "Fire Department" page runs its own.
- GOTCHA (HazMat response vs. CUPA): Emergency HazMat response is contracted to the **Belmont FPD HazMat Team**, which is distinct from the County Environmental Health CUPA that handles permitting/inspection.
