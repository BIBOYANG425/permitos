## CUPA (Unified Program)

- **Not its own CUPA** — served by Nevada County Environmental Health Department (the countywide CUPA), whose western-county office is in Nevada City. HMBP/CERS, hazardous waste generator, UST, and APSA programs for businesses in Nevada City are administered by the county.
- https://www.nevadacountyca.gov/2144/Hazardous-Materials-CUPA

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- Nevada City contracts its fire services through the City of Grass Valley Fire Department (GVFD). After a 2018 shared-administration pilot, the Nevada City and Grass Valley fire departments fully merged administration in 2020 under a single fire chief; Nevada City Station 5 (201 Providence Mine Road) remains staffed under the arrangement. Nevada City, Grass Valley, and the Nevada County Consolidated Fire District operate a long-standing Joint Operational Area (JOA) (boundary-drop / closest-resource response, shared station staffing). https://www.nevadacityca.gov/295/Nevada-City-Fire-Department

## Building authority

- **Contracts with Nevada County** for building department services: the City handles initial application/approval, then plans go to the Nevada County Community Development Agency Building Department for permit issuance, plan review, and inspections. The City retains its own Title 15 (Buildings and Construction) in the municipal code. City forms: https://www.nevadacityca.gov/213/Forms-Applications — County Building: https://www.nevadacountyca.gov/1814/Building-Permits-Zoning

## Notes
