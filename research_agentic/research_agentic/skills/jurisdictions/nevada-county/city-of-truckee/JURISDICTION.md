> Local EHS authority reference for the **Town of Truckee** (Nevada County's largest incorporated jurisdiction, eastern county). Orientation for the research subagent: who administers the local programs and where their adopted codes live. Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- **Not its own CUPA** — served by Nevada County Environmental Health Department (the countywide CUPA), which maintains an eastern-county office in Truckee. HMBP/CERS, hazardous waste generator, UST, and APSA programs for businesses in Truckee are administered by the county.
- https://www.nevadacountyca.gov/2144/Hazardous-Materials-CUPA

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- Truckee Fire Protection District (TFPD) — an independent all-risk special district (not a Town department) providing fire, EMS, and hazmat response across Truckee and surrounding eastern Nevada County. TFPD adopted the 2025 California Fire Code (and portions of the 2024 International Fire Code) via Ordinance 02-2025, with local WUI/climatic amendments. https://www.truckeefire.org/ — Fire codes: https://www.truckeefire.org/fire-codes

## Building authority

- Town of Truckee Community Development Department, Building & Safety Division — the Town runs its own building department (separate from Nevada County). Office at 10183 Truckee Airport Road, Truckee, CA; (530) 582-7821; online permitting via eSuite. https://www.townoftruckee.gov/209/Building-Permits

## Local code amendments

- The Town of Truckee enforces the 2025 California Building Standards Code; plan submittals on or after 2026-01-01 must comply with the 2025 codes. Town adoption of the California codes (with Town amendments) is in Municipal Code Chapter 15 / Title 15. https://www.townoftruckee.gov/266/Municipal-Code-Town-Charter (specific 2025 adoption ordinance number and Town-specific amendment list UNVERIFIED)

## Notes

- Largest incorporated jurisdiction in Nevada County (~17,400 residents) and the only one in the eastern (Tahoe-side) part of the county; runs its own Building & Safety Division but EHS/CUPA is the county's (verified).
- Fire is provided by the independent Truckee Fire Protection District, not a Town fire department; TFPD's boundaries extend beyond the Town limits into unincorporated eastern Nevada County (and historically a small Placer County portion) (TFPD as the Truckee AHJ verified; exact district boundary extent UNVERIFIED).
