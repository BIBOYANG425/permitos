## CUPA (Unified Program)

- Nevada County Environmental Health Department (within the Nevada County Community Development Agency) is the Certified Unified Program Agency (CUPA) for the entire county, including the three incorporated jurisdictions (City of Grass Valley, City of Nevada City, and the Town of Truckee). It administers the Unified Program elements: Hazardous Materials Business Plan / hazmat inventory (CERS), hazardous waste generator program, underground storage tanks (UST), and aboveground petroleum storage (APSA), plus the county Household Hazardous Waste program. Environmental Health maintains a western-county office in Nevada City and an eastern-county office in Truckee. No separate city-run CUPA and no participating agency (PA) were found; the county appears to be the sole CUPA countywide. Program contact: (530) 265-1222, Option 3.
- https://www.nevadacountyca.gov/2144/Hazardous-Materials-CUPA

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- Unincorporated county: CAL FIRE Nevada-Yuba-Placer Unit (NEU) provides state-responsibility-area (SRA) fire protection; numerous local fire protection districts cover local-responsibility areas, the largest being the Nevada County Consolidated Fire District (NCCFD), which covers ~143 square miles of western Nevada County (e.g., Alta Sierra, Banner Mountain, Hwy 49 corridor) from 10 stations. The City of Grass Valley operates a municipal fire department (Grass Valley Fire Department); since a 2018 shared-administration pilot and a full administrative merger in 2020, Nevada City contracts its fire services through Grass Valley FD (Nevada City's Station 5 is staffed under the arrangement) — the two cities and NCCFD coordinate operations under a long-standing Joint Operational Area (JOA). The Town of Truckee (eastern county) is served by the Truckee Fire Protection District. Source (CAL FIRE NEU): https://www.fire.ca.gov/ — Source (NCCFD): https://www.nccfire.com/ — Source (county Fire Districts list): https://www.nevadacountyca.gov/1314/Fire-Districts

## Building authority

- Nevada County Community Development Agency (CDA), Building Department, 950 Maidu Avenue, Nevada City, CA 95959; (530) 265-1222. Serves the unincorporated county; Nevada City contracts with the county for building department services (city does plan intake/approval, then county Building issues the permit). https://www.nevadacountyca.gov/1814/Building-Permits-Zoning

## Local code amendments

- Nevada County adopted the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24); all construction projects submitted on or after 2026-01-01 must comply with the 2025 codes (including the new standalone California Wildland-Urban Interface Code, Part 7). See the county's 2025 Building Codes and Design Criteria page: https://www.nevadacountyca.gov/2890/2025-Building-Codes-and-Design-Criteria

## Notes

- Single countywide CUPA: Nevada County Environmental Health covers the unincorporated county and all three incorporated jurisdictions (Grass Valley, Nevada City, Truckee); no city-run CUPA or participating agency was found (verified via county CUPA page; absence of a PA is inferred, not affirmatively stated by the county).
- Fire-dept merger (gotcha): the Grass Valley and Nevada City fire departments entered a shared-administration pilot in 2018 and fully merged administration in 2020, with Nevada City contracting its fire services through the Grass Valley Fire Department under a single fire chief; the two cities and the Nevada County Consolidated Fire District also operate a long-standing Joint Operational Area (JOA) with boundary-drop / closest-resource response and shared station staffing (verified via 2020 city-council merger reporting and the Nevada City FD page).
- Nevada City does not run its own building department — it contracts with Nevada County for building services while retaining its own Title 15 in the municipal code (verified).
