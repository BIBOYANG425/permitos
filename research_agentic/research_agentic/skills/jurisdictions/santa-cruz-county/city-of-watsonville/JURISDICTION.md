> Local EHS authority reference for the **City of Watsonville** (second-largest city in Santa Cruz County, southern Pajaro Valley). Orientation for the research subagent: who administers the local programs and where their adopted codes live. Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- **Not its own CUPA** — served by the County of Santa Cruz Health Services Agency, Environmental Health Services (EHS), the single county-wide CUPA. HMBP / hazwaste generator / UST / APSA / CalARP for facilities inside the City of Watsonville are administered by the county EHS CUPA.
- https://scceh.com/NewHome/Programs/HazardousMaterialsWaste.aspx

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- City of Watsonville Fire Department — municipal department providing fire suppression, EMS, fire code enforcement, and fire/life-safety inspections within city limits; 372 Airport Boulevard — https://www.watsonville.gov/2323/Fire

## Building authority

- City of Watsonville Community Development Department, Building & Safety Division (permits issued at 250 Main Street; within city limits, not the county Unified Permit Center) — https://www.watsonville.gov/

## Notes

- GOTCHA: Watsonville runs its OWN municipal fire department and OWN Building & Safety Division, but it does NOT run its own CUPA — hazardous-materials/Unified Program permitting still routes to Santa Cruz County EHS.
