> Local EHS authority reference for the **City of Capitola** (smallest incorporated city in Santa Cruz County; sometimes styled "Town of Capitola"). Orientation for the research subagent: who administers the local programs and where their adopted codes live. Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- **Not its own CUPA** — served by the County of Santa Cruz Health Services Agency, Environmental Health Services (EHS), the single county-wide CUPA. HMBP / hazwaste generator / UST / APSA / CalARP for facilities inside Capitola are administered by the county EHS CUPA.
- https://scceh.com/NewHome/Programs/HazardousMaterialsWaste.aspx

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- Central Fire District of Santa Cruz County — independent special district (result of the 2021-2022 consolidation of the former Central and Aptos/La Selva fire protection districts) providing fire and EMS to Capitola, Soquel, Aptos, Live Oak, La Selva Beach, and Rio Del Mar — https://www.centralfiresc.org/

## Building authority

- City of Capitola Building Department (one-permit system covering building/mechanical/plumbing/site drainage; within city limits, not the county Unified Permit Center) — https://www.cityofcapitola.org/building

## Notes

- GOTCHA: Capitola is served by the independent Central Fire District of Santa Cruz County (a special district), not a municipal fire department. The city runs its own building department. Hazmat/Unified Program permitting routes to Santa Cruz County EHS.
