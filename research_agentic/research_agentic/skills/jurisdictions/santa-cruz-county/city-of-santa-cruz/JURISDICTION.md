> Local EHS authority reference for the **City of Santa Cruz** (largest city in Santa Cruz County, county seat). Orientation for the research subagent: who administers the local programs and where their adopted codes live. Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- **Not its own CUPA** — served by the County of Santa Cruz Health Services Agency, Environmental Health Services (EHS), the single county-wide CUPA. HMBP / hazwaste generator / UST / APSA / CalARP for facilities inside the City of Santa Cruz are administered by the county EHS CUPA.
- https://scceh.com/NewHome/Programs/HazardousMaterialsWaste.aspx

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- City of Santa Cruz Fire Department — municipal department providing fire suppression, EMS, fire prevention/code enforcement, marine rescue, and hazmat first response within city limits — https://www.cityofsantacruz.com/government/city-departments/fire-department

## Building authority

- City of Santa Cruz Planning & Community Development, Building Division (permits/inspection within city limits; not the county Unified Permit Center) — https://www.cityofsantacruz.com/government/city-departments/planning-and-community-development

## Notes

- GOTCHA: the City of Santa Cruz runs its OWN fire department and OWN building division, but it does NOT run its own CUPA — hazardous-materials/Unified Program permitting still routes to Santa Cruz County EHS.
