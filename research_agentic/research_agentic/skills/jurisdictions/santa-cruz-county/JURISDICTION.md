> Local EHS authority reference for **Santa Cruz County**. Orientation for the research subagent: who administers the local programs and where their adopted codes live. Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- County of Santa Cruz Health Services Agency, Environmental Health Services (EHS) — designated by CalEPA in 1996 as the single county-wide CUPA. It administers all six Unified Program elements (HMBP / Hazardous Materials Release Response Plans & Inventories; CalARP; Underground Storage Tanks; Aboveground Petroleum Storage Tanks/APSA; Hazardous Waste Generators; On-site Hazardous Waste Treatment) and enforces local Santa Cruz County Code Chapter 7.100. Jurisdiction covers the unincorporated county AND all four incorporated cities (Santa Cruz, Watsonville, Capitola, Scotts Valley). No city in the county runs its own CUPA and there are no participating agencies (PAs).
- https://scceh.com/NewHome/Programs/HazardousMaterialsWaste.aspx
- http://scceh.com/Home/Programs/HazardousMaterialsPrograms-CUPA.aspx

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- Unincorporated areas not served by a local fire protection district: Santa Cruz County Fire Department (SCCFD), operated under contract by CAL FIRE San Mateo-Santa Cruz Unit (CZU); HQ at 6059 Highway 9, Felton — https://countyfire.santacruzcountyca.gov/
- Wildland / State Responsibility Area (SRA): CAL FIRE San Mateo-Santa Cruz Unit (CZU) — https://www.fire.ca.gov/ (unit also covers San Mateo and San Francisco counties)
- Mid-county incorporated/unincorporated areas (Capitola, Soquel, Aptos, Live Oak, La Selva Beach, Rio Del Mar): Central Fire District of Santa Cruz County — https://www.centralfiresc.org/
- Other independent districts: Scotts Valley Fire Protection District (serves City of Scotts Valley), Felton Fire Protection District, Pajaro Valley Fire Protection District. Cities of Santa Cruz and Watsonville run their own municipal fire departments (see city files).

## Notes

- GOTCHA (single county-wide CUPA): Santa Cruz County EHS is the ONLY CUPA in the county. All four cities — including Santa Cruz and Watsonville (which have their own fire departments and building departments) — are served by the county CUPA for HMBP/hazwaste/UST/APSA/CalARP. There is no city-run CUPA and no PA split.
- GOTCHA (county fire is CAL FIRE-contracted): the "Santa Cruz County Fire Department" is not a standalone county agency; it is staffed and operated by CAL FIRE (San Mateo-Santa Cruz / CZU Unit) under contract, covering unincorporated areas not within a local fire protection district.
- GOTCHA (fire consolidation, 2021-2022): the former Central Fire Protection District and Aptos/La Selva Fire Protection District consolidated into the new "Central Fire District of Santa Cruz County" (LAFCO-approved; board transitioned via the November 2022 election). Older references to "Aptos/La Selva Fire" or the old Central district are obsolete.
- GOTCHA (CZU wildfire context): the 2020 CZU Lightning Complex fires burned large portions of the unincorporated north county (Bonny Doon, Boulder Creek, Davenport area). Rebuild/permitting in those burn-scar areas may involve additional county CDI and CAL FIRE requirements; verify per-parcel.
