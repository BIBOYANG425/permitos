## CUPA (Unified Program)

- **Tuolumne County Division of Environmental Health** (a division of the county Community Development Department) — the single CUPA for the **entire county**, certified by CalEPA. Administers HMBP/Hazardous Materials Inventory (CERS), Hazardous Waste Generator, Underground Storage Tanks (UST), and the Aboveground Petroleum Storage Act (APSA). CalARP locally UNVERIFIED. CalEPA's October 2024–July 2025 Unified Program Performance Evaluation names the CUPA as "Tuolumne County Division of Environmental Health" and rated implementation satisfactory with improvement needed; no participating agencies and no city-run CUPA were identified.
- https://www.tuolumnecounty.ca.gov/1555/Hazardous-Materials
- https://calepa.ca.gov/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/Tuolumne-2024-FSOF.pdf (2024 Final Summary of Findings)

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- Unincorporated county: **Tuolumne County Fire Department (TCFD)**, which operates under a cooperative fire protection agreement with **CAL FIRE (Tuolumne-Calaveras Unit / TCU)**, headquartered in San Andreas. TCFD jurisdiction covers ~2,200 sq mi outside of tribal lands, community services district fire departments, fire protection districts, and the City of Sonora. CAL FIRE TCU provides state-responsibility-area (SRA) wildland protection across Tuolumne and Calaveras counties — https://www.tuolumnecounty.ca.gov/1473/Fire-Department ; https://www.fire.ca.gov/ (TCU)
- Local structure protection elsewhere is delivered by independent districts (Columbia FPD, Jamestown FPD, Tuolumne Fire District, Groveland CSD Fire) and the City of Sonora Fire Department — district AHJ boundaries UNVERIFIED per-district.
- The **Tuolumne County Fire Authority (TCFA)** is a joint powers authority formed Feb 2021 (members: City of Sonora FD, Columbia FPD, County of Tuolumne FD, Groveland CSD FD, Jamestown FPD, Tuolumne Fire District) created to place a fire-funding parcel-tax measure on the ballot — it is a funding vehicle, NOT a merger of AHJ jurisdiction — https://www.gcsd.org/tuolumne-county-fire-authority

## Notes

- **Sonora is the ONLY incorporated city** in Tuolumne County (and is the county seat); all other communities (Tuolumne, Jamestown, Columbia, Groveland, Twain Harte, etc.) are unincorporated.
- Notable structural point: both the **CUPA (Environmental Health Division)** and the **Building and Safety Division** sit inside the same **Community Development Department**.
- Federal-land / tribal carve-outs: a large eastern portion of the county is **Yosemite National Park** and **Stanislaus National Forest** (federal jurisdiction), and the **Tuolumne Band of Me-Wuk Indians** holds tribal trust land (Black Oak Casino, Tuolumne Rancheria) — county fire AHJ explicitly excludes tribal lands. Exact regulatory boundaries for these carve-outs UNVERIFIED.
