> Local EHS authority reference for **Mono County**. Orientation for the research subagent: who administers the local programs and where their adopted codes live. Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- Mono County Environmental Health Division — Hazardous Materials Business Unit / CUPA. Single countywide CUPA administering HMBP, hazardous waste generator, UST, APSA (aboveground petroleum storage), and CalARP; reporting via CERS. Covers the entire county including the Town of Mammoth Lakes (no separate city CUPA). Programs administered out of the Mammoth Lakes office.
- https://monocounty.ca.gov/environmental-health/page/electronic-reporting-and-hazardous-materials-business-plan-requirements

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- No single countywide fire department. Wildland/state-responsibility lands are served by **CAL FIRE San Bernardino/Inyo/Mono Unit**. Local structural fire protection is provided by ~11 independent fire protection districts (e.g. Antelope Valley FPD, Bridgeport FPD, Chalfant Valley, June Lake FPD, Lee Vining FPD, Long Valley FPD, Mammoth Lakes FPD, Mono City FPD, Paradise FPD, Wheeler Crest FPD, White Mountain FPD). Confirm the specific district for a given parcel.
- https://monocounty.ca.gov/sdsa
- https://www.fire.ca.gov/who-we-are/units (CAL FIRE BDU — San Bernardino/Inyo/Mono Unit)

## Building authority

- Mono County Community Development Department — Building Division. Issues building permits for the **unincorporated** areas of Mono County only; explicitly does not permit work within the Town of Mammoth Lakes.
- https://monocounty.ca.gov/building

## Local code amendments

- Building regulations are codified in Mono County Code Title 15, Chapter 15.04 (adoption of the California Building Standards Code, Title 24, in unincorporated Mono County). The 2025 Title 24 edition is the current statewide code; confirm the adopting ordinance and edition on Municode. Exact adopting ordinance number/date: UNVERIFIED.
- https://library.municode.com/ca/mono_county/codes/code_of_ordinances?nodeId=TIT15BUCO_CH15.04BURE

## Notes

- Mammoth Lakes is the county's only incorporated municipality (Town of Mammoth Lakes, incorporated 1984). All other communities (Bridgeport county seat, Lee Vining, June Lake, Walker/Coleville, Crowley Lake, Benton, etc.) are unincorporated and fall under county Building plus their local fire protection district.
- CUPA is countywide: even within the Town of Mammoth Lakes, Unified Program oversight is the county Environmental Health Division, not a city agency.
- Structural fire protection is fragmented across many independent special districts — there is no consolidated county fire department, so the AHJ depends on the parcel's district.
