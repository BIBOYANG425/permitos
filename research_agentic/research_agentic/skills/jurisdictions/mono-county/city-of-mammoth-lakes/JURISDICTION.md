> Local EHS authority reference for the **Town of Mammoth Lakes** (Mono County's only incorporated municipality). Orientation for the research subagent: who administers the local programs and where their adopted codes live. Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- **Not its own CUPA** — served by the Mono County Environmental Health Division (Hazardous Materials Business Unit / CUPA), the countywide Unified Program agency. HMBP / hazwaste-generator / UST / APSA / CalARP reporting via CERS to the county.
- https://monocounty.ca.gov/environmental-health/page/electronic-reporting-and-hazardous-materials-business-plan-requirements

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- Mammoth Lakes Fire Protection District (MLFPD) — an independent **special district** governed by an elected Board of Commissioners, NOT a Town of Mammoth Lakes municipal department. Runs fire suppression, EMS, and a Prevention Division (construction plan review, fire inspections, operational permits).
- https://mlfd.ca.gov/

## Building authority

- Town of Mammoth Lakes Building Division, within the Community and Economic Development Department. Issues building permits within Town limits (the county Building Division explicitly does not permit work inside the Town).
- https://www.townofmammothlakes.ca.gov/116/Building-Division

## Notes

- Governance gotcha: fire is a separate elected special district (MLFPD), so the fire AHJ for a Mammoth Lakes project is the District, while building permits run through the Town's own Building Division — two distinct local bodies plus the county CUPA.
