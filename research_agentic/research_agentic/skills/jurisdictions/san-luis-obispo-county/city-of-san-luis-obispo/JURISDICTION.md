> Local EHS authority reference for the **City of San Luis Obispo** (county seat, largest city in San Luis Obispo County). Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- **Participating Agency (PA), not its own CUPA** — the certified CUPA is the County of San Luis Obispo Environmental Health Services, but the **City of San Luis Obispo Fire Department's Hazardous Materials program acts as a Participating Agency** for facilities within the city, handling HMBP/inventory review, hazardous-materials plan review, and permit issuance (staffed by a Hazardous Materials Coordinator). County CUPA: https://www.slocounty.ca.gov/departments/health-agency/public-health/environmental-health-services/all-environmental-health-services/cupa-program-hazardous-materials-waste
- City Fire hazardous materials program: https://www.slocity.org/government/department-directory/fire-department/fire-prevention/hazardous-materials

## Notes

- GOTCHA (CUPA Participating Agency): unlike most cities in the county, the City of San Luis Obispo Fire Department is a Unified Program Participating Agency. Businesses inside city limits submit HMBPs and obtain hazardous-materials permits through the City Fire Dept's Hazardous Materials Coordinator rather than directly to the county — but the County remains the certified CUPA of record.
