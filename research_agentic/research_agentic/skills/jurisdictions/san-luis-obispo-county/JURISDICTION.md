Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- County of San Luis Obispo Health Agency, Public Health Department, Environmental Health Services — Hazardous Materials (CUPA) Programs. Single county-wide CUPA administering the Unified Program elements: Hazardous Materials Business Plan (HMBP), California Accidental Release Prevention (CalARP), Hazardous Waste Generator (and tiered permitting), Underground Storage Tank (UST), and Aboveground Petroleum Storage Tank (APSA/AST). The **City of San Luis Obispo Fire Department is a Participating Agency (PA)** under this CUPA for facilities inside the City of San Luis Obispo; the county CUPA covers all other incorporated cities and unincorporated areas.
- https://www.slocounty.ca.gov/departments/health-agency/public-health/environmental-health-services/all-environmental-health-services/cupa-program-hazardous-materials-waste

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- Unincorporated areas + City of Pismo Beach (by contract): San Luis Obispo County Fire Department, which is operated by CAL FIRE San Luis Obispo Unit (SLU) under a contract with the County (since 1930). Staffing via cooperative agreements among CAL FIRE, the County, Los Osos and Avila Beach Community Services Districts, and the City of Pismo Beach — https://calfireslo.org/
- Wildland / State Responsibility Area (SRA): CAL FIRE San Luis Obispo Unit (SLU) — https://www.fire.ca.gov/contact-us/locations/units

## Building authority

- County of San Luis Obispo Department of Planning & Building (unincorporated areas); online permitting via PermitSLO — https://www.slocounty.ca.gov/departments/planning-building

## Notes

- GOTCHA (CUPA Participating Agency split): the county Environmental Health CUPA is the single certified CUPA, but the **City of San Luis Obispo Fire Department acts as a Participating Agency** handling HMBP/inventory review, hazardous-materials plan review, and permit issuance for facilities within the City of San Luis Obispo. Facilities inside the city limits deal with the City Fire Dept's Hazardous Materials Coordinator, not the county, for those PA functions.
- GOTCHA (county fire = CAL FIRE contract): there is no standalone county-employee fire department — the "San Luis Obispo County Fire Department" is CAL FIRE (SLU) under contract, also serving the City of Pismo Beach by contract. Cities of San Luis Obispo, Paso Robles, Atascadero, Arroyo Grande/Grover Beach (Five Cities Fire Authority), and Morro Bay run their own municipal fire service.
- A multi-agency San Luis Obispo Regional Hazardous Materials Team handles hazmat emergency response (members from CAL FIRE/County Fire, City of San Luis Obispo Fire, Five Cities Fire, Paso Robles Fire, Atascadero Fire, County Environmental Health). This is emergency response, NOT CUPA permitting authority.
