> Local EHS authority reference for **Tulare County**. Orientation for the research subagent: who administers the local programs and where their adopted codes live. Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- Tulare County Health & Human Services Agency, Environmental Health Division — the single Certified Unified Program Agency (CUPA) for Tulare County. Runs the Unified Program (HMBP/Hazardous Materials, hazardous waste generator, UST, APSA/aboveground petroleum storage, CalARP) via CERS and a Tulare County electronic-reporting portal. No incorporated city in Tulare County operates its own CUPA — all cities (including Visalia, Tulare, Porterville, Dinuba, Lindsay, Exeter, Farmersville, Woodlake) are served by the county CUPA.
- https://tularecountyeh.org/eh/our-services/hazardous-materials-cupa

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- Tulare County Fire Department — serves the unincorporated areas of the county. It is operated cooperatively with **CAL FIRE (CAL FIRE Tulare Unit / "TUU")** under a cooperative agreement. The county fire dispatch (Fire Communications Center) also serves, by contract, the cities of Exeter, Farmersville, Lindsay, and Woodlake. https://tularecounty.ca.gov/fire
- City of Visalia runs its own municipal Visalia Fire Department (career, six stations). https://www.visalia.city/depts/fire/
- City of Tulare runs its own municipal Tulare Fire Department (career, three stations). https://www.tulare.ca.gov/government/departments/fire
- City of Porterville runs its own municipal Porterville Fire Department (career, three stations). https://www.ci.porterville.ca.us/departments/fire/index.php

## Building authority

- Tulare County Resource Management Agency (RMA) — Building Department / Permit Center, for the unincorporated areas. https://tularecounty.ca.gov/rma/planning-building/building-department

## Local code amendments

- Tulare County Ordinance Code, Part VII, Chapter 15 (Building Regulations) adopts the California Building Standards Code (Title 24). The county has been updating Chapter 15 from the 2022 edition to the **2025 California Building Standards Code**; confirm the currently adopted edition before quoting. Hosted on Code Publishing: https://www.codepublishing.com/CA/TulareCounty/html/TulareCounty07/TulareCounty0715.html (a 2025 amendment is tracked at https://ceqanet.lci.ca.gov/2025120389)

## Notes

- GOTCHA: Fire AHJ is split. Tulare County Fire (run cooperatively with CAL FIRE Tulare Unit) covers unincorporated areas plus contract cities (Exeter, Farmersville, Lindsay, Woodlake), while Visalia, Tulare, and Porterville each run their own municipal fire departments. Confirm the specific city before assuming the county is the fire AHJ.
- GOTCHA: Although several cities run their own fire departments, the CUPA is NOT split — Tulare County Environmental Health is the sole CUPA countywide (no city CUPA, no Participating Agencies). Confirmed by the CalEPA 2023 Final Summary of Findings for Tulare: https://calepa.ca.gov/wp-content/uploads/2024/10/Tulare-2023-FSOF.pdf
