> Local EHS authority reference for the **City of Madera** (Madera County seat and largest incorporated city). Orientation for the research subagent: who administers local EHS programs within the city and where its adopted codes live. Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- **Not its own CUPA** — served by the Madera County Environmental Health Division (CalEPA-designated CUPA, countywide). https://www.maderacounty.com/government/community-economic-development-department/divisions/environmental-health-division/certified-unified-program-agency-cupa

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- Madera City Fire Department — a municipal department whose personnel are managed through a CAL FIRE partnership, with the Madera City Council retaining local policy oversight. https://www.madera.gov/emergency-protective-services/fire-department/

## Building authority

- City of Madera Building & Development (Building Division), 205 W 4th Street. https://www.madera.gov/business-building-development/building-development/

## Local code amendments

- City has adopted the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24); permit applications submitted on or after 2026-01-01 are subject to the 2025 codes. City Code of Ordinances on American Legal: https://codelibrary.amlegal.com/codes/madera/latest/overview

## Notes

- GOTCHA: The Madera City Fire Department is **not a fully standalone municipal force** — personnel are managed by CAL FIRE under a strategic partnership, while the City Council keeps policy oversight. It is still the AHJ within city limits.
