Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- Madera County Environmental Health Division (within the Community & Economic Development Department) — CalEPA-designated CUPA for the **entire county including all incorporated cities** (City of Madera and City of Chowchilla). CUPA programs (HMBP, hazwaste generator / onsite treatment, UST, APSA, CalARP) are administered countywide; no city runs its own CUPA.
- https://www.maderacounty.com/government/community-economic-development-department/divisions/environmental-health-division/certified-unified-program-agency-cupa

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- Madera County Fire Department — operated under contract with CAL FIRE (career personnel provided by CAL FIRE); AHJ for the unincorporated county. https://www.maderacounty.com/government/fire-department
- County Fire Marshal's Office (Department of Fire Prevention for Development) handles plan review/inspection for unincorporated areas. https://www.maderacounty.com/government/community-economic-development-department/divisions/fire-division

## Building authority

- Madera County Community & Economic Development Department, Building Division (unincorporated county). https://www.maderacounty.com/government/community-economic-development-department/divisions/building-division

## Local code amendments

- County Code of Ordinances Title 14 (Buildings & Construction) adopts the California Building Standards Code (Title 24) for the unincorporated area. Confirm the currently adopted edition (2025 Title 24 statewide effective 2026-01-01). https://library.municode.com/ca/madera_county/codes/code_of_ordinances

## Notes

- GOTCHA: County Fire is a **CAL FIRE contract** department, not a freestanding county dept — career staffing comes from CAL FIRE under a cooperative agreement, with mutual-aid support to the cities of Madera and Chowchilla.
- Both incorporated cities (Madera, Chowchilla) operate their own fire departments but are **served by the county Environmental Health CUPA**, not their own CUPAs.
