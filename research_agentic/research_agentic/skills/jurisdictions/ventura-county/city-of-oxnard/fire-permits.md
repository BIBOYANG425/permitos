## Hazardous Materials Operational Permits

### What Requires a CFC Hazmat Operational Permit

Under CFC Chapter 50 (and related chapters), the following activities require an Oxnard Fire Department operational permit:

- Storage, use, or handling of hazardous materials above CFC permit threshold quantities
- Compressed gas storage (CFC Chapter 53)
- Flammable/combustible liquid storage (CFC Chapter 57)
- Explosives (CFC Chapter 56)
- Cryogenic fluids (CFC Chapter 55)
- Organic peroxides, oxidizers, pyrophoric materials, water-reactive solids/liquids (CFC Chapters 62, 63, 64, 66)

### Application Process

1. Contact Oxnard Fire Prevention Bureau at (805) 385-7750 to confirm permit tier and required documentation.
2. Submit application to Fire Prevention Bureau, 360 W. Second Street.
3. Fire Prevention reviews the application; may conduct a site inspection before permit issuance.
4. Permit is typically annual; renewal required before expiration.

### Hazmat Release Reporting

- Any release or threatened release of hazardous materials: **immediate verbal report to Oxnard Fire Department via 911** (required by OMC local amendment to CFC)
- Written follow-up per CUPA/HMBP program requirements

## High-Piled Combustible Storage Permits

### Trigger Thresholds (CFC 2022 §3204)

A fire permit and fire protection plan are required for high-piled combustible storage when:

| Storage type | Area threshold |
|---|---|
| Class I–IV commodities | > 500 sq ft of high-piled storage (storage piled > 12 ft high, or solid shelving > 6 ft high) |
| Group A plastics and similar high-hazard | > 500 sq ft |
| Public access storage (retail) | > 2,500 sq ft |

These are state CFC baseline thresholds; Oxnard has not confirmed local reductions [VERIFY].

### Application / Plan Check Process

1. Submit a **Fire Protection Plan** (also called a High-Piled Storage Plan or Commodity Analysis) to Oxnard Fire Prevention Bureau for plan check.
2. Plan must include: storage arrangement drawings, commodity classification, rack/shelf configuration, aisle widths, automatic sprinkler design, in-rack sprinkler design (if required), and smoke/heat vent provisions.
3. Oxnard Fire Prevention conducts plan check and may request corrections.
4. Upon approval, submit for building permit (if construction is involved) through Oxnard Development Services.
5. Inspection at completion; operational permit issued by Oxnard Fire Prevention Bureau.

> [VERIFY] Obtain the Oxnard Fire Protection Planning Guide (2022 PDF at https://www.oxnard.gov/wp-content/uploads/Fire-Protection-Planning-Guide-2022.pdf) for the official Oxnard-specific plan check requirements — direct fetch returned 403; document confirmed to exist via search index.

### Sprinkler Requirements

High-piled storage typically requires enhanced automatic sprinkler systems (ESFR or in-rack), determined by:
- Commodity classification (Class I–IV vs. Group A plastics)
- Storage height
- Rack configuration (open vs. solid shelving)

Oxnard Fire Prevention Bureau approves sprinkler design; refer to Oxnard FD published standards per SEC. 14-25 amendment.

## Combined CUPA + Fire Permit Situations

Many industrial facilities require both:
- **CUPA HMBP** (filed via CERS, reviewed by Oxnard Fire CUPA) for hazardous materials inventory above thresholds
- **CFC Hazmat Operational Permit** (applied to Oxnard Fire Prevention Bureau) for operations above CFC permit thresholds

These are parallel requirements, not substitutes for each other. Coordinate with Oxnard Fire Prevention Bureau to handle both simultaneously.
