| # | Program | Acronym | Notes |

## HMBP Program Details

- **Submission system:** California Environmental Reporting System (CERS) — https://cers.calepa.ca.gov/business
- **Who must comply:** Any business storing, using, or handling hazardous materials at or above threshold quantities (thresholds set by Health & Safety Code §25507 — generally 55 gallons liquid, 500 lbs solid, 200 cubic feet gas)
- **Initial filing:** Within 30 days of bringing threshold materials onsite
- **Annual review and resubmission:** Required every year
- **Update triggers:** Material quantities increase ≥100%; new previously-undisclosed materials; address/ownership/name changes; significant operational changes (each triggers 30-day filing requirement)
- **Required content in CERS:** Hazardous materials inventory, emergency response/contingency plan, updated site map, employee training records
- **Inspection program:** Oxnard Fire conducts routine inspections to verify CERS inventory accuracy; frequency is annual for larger/higher-risk facilities, biennial for smaller facilities [VERIFY current inspection frequency schedule with Oxnard CUPA]
- **HMBP site map instructions:** Available at https://www.oxnard.gov/wp-content/uploads/hazardous-materials-business-plan-site-map-instructions.pdf

## UST Program Details

- Oxnard Fire/CUPA handles permitting for UST modification and repair
- **Required application materials:**
  - Detailed description of proposed modifications or repairs
  - Plot plans showing tank locations, piping, secondary containment, and leak detection systems
  - State Underground Tank Forms reflecting proposed changes
  - Copy of contractor's license
  - Estimated project start date
- Inspections scheduled first-come-first-served; **call at least 72 hours in advance**
- Missed inspection appointments trigger reinspection fees before rescheduling
- Unauthorized releases: verbal report within 24 hours, written report within 5 working days
- Tank integrity testing is mandatory when tanks or piping are modified
- Electronic leak detection systems require certification after modifications
- Tank modifications covered: corrosion protection systems, piping, dispenser installations, spill containment, overfill protection, line leak detectors

## CalARP Program Details

- Oxnard Fire Department is the local implementing agency (LIA) for CalARP
- Facilities with regulated substances above threshold quantities must submit a Risk Management Plan (RMP) to the USEPA
- Oxnard FD reviews the local CalARP portion, conducts Program 2/3 inspections, and coordinates with OES for worst-case scenario planning
- [VERIFY] Whether Oxnard has any locally-adopted CalARP thresholds below the state baseline

## APSA / Aboveground Storage Tanks

- Facilities storing petroleum above APSA thresholds (≥1,320 gallons aggregate aboveground, or single tank >660 gallons) must maintain a Spill Prevention, Control, and Countermeasure (SPCC) plan
- Oxnard CUPA inspects for APSA compliance
- CERS filing required for aboveground petroleum storage data
