> Research reference for the City of Oxnard Industrial Wastewater Discharge Permit program.
> Oxnard's pretreatment authority is separate from Ventura County — it is the city's own Wastewater Division under Public Works.
> Verify numeric limits against Oxnard Municipal Code Chapter 19 (sewerage) and the current permit template before quoting.

## Issuing Authority

- **Program:** City of Oxnard Wastewater Division (under Public Works Department)
- **Physical address:** 305 W. Third Street, Oxnard, CA 93030
- **Phone:** (805) 271-2200
- **Web:** https://www.oxnard.gov/public-works/wastewater-division

The Industrial Pretreatment Program is implemented under the National Pollutant Discharge Elimination System (NPDES) mandate. Oxnard's Water Pollution Control Facility (WPCF) discharges under an NPDES ocean discharge permit and is therefore federally required to operate a local pretreatment program.

## Permit Types

The city issues at least two IWDP tiers, plus a zero-discharge category:

| Class | Who it covers |
|---|---|
| **Class A (Standard IWDP)** | Industrial users discharging above domestic strength with process wastewater flows |
| **Categorical Industrial User (CIU) permit** | Facilities in EPA categorical pretreatment standard industries (e.g., metal finishing, electroplating, food processing) |
| **Significant Industrial User (SIU) permit** | Non-categorical dischargers meeting federal SIU thresholds (>25,000 gpd or pass-through/interference potential) |
| **Zero-Discharge IWDP** | Facilities that have categorical processes but claim zero discharge to sewer; still requires a permit to document no discharge |

Application forms confirmed to exist on oxnard.gov:
- `Zero-Discharge-IWDP-App.pdf` — Zero Discharge category application
- `Industrial-WW-Permit-Class-A-2.pdf` — Class A standard permit template

> [VERIFY] The city may issue a Class B (minor) category as well; confirm with Wastewater Division.

## Application Process

1. Download the applicable application form from https://www.oxnard.gov/public-works/wastewater-division (or request from Wastewater Division).
2. Complete the form including Standard Industrial Classification (SIC) code for the facility.
3. Submit to Wastewater Division at 305 W. Third Street with the application fee ($100 application fee confirmed for the IWDP application; verify current amount).
4. Wastewater Division reviews, may conduct a site inspection, and issues or denies the permit.
5. Permit duration is annual (renewed yearly); compliance monitoring and reporting required throughout.

## Regulated Parameters

Discharge standards are established in **Oxnard Municipal Code Chapter 19 (Public Works), Article I (Sewerage System; Wastewater Disposal), SEC. 19-25 (Prohibited; Discharge Standards)** and by applicable EPA categorical pretreatment standards (40 CFR).

Parameters known to be regulated under Oxnard's local limits and/or federal categorical standards:

| Parameter | Basis |
|---|---|
| Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD) | Local limit (strength-based surcharge) |
| Total Suspended Solids (TSS) | Local limit |
| Oil & Grease (O&G) | Local limit |
| pH | Local limit (typically 6.0–12.0 s.u.) |
| Total Dissolved Solids (TDS) | Local limit — **1,850 mg/L maximum** (confirmed in OMC §19-25 for new permits issued after effective date) |
| Metals (Cd, Cr, Cu, Pb, Hg, Ni, Zn, Ag, As) | Federal categorical + local limits |
| Mercury / dental amalgam | Expressly prohibited in OMC §19-25 |
| Temperature | Prohibited if sufficient to inhibit biological treatment (typically < 104°F / 40°C at POTW) |
| Radioactive wastes | Prohibited |
| Organic solvents | Prohibited (OMC §19-25) |
| Noxious/malodorous liquids and gases | Prohibited |
| Storm water (non-process) | Prohibited from sewer discharge |
| Industrial sludges and screenings | Prohibited |
| Color-imparting substances | Prohibited |

> [VERIFY] Obtain Oxnard's current local limits table (numeric mg/L values for metals, BOD, TSS, O&G, and pH) directly from Wastewater Division. The TDS limit of 1,850 mg/L and mercury prohibition are confirmed from OMC text; all other numeric limits require direct verification.

## Reporting and Compliance

- Self-monitoring reports required (frequency varies by permit tier — monthly for SIU/CIU, quarterly or annually for smaller users)
- Baseline monitoring reports required when permits are first issued
- Periodic compliance reports required for CIUs (40 CFR 403.12)
- Slug discharge notification required immediately; written follow-up within applicable timeline
- Cross-reference: Oxnard OMC §19-25 through §19-35 for specific prohibited discharge provisions and pretreatment facility requirements

## Connection to NPDES

Oxnard's WPCF operates under an NPDES ocean discharge permit (2018–2023 permit period on file; renewal status [VERIFY]). The pretreatment program requirements flow from that NPDES permit. Industrial users must not cause pass-through or interference at the WPCF.

## Key Distinctions from County

- Oxnard runs its own pretreatment program; do NOT route to Ventura County RMA or any other county program.
- IWD permit authority is with **Public Works / Wastewater Division**, not the Fire Department CUPA.
- Stormwater coordination (separate from sewer pretreatment) is with Oxnard Public Works, stormwater hotline (805) 385-7537.
