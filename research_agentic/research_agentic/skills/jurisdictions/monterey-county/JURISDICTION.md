## CUPA (Unified Program)

- County of Monterey Health Department, Environmental Health Bureau — Hazardous Materials Management Services (HMMS). Single county-wide CUPA administering all six Unified Program elements (HMBP/CalARP, hazardous waste generator + on-site treatment/tiered permitting, UST, APSA/aboveground petroleum storage); covers all incorporated cities (including Salinas) and unincorporated areas. No city in the county runs its own CUPA.
- https://www.countyofmonterey.gov/government/departments-a-h/health/environmental-health/hazardous-materials-management/cupa-programs

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- Unincorporated areas: served primarily by the Monterey County Regional Fire District (MCRFD, formerly Salinas Rural Fire Protection District) plus other local fire protection districts — https://www.mcrfd.org
- Wildland (State Responsibility Area): CAL FIRE San Benito-Monterey Unit (BEU) — https://www.readymontereycounty.org/prepare/wildfires

## Building authority

- County of Monterey Housing & Community Development (HCD), Building Services (unincorporated areas; formerly part of the Resource Management Agency/RMA, which was split into separate departments with reorganization completed ~Nov 2023) — https://www.countyofmonterey.gov/government/departments-a-h/housing-community-development/development-services/building-services/building-codes-information

## Notes

- GOTCHA (single CUPA): unlike many large CA counties, Monterey has ONE CUPA county-wide (County Environmental Health Bureau HMMS) with no participating-agency split and no city-run CUPAs — Salinas, Monterey, and Seaside all report to the county CUPA.
- GOTCHA (fire fragmentation / consolidation history): unincorporated fire protection is split across multiple districts; MCRFD is the result of decades of annexation/consolidation of rural districts. CAL FIRE BEU handles SRA wildland. Verify the specific responding agency by location.
- GOTCHA (building reorganization): the historic "RMA" name is obsolete; building/permitting for unincorporated land now sits under Housing & Community Development (HCD).
