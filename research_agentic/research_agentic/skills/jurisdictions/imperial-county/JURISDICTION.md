## CUPA (Unified Program)

- **GOTCHA — the state runs the CUPA, not a county agency.** The CUPA for Imperial County is the California **Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC)**, authorized by CalEPA as the Imperial County CUPA effective 2005-01-01. DTSC directly runs HMBP, Hazardous Waste Generator/Tiered Permitting, and CalARP. Imperial County's **Division of Environmental Health is a Participating Agency (PA)** under the DTSC CUPA, managing/inspecting APSA (since 2021-07-01) and UST (since 2024-01-01).
- DTSC Imperial CUPA: https://dtsc.ca.gov/imperial-county-cupa/
- County PA (Environmental Health, hazmat): https://www.icphd.org/environmental-health/hazardous-materials

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- **Imperial County Fire Department** — standalone county department (NOT a CAL FIRE contract). The Fire Chief also serves as the county OES Coordinator. Covers unincorporated communities, townships, and the City of Imperial; provides contract service to areas near Holtville, Brawley, and Calipatria. https://firedept.imperialcounty.org/

## Building authority

- **Imperial County Planning & Development Services — Division of Building & Safety**. https://www.icpds.com/building

## Notes

- GOTCHA: Imperial County is one of the few California counties where the **state (DTSC) is the CUPA directly** rather than a county environmental health or county fire hazmat division. HMBP/hazwaste/CalARP submittals route to DTSC; only UST and APSA are handled by the county Environmental Health PA.
- The City of Imperial does not run its own fire suppression department — it is served by the Imperial County Fire Department.
- El Centro, Calexico, and Brawley each operate their own municipal fire departments (see city files), but none is its own CUPA — all are served by the DTSC Imperial CUPA.
