## CUPA (Unified Program)

- El Dorado County Environmental Management Department (EMD) is the sole Certified Unified Program Agency (CUPA) for the entire county, including both incorporated cities (Placerville and South Lake Tahoe). EMD administers all six Unified Program elements (HMBP/hazmat inventory, hazardous waste generator, UST, APSA/aboveground petroleum, CalARP, and the hazmat release-response/area plan), and also staffs the county Household Hazardous Waste program. No participating agencies (PAs) and no city-run CUPA are listed; EMD operates offices in Placerville and South Lake Tahoe.
- https://www.eldoradocounty.ca.gov/Public-Safety-Justice/Waste-Disposal/Hazardous-Materials-Program/CUPA

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- Unincorporated county: CAL FIRE Amador-El Dorado Unit (AEU) provides state-responsibility-area fire protection; numerous independent fire protection districts cover local-responsibility areas (e.g., El Dorado County Fire Protection District, Diamond Springs-El Dorado FPD, El Dorado Hills Fire Department, Garden Valley FPD, Georgetown FPD). Source (CAL FIRE AEU): https://www.edchiefs.org/calfire-amador-eldorado-unit — Source (EDC FPD): https://www.eldoradocountyfire.com/about-us

## Notes

- Single countywide CUPA: El Dorado County EMD covers the unincorporated county and both incorporated cities; no city in the county runs its own CUPA (verified).
- Two incorporated cities only: South Lake Tahoe and Placerville. South Lake Tahoe runs its own municipal fire department (South Lake Tahoe Fire Rescue); Placerville does not and contracts the El Dorado County Fire Protection District (verified).
- Lake Tahoe Basin development is also subject to Tahoe Regional Planning Agency (TRPA) regulation layered on top of local building review (verified for South Lake Tahoe; see city file).
