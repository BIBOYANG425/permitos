## CUPA (Unified Program)

- **Not its own CUPA** — served by El Dorado County Environmental Management Department (EMD), the countywide CUPA; EMD maintains a South Lake Tahoe office and HMBP files. https://www.eldoradocounty.ca.gov/Public-Safety-Justice/Waste-Disposal/Hazardous-Materials-Program/CUPA

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- South Lake Tahoe Fire Rescue — the City's own municipal fire department, which runs fire prevention and hazardous-materials review for the city. https://www.cityofslt.gov/2521/Fire-Department

## Building authority

- City of South Lake Tahoe Development Services Department, Building Division. https://www.cityofslt.gov/2476/Building-Division

## Local code amendments

- South Lake Tahoe adopts the California Building Standards Code (Title 24) by reference with local clarifications/amendments in City Code Chapter 6.15 (Building Regulations). The published chapter references the 2022 edition; confirm whether the city has updated to the 2025 edition (effective statewide 2026-01-01). https://www.codepublishing.com/CA/SouthLakeTahoe/html/SouthLakeTahoe06/SouthLakeTahoe0615.html

## Notes

- South Lake Tahoe operates its OWN municipal fire department (South Lake Tahoe Fire Rescue), unlike Placerville (verified).
- Development and building review in the city is layered with Tahoe Regional Planning Agency (TRPA) regulations in addition to the California Building Code and city ordinances (verified per city Building Division description).
