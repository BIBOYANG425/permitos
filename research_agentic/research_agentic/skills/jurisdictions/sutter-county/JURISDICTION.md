Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- Sutter County Environmental Health Division (within the **Development Services Department**) is the CalEPA-designated CUPA for the **entire county, including the incorporated cities of Yuba City and Live Oak**; administers HMBP, hazardous waste generator, UST, AST/APSA, and CalARP. No city-run CUPAs and no participating agencies — the county is a single-agency CUPA. Office at 1130 Civic Center Blvd, Yuba City.
- https://www.sutter.gov/government/county-departments/development-services/environmental-health/cupa

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- Sutter County Fire Department (administered under the county Office of Emergency Services) serves the unincorporated county and provides fire service to the **City of Live Oak by contract**. Formed in 1996 from the merger of the Live Oak, Sutter, and Oswald-Tudor fire departments. — https://www.sutter.gov/government/county-departments/office-of-emergency-services/sutter-county-fire-department
- The **City of Yuba City** runs its own municipal fire department (see city file).

## Building authority

- Sutter County Development Services Department, Building Services Division — enforces the California Building, Electrical, Plumbing, and Mechanical Codes for the **unincorporated county only (permits are issued only for properties outside the Cities of Yuba City and Live Oak)**. Office at 1130 Civic Center Blvd, Yuba City. — https://www.sutter.gov/government/county-departments/development-services/building-services

## Local code amendments

- Sutter County adopts the California Building Standards Code (Title 24) through Title 1300 (Building) of its County Code, hosted on Municode. ## Notes

- Sutter County has only two incorporated cities: **Yuba City** (county seat and by far the largest) and **Live Oak**. Sutter, Meridian, Nicolaus, Robbins, Rio Oso, and Tudor are unincorporated communities.
- GOTCHA: the county CUPA, Building, and Development Services offices all sit at 1130 Civic Center Blvd in Yuba City, but the county Building Division does **not** issue permits inside the cities of Yuba City or Live Oak — both cities run their own building departments.
- GOTCHA: the Yuba-Sutter Hazardous Materials Response Team (YSHMRT) is a regional emergency-response team drawing first responders from Yuba City, Marysville, Linda, Wheatland, Olivehurst, Beale AFB, and Yuba and Sutter Counties. This is mutual-aid HazMat *response*, not CUPA administration — do not confuse it with the CUPA program. Note also that neighboring **Yuba County** (yuba.gov) is a separate jurisdiction with its own CUPA; do not mix the two.
- GOTCHA: the county website migrated from suttercounty.org to **sutter.gov** (suttercounty.org links 301-redirect). Prefer sutter.gov URLs.
