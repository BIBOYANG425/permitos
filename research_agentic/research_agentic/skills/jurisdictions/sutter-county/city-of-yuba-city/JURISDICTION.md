> Local EHS authority reference for the **City of Yuba City** (county seat of Sutter County). Orientation for the research subagent: who administers the local programs here and where the city's adopted codes live. Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- **Not its own CUPA** — served by the Sutter County Environmental Health Division (Development Services Department), the countywide CalEPA-designated CUPA. Businesses in Yuba City submit HMBP / hazardous waste / UST / APSA filings through the county CUPA via CERS.
- https://www.sutter.gov/government/county-departments/development-services/environmental-health/cupa

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- City of Yuba City Fire Department — municipal fire department serving the city; separate from Sutter County Fire. — https://www.yubacity.gov/departments/fire_department/index.php

## Building authority

- City of Yuba City Development Services Department, Building Division — issues building permits and enforces Title 24 within the city limits. Located at 1201 Civic Center Blvd, Yuba City. — https://www.yuba.gov/departments/community_development/building_department/index.php

## Notes

- Yuba City is the largest city in Sutter County and the county seat. It runs its **own municipal fire department** and its **own building division**, but is **not** its own CUPA — hazmat programs are handled by the Sutter County CUPA.
- Yuba City Fire personnel participate in the regional Yuba-Sutter Hazardous Materials Response Team (YSHMRT) for HazMat emergency response; this is mutual aid, not CUPA program administration.
