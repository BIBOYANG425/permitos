## CUPA (Unified Program)

- Plumas County Public Health Agency — Environmental Health Division is the single CUPA for the entire county. It administers all Unified Program elements: Hazardous Materials Business Plan (HMBP), Underground Storage Tanks (UST), Aboveground Petroleum Storage Act (APSA/AST), Hazardous Waste Generator (HWG), California Accidental Release Prevention (CalARP), and CERS electronic reporting. No participating-agency split listed; the lone incorporated city (Portola) is served by the county CUPA. Office at 520 Main Street, Room 309, Quincy, CA 95971; (530) 283-6355.
- https://plumascounty.us/276/Hazardous-Materials-Management-CUPA
- https://www.plumascounty.us/372/Hazardous-Materials-Business-Plan-HMBP

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- No single countywide structural fire department. Unincorporated communities are served by numerous independent fire protection districts and community services districts (e.g., Beckwourth Peak FPD, Graeagle FPD, Quincy FPD, Meadow Valley FPD, Crescent Mills FPD, Hamilton Branch FPD, La Porte FPD, Peninsula FPD, Prattville-Almanor FPD, Indian Valley CSD, Plumas Eureka CSD). State Responsibility Area (SRA) wildland protection falls under CAL FIRE Lassen-Modoc Unit (covers Lassen, Modoc, Plumas and portions of Shasta), with a presence in Quincy.
- https://www.plumascounty.us/2126/Plumas-County-Fire-Districts
- https://www.fire.ca.gov/ (CAL FIRE Lassen-Modoc Unit)

## Building authority

- Plumas County Building Department — building permits, plan checks, and inspections for unincorporated areas. Office at 555 Main Street, Quincy, CA 95971; (530) 283-7011. A drop box for permit/plan submittal is also at the Chester Civic Complex.
- https://www.plumascounty.us/77/Building-Department

## Local code amendments

## Notes

- GOTCHA (single incorporated city): Portola is the **only** incorporated city in Plumas County; Quincy (county seat), Chester, Greenville, Graeagle, etc. are all unincorporated and fall under county authority for CUPA and building, with fire service fragmented across many local fire protection / community services districts.
- GOTCHA (recent fire consolidation): The Beckwourth Peak Fire Protection District, approved by voters in November 2023, began service July 1, 2024, consolidating the former Sierra Valley FPD, Gold Mountain CSD, Beckwourth FPD, and the City of Portola; the Eastern Plumas Rural FPD annexed into Beckwourth Peak FPD in December 2024. https://plumassun.org/2024/02/28/beckwourth-peak-fire-charts-a-path-in-eastern-plumas-county/ ; https://bpfpd.ca.gov/history
- Large federal-land footprint (Plumas National Forest, Lassen National Forest, BLM) — expect federal-land carve-outs where USFS/BLM, not the county, is the land manager.
