## CUPA (Unified Program)

- **Yolo County Environmental Health Division (Hazardous Materials Programs)** — the countywide CUPA listed in CalEPA's CERS regulator directory (Regulator ID 1083). It administers the Unified Program for the unincorporated county and the cities of **Davis, Woodland, and Winters**, plus oversight of the **UC Davis** campus (Yolo County EH is UC Davis's CUPA; the campus runs an in-house self-audit arrangement agreed with the County — it is NOT its own CUPA). Covers HMBP/CERS inventory, Hazardous Waste Generator + Onsite Treatment (Tiered Permitting), UST, APSA/SPCC, and CalARP. Office: 292 West Beamer Street, Woodland, CA 95695; (530) 666-8646; environmental.health@yolocounty.org.
- https://www.yolocounty.gov/government/general-government-departments/community-services/environmental-health-division/hazardous-materials-programs
- CERS regulator record: https://cersapps.calepa.ca.gov/Public/Directory/RegulatorDetails/1083
- **SPLIT / Participating Agency:** the **City of West Sacramento Fire Department** runs the Unified Program inside West Sacramento city limits as a **Participating Agency (PA)** under the Yolo County CUPA umbrella. See the West Sacramento city file. — https://www.cityofwestsacramento.org/government/departments/fire/community-risk-reduction/hazardous-materials-program/cupa-program

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- **No countywide county fire department.** The unincorporated area is served by **independent fire protection districts** (e.g., Springlake FPD, West Plainfield FPD, and others) coordinated through county OES and mutual/automatic aid. — https://www.yolocounty.gov/government/board-of-supervisors/advisory-bodies/special-district-directory/fire-protection-districts
- Each of the four incorporated cities runs its **own municipal fire department**: **Davis FD**, **Woodland FD**, **West Sacramento FD**, and **Winters FD**. The **UC Davis Fire Department** is a separate campus department that also leads the county multi-agency hazmat team. See city files for Davis, Woodland, and West Sacramento. — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yolo_County,_California

## Building authority

- **Yolo County Building Inspection Services (Community Services Department)**, 292 West Beamer Street, Woodland, CA 95695; (530) 666-8037; building.division@yolocounty.gov. Reviews plans, issues permits, and inspects construction in the **unincorporated** county; each incorporated city permits within its own limits. — https://www.yolocounty.gov/government/general-government-departments/community-services/building-inspection-services

## Local code amendments

- Yolo County Code **Title 7 (Building Regulations), Chapter 1 (California Codes and Local Amendments Thereto)** adopts the California Building Standards Code (Title 24) with local amendments; Sec. 7-1.02 adopts the codes and Sec. 7-1.04 lists amendments to the California Building Code. ## Notes

- GOTCHA (CUPA split — one PA): Yolo County EH is the CUPA for the unincorporated county and the cities of Davis, Woodland, and Winters, but the **City of West Sacramento Fire Department is a Participating Agency** running the Unified Program inside West Sacramento. A West Sacramento facility reports to the City FD, not directly to County EH. — https://www.cityofwestsacramento.org/government/departments/fire/community-risk-reduction/hazardous-materials-program/cupa-program
- GOTCHA (UC Davis is NOT its own CUPA): despite operating its own fire department and a large EH&S hazmat program, **UC Davis's CUPA is Yolo County EH**; the campus uses a self-audit system agreed with the County. Do not treat UC Davis as an independent CUPA. — https://safetyservices.ucdavis.edu/units/ehs/hazardous-materials-management/cupa-self-audit-program-summary
- GOTCHA (no county fire department): there is no consolidated county fire agency; the unincorporated area is a patchwork of independent fire protection districts, and all four cities run their own municipal FDs.
- Winters is the smallest of the four cities and is served by the County CUPA; no separate Winters city file is maintained (its FD is municipal and its building permitting is handled by the City of Winters — verify on the live City of Winters page before quoting).
