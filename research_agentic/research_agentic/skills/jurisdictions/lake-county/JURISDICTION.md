## CUPA (Unified Program)

- Lake County Division of Environmental Health (Health Services Department) — single CUPA for the **entire county including the cities of Clearlake and Lakeport**; permits and inspects hazardous waste generators and on-site treatment, hazardous materials handlers (HMBP), underground storage tanks (UST), and aboveground storage tanks (AST/APSA), and handles Treated Wood Waste. No city-run CUPAs or participating agencies (PAs) were found. CalARP administration by this CUPA is UNVERIFIED.
- https://www.lakecountyca.gov/374/Hazardous-Materials-Management-CUPA

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- No consolidated county fire department; the county is served by a patchwork of independent fire protection districts plus state and federal wildland agencies. Districts include Lake County Fire Protection District, Lakeport Fire District, Kelseyville Fire Protection District, Northshore Fire Protection District, South Lake County Fire Protection District, and others — https://firesafelake.org/lake-county-fire-departments/
- CAL FIRE Sonoma-Lake-Napa Unit (LNU) provides wildland fire protection in State Responsibility Area (SRA) lands within the county (Battalions 1417 and 1418) — https://firesafelake.org/lake-county-fire-departments/

## Building authority

- Lake County Community Development Department, Building Division (Building Safety) — building permits, plan check, and inspection for the unincorporated county — https://www.lakecountyca.gov/444/Building-Safety

## Notes

- Lake County has only two incorporated cities: **Clearlake** (largest, county's largest city) and **Lakeport** (county seat). Other named places (Kelseyville, Lower Lake, Middletown, Clearlake Oaks, Nice, Lucerne, Upper Lake, Cobb) are unincorporated communities.
- GOTCHA: neither Clearlake nor Lakeport runs its own CUPA — both are covered by the single Lake County Environmental Health CUPA.
- GOTCHA: neither incorporated city runs a municipal fire department. The City of Clearlake is served by the **Lake County Fire Protection District** and the City of Lakeport is served by the **Lakeport Fire District** — both are independent fire protection districts, not city departments.
- Workflow note: fire district fees/sign-off are collected by the local fire district before a building permit application is processed (per City of Lakeport guidance).
