> Local EHS authority reference for the **City of Lakeport** (Lake County seat). Orientation for the research subagent: who administers the local programs and where their adopted codes live. Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- **Not its own CUPA** — served by the Lake County Division of Environmental Health (countywide CUPA for HMBP, hazardous waste generator, UST, AST/APSA).
- https://www.lakecountyca.gov/374/Hazardous-Materials-Management-CUPA

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- **Lakeport Fire District** (also referred to as Lakeport Fire Protection District) — independent fire protection district serving the City of Lakeport; not a city-run department. Handles fire/sprinkler plan submittals and Fire Code enforcement. — https://www.lakeportfire.com/
- CAL FIRE Sonoma-Lake-Napa Unit (LNU) provides wildland protection on SRA lands — https://firesafelake.org/lake-county-fire-departments/

## Building authority

- City of Lakeport Community Development Department, Building Division — issues building permits and inspections within the city (online permit portal via OpenGov). — https://www.cityoflakeport.com/community_development/building/

## Local code amendments

- City of Lakeport adopts the California Building Standards Code (Title 24) by reference in its Municipal Code. Confirm the adopted edition on the live page; UNVERIFIED whether the city has adopted the 2025 edition (statewide effective 2026-01-01) versus the prior 2022 edition. — https://www.cityoflakeport.com/community_development/building/

## Notes

- GOTCHA: Lakeport does NOT run its own CUPA or its own municipal fire department — CUPA is the county Environmental Health, fire is the independent Lakeport Fire District.
- Workflow note: per City of Lakeport guidance, the local fire district collects fire fees / signs off before the building permit application is processed.
