> Local EHS authority reference for the **City of Clearlake** (Lake County). Orientation for the research subagent: who administers the local programs and where their adopted codes live. Verify the live page before quoting; the 2025 California Building Standards Code (Title 24) replaced the 2022 edition statewide on 2026-01-01, so confirm the adopted edition.

## CUPA (Unified Program)

- **Not its own CUPA** — served by the Lake County Division of Environmental Health (countywide CUPA for HMBP, hazardous waste generator, UST, AST/APSA).
- https://www.lakecountyca.gov/374/Hazardous-Materials-Management-CUPA

## Fire authority (AHJ)

- **Lake County Fire Protection District** (LCFPD) — independent fire protection district serving the City of Clearlake and surrounding southeast-shore areas; not a city-run department. Fire Code enforcement / fire plan review is handled by the district. — https://www.lakecountyfire.com/
- CAL FIRE Sonoma-Lake-Napa Unit (LNU) provides wildland protection on SRA lands — https://firesafelake.org/lake-county-fire-departments/

## Building authority

- City of Clearlake Building Department (within the Community Development Department) — issues building permits, performs plan check and inspections within the city via an online permit portal. — https://clearlake.ca.us/394/Building-Department

## Local code amendments

- City of Clearlake adopts construction codes in the Clearlake Municipal Code, Title/Chapter IX (Building and Housing). Confirm the adopted Title 24 edition on the live page; UNVERIFIED whether the city has adopted the 2025 edition (statewide effective 2026-01-01) versus the prior 2022 edition. — https://clearlake.municipal.codes/CMC/ChIX

## Notes

- GOTCHA: Clearlake does NOT run its own CUPA or its own municipal fire department — CUPA is the county Environmental Health, fire is the Lake County Fire Protection District.
- Clearlake DOES run its own Building Department (separate from the county Building Safety division).
