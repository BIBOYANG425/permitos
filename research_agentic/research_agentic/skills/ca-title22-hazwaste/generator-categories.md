## California Generator Categories (as of 2022 Generator Improvement Rule)

| Category | Monthly generation | Accumulated on-site | Accumulation time |
|---|---|---|---|
| **VSQG** (Very Small Quantity Generator) | ≤ 100 kg/month (~22 gal) | ≤ 1,000 kg at any time | No time limit |
| **SQG** (Small Quantity Generator) | 100–1,000 kg/month | ≤ 6,000 kg | 270 days |
| **LQG** (Large Quantity Generator) | ≥ 1,000 kg/month | No on-site limit | 90 days |

**Acute hazardous wastes** (P-list): 1 kg/month trigger is LQG threshold regardless of total quantity.

## California-Specific Differences from Federal
- CA does NOT recognize the federal VSQG "conditional exclusion" — California VSQGs must manage waste under full CA standards (22 CCR 66262.10)
- CA has additional categories: **Exempt Small Quantity Generator** for certain wastes
- CA manifest required for all off-site shipments regardless of quantity

## Determination Process
1. Count ALL hazardous waste generated in a calendar month
2. Include characteristic wastes (ignitable, corrosive, reactive, toxic) + listed wastes (F, K, P, U lists)
3. Count acutely hazardous wastes separately — 1 kg/month makes you an LQG for that month
4. Category = highest applicable category for that month
