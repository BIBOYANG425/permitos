description: Local zoning clearance / conditional use permit (CUP) — a use must be allowed in its zoning district; uses that are conditionally permitted require a CUP, and uses that do not conform require a variance, from the local planning authority. Governed by the local zoning ordinance and general plan, not a single statewide code.
when_to_use: Use for the `land_use` coverage family — the project establishes or changes a use/occupancy and zoning-district allowance must be confirmed.
---

> ORIENTATION ONLY. The path below orients the search; it is not evidence. Fetch and quote the controlling jurisdiction's zoning ordinance (use tables for the specific district) and CUP/variance procedures verbatim. Zoning is local — resolve the controlling city/county first. Never cite a SKILL.md.

## Triggers

- Establishing a new use, intensifying an existing use, or changing occupancy at the site.
- Adding equipment/operations that may not be a permitted use in the current zoning district.

## Determination path

| Question | Expected path | Primary source to fetch and quote |
| --- | --- | --- |
| What zoning district is the parcel in? | from the local zoning map | Local zoning map / GIS |
| Is the use permitted-by-right, conditional, or prohibited? | district use table | Local zoning ordinance use tables |
| If conditional | Conditional Use Permit (CUP) | Local zoning ordinance CUP procedures |
| If non-conforming | Variance | Local zoning ordinance variance procedures |

## Exemptions

- A use permitted by right in the district needs only a zoning clearance, not a CUP — confirm against the district use table.
- Legal non-conforming uses may continue under limits — verify the local non-conforming use provisions.
- The controlling authority is the city if incorporated, otherwise the county — resolve jurisdiction before citing a code.
