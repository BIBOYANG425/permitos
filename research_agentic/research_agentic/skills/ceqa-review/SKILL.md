## Triggers

- The project requires a discretionary approval (use permit, variance, rezoning, development agreement) from a public agency.
- The activity is a "project" under CEQA that may cause a direct or reasonably foreseeable indirect physical change in the environment.

## Thresholds / determination path

| Question | Expected path | Primary source to fetch and quote |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Is it a "project"? | activity with potential physical environmental change | PRC 21065 / Guidelines 15378 |
| Is it ministerial? | ministerial permits are exempt | Guidelines 15268 |
| Categorical exemption? | Classes 1–33 (e.g. Class 1 existing facilities, Class 3 new small structures) | Guidelines 15301–15333 |
| Otherwise | Initial Study -> Neg Dec / MND / EIR | Guidelines 15063+ |

## Exemptions

- Statutory exemptions (PRC) and categorical exemptions (Guidelines Classes 1–33) can remove a project from CEQA — but exceptions (15300.2: location, cumulative impact, unusual circumstances) can defeat a categorical exemption; verify against the fetched Guidelines.
- Purely ministerial permits are not subject to CEQA — confirm the approval is ministerial vs discretionary.
