## Most Relevant Categorical Exemptions for Industrial/EHS Projects

### Class 1 — Existing Facilities (14 CCR 15301)
**Exempt:** Minor alterations to existing private structures/facilities involving negligible expansion of use.
- Permitting interior/exterior alterations to existing industrial buildings (up to 10,000 sq ft in urbanized areas)
- Operation, repair, maintenance of existing equipment
- Adding equipment to existing facility with no change in land use

### Class 2 — Replacement/Reconstruction (15302)
**Exempt:** Replacement/reconstruction of existing structures at same location with same purpose.
- Replacing in-kind equipment that fails
- Rebuilding a tank, tank farm, or treatment system at same location

### Class 3 — New Construction of Small Structures (15303)
**Exempt:** Construction of limited numbers of new, small facilities.
- Up to 2,500 sq ft of commercial/industrial structure in urbanized area
- Water main, sewage, electrical installation (normal municipal operation)

### Class 11 — Accessory Structures (15311)
**Exempt:** Construction of minor structures accessory to existing commercial/industrial facilities.
- Small parking areas, fencing, minor utility extensions

### Class 43 — Extended Deadlines (15343) [rare]

## Exceptions to Categorical Exemptions (15300.2)
A categorical exemption DOES NOT APPLY if:
- **Location**: project is in sensitive area (rare plant/animal habitat, scenic highway, floodplain, hazardous waste site)
- **Cumulative impact**: project is part of series that may have significant cumulative effect
- **Significant effect**: unusual circumstances mean project may have significant effect
- **Scenic highway**: project may damage scenic resources near an eligible scenic highway
- **Hazardous waste site**: project is on a Cortese List site

## How to Apply an Exemption
1. Identify applicable exemption class
2. Check all five exceptions — document that none apply
3. File a Notice of Exemption (NOE) with the county clerk and OPR within 5 days of approval
4. 35-day statute of limitations begins on NOE filing date
