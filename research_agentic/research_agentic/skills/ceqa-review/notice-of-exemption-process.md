# Notice of Exemption (NOE) — Filing Process and 35-Day Statute of Limitations

**Sources:**
- PRC § 21152 — https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displaySection.xhtml?lawCode=PRC&sectionNum=21152.
- PRC § 21167(d) — https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displaySection.xhtml?lawCode=PRC&sectionNum=21167.
- Fetched: 2026-06-03

---

## What Is a Notice of Exemption

A Notice of Exemption (NOE) is a filing made by a public agency (or an authorized private applicant) after a discretionary project is approved and the agency determines the project is exempt from CEQA. Filing the NOE starts the running of a short 35-day statute of limitations on legal challenges to the exemption determination.

Without an NOE, challengers have **180 days** from the agency's decision or project commencement to file suit.

---

## Who May File

Per PRC § 21152:
- The **lead agency** (public agency approving the project).
- **Persons specified in PRC § 21065(b) or (c)** — applicants carrying out the project — when the local agency has issued a certificate of determination confirming the exemption.

---

## Where to File

Notices must be filed with **both**:

1. **County Clerk** of each county where the project is located.
2. **State Clearinghouse** in the Office of Planning and Research (now housed at LCI — https://lci.ca.gov/).

Electronic filing is required where available.

---

## Certificate of Determination (Private Applicants)

When filed by a **private party**, the NOE must include a **certificate of determination attached to it, issued by the local agency**, confirming the project's exemption status. The agency's certification is the predicate for the private party's filing.

---

## Posting and Record Requirements

| Step | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| County Clerk posting | Must post within **24 hours** of receipt |
| Duration of posting | **30 days** |
| Return of notice | County Clerk returns notice to agency with documentation of posting after the 30-day period |
| State Clearinghouse posting | Posts within **24 hours** of receipt; maintained for **30 days** |
| Agency record retention | Local agencies must retain NOEs for **not less than 12 months** |

---

## The 35-Day Statute of Limitations

PRC § 21167(d) provides the key limitation period:

> "An action or proceeding alleging that a public agency has improperly determined that a project is not subject to this division pursuant to subdivision (b) of Section 21080 shall be commenced within 35 days from the date of the filing by the public agency" of the notice of exemption or notice of determination.

**Key rules:**
- The 35-day clock starts from the **date the agency files** the NOE — not from the date of posting or the date of the underlying approval.
- If **no NOE is filed**, the statute of limitations is **180 days** from the agency's decision to approve the project or the commencement of the project, whichever is earlier.
- The 180-day period is significantly more permissive for potential challengers — filing an NOE is therefore strategically important for agencies and applicants seeking legal finality.
- The statute was amended effective January 1, 2023.

---

## Types of CEQA Exemptions That Use the NOE

An NOE is typically filed when a project qualifies under:

| Exemption Type | Description |
|----------------|-------------|
| **Statutory Exemptions** | Specific activities exempted directly by CEQA statute (e.g., emergency repairs, certain infill housing) |
| **Categorical Exemptions** | Classes of projects the Resources Agency has determined generally do not have significant effects (e.g., Class 1 – existing facilities; Class 3 – new small structures; Class 15 – minor land divisions) |
| **Common-Sense Exemption** | Projects where "it can be seen with certainty that there is no possibility that the activity may have a significant effect" (CEQA Guidelines § 15061(b)(3)) |

**Note:** Ministerial projects do not require an NOE because CEQA does not apply to them at all — they are not "approved" under CEQA's framework. The NOE applies to discretionary projects that are nonetheless exempt.

---

## Practical Checklist for NOE Filing

- [ ] Confirm the project is discretionary and qualifies for a recognized exemption (statutory or categorical).
- [ ] Check for "unusual circumstances" that might override a categorical exemption.
- [ ] Prepare the NOE form identifying: project description, location, applicant, agency, and the specific exemption relied upon.
- [ ] If filed by private applicant: obtain the agency's certificate of determination first.
- [ ] File simultaneously with County Clerk (all relevant counties) and State Clearinghouse.
- [ ] Confirm electronic filing capability with State Clearinghouse (CEQA Submit portal at lci.ca.gov).
- [ ] Document the filing date — this is the trigger for the 35-day limitation period.
- [ ] Retain a copy of the posted notice for at least 12 months.
