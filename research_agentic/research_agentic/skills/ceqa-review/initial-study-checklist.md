## What is an Initial Study?
A preliminary analysis to determine if a project **may have a significant effect on the environment**. Required before preparing an EIR or Negative Declaration.

## Appendix G Checklist Topics (14 CCR Appendix G)
The standard checklist evaluates impacts in 18 topic areas:
1. Aesthetics
2. Agriculture and Forestry Resources
3. Air Quality ← **key for industrial/EHS projects**
4. Biological Resources
5. Cultural Resources
6. Energy
7. Geology and Soils
8. Greenhouse Gas Emissions ← **key for industrial projects**
9. Hazards and Hazardous Materials ← **key for EHS projects**
10. Hydrology and Water Quality ← **key for stormwater/wastewater**
11. Land Use and Planning
12. Mineral Resources
13. Noise
14. Population and Housing
15. Public Services
16. Recreation
17. Transportation
18. Tribal Cultural Resources
19. Utilities and Service Systems
20. Wildfire
21. Mandatory Findings of Significance

## Significance Thresholds for EHS-Relevant Topics
**Hazards (Item 9):** Significant if project would:
- Emit hazardous materials creating substantial risk to public or environment
- Be located on a Cortese List (hazardous waste) site
- Impair emergency response
- Expose people to wildland fire risk

**Air Quality (Item 3):** Consult local air district thresholds of significance (e.g., SCAQMD Rule 2202 thresholds: 55 lbs/day ROG or NOx).

## Outcomes
- **No significant effects**: Negative Declaration (ND)
- **Significant effects mitigated to less-than-significant**: Mitigated Negative Declaration (MND)
- **Significant unavoidable effects**: Environmental Impact Report (EIR)
