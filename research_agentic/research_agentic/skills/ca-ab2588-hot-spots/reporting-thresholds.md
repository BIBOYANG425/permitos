## Program Overview

AB 2588 (1987) created the Air Toxics "Hot Spots" Information and Assessment Act. It requires stationary sources to quantify and report toxic air contaminant (TAC) emissions, undergo risk assessment, and notify nearby residents when significant risk is found. The program is administered by local air pollution control districts and air quality management districts (collectively "air districts") under CARB statewide guidelines.

## Who Must Report — The Two-Track Inclusion System

### Track 1: Mandatory Inclusion (District SIC Code Lists)

Each air district publishes a list of Standard Industrial Classification (SIC) codes whose facilities are automatically included. Typical mandatorily-included SIC categories include:

| Sector | Common SIC Codes |
|---|---|
| Chemical manufacturing | 2800–2899 |
| Petroleum refining | 2911, 2990–2999 |
| Metal plating and finishing | 3460–3499, 3470–3479 |
| Dry cleaning | 7216 |
| Auto painting/body shops | 7532–7539 |
| Semiconductor fabrication | 3674 |
| Medical/dental (ethylene oxide sterilization) | 8011, 8021 |
| Batteries/lead reclaiming | 3691, 5093 |
| Commercial printing | 2750–2759 |
| Agricultural chemical dealers | 5191 |

### Track 2: Discretionary Inclusion

Districts may require any facility to submit an inventory if it may emit TACs in quantities of potential public health significance, regardless of SIC code. Key factors:
- Facility uses, stores, or processes substances on the CARB AB 2588 Substance List
- Proximity to sensitive receptors (schools, hospitals, residences)
- Prior complaint history or screening flags

### Track 3: Voluntary Participation

Operators may voluntarily submit an inventory; districts may treat voluntary submissions as program participants with the same ongoing obligations.

## The AB 2588 Substance List

CARB maintains a list of approximately 244 substances subject to AB 2588 reporting (17 CCR §93300 Appendix A). Core categories:

- Carcinogens: benzene, formaldehyde, 1,3-butadiene, ethylene oxide, chromium VI, arsenic, cadmium, beryllium, nickel, perchloroethylene, vinyl chloride, dioxins/furans
- Non-cancer HAPs: hydrogen fluoride, hydrogen chloride, ammonia, hydrogen sulfide, methanol, toluene, xylene
- Diesel particulate matter (DPM) — added as a TAC in 1998 per CARB Diesel Risk Reduction Plan
- Lead compounds

## Reporting Frequency

| Facility Priority | Reporting Frequency |
|---|---|
| High priority | Quadrennial (every 4 years) per HSC §44344(a) |
| Low priority | Every 4 years unless district grants longer interval |
| Below significance threshold (post-assessment) | District may grant exemption or extended cycle |

## Submittal Requirements

What to submit in the Emission Inventory Questionnaire (EIQ) or district equivalent:
- Facility identification (name, address, SIC code, contact person)
- Process and equipment list with annual operating hours
- TAC mass emissions (pounds/year or tons/year per substance)
- Emission calculation method (AP-42 emission factors, mass balance, CEMS, or source test)
- Supporting calculations and data

Where to submit: Local air district only — no statewide CARB portal.
- SCAQMD: Emissions Reporting System (ERS) at https://ers.aqmd.gov
- BAAQMD: Online emissions inventory at https://www.baaqmd.gov/permits/emissions-inventory
- SJVAPCD: https://www.valleyair.org/busind/comply/ab2588/hotspots.htm

## Decision Tree: Is This Facility Required to Report?

Step 1: Is the facility SIC code on the district mandatory inclusion list?
  YES → Required to submit emission inventory
  NO  → Continue

Step 2: Does the facility use/store/emit any AB 2588 Substance List chemical?
  NO  → AB 2588 likely does not apply; document basis
  YES → Continue

Step 3: Has the district issued a notice of inclusion?
  YES → Comply per district schedule
  NO  → Discretionary inclusion possible; check district records

Step 4: Has any prior AB 2588 inventory been filed?
  YES → Ongoing quadrennial obligations apply
  NO  → Monitor for district-initiated inclusion
