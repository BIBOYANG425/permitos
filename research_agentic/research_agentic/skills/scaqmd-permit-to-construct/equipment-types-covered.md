# SCAQMD Permit to Construct — Equipment Types Covered

## Scope (Rule 201)

A PC is required for **any equipment or agricultural permit unit** whose:
- Use **may cause the issuance of air contaminants**, OR
- Use **may eliminate, reduce, or control the issuance of air contaminants** (i.e., control equipment also requires a permit).

This includes new construction, erection, installation, alteration, or replacement.

## Permit Types by Pathway

The following permit types are available under Regulation II:

| Permit Type | Description |
|---|---|
| **Permit to Construct (PC)** | Required before installation/alteration of new or relocated equipment that may emit or control air contaminants. |
| **Permit to Operate (PO)** | Required to operate installed equipment that has demonstrated compliance. One-step combined PC+PO may be issued for off-the-shelf ("certified") equipment. |
| **Certified Equipment Permit** | Issued to a manufacturer/distributor for a specific model series; certifies BACT compliance. Permits for individual units are then streamlined (Registration Permits). Valid one year, renewable. |
| **Registration Permit** | Permit to Construct and Permit to Operate issued for equipment previously certified under a Certified Equipment Permit. |
| **Title V Permit** | Federal operating permit for **major stationary sources** under the Clean Air Act. Required in addition to, not instead of, state/district permits. |
| **RECLAIM** | Cap-and-trade program for large NOx and SOx emitters. New facilities cannot currently enter RECLAIM. |
| **Facility Permit** | Consolidated permit covering all equipment at a facility. |
| **Plans** | Required under certain rules (e.g., Ambient Air Monitoring Plans, Soil Excavation Plans) to demonstrate operational compliance. |

## Equipment Categories Subject to Full PC (Table IB of Rule 301 — representative examples)

Rule 301, Table IB maps specific equipment/process types to fee schedules. Examples of equipment that require a standard PC:

- **Combustion equipment** — boilers, process heaters, engines, turbines above Rule 219 thresholds (e.g., boilers >2,000,000 Btu/hr; engines >50 bhp that are not otherwise exempt).
- **Abrasive blasting equipment** — open-air or large-cabinet blast operations not meeting Rule 219 exemption criteria.
- **Coating and adhesive equipment** — spray booths, coaters, laminators exceeding Rule 219 VOC thresholds (e.g., >3 lb/day uncontrolled VOC, or >1 gal/day organic solvent coatings).
- **Storage tanks** — organic liquid storage exceeding Rule 219 thresholds (e.g., >160,400 L / 40,000 gal liquid fuel storage; floating-roof tanks; heated VOC storage).
- **Control equipment** — afterburners, catalytic oxidizers, baghouses, scrubbers, activated carbon adsorbers, electrostatic precipitators, and all other air pollution control devices.
- **Printing operations** — graphic arts equipment exceeding Rule 219 VOC or usage limits (>3 lb/day uncontrolled VOC; >2 gal/day non-UV inks).
- **Plastic/composite/rubber processing** — presses, molds, extruders where VOC-containing materials are present and Rule 219 exemptions are not met.
- **Pharmaceutical/food processing** — equipment using >1 gal/day VOC solvents or above food-oven BTU thresholds.
- **Natural gas/crude oil production equipment** — equipment beyond the Rule 219 Primary Recovery exemptions.
- **Petroleum refinery processes** — alkylation, amine treating, catalytic cracking, sulfur recovery units, flares (covered under Rule 301, Table IB, Schedule E–H).
- **Agricultural permit units** — operations exceeding Rule 219(d)(17) thresholds (see Table 1: 5.0 TPY VOC, NOx; 35 TPY SOx, PM10; 25 TPY CO in the South Coast Air Basin).

## Equipment Explicitly **Not** Covered (Rule 219 exemptions — see exempt-equipment-list.md)

Equipment categories listed in Rule 219(d)(1)–(18) do not require a PC, subject to conditions. Key categories:
- Motor vehicles and marine vessels (California Vehicle Code definition).
- Internal combustion engines ≤50 bhp.
- Boilers/heaters ≤2,000,000 Btu/hr on natural gas/LPG/methanol.
- Micro-turbines ≤3,500,000 Btu/hr (cumulative facility output <2 MW, CARB-certified).
- Residential equipment (≤4-family dwelling, used by owner/occupant).
- Laboratory bench-scale and non-production research equipment.
- Equipment covered by PERP (Statewide Portable Equipment Registration Program).

## Replacement Equipment

Replacement of **identical equipment** at a non-major source (where a PO was previously granted) does not require a new PC (Rule 219(d)(3)(C)). At federal major sources, replacement must qualify as routine maintenance under U.S. EPA guidance (Rule 219(d)(3)(D)).

## Control Equipment Note

All air pollution control equipment—regardless of type—requires its own permit alongside the emission-generating equipment it serves, unless the control device is venting equipment that is itself exempt under Rule 219.
