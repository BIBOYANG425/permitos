## Triggers

- New, altered, or replaced equipment that may emit air contaminants — the Rule 201 Permit to Construct trigger.
- Coating booths, ovens, dryers, combustion units, or process equipment that vents to atmosphere.
- Solvent or coating use that emits volatile organic compounds (VOC) — check whether VOC content/usage drives additional review or a higher permit tier (use the analyze_voc_content tool on the SDS).

## Thresholds

| What to look for | Expected orientation | Primary source to fetch and quote |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Permit to Construct required | Building/erecting/installing/altering/replacing equipment that may emit air contaminants without written authorization is prohibited | SCAQMD Rule 201 |
| VOC / solvent review | Confirm whether solvent VOC content or throughput triggers extra review under the Permit to Construct framework | SCAQMD Rule 201 |

## Exemptions

- A separate exemption path may exist (SCAQMD Rule 219) or a registration path (Rule 222) — those are evaluated by their own hypotheses; do not assume an exemption here. A failed exemption returns the equipment to the Rule 201 Permit to Construct requirement.
