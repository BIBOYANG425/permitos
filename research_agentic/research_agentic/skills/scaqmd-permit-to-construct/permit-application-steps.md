# SCAQMD Permit to Construct — Application Steps

## Statutory Trigger (Rule 201)

> "A person shall not build, erect, install, alter or replace any equipment or agricultural permit unit, the use of which may cause the issuance of air contaminants or the use of which may eliminate, reduce or control the issuance of air contaminants without first obtaining written authorization for such construction from the Executive Officer."

A Permit to Construct (PC) is required **before** installation of new or relocated equipment, or alteration of existing equipment.

## Pre-Application Decision Tree

Before filing, applicants must work through:

1. **Check Rule 219 exemptions** — Is the equipment type listed as not requiring a written permit? If yes, no PC needed (but Rule 222 filing may still apply).
2. **Check Rule 222 filing** — Does the equipment fall under the registration/filing program? If yes, file under Rule 222 instead.
3. **Check Certified Equipment Permit** — Is there an existing District certification for the equipment model? If yes, a simplified process applies.
4. **Standard Permit Application** — If none of the above apply, file a full PC application.

## Application Review Process (Five-Step)

1. **Screening** — Initial determination of completeness and permit pathway.
2. **Completeness Verification** — District confirms all required information has been submitted.
3. **Comprehensive Compliance Evaluation** — Engineering review against all applicable SCAQMD, state, and federal rules, including BACT (Best Available Control Technology) analysis.
4. **Approval or Denial** — Executive Officer issues written decision.
5. **Permit Issuance** — Written Permit to Construct issued; remains valid until PO is granted or denied, or application is canceled.

## Application Portal and Contact

- **Phone:** (909) 396-3385

## After Construction — Permit to Operate

A Permit to Construct converts to a **Temporary Permit to Operate** (Rule 202) once the applicant completes final construction and commences operation. The date specified as the estimated completion date on the PC application is deemed the commencement of operation unless the applicant notifies the District otherwise in writing. The PO process then follows.

## Failure to Obtain Permit — Consequences (Rule 301)

Operating equipment without a required PC or PO results in:
- Permit processing fee increased to **150% of the standard Table FEE RATE-A fee** (waived to standard rate for first-time small businesses with no prior NOV).
- Retroactive **annual operating fees for three full years** (or actual years of operation if less than three).
- Potential Notice of Violation.

## Small Business Discount

Applicants qualifying as a **Small Business** under Rule 102 pay **50% of the Table FEE RATE-A permit processing fee**.

Small Business definition for failing-to-obtain-permit higher fee purposes:
- Non-manufacturer: ≤25 employees AND ≤$1,000,000 gross annual receipts.
- Manufacturer: ≤50 employees AND ≤$5,000,000 gross annual receipts.
- Not-for-profit training center.

## Application Cancellation

If a PC application is canceled before District evaluation has been initiated, fees are refunded less an application cancellation fee of **$310.14**.
