# SCAQMD Permit to Construct — Permit Triggers

> PRIMARY SOURCES TO FETCH AND QUOTE: https://www.aqmd.gov/docs/default-source/rule-book/reg-ii/rule-201.pdf  and https://www.aqmd.gov/docs/default-source/rule-book/reg-ii/rule-202.pdf. Never cite this reference file as evidence; it orients the agent toward what to look for and quote in the fetched rule text.

## Core Statutory Trigger (Rule 201(b))

Rule 201(b) prohibits any person from **building, erecting, installing, altering, or replacing** any article, machine, equipment, or other contrivance that may cause the issuance of air contaminants **without first obtaining written authority to construct from the Executive Officer**. The permit must be obtained *before* the physical act begins — there is no retroactive path.

The operative phrase is "**may** cause the issuance of air contaminants": the trigger is the *potential* to emit, not confirmed actual emissions. This is an extremely broad trigger that covers:

- New construction of any equipment with combustion, solvent use, spray application, coating, printing, or other emission-generating processes
- Alteration that changes the design, capacity, process rate, fuel type, or control device configuration
- Replacement (even in-kind) unless a specific exemption or registration path applies

## Jurisdiction Boundary

Rule 201 applies to all stationary sources operating in the **South Coast Air Quality Management District (SCAQMD)**, which covers:
- Los Angeles County (excluding the Antelope Valley portion)
- Orange County (all)
- Riverside County (western portion)
- San Bernardino County (western portion, excluding desert areas administered by Mojave Desert AQMD)

If the project site is not in SCAQMD jurisdiction, Rule 201 does not apply — confirm the AQMD district via the SCAQMD jurisdiction map before any permit analysis.

## Trigger Decision Tree

```
Is the project in SCAQMD jurisdiction?
  NO → Rule 201 does not apply
  YES ↓

Does the project build, erect, install, alter, or replace any article,
machine, equipment, or other contrivance?
  NO → No permit triggered on this basis
  YES ↓

May that equipment cause the issuance of air contaminants?
  NO (e.g., purely mechanical, no process/combustion/solvent) → No trigger
  YES ↓

Does a Rule 219 exemption apply (see exemptions.md)?
  YES (all conditions met) → No permit required; maintain records
  NO ↓

Does a Rule 222 registration path apply?
  YES → File registration instead of full permit application
  NO ↓

→ RULE 201 PERMIT TO CONSTRUCT REQUIRED before construction begins
```

## "Alteration" Trigger — Key Nuance

Altering existing equipment is as significant as installing new equipment. Alterations that typically trigger Rule 201 review include:

- Increasing production rate, throughput, or operating hours beyond permit conditions
- Changing the fuel type (e.g., natural gas to diesel, or adding a backup fuel)
- Replacing a burner, combustor, or control device with a different model
- Adding a new process line that uses an existing stack or shared emission point
- Changing chemical formulations in a coating or solvent-use operation that increase VOC content or emission rate

**Replacement in kind** (identical model, same capacity, same fuel, same controls) *may* qualify for an exemption under Rule 219(e)(1) for emission units that are identical replacements — [VERIFY IN PRIMARY SOURCE that Rule 219(e)(1) covers in-kind replacement and all stated conditions].

## Relationship to Rule 202 (Permit to Operate)

Rule 201 (Permit to Construct) and Rule 202 (Permit to Operate) are sequential requirements:

1. **Rule 201**: Written authorization before any construction, installation, or modification begins.
2. **Rule 202**: Written authorization to operate the equipment after construction is complete and the Executive Officer has performed (or waived) an inspection.

A facility may not operate permitted equipment under Rule 202 unless it first obtained a valid Rule 201 Permit to Construct. Conversely, a Permit to Construct alone does not authorize operation — Rule 202 must be obtained before the equipment is put into service.

## Timing and "Written Authority" Requirement

- The Rule 201 application must be submitted and the permit issued *before* any physical construction activity begins.
- Emergency or after-the-fact permits are not available; unpermitted construction is a violation subject to Notice of Violation (NOV) and penalties.
- SCAQMD may authorize construction to proceed by written letter before the formal permit is issued if conditions warrant — [VERIFY IN PRIMARY SOURCE the exact mechanism].

## Common Misapplications to Flag

| Situation | Correct Analysis |
|---|---|
| Equipment is "already installed" when reviewed | No retroactive permit; existing violation — do not assume compliant |
| Equipment is "identical to existing" | May be exempt under Rule 219 but must verify each condition; do not assume |
| Emissions are "de minimis" | No de minimis exception under Rule 201; threshold is "may emit" |
| Equipment is "portable" | Portability does not exempt from Rule 201; check Rule 219 portable-equipment categories |
| Vendor says "no permit needed" | Vendor opinion is not a permit; SCAQMD written authorization is required |
