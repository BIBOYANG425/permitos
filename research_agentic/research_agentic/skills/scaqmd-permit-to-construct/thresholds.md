# SCAQMD Permit to Construct — Significance Thresholds & Emission Limits

> PRIMARY SOURCES: SCAQMD Regulation XIII (Rules 1300–1342) — fetch from https://www.aqmd.gov/home/rules-compliance/rules/scaqmd-rule-book/regulation-xiii and SCAQMD NSR guidance. Also fetch SCAQMD Rule 202 for operating conditions. Verify all numbers in primary sources before citing.

## Why Thresholds Matter

A Permit to Construct under Rule 201 is triggered by ANY potential to emit air contaminants (no minimum threshold). However, the *level of review* escalates significantly based on numeric emission thresholds:

| Review Level | Trigger | Key Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| Basic Rule 201 | Any potential to emit | BACT + operational conditions |
| NSR "Significant" | Net emissions increase ≥ NSR significance threshold | BACT + offset credits |
| Major Modification | NSR + facility is major source | Federally enforceable permit; possible PSD/Title V interaction |
| CEQA Significance | Emissions ≥ SCAQMD CEQA thresholds | CEQA air quality analysis required |

## BACT (Best Available Control Technology) Applicability

BACT is required for:
- All permitted emission units (Rule 201 requires BACT as a condition of every Permit to Construct)
- The standard is technology-based, not just emission-rate-based

SCAQMD maintains a BACT/BARCT clearinghouse — the applicable BACT determination depends on equipment type, fuel, and size. Key BACT-required parameters typically include:

- Combustion equipment: NOx emission rate (e.g., lb/MMBtu), CO rate, operating hours/year
- Coating operations: VOC content limit (g/L or lb/gal), transfer efficiency, control device efficiency
- Process equipment: compound-specific emission factor with control

## CEQA Air Quality Significance Thresholds

SCAQMD publishes separate CEQA significance thresholds that govern environmental review (California Environmental Quality Act) separate from permit-level NSR thresholds. These are *screening* thresholds for CEQA impact assessment:

| Pollutant | CEQA Construction Threshold (lbs/day) | CEQA Operations Threshold (lbs/day) |
|---|---|---|
| VOC / ROG | 75 lbs/day | 55 lbs/day |
| NOx | 100 lbs/day | 55 lbs/day |
| CO | 550 lbs/day | 550 lbs/day |
| PM10 | 150 lbs/day (exhaust) | 150 lbs/day |
| PM2.5 | 55 lbs/day | 55 lbs/day |
| SOx | 150 lbs/day | 150 lbs/day |
| Lead | 3 lbs/day | 3 lbs/day |

## Portable Equipment Thresholds

Portable equipment registered under the California Portable Equipment Registration Program (PERP) and meeting emission limits may operate district-wide without an individual district permit. Key PERP conditions include:
- Unit-specific emission limits (NOx, PM, CO) set by CARB
- Annual registration with CARB
- Site notification requirement (typically 72 hours notice to host district)

## SCAQMD Health Risk Thresholds (Toxic Air Contaminants)

For equipment emitting Toxic Air Contaminants (TACs), SCAQMD applies a health-risk-based screening:
- Cancer risk > 10 in one million: typically triggers detailed Health Risk Assessment (HRA)
- Non-cancer Hazard Index > 1.0: triggers HRA
- SCAQMD Rule 1401 sets allowable risk levels for new equipment with TAC emissions
- AB 2588 Hot Spots program has separate reporting thresholds

These are in addition to (not instead of) NSR mass-emission thresholds.
