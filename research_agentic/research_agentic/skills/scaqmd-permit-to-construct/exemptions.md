# SCAQMD Permit to Construct — Exemptions

> PRIMARY SOURCE: https://www.aqmd.gov/docs/default-source/rule-book/reg-ii/rule-219.pdf (Rule 219 full text) and https://www.aqmd.gov/docs/default-source/rule-book/reg-ii/rule-222.pdf (Rule 222). Fetch and quote the rule text verbatim; never cite this file as evidence.

## Three Exemption Pathways

### Path 1: Rule 219 Categorical Exemptions (No Permit Required)

Rule 219 lists specific equipment categories that are **exempt from the permit requirements of Rules 201 and 202** when ALL conditions for that category are satisfied. The exemption is self-executing (no application required), but the facility must be able to demonstrate compliance with every listed condition at any time.

**Key structural rule**: Each exemption category in Rule 219 is accompanied by conditions (size limits, emission rates, operating restrictions, recordkeeping). If ANY single condition is not met, the exemption does not apply and a Rule 201 Permit to Construct is required.

#### Exemption Verification Checklist

For each Rule 219 exemption claimed, the agent must verify:
1. The equipment type precisely matches the category description (not just "similar")
2. Every numeric condition (size, rate, hours, emission factor) is met
3. Any recordkeeping or operational restriction required by the exemption is (or will be) maintained
4. The exemption is not conditioned on prior SCAQMD notification or approval (some categories require notification even if no permit is required)

### Path 2: Rule 222 Registration (Permit Substitute, Not Exemption)

Rule 222 authorizes certain equipment categories to **register with SCAQMD instead of obtaining a full Permit to Construct**. This is a *different compliance path*, not an exemption from regulation. The equipment is still regulated; registration substitutes for the full permit process.

Key differences from Rule 219 exemption:
- Registration must be actively filed with SCAQMD; it is not self-executing
- Annual registration fees apply
- SCAQMD issues a registration certificate that functions as the operating authorization
- Equipment must continue to meet Rule 222 conditions or revert to the full permit process

Typical Rule 222 categories include small combustion equipment, portable equipment, and specified process equipment below certain throughput thresholds — [VERIFY current category list in primary source].

### Path 3: Rule 201(d) Administrative Exclusions

Rule 201(d) contains specific exclusions from the permit requirement that are distinct from the Rule 219 categorical exemptions. These may include:
- Equipment used solely for research and development (R&D) under specific conditions
- Equipment installed for emission testing or monitoring only
- Certain construction equipment that is truly temporary and not a stationary source

## Exemption Decision Flow

```
Does Rule 219 list this equipment type?
  NO → No Rule 219 exemption; go to Rule 222 check
  YES ↓

Do ALL conditions for that Rule 219 category apply?
  NO (even one condition fails) → No exemption; full Rule 201 permit required
  YES ↓

→ Rule 219 exemption applies; NO permit required
   (Document compliance; maintain required records)

---

Does Rule 222 list this equipment category?
  NO → No registration path; full Rule 201 permit required
  YES ↓

File Rule 222 registration; pay fee; obtain certificate
→ Registration issued; equipment may be constructed/operated under registration
```

## Critical Caution: Exemption vs. Compliance

An exemption from the *permit requirement* does not exempt equipment from:
- Applicable emission standards and operational rules (e.g., Rule 1110.2 for IC engines, Rule 1146 for boilers)
- BACT/BARCT requirements that apply independently
- New Source Review (NSR) significance thresholds if the project is a major modification
- Title V permit conditions if the facility already holds a Title V permit

Always analyze Rule 201 exemptions separately from substantive compliance with emission standards.

## Interaction with NSR / Major Source Analysis

Even if an individual piece of equipment qualifies for a Rule 219 exemption, the cumulative project may trigger **New Source Review (NSR)** or **Prevention of Significant Deterioration (PSD)** if the facility-wide net emissions increase exceeds significance thresholds (see thresholds.md). NSR analysis operates at the project/facility level, not the unit level. A Rule 219 exemption at the equipment level does not resolve an NSR concern at the facility level.
