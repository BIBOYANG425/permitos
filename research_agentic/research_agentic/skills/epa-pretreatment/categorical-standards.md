## What Are Categorical Standards?
EPA sets **technology-based** effluent limits for specific industrial categories that discharge to POTWs. These are floor limits — the local POTW (IUP permit) may set stricter local limits.

## Key Industrial Categories (40 CFR subchapter N)
| Industry | CFR Part | Key Parameters |
|---|---|---|
| Metal finishing | 433 | Cadmium, chromium, copper, lead, nickel, zinc, cyanide, TSS, oil/grease |
| Electroplating | 413, 433 | Chromium, cyanide, nickel, zinc, copper |
| Printed circuit boards | 469 | Copper, lead, tin, cyanide |
| Semiconductor manufacturing | 469 | Copper, fluoride, total toxic organics |
| Pharmaceutical mfg | 439 | BOD, COD, TSS, AOX |
| Pulp/paper | 430 | BOD, TSS, AOX, dioxins |
| Food processing | 405-418 | BOD, TSS, oil/grease |
| Textile mills | 410 | BOD, TSS, color, sulfide |
| Auto/parts cleaning | 438 | Oil/grease, metals |

## Applicability Determination
1. Identify your industrial process and products
2. Look up applicable 40 CFR part for that category
3. Determine if "significant industrial user" (SIU): subject to categorical standards OR discharge ≥25,000 gpd OR designated by POTW
4. Get an Industrial User Permit (IUP) from your POTW/CUPA

## Combined Wastestream Formula
If process wastewater is combined with other streams before treatment, use the combined wastestream formula to compute applicable limits (40 CFR 403.6(e)).
