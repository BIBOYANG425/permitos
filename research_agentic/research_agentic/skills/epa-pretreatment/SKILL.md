## Triggers

- An industrial user discharges process wastewater to a publicly owned treatment works (POTW).
- The facility falls in an industrial category covered by a national categorical pretreatment standard.
- The discharge meets a Significant Industrial User (SIU) definition by flow or by being subject to categorical standards.
- Discharge could cause pass-through or interference at the POTW, or violate prohibited-discharge standards.

## Thresholds

| What to look for | Expected orientation | Primary source to fetch and quote |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Categorical industrial user | Facility in a regulated industrial category subject to national categorical standards | EPA National Pretreatment Program |
| Significant Industrial User (flow) | Process wastewater flow at a regulatory significance level (commonly framed around 25,000 gallons/day of process wastewater) | EPA National Pretreatment Program |
| Prohibited discharges | General and specific prohibitions apply to all industrial users regardless of size | EPA National Pretreatment Program |

## Exemptions

- Discharges that are exclusively domestic/sanitary (no process wastewater) generally fall outside categorical pretreatment standards — confirm the discharge characterization against the fetched source.
- Local control authorities (the POTW) may impose more stringent local limits than the federal program — the national program orients the analysis; the local limits may be stricter.
- Whether a user is "significant" turns on definitions in the fetched regulation; do not assume non-significance without checking flow and categorical status.
