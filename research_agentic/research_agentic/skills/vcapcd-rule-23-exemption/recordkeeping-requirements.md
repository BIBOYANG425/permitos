# VCAPCD Rule 23 — Recordkeeping for Exempt Operations

## The Universal Substantiation Requirement (Rule 23 Preamble, Verbatim)

> "The owner or operator shall provide, as required by the District, calculations, usage records, emissions records and/or operational data as necessary to substantiate any exemptions that apply to the subject facility."

Rule 23 does not specify a minimum retention period in its own text. However, the obligation to provide records "as required by the District" means records must be kept current and retrievable at any time. There is no stated deadline after which old records can be discarded.

**What VCAPCD may request at any time:**
- Calculations showing how the facility determined it qualifies for the exemption
- Usage records (material purchase logs, consumption logs, delivery receipts)
- Emissions records (ROC emission estimates, inventory reports)
- Operational data (process throughput, operating hours, equipment parameters)

## Section F Threshold-Based Exemptions — Specific Records Required

The Section F exemptions hinge on rolling 12-month emission totals. For every threshold-based exemption, the facility must be able to reconstruct those rolling totals on demand.

### F(13) — Graphic Arts Operations (Including Ink-Jet)

**Exemption threshold:** Less than 200 pounds each of ROC, methylene chloride, 1,1,1-trichloroethane, and perchloroethylene lost to the atmosphere during every rolling period of 12 consecutive calendar months.

**Records that substantiate the F(13) exemption:**

1. **Monthly material usage logs** for each product used in the graphic arts operation:
   - Inks (by product name and SKU)
   - Ink additives
   - Fountain solutions
   - Substrate surface preparation products
   - Application equipment cleaning solvents
   - Coatings used in the operation
   - Adhesives for binding or gluing printed substrates

2. **Safety Data Sheets (SDS) / Technical Data Sheets (TDS)** for each product, showing:
   - ROC content (grams ROC per liter of product as applied, or % by weight)
   - Whether any methylene chloride, perchloroethylene, or 1,1,1-trichloroethane is present

3. **ROC emission calculations** for each trailing 12-month window, showing:
   - Volume of each material used in the period (gallons or liters)
   - ROC content of each material (lbs ROC/gal or g ROC/L)
   - Calculated ROC pounds emitted per material per period
   - Total ROC, methylene chloride, 1,1,1-trichloroethane, and perchloroethylene emitted in aggregate

4. **Segregation documentation** showing which emissions were NOT counted (cold cleaners and vapor degreasers are explicitly excluded from the F(13) calculation by the rule text)

5. **Delivery receipts or purchase invoices** corroborating the usage quantities in the logs

### F(11)(b) — Coating Operations

Same 200-lb/12-month threshold per pollutant. Records must capture emissions from coatings, thinning, substrate surface preparation, and application equipment cleaning. Must document exclusion of cold cleaner/vapor degreaser emissions and emissions already counted in a graphic arts operation.

### F(12) — Adhesive/Sealant Operations

Same 200-lb/12-month threshold per pollutant. Records must capture emissions from adhesives, sealants, adhesive/sealant primers, thinning, substrate surface preparation, and application equipment cleaning. Must document exclusion of cold cleaner/vapor degreaser emissions and emissions already counted in a graphic arts operation.

### F(10)(d) — Solvent Cleaning Operations

Same 200-lb/12-month threshold per pollutant. Records must cover all solvent cleaning activities at the stationary source (cold cleaners, vapor degreasers, wipe cleaning, dip cleaning, flush cleaning) except those covered by the F(10)(a), (b), or (c) sub-exemptions.

### F(10)(c) — Cold Cleaners

Threshold: less than 1,000 pounds ROC per rolling 12-month period, with additional equipment-specification conditions (initial boiling point > 150°C, liquid surface area < 1 sq meter). Records: solvent purchase/usage logs, boiling point documentation for each solvent, equipment specs confirming liquid surface area.

### F(14) — Polyester Resin Operations

Threshold: less than 240 gallons per rolling 12-month period. Records: purchase and usage logs in gallons for all polyester resin materials, cross-linking agents, catalysts, gel coats, inhibitors, accelerators, promoters, and any other ROC-containing materials. Inert filler and cleaning material are excluded.

### F(15) — Other Organic Solvent Operations

Same 200-lb/12-month threshold per pollutant. Records: material usage logs and ROC content documentation.

## Capacity-Based Exemptions

For exemptions that turn on equipment capacity or power ratings (e.g., F.1–F.4 tank volumes, D.6 engine power, B.7 blast cabinet volume, B.8 batch mixer volume), maintain:
- Equipment specification sheets or manufacturer documentation confirming the relevant capacity or power rating
- Any modifications that could change the rated capacity

---

## Emergency Engine Records (D.7)

For emergency internal combustion engines:
- Maintenance activity log, documenting hours run — must confirm maintenance does not exceed 50 hours per calendar year per engine
- Incident records documenting each actual emergency use

## Practical Guidance

**Rolling 12-month tracking:** Exemptions use a rolling 12-month window, not a calendar year. A facility should check its trailing 12-month total at the end of each month. If the total ever reaches or exceeds 200 lbs (for any of the four pollutants), the operation is no longer exempt for that period.

**When exemption is lost:** If emissions reach or exceed the threshold, the facility must immediately stop operating without a permit and apply for a PTO. A Temporary PTO may be issued during processing per Rule 10, Section B.4.

**Record format:** Rule 23 does not mandate a specific format. A simple spreadsheet with columns for month, product name, volume used, ROC content (per SDS), and calculated ROC pounds is sufficient.

**Suggested retention period:** Although Rule 23 does not state a minimum retention period, retaining records for at least 5 years is prudent, consistent with the standard practice for air district compliance records and consistent with Rule 74.19 recordkeeping terms that apply if a permit is eventually required.
