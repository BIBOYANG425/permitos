# VCAPCD Rule 23, Section F.13 — Graphic Arts Operations Exemption

## The Threshold Rule (exact text)

> "Graphic arts operations (including but not limited to packaging gravure, publication gravure, flexographic printing, screen printing, letterpress, lithographic printing, or ink jet printing) where **less than 200 pounds each** of ROC, methylene chloride, 1,1,1-trichloroethane, and perchloroethylene are lost to the atmosphere during every rolling period of 12 consecutive calendar months."

## Key Numbers

| Pollutant | Threshold for Exemption |
|---|---|
| ROC (Reactive Organic Compounds / VOC) | **< 200 lbs per rolling 12-month period** |
| Methylene chloride | < 200 lbs per rolling 12-month period |
| 1,1,1-trichloroethane | < 200 lbs per rolling 12-month period |
| Perchloroethylene | < 200 lbs per rolling 12-month period |

All four thresholds must be met independently. Exceeding **any one** triggers the permit requirement.

## What "Graphic Arts Operations" Emissions Include (Section F.13)

The 200-lb calculation must include ALL of the following emission sources associated with the graphic arts operation:
- Inks
- Ink additives
- Fountain solutions
- Substrate surface preparation
- Application equipment cleaning
- Coatings
- Adhesives for binding or gluing printed substrates

**Explicitly excluded from the calculation:**
- Emissions from cold cleaners
- Emissions from vapor degreasers

## Printing Technologies Covered (non-exhaustive list per F.13)

- Packaging gravure
- Publication gravure
- Flexographic printing
- Screen printing
- Letterpress
- Lithographic printing
- Ink jet printing

## Rolling 12-Month Period

The threshold is not measured on a calendar-year basis. It applies to **every rolling period of 12 consecutive calendar months**. A facility must ensure that at no point does any trailing 12-month window exceed 200 lbs of any covered pollutant.

## Relationship to Other Exemptions

Section F.9 provides a separate exemption for **office and administrative use** of ink, marking pens, ink pads, glues, adhesives, and cleaning solvents. However, this exemption **does not apply** to production activities at facilities involved in graphic arts operations. Such production activities must be evaluated under F.13.

## If the Threshold Is Crossed

Once ROC emissions from graphic arts operations reach or exceed 200 lbs in any rolling 12-month period:
1. A Permit to Operate under Rule 10 is required.
2. The operation becomes subject to VCAPCD Rule 74.19 (Graphic Arts), which imposes specific ROC content limits on inks, coatings, adhesives, fountain solutions, and cleaning solvents.
3. Full recordkeeping obligations under Rule 74.19 Section D apply.

## Substantiation Requirement

Per the Rule 23 preamble: "The owner or operator shall provide, as required by the District, calculations, usage records, emissions records and/or operational data as necessary to substantiate any exemptions that apply to the subject facility."

Facilities claiming this exemption should maintain monthly usage records for all inks, solvents, and related materials to demonstrate ongoing compliance with the < 200 lb threshold.
