# CA Construction General Permit — Rain Event Action Plan (REAP)

**Permit:** Order WQ 2022-0057-DWQ (supersedes Order 2009-0009-DWQ)
**NPDES No.:** CAS000002
**Adopted:** September 8, 2022; **Effective:** September 1, 2023
**Expires:** August 31, 2028
**Source:** Order WQ 2022-0057-DWQ and CGP FAQ page

---

## REAP Has Been Eliminated in the 2022 CGP

The **Rain Event Action Plan (REAP) requirement was removed** in the 2022 Construction General Permit. The REAP was a pre-storm planning document required under the prior 2009 CGP (Order 2009-0009-DWQ).

The State Water Board eliminated the REAP based on analysis showing it was not providing valuable data or meaningfully improving water quality outcomes, while imposing significant administrative burden on dischargers.

---

## What Replaced REAP: Pre-Storm Inspection Requirements

Under the 2022 CGP, pre-storm preparation is now captured through the **inspection and visual observation framework** (Section V, Attachment D) rather than a standalone REAP document. The QSP is responsible for:

> "Within 72 hours prior to a forecasted Qualifying Precipitation Event to inspect areas of concern to verify the status of any deficiencies, BMPs, or other identified issues at the site. If extended forecast precipitation data (greater than 72 hours) is available from the National Weather Service, the pre-precipitation event inspection may be done up to **120 hours** in advance." (Section V.D.2.b)

### Forecasted Precipitation Event / Qualifying Precipitation Event Definitions

**Forecasted Precipitation Event (Attachment B):** Any weather pattern forecasted to have a 50 percent or greater chance of producing 0.5 inches of precipitation in a 24-hour period in the project area. Precipitation events end when there are two sequential 24-hour periods with less than 0.25 inches forecast.

**Qualifying Precipitation Event (Attachment B):** Any weather pattern that is forecast to have a **50 percent or greater Probability of Precipitation (PoP)** and a **Quantitative Precipitation Forecast (QPF) of 0.5 inches or more** within a 24-hour period. The event begins with the 24-hour period when 0.5 inches has been forecast and continues on subsequent 24-hour periods when 0.25 inches or more is forecast.

## SWPPP-Based Storm Preparation (Section IV.O and Attachment D)

Although the REAP form is gone, the **SWPPP** must include a **Construction Site Monitoring Program** that describes methods and procedures for monitoring discharges in accordance with the applicable Attachment D or E, including:

- Visual inspection locations, inspection procedures, and follow-up tracking procedures.
- Applicable sampling locations, collection, and handling procedures.
- Chain of Custody form used when handling and shipping samples.
- Identification of analytical methods and related method detection limits for each parameter.

BMPs must be implemented **immediately** before any forecasted precipitation event. The SWPPP must identify site-specific BMPs and the personnel responsible for implementing them.

---

## Numeric Action Levels for Qualifying Precipitation Events (Section IV.C.3, Table 1)

For Risk Level 2 and 3 sites, sampling is required during Qualifying Precipitation Events. The following Numeric Action Levels apply:

| Parameter | Numeric Action Level | Units |
|-----------|---------------------|-------|
| pH | Lower = 6.5, Upper = 8.5 | pH units |
| Turbidity | 250 | NTU |

A NAL exceedance occurs when the field reading, obtained per each discharge location per day of each qualifying precipitation event, exceeds these thresholds.

---

## Inactive Project Provisions (Section III.G)

For inactive projects (construction suspended 30 or more days), a QSP or trained delegate must visually inspect the site at least **once every calendar month** and **prior to any weather pattern forecasted to have a 50 percent or greater chance of 0.5 inches or more in a 24-hour period** (Section III.G.2.b).

---

## Reference to Prior 2009 CGP REAP Requirements

The former REAP under Order 2009-0009-DWQ required dischargers to prepare a written plan prior to each rain event addressing:
- BMP implementation status
- Persons responsible for implementing BMPs
- Identification of areas of concern
- Proposed corrective actions

This requirement was abolished effective September 1, 2023, when the 2022 CGP took effect. Any hypotheses or AI research referencing REAP requirements for active California construction projects should note this change and apply the 2022 CGP inspection framework instead.

## Annual Reporting (Section IV.P)

> "The discharger shall electronically certify and submit an Annual Report through SMARTS **by September 1st** for the previous reporting period from July 1st through June 30th if a WDID number is active for at least 90 days within the reporting period." (Section IV.P.1)

The Annual Report shall consist of:
- a. Summary of all stormwater sampling and monitoring reports and supporting documents (e.g., laboratory reports);
- b. Summary of all corrective actions taken during the compliance year;
- c. Identification and explanation of compliance activities or corrective actions that were not implemented;
- d. Summary of all General Permit violations;
- e. Names of individuals who performed site inspections, sampling, visual inspections, and/or measurements;
- f. Date, place, time of site inspections, sampling, visual inspections, and/or measurements, including the amount of precipitation measured in inches; and
- g. All visual inspection and sample collection exception records and reports.
