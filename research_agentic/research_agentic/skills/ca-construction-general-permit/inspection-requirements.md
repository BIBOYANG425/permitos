# CA Construction General Permit — Inspection Requirements by Risk Level

**Permit:** Order WQ 2022-0057-DWQ
**NPDES No.:** CAS000002
**Adopted:** September 8, 2022; **Effective:** September 1, 2023
**Expires:** August 31, 2028
**Source:** Order WQ 2022-0057-DWQ, Section V (Site Roles and Personnel), Attachment D (Traditional Construction Risk Level Requirements)

---

## Overview of Inspector Roles

The 2022 CGP establishes two levels of qualified stormwater personnel:

**QSD — Qualified SWPPP Developer**
A qualified stormwater professional authorized by the discharger to develop and revise SWPPPs. The QSD may also perform the work of a QSP.

**QSP — Qualified SWPPP Practitioner**
A qualified stormwater professional authorized by the discharger to conduct non-stormwater and stormwater visual observations, sampling, and implementation of all elements of the SWPPP.

---

## Risk Level Classification

Risk Levels are determined using the State Water Board's standard risk determination (via SMARTS GIS map method) or a site-specific method, combining:
- **Sediment Risk** (R factor × K factor × LS factor)
- **Receiving Water Risk** (based on proximity to impaired or sensitive water bodies)

Sites that discharge to an unlisted receiving water tributary to a sediment-sensitive waterbody, within the HUC 10 watershed scale, are considered high receiving water risk.

The risk determination produces three levels:
- **Risk Level 1 (RL1):** Low sediment risk AND low receiving water risk
- **Risk Level 2 (RL2):** Medium sediment risk OR medium/high receiving water risk
- **Risk Level 3 (RL3):** High sediment risk AND high receiving water risk

## QSD Inspection Requirements (Section V.C.4)

The discharger shall ensure that a QSD performs the following on-site visual inspections (these are minimum requirements and may be increased during high-risk construction, excessive site problems, or other conditions):

- **a.** Within 30 days of construction activities commencing on a site;
- **b.** Within 30 days of a discharger replacing the QSD;
- **c.** **Twice annually** — once August through October and once January through March;
- **d.** Within **14 calendar days** after a numeric action level exceedance; and
- **e.** Within the time period requested in writing from Water Board staff.

These QSD inspections apply to **all Risk Levels**.

---

## QSP Inspection Requirements (Section V.D.2)

The discharger shall ensure that a QSP performs the following on-site visual inspections (minimum requirements — may be increased by the discharger or QSD):

### Inspections Applicable to ALL Risk Levels

- **a. Once every calendar month** (ongoing throughout the project)

- **b. Pre-storm inspection:** Within **72 hours prior** to a forecasted Qualifying Precipitation Event to inspect areas of concern and verify BMP status. If extended forecast data (>72 hours) is available from the National Weather Service, the pre-storm inspection may be done **up to 120 hours in advance**.

- **c. Post-NAL-exceedance:** Within **14 days after a numeric action level exceedance**, the QSP shall visually inspect the drainage area of exceedance and document any areas of concern.

- **d. Pre-termination/change:** Prior to the submittal of a General Permit Notice of Termination or Change of Information (for acreage changes) of all or part of a site.

## Risk Level-Specific Sampling Requirements (Section IV.C.3, Attachment D)

The 2022 CGP uses Numeric Action Levels (NALs) for pH and turbidity, with sampling triggered by Qualifying Precipitation Events (QPE):

### Risk Level 1 — No Sampling Required
Risk Level 1 dischargers are not required to collect and analyze stormwater samples for pH or turbidity. They must comply with narrative effluent limitations (BAT/BCT) and maintain SWPPP and BMP compliance.

> "Risk Level 1 dischargers shall comply with the requirements included in Attachment D, D.1, and D.2 of this General Permit." (Section IV.F)

### Risk Level 2 — Sampling Required for QPEs
Risk Level 2 dischargers must collect and analyze stormwater samples for pH and turbidity during each Qualifying Precipitation Event per discharge location per day. Numeric Action Levels apply:
- **pH:** 6.5 to 8.5 pH units
- **Turbidity:** 250 NTU

> "Risk Level 2 dischargers shall comply with the requirements included in Attachment D, D.1, and D.2 of this General Permit." (Section IV.G)

### Risk Level 3 — Sampling Required + ATS Option
Risk Level 3 dischargers face the same sampling and NAL requirements as RL2, plus they are the primary candidates for active treatment systems (Attachment F) given the high-risk site conditions. All RL3 numeric action levels:
- **pH:** 6.5 to 8.5 pH units
- **Turbidity:** 250 NTU

> "Risk Level 3 dischargers shall comply with the requirements included in Attachment D, D.1, and D.2 of this General Permit." (Section IV.H)

If an ATS is deployed, ATS-specific numeric **effluent limitations** (not mere NALs) apply:
- **pH:** 6.0 to 9.0
- **Turbidity:** 10 NTU (daily flow-weighted average) / 20 NTU (single sample)

## Numeric Action Levels Table (Section IV.C.3, Table 1)

Applies to Risk Level 2 and 3 sites (and Type 2 and 3 Linear Underground/Overhead projects):

| Parameter | Test Method | Discharger Type | Detection Limit | Units | Numeric Action Level |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|-----------------|-------|---------------------|
| pH | Field test with calibrated portable instrument using EPA-approved procedures | Risk Level 2 and 3; Risk Type 2 and 3 | 0.2 | pH Units | Lower = **6.5**, Upper = **8.5** |
| Turbidity | EPA 0180.1 and/or field test with calibrated portable instrument | Risk Level 2 and 3; Risk Type 2 and 3 | 1 | NTU | **250** |

> "A numeric action level exceedance for pH occurs when the reading, obtained per each discharge location per day of each qualifying precipitation event, is below the lower value or above the upper value." (Section IV.C.3.c)

> "An exceedance of the turbidity numeric action level occurs when the field reading, obtained per each discharge location per day of each qualifying precipitation event, is over 250 NTU." (Section IV.C.3.d)

**Important distinction (from Attachment B):** A NAL exceedance is **not a violation** of the General Permit by itself. Failing to report and failing to modify BMP implementation to prevent further NAL exceedances **is** a violation.

## QSP Verification Responsibilities (Section V.D.3)

The QSP shall verify the following on each inspection:

- a. All BMPs required in the SWPPP are implemented, correctly installed, inspected, and maintained;
- b. Track-out of construction-related material at site entrances and exits is controlled;
- c. The SMARTS-generated WDID number notification form is in a site location viewable by the public;
- d. Sampling protocols are correctly performed as described in the SWPPP;
- e. Contact information for the discharger, Legally Responsible Person, QSD(s), and QSP(s) is current in SMARTS; and
- f. Photo documentation of problem areas is included in the SWPPP and available upon request.

---

## Inactive Project Inspection Requirements (Section III.G)

When all construction activities (including passive treatment, ATS, and/or active equipment) are suspended for **30 or more days**, the discharger may submit a Change of Information through SMARTS. Upon Regional Water Board approval:

- A QSD shall visit the inactive project within **14 days** of RWB approval to verify the SWPPP is being implemented.
- A QSP or trained delegate shall visually inspect the inactive project at least **once every calendar month** and **prior to any weather pattern forecasted to have a 50 percent or greater chance of 0.5 inches or more in a 24-hour period**.

## Notice of Termination Inspection

Before a discharger can terminate General Permit coverage, a **QSP-prepared final Notice of Termination inspection** is required as part of the Notice of Termination package submitted through SMARTS (Section III.H.2.b). The final site map and photos demonstrating final stabilization must accompany this inspection report.

---

## Key Personnel Certification

Both QSD and QSP must hold **active and current certificates** verified by the discharger. Engineering and/or geology work performed for the site must be conducted by a **California licensed professional** (Section V.A.3).
