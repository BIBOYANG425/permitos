# CA Construction General Permit — Active Treatment System (ATS) Requirements

**Permit:** Order WQ 2022-0057-DWQ
**NPDES No.:** CAS000002
**Adopted:** September 8, 2022; **Effective:** September 1, 2023
**Source:** Order WQ 2022-0057-DWQ, Section IV.I and Attachment F (Active Treatment System Requirements)

---

## Overview

An ATS is not limited to Risk Level 3 sites under the 2022 CGP — any discharger may choose to implement one. However, ATS use is most common at Risk Level 3 sites given their high sediment risk and/or sensitive receiving waters. ATS use is **optional** but triggers mandatory numeric effluent limitations (not mere action levels).

> "Active treatments systems are treatment technologies that employ chemical coagulation, chemical flocculation, or electrocoagulation to reduce turbidity caused by fine suspended sediment, and/or to control pH levels. An active treatment system relies on enclosed computerized systems with pumps, filters, and real-time controls." (Attachment F, Section A.2; also Attachment B Glossary)

> "Dischargers implementing an active treatment system shall comply with all of the requirements in Attachment F of this General Permit." (Section IV.I)

> "All dischargers implementing active treatment systems are subject to the numeric effluent limitations required in Attachment F." (Section IV.C.2.a)

---

## A. General ATS Requirements (Attachment F, Section A)

### A.1 — Full Compliance Required
The discharger choosing to implement an ATS shall comply with **all** the requirements in Attachment F.

### A.3 — Lead Person / Operator Qualification
> "The discharger shall assign a lead person (or project manager) who has either a minimum of **five years construction stormwater experience** or who is a licensed contractor specifically holding a **California Class A Contractors license** to oversee the operation of the active treatment system." (Attachment F, A.3)

### A.4 — ATS Bypass Conditions
An ATS may be bypassed if:
- a. All discharges from the watershed area the ATS was designed to treat are in compliance with numeric action levels, numeric effluent limitations, and receiving water limitations through applicable monitoring; and
- b. If dewatering is occurring as part of the bypass, the discharger complies with dewatering requirements in Attachment J.

### A.5 — Local Pre-Treatment
If ATS effluent is locally authorized for discharge to a sanitary sewer, the discharger must comply with local sanitation agency pre-treatment requirements and include proof of authorization in the ATS Plan.

## B. Design Criteria and Specifications (Attachment F, Section B)

### B.1 — Design Storm
> "The active treatment system shall be designed to capture and treat (within a 72-hour period) a volume equivalent to the runoff from a **10-year, 24-hour storm event** using a watershed coefficient of 1.0." (Attachment F, B.1.a)

The watershed runoff coefficient used to size the ATS shall be **1.0** (B.1.b).

Runoff in excess of the design storm shall not be routed through the ATS; it must meet the bypass requirements in Section A.4.

### B.2 — Treatment Chemicals (Coagulation/Flocculation)
The discharger shall select treatment chemical(s) capable of complying with the technology-based numeric effluent limitations by using one of the following methods:
- **Jar testing:** At minimum, six site-specific jar tests (per treatment chemical, with one test serving as a control) per site, using water samples representative of typical site conditions, in accordance with ASTM D2035.
- **Single field jar tests** may be conducted during the project if conditions change (e.g., different soil types).

### B.3 — Filtration
> "The active treatment system shall include a filtration step between the coagulant treatment train and the effluent discharge. This is commonly provided by sand, bag, or cartridge filters." (Attachment F, B.3.a)

All backwash water shall be removed, disposed of, or recirculated to the beginning of the treatment process.

### B.4 — Instrumentation Requirements
The ATS shall be equipped with instrumentation that automatically measures and records effluent water quality data and flow rate. Minimum data recorded:
- i. Influent turbidity
- ii. Effluent turbidity
- iii. Influent pH
- iv. Effluent pH
- v. Residual chemical
- vi. Effluent flow rate
- vii. Effluent flow volume
- viii. Total volume
- ix. Freeboard on storage

> "Systems shall be equipped with a data recording system, such as data loggers or webserver-based systems, which records each measurement on a frequency no longer than once every **15 minutes**." (Attachment F, B.4.c)

Cumulative flow volume shall be recorded **daily**. The data recording system shall have capacity to record a minimum of **7 days** of continuous data.

Instrumentation systems shall provide **auto shutoff or recirculation** when effluent measurements exceed turbidity or pH numeric action levels or numeric effluent limitations (B.4.e).

The system shall default to **recirculation mode or safe shutdown** upon system upset, power failure, or other catastrophic event (B.4.f).

## C. Maintenance Requirements (Attachment F, Section C)

### C.1 — Operation and Maintenance
The discharger shall operate and maintain the ATS in accordance with the site-specific **Operation and Maintenance Manual** (Section C.1.a).

### C.2 — Residuals Management
- Sediment shall be removed from storage or treatment cells as necessary to maintain water storage, sediment storage, and settling zone capacity.
- Handling and disposal of all solids shall be in accordance with all local, state, and federal laws and regulations.

## D. Monitoring Requirements (Attachment F, Section D)

### D.1 — Daily Visual Observations
The discharger shall visually observe the ATS for proper performance **each day of operation**, including:
- All instrumentation; and
- Filter loading to confirm the final filter stage is functioning properly.

This may be done by:
- i. A designated responsible person who is on-site **at all times during treatment operations**; or
- ii. An operator continuously monitoring the ATS **off-site**, provided the system can conduct a safe shutdown autonomously when the operator connection is lost and the system is discharging above specified levels (requires redundant monitoring and self-diagnostic capability).

### D.2 — Continuous Water Quality Monitoring
- Flow at **15-minute intervals** for total volume treated and discharged (D.2.a)
- Influent and effluent **pH** at 15-minute intervals (D.2.b)
- Influent and effluent **turbidity** (expressed in NTU) at 15-minute intervals (D.2.c)
- Type and amount of chemicals used for pH adjustment (D.2.d)
- **Dose rate** of chemicals used (expressed in mg/L) 15 minutes after startup and every **8 hours** of operation (D.2.e)
- Effluent residual chemical and/or additive levels, with **monthly laboratory duplicates** for residual coagulant analysis (D.2.f)

### D.3 — Residual Chemical and Toxicity Monitoring
Residual chemical test methods must:
- Have a method detection limit of **10 percent or less** of the Maximum Allowable Threshold Concentration (MATC) for the most sensitive species (D.3.a)
- Produce a result within **1 hour** of sampling (D.3.b)
- Be validated by a **State Water Board ELAP-accredited laboratory** (D.3.c)

**MATC Definition (Attachment B):** The MATC is the geometric mean of the No Observed Effect Concentration (NOEC) and Lowest Observed Effect Concentration (LOEC) Acute and Chronic toxicity results for the most sensitive species to the specific coagulant used.

Dischargers who cannot meet these residual chemical test requirements must operate the ATS in **batch treatment mode** and conduct acute toxicity testing on each batch prior to discharge (D.3.d–f).

**Toxicity Test Species:** Fathead minnow (*Pimephales promelas*) or Rainbow trout (*Oncorhynchus mykiss*), using 96-hour acute test protocols per U.S. EPA-821-R-02-012.

## D.4 — Numeric Effluent Limitations for ATS Discharges

All ATS effluent must comply with the following technology-based numeric effluent limitations:

### Table 1 — ATS Numeric Effluent Limitations (Attachment F, Section D.4, Table 1)

| Parameter | Test Method | Method Detection Limit | Reporting Units | Numeric Effluent Limitation |
|-----------|-------------|----------------------|-----------------|----------------------------|
| pH | Field test with calibrated portable instrument | 0.2 | pH Units | Lower = **6.0**, Upper = **9.0** |
| Turbidity | EPA 0180.1 and/or field test with calibrated portable instrument | 1 | NTU | **10 NTU** (daily flow-weighted average of all samples) **AND 20 NTU** (any single sample) |
| Residual Chemicals | U.S. EPA-approved test method for specific pollutant parameter | Less than 10% of MATC | Dependent on test method | **Less than 10% of MATC** for most sensitive species to the chemical used |

> "pH of all active treatment system discharges shall be within the range of **6.0 to 9.0**." (Attachment F, D.4.b.i)

> "Turbidity of all active treatment system discharges shall be less than **10 NTU** for daily flow-weighted average of all samples and **20 NTU** for any single sample." (Attachment F, D.4.b.ii)

> "Residual Chemical shall be **< 10 percent of Maximum Allowable Threshold Concentration** for the most sensitive species to the chemical used." (Attachment F, D.4.b.iii)

### Compliance Precipitation Event Exception (D.4.d)
Discharges from ATS shall comply with numeric effluent limitations unless the precipitation event causing the discharge is determined, after the fact, to be equal to or larger than the **compliance precipitation event** (10-year, 24-hour storm).

### Response to Exceedances (D.4.c)
If an analytical result exceeds the pH or turbidity numeric effluent limitation, or exceeds the residual chemical numeric effluent limitation, **the discharger shall cease discharge from the ATS** and comply with the reporting requirements in Section E.3 of Attachment F.

## E. Reporting Requirements (Attachment F, Section E)

### E.1 — Active Treatment System Plan
The discharger shall prepare an **Active Treatment System Plan** (ATS Plan) that shall be:

> "Electronically certified and submitted through SMARTS as an attachment to the SWPPP, at least **14 days prior** to the planned operation of the active treatment system, and a copy shall be available on-site during active treatment system operation." (Attachment F, E.1.b)

The ATS Plan must include at minimum:
- i. Contact information of all personnel responsible for monitoring and maintaining the ATS;
- ii. A map depicting the watershed area treated by the ATS (in acres);
- iii. Specifications of any storage ponds, tanks, or other stormwater containment;
- iv. The treatment capacity of the ATS (defined as the number of hours needed to treat the captured volume from a given design storm, using a watershed runoff coefficient of 1.0);
- v. An ATS Operation and Maintenance Manual covering installation, operation, and maintenance procedures; pump/generator/control system information; and a failure plan specifying failure indicators, shutdown procedures, and Regional Water Board contact;
- vi. A monitoring and sampling plan including QA/QC documentation specifying calibration methods, method detection limits, and procedures for monthly laboratory duplicates;
- vii. An ATS Health and Safety Plan; and
- viii. An ATS Spill Prevention and Response Plan.

### E.2 — Visual Observations
All completed inspection checklists and related documentation shall be kept with the SWPPP on-site or electronically.

### E.3 — Water Quality Monitoring Reporting
- At minimum every **30 days**, the Legally Responsible Person shall electronically certify and submit ATS field data through SMARTS (E.3.a).
- Any violations of water quality standards shall be immediately reported to the discharger, who shall notify the Regional Water Board (E.3.c).
- Dischargers in violation of ATS numeric effluent limitations shall electronically certify and submit results through SMARTS **within 24 hours** of obtaining results (E.3.d).
- A **Numeric Effluent Limitation Violation Report** shall be certified and submitted in SMARTS **within 14 days** after the numeric effluent limitation exceedance has been identified (E.3.e).

The Numeric Effluent Limitation Violation Report shall include:
- i. The analytical methods, reporting units, and method detection limits for each parameter;
- ii. Date, place, time of sampling, visual observation, and/or measurements; and
- iii. An assessment of what caused the ATS to exceed the numeric effluent limitation, and proposed corrective actions.

## ATS vs. Passive Treatment: Key Distinction

| Feature | Active Treatment System (ATS) | Passive Treatment (Attachment G) |
|---------|------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| Technology | Chemical coagulation/flocculation or electrocoagulation; computerized with pumps, filters, real-time controls | Natural or synthetic chemicals; no computerized enclosed systems |
| Triggers | Numeric Effluent Limitations (violations = mandatory minimum penalties) | Narrative requirements; NAL-based |
| Effluent limit: Turbidity | 10 NTU (daily avg) / 20 NTU (single) | No specific NTU limit (passive standard) |
| Effluent limit: pH | 6.0–9.0 | Narrative |
| SMARTS submission | ATS Plan 14 days before operation; data every 30 days | Passive Treatment Plan 14 days before operation |
