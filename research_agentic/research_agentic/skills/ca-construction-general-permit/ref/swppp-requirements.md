## SWPPP Mandate

A Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan (SWPPP) is **required for all CGP-covered projects**, regardless of Risk Level. The SWPPP must be:
- **Prepared by a Qualified SWPPP Developer (QSD)** — a licensed Civil Engineer, Geologist, or person holding a CPESC, CESSWI, or equivalent certification approved by the State Water Board [VERIFY CURRENT APPROVED CERTIFICATION LIST IN PRIMARY SOURCE]
- **Completed before any ground disturbance**
- **Maintained on-site** (physical or electronic copy) throughout construction
- **Uploaded to SMARTS** and available for public inspection upon request

## Required SWPPP Content — All Projects

Per CGP Attachment B, every SWPPP must include:

### 1. Project Description
- Project name, address, legal description
- Total DSA (acres) and grading schedule
- Receiving water identification (name, 303(d) status, beneficial uses)
- Risk Level determination with supporting calculations
- Names and contact info for QSD, QSP, Legally Responsible Person (LRP)

### 2. Site Map
- Drainage patterns and flow directions
- Location of all storm drains, inlets, and outlets
- Areas of soil disturbance
- Location of construction materials staging areas, waste management areas
- Proximity to receiving waters and any buffers required

### 3. BMP Selection and Design

**Erosion Control BMPs** (prevent soil detachment):
- Scheduling (sequence construction to minimize exposed area and duration)
- Mulching, hydraulic mulch, bonded fiber matrix (BFM)
- Hydroseeding and re-vegetation
- Geotextile blankets/mats on slopes
- Wind erosion control (watering, tarps) — required in high-wind areas

**Sediment Control BMPs** (capture detached soil before it leaves site):
- Silt fence (maximum slope length and spacing requirements apply)
- Fiber rolls / wattles
- Sediment basins — required when contributing drainage area exceeds 5 acres draining to a single exit point [VERIFY IN PRIMARY SOURCE]
- Inlet protection (rock bags, filters around all storm drain inlets)
- Check dams in swales
- Street sweeping for tracking control

**Non-Stormwater Management BMPs**:
- Concrete washout areas (lined, bermed, minimum 50 feet from storm drains [VERIFY])
- Vehicle and equipment fueling areas
- Spill prevention for materials storage
- Portable restroom management

**Good Housekeeping BMPs**:
- Material delivery and storage procedures
- Solid waste management
- Hazardous waste management

### 4. Inspection Schedule and Documentation
- Required inspections (see timing below)
- Forms and checklists (CGP Appendix 2 or equivalent)
- Corrective action procedures

### 5. REAP Procedures (RL2 and RL3 only)
Reference to Rain Event Action Plan triggers and steps (see rain-event-action-plan.md)

## QSD vs. QSP Roles

| Role | Qualification | Responsibility |
|------|--------------|----------------|
| Qualified SWPPP Developer (QSD) | PE, PG, or approved certification | Prepare and certify SWPPP; determine Risk Level; design BMPs |
| Qualified SWPPP Practitioner (QSP) | QSD or approved lower certification | Day-to-day implementation; conduct inspections; prepare REAPs; submit reports |

A single individual may hold both QSD and QSP roles.

## SWPPP Inspection Requirements

| Inspection Type | Timing | Who Conducts |
|----------------|--------|-------------|
| Pre-rain inspection | Before any qualifying storm event (forecast ≥ 0.5 inch/24 hours) | QSP |
| Post-storm inspection | Within 48 hours after storm event ends | QSP |
| Weekly inspection | Every 7 calendar days during rainy season (October 1 – April 30) when site is active | QSP |
| Non-rainy season | At least once every 30 calendar days for active sites | QSP |
| Final inspection | Before NOT (Notice of Termination) submitted | QSD or QSP |

Rainy season: **October 1 through April 30** (statewide default) [VERIFY regional variations in primary source]

## SWPPP Amendments

SWPPP must be amended when:
- Project scope changes (additional DSA, new phase)
- A BMP fails or proves inadequate during an inspection
- There is a discharge that causes or contributes to an exceedance of water quality standards
- Discharger receives notice of violation or inspection report requiring changes
- Risk Level changes

Amendments must be documented, dated, and uploaded to SMARTS. The LRP or QSD must certify material amendments.

## Minimum BMP Requirements by Risk Level

| BMP Category | RL1 | RL2 | RL3 |
|-------------|-----|-----|-----|
| Erosion controls | Required | Required | Required |
| Sediment controls | Required | Required | Required |
| Sediment basin (if > 5 ac draining to one outlet) | Required | Required | Required |
| REAP | Not required | Required | Required |
| ATS | Not required | May be required | Likely required |
| Numeric discharge monitoring | No | Yes | Yes |

## Sediment Basin Sizing

Where required, sediment basins must provide:
- Minimum detention volume: **3,600 cubic feet of storage per acre of drainage area** [VERIFY IN PRIMARY SOURCE]
- Outlet design must prevent short-circuiting
- Emergency spillway required

## Public Availability

The SWPPP (excluding certain confidential business information) must be made available to the public upon request within 10 days. The document is publicly viewable on SMARTS.
