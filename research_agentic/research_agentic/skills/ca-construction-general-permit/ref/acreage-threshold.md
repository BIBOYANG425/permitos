## Applicability: The Two-Trigger Test

Construction stormwater permit coverage under California's CGP is required when **either** of the following is true:

1. **Direct acreage trigger**: The project disturbs **one (1) or more acres** of soil through clearing, grading, grubbing, excavation, or any other soil-disturbing activity.
2. **Common plan of development (CPOD) trigger**: The project disturbs **less than one acre** but is part of a **larger common plan of development or sale** whose combined disturbance is one or more acres.

**Both triggers are independently sufficient** — a site meeting either one requires CGP coverage.

## Disturbance Area Calculation

"Disturbed soil area" (DSA) is the total land area where natural ground cover or existing pavement is removed, graded, or otherwise altered to expose underlying soil. Include:
- Clearing and grubbing footprint
- Grading and cut/fill areas
- Staging and laydown areas where soil is disturbed
- Access roads constructed for the project
- Utility trenches and bore pits

Do NOT exclude:
- Areas disturbed only temporarily
- Areas re-seeded or re-paved mid-project (count the peak simultaneous disturbed footprint [VERIFY IN PRIMARY SOURCE])

## Common Plan of Development Rule

The CPOD rule is the most frequently missed trigger. A common plan exists when:
- A single developer subdivides land and constructs homes/commercial structures across multiple phases
- Multiple contractors work on separate lots within a master-graded subdivision
- A phased infrastructure project (road widening, utility corridor) releases individual contracts
- A shopping center is developed lot-by-lot after a single master grading permit

**Key: the common plan boundary is the master site plan or the original subdivision map**, not the individual contractor's work order. If parcels share a common developer, plan, or approval, aggregate all disturbance.

**Worked Example**: A 5-lot subdivision graded under a single tentative map. Each lot = 0.25 acres. Total = 1.25 acres → CGP required for ALL lots, even if sold to separate builders for individual construction.

## Exemptions from CGP Coverage

| Exemption | Conditions | Citation |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| De minimis disturbance | < 1 acre AND not part of CPOD | CGP Section II.A |
| Agricultural land management practices | Certain farm operations (check CGP Appendix) | CGP Section II.B; 40 CFR 122.26(b)(14) |
| Routine maintenance | Restoring original line, grade, and capacity of drainage facility | [VERIFY IN PRIMARY SOURCE] |
| Emergency projects | Granted by Regional Water Board — temporary | CGP Section II.C [VERIFY IN PRIMARY SOURCE] |

Note: There is NO blanket exemption for infill projects, brownfield redevelopment, or LEED projects.

## Pre-Application Checklist

1. Calculate total DSA including all CPOD parcels
2. Compare to 1-acre threshold
3. If < 1 acre, confirm no common plan applies (check master grading permit, subdivision map, developer identity)
4. Identify project Risk Level before NOI submission (see risk-levels.md)
5. Register in SMARTS (see smarts-reporting.md) and submit NOI
6. Develop SWPPP before any ground disturbance (see swppp-requirements.md)
