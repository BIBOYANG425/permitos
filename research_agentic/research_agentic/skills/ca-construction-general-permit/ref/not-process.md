## NOI: Notice of Intent to Obtain Permit Coverage

### Who Must File
The **Legally Responsible Person (LRP)** — the owner of the land, the general contractor, or another party who assumes permit responsibility — must submit the NOI. Joint enrollees (owner + contractor) are allowed; both LRPs must certify.

### Filing System
All NOI submissions, amendments, and reports are filed through **SMARTS** (Stormwater Multiple Application and Report Tracking System):
- Portal: https://smarts.waterboards.ca.gov/
- Registration: each LRP must create an account and register the project
- Supporting documents (SWPPP, site map, Risk Level calculation) must be uploaded at time of NOI

### NOI Required Information
- Project name, address, APN, and legal description
- Total disturbed soil area (DSA) in acres
- Estimated project start and end dates
- Receiving water name and 303(d) status
- Risk Level (1, 2, or 3) with calculation documentation
- Nearest upstream USGS HUC 12 Watershed ID [VERIFY IN PRIMARY SOURCE]
- QSD name, certification type, and number
- QSP name (if different from QSD)
- SWPPP upload (required before NOI is accepted)

### NOI Timing

| Scenario | Filing Deadline |
|---------|----------------|
| Standard new project | Submit and receive WDID before any ground disturbance |
| Emergency projects | As soon as practicable (notify Regional Water Board immediately) |
| Projects already underway without coverage | Immediately — late enrollment does not eliminate liability for un-permitted discharge |

**WDID (Waste Discharger Identification Number)**: Assigned by the State Water Board after NOI acceptance. Must be posted at the construction entrance and used on all correspondence.

## NOI Amendments

File an amended NOI in SMARTS when:
- Project boundary expands (new acreage disturbed)
- LRP changes (ownership transfer — see below)
- Risk Level determination changes
- Receiving water changes due to project redesign

## Permit Transfer (Change of LRP)

When the property is sold or project responsibility transfers:
1. The new LRP (transferee) must file a new NOI in SMARTS before assuming control
2. The original LRP may file a NOT upon confirmed coverage by the new LRP — otherwise both remain jointly liable
3. Partial transfers (one phase sold) require careful coordination — both LRPs may need simultaneous coverage

## NOT: Notice of Termination

### Eligibility Requirements — ALL Must Be Met

A project may not file a NOT until all of the following conditions are satisfied:

| Condition | Requirement |
|-----------|------------|
| Final stabilization | All soil-disturbing activities complete AND entire site has achieved 70% vegetative cover of its pre-disturbance condition OR equivalent permanent physical stabilization (e.g., concrete, asphalt) [VERIFY EXACT COVER % IN PRIMARY SOURCE] |
| Temporary BMPs removed | All temporary BMPs (silt fence, fiber rolls, inlet protection) removed and disposed of properly |
| Permanent BMPs operational | All permanent stormwater control measures (detention basins, bioretention, inlet filters) installed, operational, and responsibility transferred to a long-term operator |
| Outstanding violations resolved | No pending enforcement actions or unresolved violations under the permit |
| Annual report filed | The final annual report for the final year of coverage must be submitted before NOT |
| Discharge requirements met | No ongoing non-stormwater discharges that would require separate coverage |

### NOT Filing Process
1. Confirm all stabilization criteria are met
2. Conduct final site inspection (QSD or QSP)
3. Prepare final inspection documentation
4. Log into SMARTS and submit NOT with:
   - Certification that all NOT conditions are satisfied
   - Date of final stabilization
   - Description of permanent BMPs installed
5. NOT becomes effective when accepted by the State Water Board [VERIFY — some permits auto-accept; confirm current practice]

### Partial NOT
Where a project has multiple phases and one phase is fully stabilized, a partial NOT may be available to reduce acreage under permit and potentially reduce annual fees. [VERIFY availability and process in CGP Section VI or SMARTS user guide]

### Consequences of Premature NOT
If NOT is filed before all conditions are met:
- Continued construction activity without permit coverage is a violation of the CGP and CWA
- Potential for Notice of Violation (NOV), Administrative Civil Liability (ACL), and referral to Regional Water Board
- Third-party citizen suits under CWA Section 505 are possible
