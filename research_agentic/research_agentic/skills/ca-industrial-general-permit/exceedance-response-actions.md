# CA Industrial General Permit — Exceedance Response Actions (ERAs)

**Permit:** Order 2014-0057-DWQ (amended by Orders 2015-0122-DWQ and 2018-0028-DWQ)
**NPDES No.:** CAS000001
**Source:** IGP Order 2014-0057-DWQ, Section XII (Exceedance Response Actions), pp. 48–55

---

## Overview of the ERA Framework

The ERA system creates a three-tier status for each monitored parameter at each facility:

1. **Baseline Status** — All new Dischargers begin here. No NAL exceedances have occurred.
2. **Level 1 Status** — Triggered when an NAL exceedance occurs while at Baseline.
3. **Level 2 Status** — Triggered when an NAL exceedance occurs while already at Level 1.

Status is parameter-specific: a facility can be at Level 2 for TSS and Baseline for pH simultaneously.

> "At the beginning of a Discharger's NOI Coverage, all Dischargers have Baseline status for all parameters." (Section XII.B)

---

## NAL Exceedance Types (Section XII.A)

### Annual NAL Exceedance
Occurs when the **annual average** concentration for a parameter (computed across all QSE samples in the reporting year for the entire facility) exceeds the Annual NAL in Table 2.

### Instantaneous Maximum NAL Exceedance
Occurs when **two (2) or more** individual sample results for any single parameter within a reporting year exceed the Instantaneous Maximum NAL value, or are outside the pH range.

## Level 1 ERA — Triggered by First NAL Exceedance (Section XII.C)

### Trigger
> "A Discharger's Baseline status for any given parameter shall change to Level 1 status if sampling results indicate an NAL exceedance for that same parameter." (Section XII.C)

Level 1 status commences on **July 1 following the reporting year** in which the exceedance occurred.

### Level 1 ERA Evaluation — Due by October 1 (Section XII.C.1)

By **October 1** following commencement of Level 1 status, the Discharger shall:

a. Complete an evaluation, **with the assistance of a QISP**, of the industrial pollutant sources at the facility that are or may be related to the NAL exceedance(s); and

b. Identify in the evaluation the corresponding BMPs in the SWPPP and any additional BMPs and SWPPP revisions necessary to prevent future NAL exceedances.

The evaluation may focus on the drainage areas where the NAL exceedance(s) occurred, but **all drainage areas shall be evaluated**.

### Level 1 ERA Report — Due by January 1 (Section XII.C.2)

Based upon the evaluation, as soon as practicable but no later than **January 1 following commencement of Level 1 status**, the Discharger shall:

i. **Revise the SWPPP** as necessary and implement any additional BMPs identified in the evaluation;

ii. **Certify and submit via SMARTS a Level 1 ERA Report** prepared by a QISP that includes:
   1. A summary of the Level 1 ERA Evaluation; and
   2. A detailed description of the SWPPP revisions and any additional BMPs for each parameter that exceeded an NAL.

iii. **Certify and submit via SMARTS the QISP's identification number, name, and contact information** (telephone number, email address).

### Returning to Baseline from Level 1

A Discharger's Level 1 status for a parameter will **return to Baseline** once:
- A Level 1 ERA report has been completed,
- All identified additional BMPs have been implemented, and
- Results from **four (4) consecutive QSEs** sampled subsequent to BMP implementation indicate **no additional NAL exceedances** for that parameter.

### NAL Exceedances Prior to Level 1 BMP Implementation

Prior to implementation of additional BMPs identified in the Level 1 ERA Evaluation or October 1 (whichever comes first), sampling results for parameters being addressed by those BMPs will **not** be included in annual average or instantaneous NAL exceedance calculations in SMARTS.

## Level 2 ERA — Triggered by NAL Exceedance While at Level 1 (Section XII.D)

### Trigger
> "A Discharger's Level 1 status for any given parameter shall change to Level 2 status if sampling results indicate an NAL exceedance for that same parameter while the Discharger is in Level 1." (Section XII.D)

Level 2 status commences on **July 1 following the reporting year** in which the Level 1 NAL exceedance(s) occurred.

### Level 2 ERA Action Plan — Due by January 1 (Section XII.D.1)

Dischargers with Level 2 status shall certify and submit via SMARTS a **Level 2 ERA Action Plan** prepared by a QISP that:

- Addresses each new Level 2 NAL exceedance by **January 1** following the reporting year in which the NAL exceedance(s) occurred.
- Identifies which demonstration(s) (see Section XII.D.2.a through c) the Discharger has selected to perform.
- Addresses the drainage areas with corresponding Level 2 NAL exceedances.
- Includes a **schedule and detailed description of the tasks** required to complete the selected demonstration(s).

> "All elements of the Level 2 ERA Action Plan shall be implemented as soon as practicable and completed **no later than 1 year** after submitting the Level 2 ERA Action Plan." (Section XII.D.1.d)

### Level 2 ERA Technical Report — Due January 1 of Following Year (Section XII.D.2)

> "On January 1 of the reporting year following the submittal of the Level 2 ERA Action Plan, a Discharger with Level 2 status shall certify and submit a **Level 2 ERA Technical Report** prepared by a QISP that includes one or more of the following demonstrations:" (Section XII.D.2)

The Discharger must choose one or more of three demonstration pathways:

#### a. Industrial Activity BMPs Demonstration
- A description of industrial pollutant sources and corresponding industrial pollutants related to the NAL exceedance(s);
- An evaluation of all pollutant sources associated with industrial activity;
- Where all implemented BMPs achieve compliance with effluent limitations and are expected to eliminate future NAL exceedances: a description and analysis of all implemented BMPs;
- Where BMPs achieve compliance but are NOT expected to eliminate future NAL exceedances: additionally provide:
  1. An evaluation of any additional BMPs that would reduce or prevent NAL exceedances;
  2. Estimated costs of the additional BMPs evaluated; and
  3. An analysis describing the basis for the selection of BMPs implemented in lieu of additional BMPs evaluated but not implemented.
- If an alternative design storm standard for treatment control BMPs will achieve compliance: provide an analysis describing the basis for the alternative design storm standard.

#### b. Non-Industrial Pollutant Source Demonstration
The Discharger must demonstrate that the NAL exceedance is attributable solely to non-industrial pollutant sources (storm water run-on from adjacent properties, aerial deposition, or on-site non-industrial sources). This shall include:
- A statement identifying the non-industrial source;
- A statement that all potential commingled pollutant sources have been identified and evaluated;
- A description of any on-site industrial pollutant sources contributing to the NAL exceedance;
- An assessment of relative contributions from run-on vs. industrial activity;
- A summary of all existing BMPs for that parameter; and
- An evaluation of all on-site/off-site monitoring data demonstrating the source.

#### c. Natural Background Pollutant Source Demonstration
The Discharger must demonstrate that the NAL exceedance is attributable solely to natural background pollutants not disturbed by industrial activities. This shall include:
- A statement that the NAL exceedance is attributable to natural background;
- A summary of previously collected data describing natural background pollutant levels;
- A summary of relevant research and published literature;
- A map showing the reference site location;
- Reference site and test site elevation;
- Available geology and soil information;
- Photographs showing site vegetation;
- Site reconnaissance survey data regarding presence of roads, outfalls, or other human-made structures; and
- Records from relevant state or federal agencies indicating no known mining, forestry, or other human activities upstream of the proposed reference site.

### Level 2 Technical Report Submittal and Updates (Section XII.D.3)

- The Level 2 ERA Technical Report is certified and submitted via SMARTS.
- The Water Boards may review and reject the Technical Report and direct further action.
- Dischargers who have submitted a Level 2 Technical Report are **only required to annually update** it based on additional NAL exceedances of the same parameter and same drainage area, or if facility changes occur.
- The Level 2 ERA Technical Report **shall be certified and submitted via SMARTS with each Annual Report**.
- If there are no changes warranting re-submittal, the Discharger certifies this in the Annual Report.

### Level 2 Implementation Extension (Section XII.D.5)

Dischargers needing additional time to submit the Level 2 ERA Technical Report may apply for a single automatic extension of **up to 6 months** by submitting through SMARTS:
- Reasons for the time extension;
- A revised Level 2 ERA Action Plan with updated schedule; and
- A description of any additional temporary BMPs to be implemented.

Extensions totaling more than 6 months require written approval from the Water Boards.

---

## Returning to Baseline from Level 2 (Section XII.D.4)

Dischargers who submit an **Industrial Activity BMPs Demonstration** and implement BMPs to prevent future NAL exceedances shall return to Baseline status if **four (4) subsequent consecutive QSEs** indicate no additional NAL exceedances for that parameter.

Dischargers are **ineligible to return to Baseline** if they submitted:
- An Industrial Activity BMP Demonstration showing BMPs are not expected to eliminate future NAL exceedances (Section XII.D.2.a.iv);
- A Non-Industrial Pollutant Source Demonstration; or
- A Natural Background Pollutant Source Demonstration.

## ERA Timeline Summary

| Event | Deadline |
|-------|----------|
| NAL exceedance occurs in reporting year | Identified via SMARTS |
| Level 1 / Level 2 status commences | July 1 following the exceedance year |
| Level 1 ERA Evaluation complete | October 1 following status commencement |
| Level 1 ERA Report (QISP-prepared) submitted via SMARTS | January 1 following status commencement |
| Level 2 ERA Action Plan submitted via SMARTS | January 1 following status commencement |
| Level 2 ERA Action Plan elements implemented | No later than 1 year after Action Plan submittal |
| Level 2 ERA Technical Report submitted via SMARTS | January 1 of the year following Action Plan submittal |
| Level 2 ERA Technical Report updated with Annual Report | July 15 annually |

---

## QISP Requirement

All Level 1 ERA Reports and Level 2 ERA Action Plans and Technical Reports must be **prepared by a QISP** (Qualified Industrial Storm Water Practitioner). The QISP's credential number, name, telephone number, and email address must be submitted via SMARTS.

Compliance Group Leaders prepare consolidated Level 1 ERA Reports and individual Level 2 ERA Action Plans/Technical Reports for their Compliance Group Participants (Sections XIV.B.3–6).
