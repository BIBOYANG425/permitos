# CA Industrial General Permit — Annual Report Requirements

**Permit:** Order 2014-0057-DWQ (amended by Orders 2015-0122-DWQ and 2018-0028-DWQ)
**NPDES No.:** CAS000001
**Effective:** July 1, 2015 (2018 amendments effective July 1, 2020)
**Expires:** June 30, 2020 (permit re-adoption in progress as of 2026)
**Source:** IGP Order 2014-0057-DWQ, Section XVI (Annual Report), pp. 59–60

---

## Due Date

> "The Discharger shall certify and submit via SMARTS an Annual Report no later than **July 15th** following each reporting year using the standardized format and checklists in SMARTS." (Section XVI.A)

The reporting year runs **July 1 through June 30**. The Annual Report covers the preceding 12-month period and is due by July 15 of each year.

**Note:** The older Order 97-03-DWQ required annual reports by July 1. The current permit shifted the deadline to July 15 to give Dischargers additional time.

---

## Submission Platform: SMARTS

All Annual Reports must be **electronically certified and submitted via SMARTS** (Storm Water Multiple Application and Report Tracking System), located at smarts.waterboards.ca.gov.

> "This General Permit requires that the Discharger certify and submit all Permit Registration Documents (PRDs) for Notice of Intent (NOI) and No Exposure Certification (NEC) coverage via the State Water Board's Storm Water Multiple Application and Report Tracking System (SMARTS) website." (Section I.17)

Submissions may be made by the Discharger directly or by a designated **Duly Authorized Representative**.

---

## Required Contents of the Annual Report (Section XVI.B)

The Discharger shall include in the Annual Report:

1. **A Compliance Checklist** that indicates whether a Discharger complies with, and has addressed all applicable requirements of this General Permit;

2. **An explanation for any non-compliance** of requirements within the reporting year, as indicated in the Compliance Checklist;

3. **An identification, including page numbers and/or sections, of all revisions made to the SWPPP** within the reporting year; and,

4. **The date(s) of the Annual Evaluation.**

---

## Annual Comprehensive Facility Compliance Evaluation (Annual Evaluation) — Section XV

The Discharger must conduct **one Annual Evaluation per reporting year** (July 1 to June 30), conducted no fewer than 8 months and no more than 16 months after the previous Annual Evaluation. The Annual Evaluation must at minimum include:

- A. A review of all sampling, visual observation, and inspection records conducted during the previous reporting year;
- B. An inspection of all areas of industrial activity and associated potential pollutant sources for evidence of, or the potential for, pollutants entering the storm water conveyance system;
- C. An inspection of all drainage areas previously identified as having no exposure to industrial activities and materials;
- D. An inspection of equipment needed to implement the BMPs;
- E. An inspection of any BMPs;
- F. A review and effectiveness assessment of all BMPs for each area of industrial activity to determine if the BMPs are properly designed, implemented, and are effective; and,
- G. An assessment of any other factors needed to comply with the requirements in Section XVI.B.

The SWPPP shall be revised as appropriate, and revisions implemented within **90 days** of the Annual Evaluation.

---

## Sampling Data Reporting (Section XI.B.11)

Analytical results from storm water sampling must be submitted via SMARTS separately from (and in addition to) the Annual Report:

> "The Discharger shall submit all sampling and analytical results for all individual or Qualified Combined Samples via SMARTS **within 30 days** of obtaining all results for each sampling event." (Section XI.B.11.a)

SMARTS automatically calculates running annual averages and compares them to NAL values.

---

## ERA-Related Submittals Tied to Annual Report Cycle

Dischargers with Level 2 status must submit or update the **Level 2 ERA Technical Report** prepared by a QISP concurrently with each Annual Report (Section XII.D.3.c). If there are no changes warranting re-submittal, the Discharger certifies this fact in the Annual Report itself.

## Monitoring Reporting Period Reference

| Period | Dates |
|--------|-------|
| First half of reporting year | July 1 – December 31 |
| Second half of reporting year | January 1 – June 30 |
| Annual Report due date | July 15 (following the June 30 close) |

---

## Key Personnel: QISP Requirement

The Annual Evaluation and any ERA reports must be prepared with QISP (Qualified Industrial Storm Water Practitioner) involvement. The QISP's identification number, name, telephone number, and email address must be certified and submitted via SMARTS.

---

## Inactive Mining Operations Exception (Section XIII)

Dischargers who have certified their inactive mining operation under Section XIII are **not subject** to Annual Report requirements in Section XVI.B.4, provided they meet all conditions in Section XIII.A.
