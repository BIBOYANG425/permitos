# CA Industrial General Permit — Numeric Action Level (NAL) Values

**Permit:** Order 2014-0057-DWQ (amended by Orders 2015-0122-DWQ and 2018-0028-DWQ)
**NPDES No.:** CAS000001
**Source:** IGP Order 2014-0057-DWQ, Table 2 (p. 43) and Section XI (Monitoring)

---

## Overview

The IGP does not use the term "benchmark values." Instead, it uses **Numeric Action Levels (NALs)**, which serve the equivalent function: they are the numeric thresholds against which storm water sample results are compared. Exceeding an NAL triggers Exceedance Response Actions (ERAs).

There are two types of NAL exceedances (Section XII.A):

- **Annual NAL exceedance:** The average concentration for a parameter across all samples in the reporting year exceeds the Annual NAL.
- **Instantaneous Maximum NAL exceedance:** Two or more individual samples in a reporting year exceed the Instantaneous Maximum NAL (or are outside the pH range).

## Minimum Required Parameters (All Dischargers)

All Dischargers must monitor for:
- **Total Suspended Solids (TSS)** — Annual NAL: 100 mg/L; Instantaneous: 400 mg/L
- **Oil and Grease (O&G)** — Annual NAL: 15 mg/L; Instantaneous: 25 mg/L
- **pH** — Instantaneous range: 6.0–9.0 (no annual NAL)

## How NAL Exceedances Are Determined (Section XII.A)

### Annual NAL Exceedance
> "An annual NAL exceedance occurs when the average of all the analytical results for a parameter from samples taken within a reporting year exceeds the annual NAL value for that parameter listed in Table 2." (Section XII.A.1)

The annual average is computed by SMARTS automatically from all qualifying storm event (QSE) samples submitted.

### Instantaneous Maximum NAL Exceedance
> "An instantaneous maximum NAL exceedance occurs when **two (2) or more** analytical results from samples taken for any single parameter within a reporting year exceed the instantaneous maximum NAL value (for TSS and O&G) or are outside of the instantaneous maximum NAL range for pH." (Section XII.A.2)

A single sample exceeding the instantaneous NAL does **not** by itself trigger ERA Level 1; a second exceedance in the same reporting year does.

## Sampling Requirements (Section XI.B)

### Qualifying Storm Event (QSE) Definition
A QSE is a precipitation event that:
- Produces a discharge for at least one drainage area; and
- Is preceded by 48 hours with no discharge from any drainage area.

### Sampling Frequency
- **Standard:** 2 QSEs in the first half (July 1–Dec 31) and 2 QSEs in the second half (Jan 1–June 30) = 4 QSEs per year minimum.
- **Compliance Group Participants:** 1 QSE per half-year = 2 QSEs minimum.
- **Sampling Frequency Reduction:** Dischargers with 4 consecutive QSEs with no NAL exceedances may reduce to 1 QSE per half-year.

### Sample Collection Timing
Samples must be collected within **4 hours** of the start of discharge (or start of facility operations if the QSE begins within the previous 12-hour period).

### Reporting Deadline
All sampling and analytical results must be submitted via SMARTS within **30 days** of obtaining all results for each sampling event.
