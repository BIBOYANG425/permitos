## SWPPP Requirement
All IGP enrollees must prepare and implement a **Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan (SWPPP)** before operations begin. The SWPPP must be:
- Kept on site and available for inspection
- Updated within 90 days of any change in operations/BMPs
- Available to local agencies and the Regional Board on request

## Minimum BMPs Required by IGP
1. **Good Housekeeping** — regular cleanup, dry sweeping instead of hosing, material storage under cover
2. **Preventive Maintenance** — regular inspection/maintenance of equipment and BMPs
3. **Spill/Leak Prevention** — containment for storage areas, drip pans, proper labeling
4. **Employee Training** — annual training on stormwater requirements, BMPs, emergency response
5. **Non-Stormwater Discharges** — identify and eliminate (or get separate permit for) all non-stormwater discharges
6. **Erosion/Sedimentation Controls** — where applicable (grading, construction areas)

## Industry-Specific BMPs
Certain SIC codes have additional BMP requirements in Attachment A of the IGP:
- SIC 5171 (petroleum bulk terminals): secondary containment, spill response plan
- SIC 4953 (refuse systems): leachate containment
- Vehicle maintenance: drip pans, covered fueling areas
