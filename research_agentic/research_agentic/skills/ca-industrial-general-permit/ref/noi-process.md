## Who Must File an NOI

Any operator of a facility with "storm water discharge associated with industrial activity" under 40 CFR 122.26(b)(14) — unless the facility qualifies for a NEC or is covered by a different NPDES permit — must file a Notice of Intent to obtain IGP coverage. The NOI is the mechanism for obtaining coverage under the statewide general permit.

The **operator** (not the property owner unless they also operate) is the responsible party. For multi-tenant industrial facilities, each tenant operating an industrial activity must determine whether they independently need IGP coverage.

## Pre-Filing Requirements

Before filing the NOI, the operator must:

1. **Determine the Permit Risk Level (Level 1, 2, or 3)** based on the Receiving Water Limitations (RWL) and site characteristics (see monitoring.md for Risk Level details).
2. **Develop a Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan (SWPPP)** — the SWPPP must be certified and on-site before or at the time of NOI submission. The IGP requires the SWPPP to be prepared and implemented before submitting the NOI [VERIFY IN PRIMARY SOURCE — some interpretations allow NOI first with SWPPP to follow within a defined period].
3. **Identify the Legally Responsible Person (LRP)** — this is the individual who certifies the NOI. For corporations this is typically a VP or higher; for LLCs a manager; for partnerships a general partner. The LRP must register in SMARTS.
4. **Identify a Duly Authorized Representative (DAR)** if the LRP wants to delegate electronic submission authority in SMARTS.

## NOI Filing Steps in SMARTS

```
Step 1: Register LRP account at https://smarts.waterboards.ca.gov
         → LRP receives SMARTS account confirmation via email

Step 2: Create Facility record
         → Enter facility name, address, SIC code, contact information
         → Confirm receiving water (storm drain, creek, or direct discharge)
         → System generates draft Waste Discharger ID (WDID) upon facility creation

Step 3: Complete NOI electronic form
         → Confirm coverage start date (date industrial activity begins or began)
         → Enter Risk Level determination (see monitoring.md)
         → Upload or link to SWPPP (some versions of SMARTS accept SWPPP summary)
         → Enter Annual Fee payment method information

Step 4: Pay Annual Fee
         → Fee is assessed for the first year upon NOI submission
         → See fees.md for current fee schedule

Step 5: LRP or DAR certifies and submits NOI
         → Electronic signature in SMARTS constitutes legal certification
         → False certification subjects certifier to federal/state penalty

Step 6: SMARTS issues WDID (Waste Discharger Identification number)
         → This is the facility's permit ID — 9-digit format: XX-XX-XXXXXX
         → WDID must appear on all reports, monitoring forms, SWPPP
```

## Timelines

| Trigger / Event | Deadline to File NOI |
|-----------------|----------------------|
| New facility commencing industrial activity | Before discharge begins [VERIFY — typically "prior to commencement of discharge"] |
| Existing facility that was previously covered (lapsed coverage) | Immediately; prior discharge without permit is violation |
| Facility that discovers retroactive IGP obligation | As soon as practicable; consult Regional Water Board for enforcement discretion |
| Change of operator (sale, lease assignment) | New operator must file new NOI; prior NOI does not transfer automatically |
| Change of industrial activity significantly affecting Risk Level | NOI amendment or new NOI may be required [VERIFY IN PRIMARY SOURCE] |

**Important**: The IGP's general prohibition is that no industrial stormwater discharges may occur without permit coverage. Filing the NOI late does not erase the unpermitted discharge period — enforcement risk remains for the gap period.

## SWPPP Certification Requirement

The SWPPP must be signed by the LRP (not the consultant who prepared it). Certification language is approximately: "I certify under penalty of law that this document and all attachments were prepared under my direction or supervision in accordance with a system designed to assure that qualified personnel properly gather and evaluate the information submitted…"

The SWPPP must be maintained on-site or at a readily accessible location and made available to SWRCB or RWQCB inspectors on request.

## Change-of-Operator (Transfer) Process

When a facility is sold or the operator changes:

1. **Prior operator** must file a Notice of Termination (NOT) in SMARTS within 90 days of ceasing industrial operations [VERIFY IN PRIMARY SOURCE].
2. **New operator** must file a new NOI before or when they begin industrial activity.
3. There is no automatic transfer of IGP coverage.
4. If new operator discovers old NOI and no NOT was filed, they should file their own NOI and the prior operator should file a retroactive NOT.

## Notice of Termination (NOT)

To terminate IGP coverage when industrial operations cease:

- File NOT in SMARTS
- SWPPP must be retained for 5 years after NOT filing [VERIFY IN PRIMARY SOURCE]
- Outstanding Annual Reports and monitoring data must be filed for all reporting periods prior to termination
- Fees for the year of termination are generally not refunded [VERIFY IN PRIMARY SOURCE]

## Common NOI Errors and Traps

1. **Wrong SIC code entered**: SMARTS does not validate SIC codes; an incorrect SIC can affect Risk Level determination and enforcement.
2. **LRP not senior enough**: Using a consultant or staff member as LRP without proper delegation violates certification requirements.
3. **SWPPP not ready at filing**: If SWPPP is not complete, the NOI may be filed but BMPs must be implemented before discharge occurs.
4. **Receiving water misidentified**: The receiving water affects the Risk Level; getting this wrong leads to incorrect monitoring requirements.
5. **Old WDID reuse**: If a prior tenant had a WDID for the same address, do not reuse it — file a new NOI.
