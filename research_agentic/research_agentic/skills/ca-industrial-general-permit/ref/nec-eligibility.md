## What the NEC Does

A No Exposure Certification is a conditional exclusion from NPDES stormwater permit coverage under CWA § 402(l)(2) and 40 CFR 122.26(g). A facility with a valid NEC is not required to obtain IGP coverage, file an NOI, develop a SWPPP, or submit monitoring data — provided all industrial materials and activities remain protected from precipitation and runoff.

The NEC is **not** a permit exemption; it is an exclusion from the permit requirement itself. If exposure conditions change, the NEC must be revoked and an NOI filed within the IGP's applicable timeframe.

## NEC Eligibility Criteria — The "No Exposure" Test

All of the following must be true for NEC eligibility (40 CFR 122.26(g)(1)):

| Condition | What "No Exposure" Means |
|-----------|--------------------------|
| Industrial materials | All raw materials, finished products, by-products, intermediate products, waste materials are stored inside buildings or under permanent cover |
| Industrial equipment | All machinery, equipment used in industrial processes is stored inside or under permanent cover |
| Vehicle maintenance areas | No vehicle maintenance (fueling, washing, repairs) occurs outside — OR all maintenance areas are covered with no stormwater contact |
| Industrial areas | Loading docks, transfer areas, staging areas all have overhead cover with no stormwater contact |
| Discharge paths | No floor drains, catch basins, or drains in industrial areas discharge stormwater to surface waters |
| Waste management | Dumpsters and containers for industrial wastes are under cover or otherwise protected from precipitation |

**"Permanent cover" means** a building or permanent structure with a roof; tarps, awnings, or temporary covers that can blow open do NOT qualify as permanent cover [VERIFY IN PRIMARY SOURCE for current SWRCB definition].

## Who CANNOT Obtain a NEC

Certain facility types are categorically ineligible for the NEC regardless of actual exposure:

- **Hazardous waste treatment, storage, and disposal facilities (TSDFs)** with RCRA permits (category iii)
- **Steam electric power generating facilities** (category vi)
- **Standard Industrial Classification (SIC) codes for:**
  - Facilities with coal piles
  - Coke production facilities
  - Cement manufacturing (SIC 3241)
  - Asphalt concrete/paving facilities (SIC 2951, 2952)
  - Airports with de-icing programs (SIC 4512, 4581) [VERIFY IN PRIMARY SOURCE]

## NEC Filing Process in SMARTS

1. **Log into SMARTS** (Stormwater Multiple Application and Report Tracking System): https://smarts.waterboards.ca.gov
2. **Create a facility record** if new, or search for existing facility.
3. **Complete NEC form** — certifier must be a duly authorized representative (DAR) of the facility operator.
4. **Certify conditions** — the form requires checking each of the no-exposure conditions; a false certification is a violation of the federal Clean Water Act and subjects the certifier to criminal liability.
5. **Submit** — no fee is required for an NEC under current IGP [VERIFY IN PRIMARY SOURCE for any current fee schedule changes].
6. **Confirmation** — SMARTS generates a NEC ID upon submission; the facility is considered excluded from that point forward unless SWRCB rejects.

## Inspections and Enforcement

The SWRCB and Regional Water Quality Control Boards (RWQCBs) can inspect NEC facilities at any time. If inspectors find industrial materials exposed to stormwater at a certified NEC facility, the facility may be cited for an unpermitted discharge — a significant enforcement exposure because the facility had no SWPPP or BMPs in place.

**Practical note**: Facilities in industrial parks where neighbors have IGP coverage are sometimes inspected during IGP inspections of adjacent facilities. NEC certifiers should maintain a documented inspection log showing all materials are protected.
