## Overview of Recordkeeping Obligations

Rule 222 registrants must maintain records sufficient to demonstrate that:
1. Each solvent in use meets the VOC content limit for its equipment category.
2. Annual solvent throughput (gallons/year per cleaning unit) remains within the registered quantity or, if unbounded, is tracked for emission calculations.
3. All operating requirements (covers, freeboard ratios, control equipment) are in place and functioning.
4. Equipment type has not changed without re-registration.

## Required Records — Minimum Set

### A. Solvent Identity and VOC Content Records

| Record Item | Detail Required | Retention |
|---|---|---|
| Solvent product name | Full commercial name and part number | 5 years |
| Manufacturer / supplier | Company name and contact | 5 years |
| SDS date | Most recent Safety Data Sheet date | Keep current SDS + prior version |
| VOC content (g/L) | From SDS Section 9 or lab analysis | 5 years |
| Exempt compounds listed | Identify any CARB-exempt compounds excluded from VOC calculation | 5 years |

Key rule: whenever a solvent product is changed or reformulated, a new SDS must be obtained and the VOC content recalculated **before** the new solvent is put into service.

### B. Throughput / Usage Records

| Record Item | How to Track | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Solvent purchased (gallons) | Purchase invoices, delivery receipts | Each delivery |
| Solvent added to each machine | Log by machine/tank ID | Each fill event |
| Solvent disposed / recycled (gallons) | Waste manifests or recycler receipts | Each disposal event |
| Calculated annual usage | Purchases + beginning inventory − ending inventory − waste off-site | Annually (calendar year or fiscal year) |

> Tip: Material balance approach (purchases − waste − ending inventory = consumption) is acceptable and easily auditable from purchasing records.

### C. Equipment Description Records

- Equipment type (cold cleaner, vapor degreaser, conveyorized, etc.)
- Tank dimensions (length × width × depth in inches or cm) — used to verify freeboard ratio
- Year installed / registration date
- Control equipment installed (covers, refrigerated freeboard chiller, carbon adsorber, etc.) with manufacturer and model
- Maintenance log for control equipment (chiller temperature readings, carbon replacement dates)

### D. Freeboard Ratio Verification Log (Vapor Degreasers)

Freeboard ratio = (distance from liquid surface to tank lip) ÷ (smaller of tank length or width).

Operators must periodically confirm the freeboard ratio is maintained:

| Measurement | Minimum Acceptable | Log Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Freeboard depth (inches) | ≥ 0.75 × tank width [VERIFY ratio in primary source] | Monthly or after solvent additions |
| Refrigerated chiller temperature | ≤ [VERIFY: typically −15°C to 0°C depending on solvent] | Each operating shift |

### E. Complaint / Malfunction Log

- Log any equipment malfunction that could increase VOC emissions (broken cover, chiller failure, loss of refrigerant).
- Log corrective action taken and date resolved.
- SCAQMD Rule 430 requires breakdown notifications for excess emissions — confirm whether a Rule 222 malfunction requires notification if VOC limits are exceeded during the event. [VERIFY in Rule 430 and primary source]

## Reporting Requirements

Rule 222 is primarily a **registration + self-certification** program; ongoing **routine reports** to SCAQMD are generally NOT required unless:

- Annual throughput significantly exceeds the registered estimate — operator must amend registration.
- Equipment type changes — amendment required within [VERIFY: typically 30 days of change].
- Inspection-triggered reporting: if SCAQMD inspector identifies a violation, a compliance schedule or written response may be required.

SCAQMD may conduct periodic compliance inspections (announced or unannounced). Records must be available for inspector review at the facility.

## Record Retention Period

- **5 years** from the date the record was created (standard SCAQMD requirement under Rule 109).
- Records must be kept at the facility or at a designated off-site location with same-day retrieval capability.
- Electronic records (spreadsheets, PDFs) are acceptable if legible and organized.

## Suggested Recordkeeping System

A simple compliance binder or shared drive folder organized as follows minimizes inspection risk:

```
/Rule-222-Compliance/
  /SDSs/                   ← Current SDS for each solvent in use; archive prior SDSs
  /Usage-Logs/             ← Annual spreadsheet: solvent purchases, additions, disposals
  /Equipment-Forms/        ← Copy of filed registration form(s); SCAQMD acknowledgment
  /Freeboard-Logs/         ← Monthly freeboard measurements for vapor degreasers
  /Control-Equipment/      ← Chiller maintenance, carbon change log
  /Inspection-Reports/     ← SCAQMD inspection letters and responses
```

## Interaction with Other Recordkeeping Programs

| Program | Overlapping Record | Tip |
|---|---|---|
| HMBP (Hazardous Materials Business Plan) | Solvent inventory quantities | Use same inventory records; HMBP threshold is 55 gal liquid; Rule 222 records per-unit usage |
| Cal/OSHA SDS Access (Title 8 §5194) | SDS availability for workers | One SDS binder serves both; Rule 222 requires VOC content specifically documented |
| Hazardous Waste Generator (Title 22) | Waste solvent manifests | Same manifests; keep copies for 3 years (waste) and 5 years (Rule 222) — use 5-year retention for both |
| Title V Major Source (if applicable) | Annual emissions statements | If the facility is a Title V source, Rule 222 usage data feeds annual emissions inventory |

## Common Inspection Findings (What to Verify)

1. SDS on hand is outdated (product reformulated; VOC content higher than assumed).
2. No throughput log — inspector cannot verify solvent usage stayed within registered amount.
3. Freeboard measurements not taken — vapor degreaser may be non-compliant.
4. Control equipment not maintained — chiller off, cover broken.
5. Solvent changed without amending registration.
6. Different solvent than what is listed on the registration form being used.
