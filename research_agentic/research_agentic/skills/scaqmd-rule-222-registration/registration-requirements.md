## What Rule 222 Does

Rule 222 establishes a **registration pathway** for specified non-automotive solvent cleaning operations. Registration substitutes for a Permit to Construct (Rule 201) and Permit to Operate (Rule 203) for qualifying equipment. Registration is NOT an exemption — the operation is still regulated but through a streamlined administrative track.

## Applicability Decision Tree

```
Does the operation involve solvent cleaning?
  └─ No → Rule 222 does not apply
  └─ Yes: Is it automotive? (motor vehicle refinishing, body shops)
       └─ Yes → Not covered by Rule 222; see Rule 1171 or Rule 1151
       └─ No: Is the operation in one of the listed Rule 222 equipment categories?
            └─ No → Rule 201 Permit to Construct required
            └─ Yes: Does VOC usage fall within the registration threshold?
                 └─ Yes → Rule 222 registration applies
                 └─ No → May require full permit; verify with SCAQMD
```

## Equipment Categories Eligible for Registration

Rule 222 covers non-automotive solvent cleaning operations, which include (verify exact list in fetched PDF):

- Cold solvent cleaning machines (batch-loaded dip tanks, spray guns, wipe cleaning)
- Open-top vapor degreasers below the applicable size threshold
- Conveyorized degreasers (cold or hot)
- Squeegee-type cleaning operations
- Aerosol solvent cleaning (subject to VOC content limits)

Key distinction: "Non-automotive" means the substrate being cleaned is NOT a motor vehicle body or component at a vehicle refinishing facility. Industrial parts cleaning (metal fabrication, electronics, aerospace components) generally falls under Rule 222 scope.

## Registration Filing Requirements

To register under Rule 222, the operator must submit to SCAQMD:

1. **Completed Form 400-R** (Equipment Registration form) or the equivalent online submission via SCAQMD's eSMARTS portal — [VERIFY IN PRIMARY SOURCE: current form number]
2. Facility identification: AIN (Assessor Identification Number), facility address, contact information
3. Equipment description: number and type of cleaning units, size/capacity, operating schedule
4. Solvent identification: product name, manufacturer, SDS reference, VOC content (grams/liter or weight-percent)
5. Annual throughput estimate: gallons of solvent used per year, per cleaning machine
6. Control equipment description (if any): covers, freeboard ratios, refrigerated freeboard chillers
7. Signed certification that all Rule 222 operating requirements will be met

## When Registration Converts Back to Permit

A Rule 222 registration becomes invalid and a Rule 201 Permit to Construct is required if:

- The equipment type changes (e.g., adding a vapor degreaser that was not registered)
- Throughput increases beyond the registered annual volume by more than 10% [VERIFY IN PRIMARY SOURCE]
- The solvent is changed to one with higher VOC content that would violate Rule 222 VOC limits
- The operation adds air pollution control equipment requiring permit-level review
- The facility becomes a major source under Rule 201/202 thresholds for VOC

## Compliance Pathway Steps

1. Identify all solvent cleaning operations by type and location.
2. Confirm each operation is non-automotive.
3. Match each unit to a Rule 222 equipment category (fetch PDF, Section (b) Definitions and (c) Applicability).
4. Obtain SDS for each solvent in use — record VOC content in g/L.
5. Calculate annual VOC emissions = (gallons/year × solvent density × VOC weight fraction).
6. If within registration limits, complete eSMARTS online registration or paper Form 400-R.
7. Pay registration fee (see annual-fees.md).
8. Receive SCAQMD registration certificate — post or keep on file at facility.
9. Implement all Rule 222 operating and recordkeeping requirements before commencing or continuing operation.
