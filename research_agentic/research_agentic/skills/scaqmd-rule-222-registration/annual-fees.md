# SCAQMD Rule 222 — Registration Filing Fees

## What Rule 222 Is

Rule 222 provides an **alternative to a written permit** for specific emission sources that are exempt from a written permit under Rule 219. Instead of a Permit to Construct / Permit to Operate, owners/operators submit a filing containing:
1. A description of the emission source;
2. Data necessary to estimate emissions; and
3. Information to determine compliance with applicable rules and regulations.

A filing under Rule 222 is **not a permit** — it is a registration/notification requirement. However, the source must still operate in compliance with all applicable South Coast AQMD, state, and federal rules.

## Fee Schedule (Rule 301, Subdivision (u) — Fees for Non-permitted Emission Sources Subject to Rule 222)

Effective July 1, 2026 (Rule 301 as amended May 1, 2026):

| Fee Type | Amount |
|---|---|
| **Initial filing fee** (prior to operation of each emission source) | **$301.28** per emission source |
| **Annual renewal fee** (on annual re-filing date set by Executive Officer) | **$301.28** per emission source |
| **Change of owner/operator or location** (new filing required) | **$301.28** per emission source |

**Note:** Exception — non-retrofitted boilers have a different renewal fee structure (see Rule 301 for details).

### Fee Payment Rules

- Initial filing fee is **non-refundable and non-transferable**.
- The Executive Officer sets an annual re-filing date; owners/operators are notified at least 30 days before the due date by mail, electronic mail, or other electronic means.
- If the annual re-filing fee is not paid within **30 days after the due date**, the filing expires and is no longer valid.
- The owner/operator is notified of expiration and consequences of operating without a valid filing.

## Consolidated Filing for High-VOC Facilities (Rule 222(b)(2))

An additional Rule 222 filing obligation applies to facilities that:
- Are located at a **single facility** with **no written permit** for any other emission source, AND
- **Emit ≥4.0 tons VOC/calendar year** (or emitted ≥4.0 tons VOC in Fiscal Year July 1, 2006 – June 30, 2007) from:
  - (A) Printing operations exempt under Rule 219(d)(8)(A) and (d)(8)(G);
  - (B) Coating/adhesive/laminating equipment exempt under Rule 219(d)(12)(F) and (d)(12)(J); and/or
  - (C) Hand application of VOC-containing materials exempt under Rule 219(d)(15)(D).

A **single consolidated filing** covering all listed categories is required. Facilities above this threshold must also report VOC emissions under the Annual Emissions Reporting program (Rule 301).

## Compliance Requirements Under Rule 222 (Rule 222(d))

Owners/operators subject to Rule 222 must:
- **(A)** Comply with all applicable South Coast AQMD, state, and federal rules and regulations.
- **(B)** Comply with all operating conditions specified on the filing.
- **(C)** Submit required information to the South Coast AQMD in the Executive Officer's prescribed format, including: hours of operation, materials used/processed, fuel usage, throughput, and operating parameters. Change of location or change of owner/operator requires a new filing **before** operation.
- **(D)** Maintain and make available records upon request (hours, materials, fuel usage, throughput, operating parameters).
- **(E)** Pay all required fees pursuant to Rule 301.
- **(F)** Maintain a copy of the filing receipt on-site for the life of the equipment; make available upon request.
- **(G)** Maintain records for **3 years** sufficient to verify emissions and demonstrate compliance with operating conditions. Records may document compliance with daily emission limits for food ovens on a calendar monthly basis divided by 30.
- **(H)** Not remove air pollution control equipment unless replacement achieves equal or greater efficiency and is first approved in writing by the Executive Officer.
- **(I)** For facilities subject to (b)(2): report annual VOC emissions under the Annual Emissions Reporting program (Rule 301).

## Compliance Dates

- New sources (on or after effective date in Table I): must comply with requirements (A), (B), (C), (E), and (H) **before** installation/operation.
- Existing sources (installed before effective date, no current PO): must comply with all of subdivision (d) within **6 months** of effective date or when the source becomes subject to Rule 222.
- Existing sources (installed before effective date with a current PO): District notifies; permittee must comply within **60 days** of notification (PO is transferred to the filing system).
- Failure to comply is a **violation of Rule 222**.

## Exemptions from Rule 222 (Rule 222(f))

Rule 222 does **not** apply to:
1. Emission sources used exclusively in connection with a structure designed and used exclusively as a **dwelling for not more than four families**, where equipment is used by the owner or occupant; or
2. Emission sources with a **Permit to Operate** issued by South Coast AQMD.

## Agricultural Diesel Engine Additional Requirement (Rule 222(d)(2))

Owners/operators of agricultural sources subject to Rule 222 must also comply with CARB ATCM registration requirements for stationary diesel-fueled agricultural engines rated >50 bhp (California Code of Regulations, Title 17, Sections 93115.3(a) and 93115.8(c), as of April 7, 2023).
