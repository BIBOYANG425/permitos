The agent must still fetch the cited primary source and quote it verbatim: the skill ORIENTS, the fetched regulation is the PROOF. Never cite a SKILL.md as evidence. In particular, the common shorthand that "HMBP applies to all hazardous material storage" is overbroad — the threshold quantities below govern.

## Triggers

- A business handles or stores a hazardous material at or above a reporting threshold quantity.
- Aggregate on-site inventory of a hazardous material meets the per-state-of-matter threshold (liquid / solid / compressed gas).
- New or increased chemical inventory from the project change that pushes a material across its threshold.

## Thresholds

| State of matter | Expected threshold (at or above) | Primary source to fetch and quote |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Liquid | 55 gallons | California HMBP / CUPA program |
| Solid | 500 pounds | California HMBP / CUPA program |
| Compressed gas | 200 cubic feet (at standard temperature and pressure) | California HMBP / CUPA program |

## Exemptions

- Quantities below all applicable thresholds generally do not trigger an HMBP — confirm the exact threshold language and any aggregation rule in the fetched source.
- Certain materials and circumstances carry their own thresholds or exclusions (for example specified consumer products or pre-packaged retail goods) — verify against the fetched CUPA/HMBP guidance rather than assuming.
- Lower or facility-specific thresholds may be set by the local CUPA; the cited statewide guidance is the orientation, the local rule may be stricter.
