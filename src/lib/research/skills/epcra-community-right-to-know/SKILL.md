## Triggers

- A facility stores a hazardous chemical for which OSHA requires a Safety Data Sheet (SDS) at or above the reporting threshold (§311 SDS submission, §312 annual Tier II inventory).
- A facility has an Extremely Hazardous Substance (EHS) present at or above its Threshold Planning Quantity (§302 emergency planning notification).
- Reporting runs to the State Emergency Response Commission (SERC), Local Emergency Planning Committee (LEPC), and local fire department.

## Thresholds

| What to look for | Expected orientation | Primary source to fetch and quote |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Tier II hazardous chemical inventory | Hazardous chemical present at or above 10,000 lb at any one time during the year | EPA EPCRA program |
| Extremely Hazardous Substance | Present at or above its Threshold Planning Quantity (TPQ), or 500 lb — whichever is lower | EPA EPCRA program |
| §311 SDS reporting | SDS (or a list of SDS chemicals) submitted for chemicals over threshold | EPA EPCRA program |

## Exemptions

- Substances exempted from the OSHA HazCom SDS requirement (e.g. certain consumer products used as a consumer would, foods, drugs) are generally outside EPCRA §311/§312 — verify the specific exclusion against the fetched EPA text.
- Quantities below the applicable threshold (10,000 lb general; lower TPQ/500 lb for EHS) do not trigger Tier II reporting.
- EPCRA is federal and additive to California's HMBP; do not treat one as satisfying the other.
