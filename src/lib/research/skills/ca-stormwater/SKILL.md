## Triggers

- Construction activity that disturbs soil — compare disturbed acreage against the one-acre threshold (Construction General Permit, CGP).
- Smaller sites that are part of a larger common plan of development totaling one or more acres.
- Industrial facility whose SIC code (or the NAICS equivalent) falls in a category regulated by the Industrial General Permit (IGP).
- Outdoor industrial activity, material storage, or process areas exposed to stormwater.

## Thresholds

| What to look for | Expected orientation | Primary source to fetch and quote |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Construction stormwater (CGP) | Disturbance of one (1) or more acres of soil | Construction General Permit |
| Common plan of development | Smaller parcels aggregating to >= 1 acre still trigger coverage | Construction General Permit |
| Industrial stormwater (IGP) | Facility SIC/NAICS in a regulated industrial classification | Industrial General Permit |

## Exemptions

- Construction below one acre and not part of a larger common plan generally falls outside CGP coverage — confirm the acreage and common-plan facts against the fetched permit.
- A facility may file a No Exposure Certification (NEC) to be conditionally excluded from IGP coverage when industrial materials and activities are not exposed to stormwater — verify the exclusion conditions in the fetched source.
- An SIC/NAICS outside the regulated industrial categories is not covered by the IGP.
