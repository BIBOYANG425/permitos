## Triggers

- A stationary source whose potential-to-emit (PTE) reaches "major source" levels for one or more regulated air pollutants.
- A source subject to a standard (NSPS, NESHAP/MACT) that independently requires a Title V permit.
- A source in an ozone (or other) nonattainment area, where major-source thresholds are lower than the general 100 tons-per-year level.
- Note: Title V is an OPERATING permit and is distinct from a construction/preconstruction permit (the SCAQMD Permit to Construct). A project can need both.

## Thresholds

| What to look for | Expected orientation | Primary source to fetch and quote |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Major source (criteria pollutant) | Potential-to-emit at or above 100 tons per year of a regulated pollutant (lower in nonattainment areas) | EPA Title V operating permits |
| Major source (hazardous air pollutants) | 10 tons/year of a single HAP, or 25 tons/year of combined HAPs | EPA Title V operating permits |
| Permit obligation | A major source must obtain a Title V operating permit that consolidates its applicable requirements | EPA Title V operating permits |

## Exemptions

- Sources below major-source thresholds with no independent Title V trigger are generally not required to obtain a Title V permit (they may still need a district construction permit).
- Some source categories are deferred or exempted by rule; confirm against the fetched EPA guidance and the implementing district/state program before treating a source as exempt.
- A synthetic minor source may stay below major-source thresholds via federally enforceable permit limits — verify the limit exists in the fetched permit/source, do not assume it.
