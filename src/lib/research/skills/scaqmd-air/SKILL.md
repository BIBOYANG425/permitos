## Triggers

- New, altered, or replaced equipment that may emit air contaminants (Rule 201 — Permit to Construct).
- Coating booths, ovens, dryers, combustion units, or process equipment that vents to atmosphere.
- Solvent or coating use that emits volatile organic compounds (VOC) — check whether VOC content/usage drives additional review or a higher permit tier.
- Equipment that appears on the Rule 219 exempt list — confirm every listed condition is actually met before treating it as exempt.
- Equipment in a category Rule 222 names for registration rather than a full permit.

## Thresholds

| What to look for | Expected orientation | Primary source to fetch and quote |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Permit to Construct required | Building/erecting/installing/altering/replacing equipment that may emit air contaminants without written authorization is prohibited | Rule 201 |
| Rule 219 exemption | Equipment matches a listed category AND all listed conditions are satisfied | Rule 219 |
| Rule 222 registration path | Specified equipment category eligible to register instead of obtaining a permit | Rule 222 |
| VOC / solvent review | Confirm whether solvent VOC content or throughput triggers extra review under the Permit to Construct framework | Rule 201 |

## Exemptions

- Rule 219 lists equipment that may be exempt from written permit requirements — an exemption applies only when every listed condition for that equipment is met. Do not assume an exemption; verify against the fetched rule text.
- Rule 222 can substitute registration for a permit for specified categories; this is a different path, not an exemption from regulation.
- A failed exemption condition pushes the equipment back to the Rule 201 Permit to Construct requirement.
