## Triggers

- A process involves a chemical on the Appendix A list of highly hazardous chemicals at or above the listed threshold quantity (TQ).
- A process involves 10,000 pounds or more of a flammable liquid or flammable gas in one location.
- "Process" is read broadly: any activity involving a highly hazardous chemical, including storage, use, handling, or on-site movement, and interconnected/co-located vessels are aggregated.

## Thresholds

| What to look for | Expected orientation | Primary source to fetch and quote |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Listed highly hazardous chemical | Quantity at or above the Appendix A threshold quantity for that chemical | 29 CFR 1910.119 |
| Flammable liquids/gases | 10,000 lb or more in one location triggers PSM | 29 CFR 1910.119 |
| Covered process | A covered process must implement the full 14-element PSM program (PHA, operating procedures, MOC, etc.) | 29 CFR 1910.119 |

## Exemptions

- Retail facilities, oil/gas well drilling or servicing operations, and normally unoccupied remote facilities are excluded — confirm the specific exclusion against the fetched standard.
- Flammable liquids stored in atmospheric tanks or transferred below their normal boiling point without chilling/refrigeration may be excluded from the flammable-quantity trigger; verify against the rule text.
- Hydrocarbon fuels used solely for workplace consumption as a fuel (e.g. propane for comfort heating) are not covered if not part of a process containing another highly hazardous chemical.
